## HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT

Each role section follows the same structure:
1. **Daily/Weekly Rhythm** -- when things happen, not just what happens
2. **Before Tasks** -- prep work, often invisible and time-consuming
3. **After Tasks** -- follow-up that piles up and gets skipped
4. **Time-Sensitive Triggers** -- the sneaky deadlines that blindside people
5. **Stress & Anxiety Points** -- where burnout and mistakes actually come from
6. **Data Sources** -- what they live in and what they ignore
7. **Proactive AI Moments** -- concrete assistant messages, "how did it know?" style

## 1.1 SOCIAL WORKER

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Monday:** Case review week ahead; court reports due if in court week; supervision prep
- **Tues-Thurs:** Home visits, school visits, hospital checks, family meetings, inter-agency meetings
- **Friday:** Admin catch-up, urgent referrals, weekend safety planning for at-risk cases
- **Daily:** Email triage (referrals, school/hospital flags, police reports); case note entry; phone calls returned
- **Weekly:** Caseload review with supervisor; multidisciplinary team (MDT) meetings; court liaison
- Key rhythm: **Field-heavy Tue-Thu, admin-heavy Mon & Fri**. But crises don't care about rhythm.

### Before Tasks
- Review case files before every home visit -- check last contact date, any recent incidents, court orders in effect
- Check if any protection orders or legal conditions apply before visiting
- Confirm visit with family (or deliberately don't, if unannounced check needed)
- Pre-read school/GP/hospital notes for multi-party meetings
- Prepare risk assessment tools before initial assessments
- Brief supervisor on complex cases before making any recommendations

### After Tasks
- Case note must be written within 24-48 hours (often mandated; in statutory services, sometimes within same day)
- Referrals to other agencies logged and tracked
- Court report drafts sent to supervisor for sign-off
- Follow-up calls to families after crisis visits
- Escalation notes when risk level changes
- Update chronology (the running timeline of key events in a case -- legally critical)

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Court deadlines** -- reports due 5-7 days before hearing; hearing dates often confirmed late
- **Child protection conference dates** -- must have assessment completed and report circulated 3 days prior
- **Statutory visit frequencies** -- e.g., at-risk children must be visited every 10-15 days; missed visits = compliance breach
- **Initial assessment windows** -- referrals must be responded to within 24h (urgent) or 5 working days (standard)
- **Placement review dates** -- foster care/residential placements reviewed at set intervals (28 days, 3 months, 6 months)
- **Section 47 enquiry timelines** -- in UK child protection, strategy discussion must happen within 1 working day of referral
- **Care plan review dates** -- set in court orders; missing them triggers legal consequences
- **Supervision deadlines** -- mandatory supervision frequency (monthly in most services); undocumented supervision = risk

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Chronic caseload overload** -- UK average is 25+ cases against recommended 12-15
- **Emotional weight** -- child abuse, domestic violence, deaths; secondary trauma is real
- **Documentation burden** -- up to 80% of time can be admin; case notes, risk assessments, reports
- **Fear of missing something** -- one missed visit, one unseen risk, and the consequences are catastrophic
- **Court exposure** -- being cross-examined on their assessments and decisions
- **Duty cover** -- being rostered for "duty" means handling all incoming referrals that day; unpredictable and exhausting
- **Lack of inter-agency communication** -- schools, GPs, police all using different systems; piecing together a picture manually

### Data Sources
- **Case management systems:** LiquidLogic, ICS, Mosaic, Caredirector, Eclipse (UK); state systems in AU/US
- **Police intelligence:** CRIS reports, domestic violence flagging
- **School systems:** SIMS, Arbor
- **NHS/GP systems:** Summary Care Record (if access granted)
- **Court portals:** HMCTS for UK court listings
- **Email** -- still the primary inter-agency communication channel
- **Paper files** -- yes, still. Many services have hybrid systems.

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"Marcus Johnson's statutory visit is overdue -- last contact was 16 days ago and his visit frequency is every 14 days. Want me to slot it in tomorrow morning before your court prep?"**

> **"You have a child protection conference for the Okafor family in 4 days. Your report hasn't been started yet -- I found the last assessment in your case file. Want me to draft a structure using that as the base?"**

> **"You're on duty tomorrow. The last three duty rosters ran 6-8 new referrals before lunch. Want me to clear your afternoon of scheduled calls so you have capacity?"**

> **"The placement review for Tyler B. is in 8 days -- the foster carer's report and your review report both need to be circulated to the team 3 days before. That means both are due in 5 days. Starting?"**

> **"You haven't logged a supervision session this month. Your next supervision with Diane is tomorrow -- want me to pull your last three case updates into a prep note?"**

## 1.2 GOVERNMENT / PUBLIC SECTOR OFFICER

*(Covers: administrative officers, policy officers, compliance officers, licensing officers, grants officers across local/national government)*

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Monday:** Ministerial/senior brief prep; agenda review for week; inbox triage
- **Tues-Wed:** Core meetings, policy drafts, stakeholder engagement
- **Thursday:** Often internal reporting deadlines; cabinet/committee papers due
- **Friday:** Sign-off cycles; minister's office review; staff debrief
- **Daily:** Processing applications, responding to FOI requests, updating internal registers
- **Monthly/Quarterly:** KPI reporting cycles; budget reconciliation; audit preparation
- Rhythm varies wildly by department type. Licensing officers are processing-heavy; policy officers are draft-heavy; compliance officers are inspection-heavy.

### Before Tasks
- **Meetings:** Brief notes; agenda review; ministerial lines to take if media risk involved
- **Inspections:** Review prior audit findings; check compliance history; prepare checklist
- **Cabinet/committee papers:** Whole-of-government consultation (other agencies must sign off); timeline coordination
- **FOI responses:** Search and collate documents; legal team sign-off if sensitive
- **Grants:** Assess eligibility; prepare assessment panel materials; conflict of interest declarations

### After Tasks
- Cabinet paper decision -> communicate to relevant agencies + implement action plan
- Inspection -> write report within set timeframe; track remediation actions
- Meeting -> minutes, action register, follow-up to responsible owners
- Grant round -> notify applicants; execute funding agreements; set up reporting milestones
- FOI response -> log in FOI register; trigger any internal policy reviews if request reveals a gap

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Budget cycle dates** -- Mid-Year Economic Outlook, MYEFO, pre-budget submissions; dates known 12 months out but ignored until close
- **Cabinet paper timetables** -- cabinet secretary publishes timetable for the year; deadlines for lodgement vs. decision dates
- **Legislation sunset clauses** -- legislation with expiry dates that require renewal; notorious for being forgotten
- **Annual report period** -- government departments must table annual reports within set timeframes after financial year end
- **FOI statutory deadlines** -- typically 20-30 working days to respond; extensions require formal request
- **Regulatory compliance cycles** -- industries have annual licence renewal windows; officers must process within processing timeframes
- **Parliamentary sitting weeks** -- questions on notice (QoN) must be answered before next sitting day; can arrive with 48h notice
- **Grants reporting milestones** -- grantees must submit reports on schedule; officer must review and clear within set timeframe

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Ministerial sensitivity** -- any work touching the minister's office can become media-exposed instantly
- **Whole-of-government consultation** -- getting sign-off from 6 other agencies before a cabinet deadline is brutal
- **Institutional memory loss** -- policies change, staff turn over, nobody knows why the rule was written that way
- **FOI anxiety** -- public servants know their emails may be released; chills candid discussion
- **Compliance without clear guidance** -- regulations are often ambiguous; officers must make judgment calls that may later be scrutinised
- **Political context shifts** -- what was approved policy last month may be politically toxic today

### Data Sources
- **Records management:** TRIM/Content Manager, SharePoint, HPE Records Manager
- **Finance systems:** Oracle Financials, SAP, TechOne
- **Grants management:** SmartyGrants, Salesforce Government Cloud
- **Cabinet systems:** CABNET or equivalent state systems
- **FOI tracking registers:** Often built in Excel or basic sharepoint; rarely purpose-built
- **Legislation registers:** AustLII, Legislation.gov.au, UK legislation.gov.uk
- **Project tracking:** Project plans in Excel or MS Project; rarely consistent

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"The FOI request from Clarke & Co. lodged on the 3rd is due in 19 working days -- that's next Thursday. You haven't started the document search yet. Want me to flag it to the records team and draft the acknowledgement today?"**

> **"The Environmental Licensing Act Section 47 has a sunset clause that expires in 4 months. There's no renewal brief in progress. Should I flag this to your manager and draft a brief timeline for renewal?"**

> **"Cabinet timetable shows your policy paper needs to lodge in 6 days. You still need sign-off from Treasury and Infrastructure. Both are unreturned as of today. Want me to draft a follow-up email to both today?"**

> **"The Q3 KPI report is due to the Minister's office in 3 days. Your last internal data pull was 3 weeks ago. Want me to ping the relevant business units for updated numbers now?"**

> **"Parliament sits in 8 days. You have 3 questions on notice outstanding from last sitting week -- responses are due the day before Parliament sits. One of them (Q147 on grants data) may require data from Finance. Starting that now?"**

## 1.3 NGO / NON-PROFIT MANAGER

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Monday:** Team check-in; funding pipeline review; weekly priorities set
- **Tues-Wed:** Program delivery oversight; stakeholder meetings; grant reporting work
- **Thursday:** Finance reconciliation; board prep if meeting approaching; donor calls
- **Friday:** Staff issues; partner comms; reflection and planning
- **Monthly:** Management reports to board; financials signed off; funder reports due
- **Quarterly/Annually:** Grant acquittals; board meetings; strategic planning cycles
- NGO managers wear every hat: HR, finance, comms, fundraising, program delivery, compliance. Context-switching is constant.

### Before Tasks
- **Funder meetings:** Pull program statistics; success stories; financial progress against budget
- **Board meetings:** Prepare board pack (financials, program report, risks, any decisions required) -- typically 7-10 days before
- **Grant applications:** Needs data from programs team, financials from finance, sign-off from board chair
- **Program events:** Venue, transport, community notification, staff allocation, catering, accessibility
- **Staff supervision:** Review case or program notes; prep talking points if performance issue

### After Tasks
- Grant acquittals -- financial + narrative report submitted within 60-90 days of period end; receipts reconciled
- Board meetings -- minutes, resolution register, action items to relevant people
- Funder site visits -- follow-up report; any commitments made in the meeting documented
- Events -- debrief, attendance report to funder, thank-you comms, waitlist follow-up
- Staff issues -- documented properly; HR file updated; follow-up date set

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Grant acquittal deadlines** -- usually 30-90 days after grant period ends; late acquittals can jeopardise future funding
- **New grant round openings** -- funders publish EOI/application windows that open and close fast; community foundation rounds often run 2-3 weeks
- **Annual report deadline** -- often tied to AGM date (which itself has statutory notice requirements)
- **Auditor scheduling** -- end-of-financial-year audit must be completed before annual report; auditor availability is constrained; needs booking 2-3 months out
- **DGR/charity status renewals** -- annual information statements due to regulator (ACNC in AU, Charity Commission in UK)
- **Insurance renewal** -- community organisations often have complex insurance (public liability, professional indemnity, volunteer cover); renewal date creeps up
- **Staff certification renewals** -- working with children checks, first aid, mandatory reporter training; expiry is per-person, per date
- **Payroll compliance** -- superannuation, award rates; annual increases often apply from 1 July; easy to miss

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Funding precarity** -- often running on 12-month grants with no certainty of renewal; existential stress
- **Thin teams** -- one sick staff member can derail an entire program
- **Board management** -- volunteer boards with good intentions and sometimes limited capacity
- **Compliance overload** -- regulators, funders, employment law, workplace health and safety, data privacy all apply
- **Competing accountability** -- accountable to funders, to community, to board, to staff; these can conflict
- **Reporting fatigue** -- same data, different templates, for every funder; none of them will standardise
- **Mission drift anxiety** -- taking money from a funder whose requirements slowly pull the org away from its purpose

### Data Sources
- **Finance:** Xero, MYOB, QuickBooks (varies by org size)
- **CRM/client data:** Salesforce (NPSP), Infoodle, Charity CRM, Apricot, or custom spreadsheets
- **Grant management:** SmartyGrants, Salesforce, spreadsheets
- **HR/payroll:** Employment Hero, Elmo, BambooHR, or manual
- **Program data:** Often bespoke spreadsheets; some use Infoodle or sector-specific tools
- **Board portal:** BoardPro, Diligent, or just email + Google Drive

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"Your acquittal to Community Foundation for the youth program is due in 18 days. The last financial reconciliation was 6 weeks ago. Your finance lead is on leave this week -- want me to draft a reconciliation request for them to complete when they're back Monday, so there's still time?"**

> **"The Foundation West grant round opens in 11 days. You applied last year -- I've found your previous application. Your eligibility, key stats, and budget have all changed since then. Want me to draft a refresh based on current data and flag the gaps?"**

> **"3 staff members' Working With Children Checks expire within the next 60 days. They can't be on site without valid checks. Want me to send renewal reminders with the application links?"**

> **"Your annual information statement to ACNC is due in 47 days. Your auditors haven't been engaged yet for the year-end audit. Last year they took 6 weeks to complete it. That leaves almost no buffer. Want me to draft the engagement email to the auditors now?"**

> **"Board meeting is in 10 days. The board pack is usually sent 5 days before -- that means it's due Thursday. Your program report and financials aren't drafted yet. Want me to build a draft board pack structure from last quarter's template and flag what's missing?"**

## 1.4 CHARITY FIELD WORKER

*(Covers: outreach workers, community support workers, volunteer coordinators, case support workers, food bank coordinators)*

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Morning:** Check roster; review client referrals; load vehicle (food, supplies, equipment)
- **Daytime:** Service delivery -- home visits, drop-in sessions, street outreach, group programs
- **Afternoon:** Case notes; client referrals to other services; stock replenishment; debrief with supervisor
- **Weekly:** Team meeting; volunteer coordination; statistics submission to manager
- High variability week to week. Volunteer-dependent services have a different rhythm to direct-service roles.

### Before Tasks
- Check which clients are expecting a visit; confirm any new referrals
- Prepare client-specific materials (food parcels, hygiene kits, activity resources)
- Brief volunteers on tasks and any client sensitivities
- Check vehicle/supply inventory (running low on items needs to be flagged before the day, not during)
- Review any new client risk flags from the system or handover

### After Tasks
- Case notes logged (often delayed -- field workers hate admin after a tiring day of direct work)
- Referrals made to appropriate services and tracked
- Incident reports filed if anything happened on site or in the field
- Stock used deducted from inventory; restocking request raised if needed
- Volunteer hours logged for compliance

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Volunteer certification** -- first aid, food handling, child safety; expires per person; one lapsed certificate can pull a volunteer off a client visit
- **Food safety compliance** -- temperature logs for perishable food delivery; must be recorded at specific intervals
- **Reporting periods** -- service delivery statistics due to funder/manager at end of month or quarter
- **Holiday season planning** -- Christmas/Easter usually require 4-6 weeks of advance coordination (extra volunteers, food donations, venue bookings)
- **Donation intake periods** -- community drives create a surge of donation sorting work; under-resourcing here creates chaos
- **Client crisis** -- no warning. A client presenting in crisis reshuffles the whole day.

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Volunteer no-shows** -- a roster gap on the day of a food distribution is a genuine operational crisis
- **Compassion fatigue** -- repeated exposure to extreme poverty, trauma, addiction, homelessness
- **Resource constraints** -- turning people away because stock ran out; deeply distressing
- **Lone worker safety** -- visiting clients in unsafe neighbourhoods or homes; safety check-in systems are often informal
- **System navigation burden** -- helping clients access services requires knowing dozens of referral pathways, eligibility criteria, waitlists
- **Under-documentation** -- when there's no time or energy for admin, records gap; creates risk

### Data Sources
- **Client management:** Salesforce NPSP, Infoodle, Link2Home, custom spreadsheets
- **Volunteer management:** Better Impact, Rosterfy, Volunteer Impact, or manual rosters
- **Inventory:** Often spreadsheet-based or whiteboard-based; rarely digital
- **Reporting:** Excel templates submitted to manager; SmartyGrants reports for funders

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"You have 4 volunteers rostered for tomorrow's food run. 2 haven't confirmed. It's 4pm -- want me to send confirmation reminders now so you have time to find replacements tonight if needed?"**

> **"Your food safety temperature log for Tuesday's delivery hasn't been submitted. The compliance window closes at end of week. Want me to pull the template and pre-fill what I can from the delivery notes?"**

> **"Your monthly service statistics report is due to the manager on Friday -- 3 days away. Based on your case notes this month you've had 47 client contacts, but the report target is 60. Want me to flag this to your supervisor now so it can be discussed in context rather than just submitted as a shortfall?"**

> **"3 of your regular volunteers have first aid certificates expiring in the next 30 days. The next available First Aid course through St John's is in 2 weeks. Want me to check availability and pre-register them?"**

## 1.5 LOCAL GOVERNMENT PLANNER

*(Covers: town planners, development assessment officers, strategic planners, urban designers)*

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Monday:** Application queue review; set week's assessment priorities; check referral agency response deadlines
- **Tues-Wed:** Application assessments; site inspections; pre-lodgement meetings with developers/applicants
- **Thursday:** Committee agenda prep (if council meeting approaching); referral follow-ups
- **Friday:** Decision-making; permit condition drafting; planning scheme amendments progress
- **Monthly:** Statutory reporting; performance against KPIs (number of applications processed, timeframes met)
- **Quarterly:** Strategic planning updates; state government liaison; planning scheme review milestones

### Before Tasks
- **Application assessment:** Check application completeness; identify referral agencies needed; review site history and planning controls
- **Pre-lodgement meetings:** Review proposal; prepare relevant planning controls summary; flag likely issues
- **Council meetings/committee:** Prepare officer's report with recommendation; get endorsed by team leader; coordinate any community submissions
- **Site inspections:** Review application plans; note any discrepancies; photograph for file

### After Tasks
- Assessment decision -> issue permit with conditions; notify applicant and any objectors
- Site inspection -> inspection report; non-compliance notices if warranted
- Committee meeting -> implement council resolution; notify affected parties
- Referral agency responses -> review conditions; incorporate into permit; advise applicant of delays
- Pre-lodgement -> send advice letter within agreed timeframe; log discussion

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Statutory assessment timeframes** -- in most jurisdictions, planning applications must be decided within 60-90 days or deemed refused/approved; clock starts at lodgement
- **Referral agency response deadlines** -- transport, environment, heritage agencies have set days to respond; if no response, officer must follow up or proceed
- **Council meeting agendas** -- items must be submitted to the council agenda coordinator by a fixed date; missing this date means waiting another full council cycle (4-6 weeks)
- **Planning scheme amendment exhibition periods** -- public exhibition windows (typically 28 days); submissions close on a fixed date
- **Development contribution triggers** -- contributions can be due at permit issue, at building permit, or at statement of compliance; easy to miss the correct trigger
- **Planning permit expiry** -- permits have two-year commencement and four-year completion deadlines; if not extended, they lapse. Applicants often miss this.
- **State government deadlines** -- planning policy updates, strategic plan lodgements, ministerial reports

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Developer pressure** -- large development applications come with lawyers, consultants, and political pressure
- **Community objection volume** -- controversial applications can generate hundreds of submissions
- **Legal exposure** -- decisions can be appealed to VCAT, Planning Court; officers must be able to defend every decision
- **Processing backlog** -- most planning departments are chronically under-resourced vs. application volume
- **Complexity of planning controls** -- overlapping zones, overlays, policy layers; each application requires detective work
- **Political interference** -- councillors sometimes pressure officers on development outcomes

### Data Sources
- **Application management:** EDRMS, Authority, Pathway, TechOne, Civica, MagiQ, PlanSA
- **Mapping/GIS:** ArcGIS, MapInfo, Council GIS systems, cadastral data
- **Planning schemes:** Online planning portals (Planning Schemes Online in VIC, ePlanning in NSW)
- **State registers:** Heritage registers, bushfire mapping, flood mapping
- **Council agendas:** Council document management systems

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"Application PA2024-0841 has been in the system for 54 days. Statutory deadline is 60 days. The referral response from the Roads Authority came back 3 days ago and hasn't been reviewed yet. Do you want me to prioritise this for today?"**

> **"The council meeting is in 9 days. Your officer's report for the Heritage Overlay Amendment hasn't been lodged with the agenda coordinator yet -- their cut-off is this Thursday. You're still waiting on the Heritage Advisor's review. Want me to send them a nudge today?"**

> **"Permit PP-2021-0334 for the Montrose Street development expires in 6 weeks -- no building permit has been lodged. The applicant hasn't been notified. Want me to draft an expiry reminder letter so they have time to request an extension?"**

> **"You have 14 applications in the queue. 4 of them are inside 10 days of their statutory deadline. Want me to sort your week's workload to front-load those 4 and draft a progress summary for your team leader?"**

## 1.6 COMMUNITY SERVICE WORKER

*(Covers: disability support workers, aged care community workers, mental health support workers, family support workers)*

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Morning:** Client schedule review; medication review if applicable; transport coordination
- **Daytime:** Client visits -- personal care, skills development, community access, social support
- **Afternoon:** Progress notes; incident reporting; handover to next shift or team member
- **Weekly:** Coordination meeting; NDIS/My Aged Care plan review awareness; supervisor check-in
- **Monthly:** Progress reports against client goals; review of service agreements

### Before Tasks
- Review client care plan before every visit -- especially if returning from leave or if client situation changed
- Check any alerts or flags from last shift (behaviour support notes, health changes, family communication)
- Coordinate transport if client needs pickup
- Prepare any activity materials or support tools
- Medication pre-count if administering

### After Tasks
- Progress note within 24 hours (often required for NDIS billing evidence)
- Incident report if anything occurred
- Handover to next worker (verbal or written)
- Expense claims for client-related purchases (petrol, activity costs)
- Update client communication log if family contact was made

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **NDIS plan review dates** -- client's funding is reviewed annually; if not prepared for review, client risks losing services
- **Service agreement expiry** -- service agreements must be renewed before funding runs out; unclaimed funding is lost
- **NDIS claim windows** -- claims must be submitted within certain timeframes; delayed notes = delayed billing = cash flow problems for the org
- **Worker registration renewals** -- NDIS Worker Screening Checks, First Aid, medication admin certificates; all expire per individual per date
- **Behaviour support plan reviews** -- must be reviewed and signed off at set intervals; missing these has compliance consequences
- **Mandatory reporting windows** -- reportable incidents to NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission must be notified within 24h (urgent) or 5 days (less urgent)

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Physical demands** -- manual handling, personal care; injury risk
- **Emotional labour** -- building deep relationships with clients who may decline, die, or lose funding
- **NDIS paperwork complexity** -- the system is designed for organisations, not individual workers; progress note requirements are onerous
- **Low pay, high responsibility** -- sector-wide issue that drives turnover
- **Lone working** -- many shifts are solo; support for complex situations is often a phone call away at best
- **Client family dynamics** -- families can be demanding, distressed, or in disagreement with care plans

### Data Sources
- **NDIS portals:** PRODA, myplace portal, PACE system
- **Care management platforms:** Brevity, Careview, Lumary, Caremaster, ShiftCare, HCP platforms
- **Communication:** Often WhatsApp group with family/team; not ideal for compliance
- **Timesheets:** ShiftCare, Tanda, Deputy, or paper

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"Client James T.'s NDIS plan review is in 7 weeks. His last progress report was 4 months ago. You'll need a current summary of his goals and progress. Want me to pull together a draft from his recent case notes?"**

> **"You logged an incident yesterday that meets the criteria for a reportable incident to the NDIS Commission. The 24-hour reporting window closes at 14:00 today. Want me to open the incident report form and pre-fill it from your notes?"**

> **"Your medication administration certificate expired 12 days ago. Two of your clients require medication support -- you may not be legally permitted to provide that until it's renewed. The next recertification course available is this Saturday. Want me to register you?"**

> **"The service agreement for Rebecca H. expires at the end of this month. Her current plan has $480 remaining. If the agreement isn't renewed before it expires, unspent funds lapse. Want me to draft a renewal for the coordinator to action?"**

## 2.1 PROPERTY MANAGER (RESIDENTIAL / COMMERCIAL)

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Monday:** New maintenance requests review; arrears run (who owes rent); inspections scheduled for the week
- **Tues-Thurs:** Routine inspections; lease renewals; new tenancy processing; contractor coordination
- **Friday:** End-of-month prep if last week of month; outstanding maintenance follow-ups; owner statements
- **Daily:** Emails (tenant, owner, contractor); rent receipting; maintenance coordination; tenancy queries
- **Monthly:** Owner statements and disbursements (exact date varies but often 1st-5th); arrears report; inspection reports sent to owners
- **Quarterly:** Rent reviews; routine inspections; EOFY compliance

### Before Tasks
- **Routine inspection:** Review tenancy file; check prior inspection report; note any outstanding issues from last visit; print/load inspection app
- **Lease renewal:** Pull current lease terms; check market rent comparisons; prepare renewal proposal; check owner's instructions
- **New tenancy:** Process application (ID, references, affordability); prepare tenancy agreement; conduct entry condition report with photos
- **Tribunal hearing (VCAT/NCAT etc):** Review tenancy history; prepare evidence bundle (lease, rent ledger, correspondence, condition reports)

### After Tasks
- Inspection -> report with photos to owner within 24-48h; any maintenance items raised as new requests; tenant notified of any issues
- Lease renewal -> executed agreement filed; rent roll updated; owner notified
- Maintenance -> follow up completion with contractor; tenant notified when resolved; invoice processed
- Tribunal -> implement order (possession date, payment plan); update file
- New tenancy -> lodge bond with authority (time-limited); entry condition report signed and stored; first rent payment confirmed

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Bond lodgement** -- must be lodged with the relevant authority within 5-15 business days of receipt (varies by state); late lodgement = fine
- **Tribunal notice periods** -- notice to remedy breach or notice to vacate must give the legally mandated notice period; serving it one day short makes it invalid
- **Routine inspection intervals** -- most states allow inspection every 3 months (some 6 months); must give 7-14 days notice beforehand; calendar management is critical
- **Rent review dates** -- fixed in lease; missing review date can mean rent stays fixed longer than intended, costing the owner money
- **Property maintenance deadlines** -- urgent repairs (water, locks, gas) have a legal response timeframe (24-48h typically)
- **Insurance renewal** -- landlord insurance is per-property; easy to miss renewal on a portfolio of 100+ properties
- **Smoke alarm/electrical safety checks** -- annual in many states; compliance must be documented; expiry per-property
- **Owner statement cycles** -- disbursement must happen by a date; trust account regulations are strict; non-compliance = licence issues
- **Lease expiry dates** -- if not actioned 60-90 days before expiry, tenant goes periodic and owner loses leverage for rent review

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Volume overload** -- a PM managing 150+ properties (common in most agencies) has literally too much to hold in their head
- **Difficult tenants** -- arrears chasing, damage disputes, noise complaints; emotionally draining
- **Demanding owners** -- owners want maximum rent, minimum maintenance spend, and instant responses
- **Legal compliance complexity** -- residential tenancy legislation varies by state and changes frequently; non-compliance is a real risk
- **Trust accounting audits** -- funds held in trust are tightly regulated; any discrepancy = professional consequences
- **Tribunal appearances** -- preparing a tribunal case on top of a full PM workload is brutal

### Data Sources
- **Property management software:** PropertyMe, PropertyTree, Palace, Rest Professional, Console Cloud, Rental Express, Buildium, AppFolio (US)
- **Inspection apps:** Inspect Real Estate, PropertyInspect, 2Apply
- **Communications:** Email, SMS platforms
- **Accounting:** Integrated in PM software + Xero for some agencies
- **Bond authority:** State-specific portal (RBO in VIC, NSW Fair Trading, etc.)
- **Tribunal portals:** VCAT, NCAT, QCAT online portals

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"You have 6 bonds to lodge this week. The oldest one -- James Kovacs at 14 Maple St -- was received 11 days ago. Most states require lodgement within 10 business days. You may already be out of compliance. Want me to flag this and prioritise the lodgement process today?"**

> **"47 Smith Street has a lease expiry in 61 days -- just outside your 60-day action window. Routine market rents in that suburb have gone up 8% since the current rent was set. Want me to draft a renewal proposal for the owner with the market comparison and a recommended new rent?"**

> **"The smoke alarm compliance check for Unit 4/22 Harbour Drive expires next month. Last year's cert is in the file. The same provider is booked for two other properties next Tuesday -- want me to add this one to that booking to save a separate call-out fee?"**

> **"Tenant Davis in 8B is 14 days in arrears. Under Victorian legislation the breach notice minimum is 14 days, meaning you can issue today. The breach notice must be served before you can progress to any further action. Want me to draft the notice now?"**

## 2.2 BODY CORPORATE / STRATA MANAGER

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Monday:** Committee correspondence; maintenance approval queue; financial queries
- **Tues-Thurs:** AGM prep (rolling -- always an AGM coming up); by-law queries; contractor management; insurance claims
- **Friday:** Weekly correspondence wrap-up; financial reviews; dispute handling
- **Monthly:** Levy notices to owners; financial statements to committees; contractor payment runs
- **Annually:** AGM, budget preparation, insurance renewal, contractor agreements

### Before Tasks
- **AGM:** Prepare agenda (with statutory required items); financial statements and budget; proxy forms; 21-28 days notice to all owners; coordinate venue or online platform; prepare minutes template
- **Committee meetings:** Agenda; outstanding action register; financial summary; maintenance updates
- **Insurance renewal:** Request renewal quotes; compare coverage; circulate to committee for approval
- **Budget preparation:** Review prior year actuals; get quotes for upcoming maintenance; sinking fund projection; present to committee before AGM

### After Tasks
- AGM -> minutes within 14-21 days (legally mandated); implement resolutions (update bank signatories, engage approved contractors, etc.)
- Maintenance job -> track to completion; ensure invoice paid; record in maintenance register
- By-law dispute -> formal notice to owner; record dispute in register; escalate if unresolved
- Insurance claim -> track with insurer; coordinate repairs; advise committee on progress
- Levy payment default -> issue reminder; escalate to debt recovery per state legislation timeline

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **AGM deadlines** -- every corporation must hold an AGM within a set period of financial year end (e.g., 15 months since last AGM in many states); missing = potential regulatory complaint
- **AGM notice periods** -- 21-28 days notice required in most jurisdictions; notice must include financial statements and motions -- all must be ready before notice goes out
- **Levy due dates** -- quarterly or annually per the budget; levies not raised on time affect cash flow
- **Levy default escalation** -- debt recovery steps have mandated notice periods before further action
- **Insurance renewal** -- strata insurance is compulsory; if it lapses, every owner in the building is uninsured; renewal date on a portfolio of 50+ plans requires active management
- **Sinking fund reviews** -- required every 10 years in many states (some 5); significant planning lead time
- **Building defect liability windows** -- statutory warranty periods (often 2 years minor, 6 years major defects); owners corps often miss these windows and lose their legal right to claim against the builder
- **Fire safety/elevator compliance** -- annual inspections with compliance certificates; if uncertified, building may need to be evacuated

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Owner disputes** -- by-law disputes, parking complaints, pet issues; owners are passionate and entitled
- **Committee dysfunction** -- volunteer committees with no governance training making binding financial decisions
- **Insurance claims complexity** -- damage to common property and lot property creates boundary disputes; insurers push back
- **Legislative complexity** -- strata laws changed significantly in most states in the last 10 years; ongoing compliance complexity
- **Building defect situations** -- major defects in newer buildings create enormous legal and financial stress
- **Portfolio scale** -- a single manager may manage 30-60+ schemes simultaneously

### Data Sources
- **Strata management software:** StrataMax, Rockend Strata Master, Urbanise, MRI Strata, Archistar Strata, Re-Leased
- **Financial:** Integrated in strata software + bank feeds
- **Insurance:** Broker systems and insurer portals
- **Document management:** strata software + SharePoint/Google Drive
- **State portals:** NSW Fair Trading, Consumer Affairs Victoria, Body Corporate and Community Management QLD

## 2.3 FACILITIES MANAGER

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Morning:** Overnight incident review; open work order queue; critical plant status check
- **Daytime:** Contractor coordination; reactive maintenance; inspections; stakeholder meetings
- **Afternoon:** Work order updates; compliance documentation; budget reconciliation
- **Weekly:** Contractor performance review; planned maintenance schedule check; safety walk
- **Monthly:** Maintenance KPI reporting; budget vs. actuals; compliance certificate review
- **Annually:** Budget planning; contract renewals; major plant maintenance scheduling

### Before Tasks
- **Contractor site visit:** Issue permit to work; safety induction if new contractor; site access arranged; work scope confirmed in writing
- **Planned maintenance:** Confirm contractor attendance; brief building occupants of any disruption; isolations and shutdowns coordinated
- **Compliance inspections (fire, lifts, HVAC):** Book authorised inspector; access to plant rooms confirmed; prior certificate located for comparison
- **Budget prep:** Gather all contract quotes; assess reactive maintenance trend; model replacement vs. repair for aging assets

### After Tasks
- Work order completed -> update CMMS (computerised maintenance management system); file compliance docs; invoice approved
- Compliance inspection -> certificate filed; any deficiencies actioned as work orders with deadlines
- Contractor performance review -> feedback documented; contract renewal decision flag
- Incident -> incident report; root cause analysis; corrective action plan
- Budget approval -> update contract/purchase order records; advise team

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Fire safety annual inspection** -- building occupancy permit and insurance can be affected if overdue; certificate must be current at all times
- **Elevator/lift compliance** -- WorkSafe/SafeWork annual inspection; out-of-cert lift must be shut down immediately
- **HVAC preventive maintenance** -- usually quarterly; missed services void warranties and create energy inefficiency
- **Cooling tower water treatment** -- Legionella risk; water testing and treatment are statutory and time-critical
- **Contractor insurance expiry** -- using a contractor whose public liability or workers' comp has lapsed creates direct liability exposure
- **WHS/OHS safety audits** -- internal quarterly, external annual; preparation workload is substantial
- **Asset replacement scheduling** -- major plant (chillers, boilers, generators) have replacement lead times of 6-18 months; failure to plan creates emergency replacement cost
- **Lease end make-good** -- commercial tenants have make-good obligations; if not scheduled ahead of end date, it becomes a dispute

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Reactive vs. planned ratio** -- reactive maintenance consuming all time means planned maintenance slips, creating more reactive maintenance (vicious cycle)
- **Budget pressure** -- capital works underfunded; maintenance budget insufficient; assets aging
- **After-hours incidents** -- HVAC failure, flooding, power outages; facilities manager is often the first call
- **Compliance exposure** -- personal liability for OHS non-compliance is real in some jurisdictions
- **Contractor reliability** -- good tradies are hard to get; bad ones create rework and liability
- **Occupant complaints** -- too cold, too hot, lift is slow; facilities managers are an easy target

### Data Sources
- **CMMS:** Maximo, ServiceNow, Archibus, FM:Systems, Infor EAM, Simpro, MEX, UpKeep, Maintenance Connection
- **BMS (Building Management System):** Schneider Electric, Honeywell, Siemens, Johnson Controls

- **Compliance register:** CMMS or dedicated compliance tool (Donesafe, Hammertech)
- **Finance:** Integration with corporate ERP or separate tracking

# PART 3: CALL CENTRE ROLES

*Note: Call centres are one of the highest-instrumented work environments on the planet. Every second is measured. This is both a burden and a data goldmine for proactive AI.*

## 3.1 CALL CENTRE AGENT

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Pre-shift (15-30 min before):** Check schedule in WFM tool; log in to all systems; review any team messages or product/policy updates; check personal metrics from previous day
- **Shift start:** Queue opens; calls begin immediately
- **Throughout shift:** Handling calls; After Call Work (ACW); on-queue/off-queue toggling; breaks at scheduled intervals
- **End of shift:** Wrap up ACW; complete any call-backs in queue; log out of all systems; shift debrief if applicable
- **Weekly:** 1:1 coaching session with TL; team meeting; access scorecard or QA feedback; shift bidding if applicable

### Before Tasks
- Check knowledge base for any recent product/policy updates (many agents skip this; causes errors on calls)
- Review personal metric trend from prior week -- especially if a QA or coaching session is coming
- Confirm break schedule for the day (shift bidding or pre-assigned)
- Mental prep for high-volume or complex queue types

### After Tasks
- Complete ACW (After Call Work) -- CRM notes, follow-up actions, case logging; time pressure because ACW time is also measured
- Schedule any promised callbacks in the CRM
- Flag any calls that required escalation or had unresolved issues for supervisor
- Self-review if QA monitoring active (in some centres, agents can hear their own calls)

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Schedule adherence** -- agents are expected to be ready to take calls at the exact start of scheduled time; even 2-3 minutes late affects adherence score
- **ACW time limits** -- many centres have target ACW time (e.g., 60-90 seconds); excessive ACW flags for review
- **Break schedule** -- breaks are precision-scheduled by WFM based on forecasted volume; early/late breaks affect service levels
- **Shift bidding windows** -- many centres run shift bidding weekly or monthly; window is often 24-48h; missing it means you get assigned whatever's left
- **Mandatory training completion** -- compliance training assigned with a deadline; incomplete training = formal HR issue
- **QA self-assessment deadlines** -- in some centres, agents must review and sign off their own QA scores within set timeframe
- **Wrap-up/callback promises** -- if you told a customer you'd call back by 3pm, that's a binding expectation

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Irate customers** -- emotionally exhausting, especially back-to-back
- **System failures** -- CRM crashes mid-call; script not loading; worst possible time
- **Not knowing the answer** -- policy changes not communicated; agent caught on call without knowledge
- **Being monitored** -- live monitoring (supervisor listening in) creates constant performance anxiety
- **Metrics performance** -- AHT, CSAT, FCR, adherence all tracked and visible; poor performance has direct career consequences
- **Queue pressure** -- when queue is building, agents feel pressure to cut calls short; creates CSAT problems; vicious cycle

### Data Sources
- **WFM platform:** NICE IEX, Genesys WFM, Verint WFM, Aspect WFM, Calabrio
- **CRM:** Salesforce Service Cloud, Zendesk, Freshdesk, ServiceNow, Microsoft Dynamics, custom CRM
- **Telephony/ACD:** Genesys Cloud, NICE CXone, Amazon Connect, Avaya, Cisco
- **QA platform:** EvaluAgent, Calabrio, NICE Quality Central, Klaus, MaestroQA
- **Knowledge base:** Confluence, ServiceNow KB, Guru, custom intranets
- **Personal dashboard:** Usually embedded in telephony or custom wallboard

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"Your shift starts in 8 minutes and there are 3 product updates posted since your last shift -- one of them is about the new returns policy which affects your queue type. You've got time to skim them before your first call."**

> **"Your AHT this week is running 2 minutes 14 seconds against a target of 1 minute 58 seconds. Your longest calls are mostly on billing dispute queries. Want me to pull up the billing dispute script and fast-reference card before you go back on queue?"**

> **"The shift bid window for next week closes at 5pm today. You haven't submitted your preferences yet. Based on your previous bids, I've pre-filled your preferred shifts -- want to review and submit?"**

> **"You promised Mrs. Okafor a callback by 2pm today -- it's 1:42pm and she's not in your active follow-up queue. Want me to flag this in your CRM now so it doesn't drop?"**

## 3.2 CALL CENTRE TEAM LEADER / SUPERVISOR

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Pre-shift (30 min):** Review overnight reports; check adherence from prior shift; team messages; flag any escalated issues
- **Shift start:** Floor walk; confirm team is logged in; check queue levels; brief any specific issues or updates
- **Throughout shift:** Real-time queue monitoring; coaching interventions; escalation handling; 1:1 floor presence; break management
- **End of shift:** Handover to next TL; daily team performance review; follow-up on any incidents; coaching notes
- **Weekly:** 1:1 coaching sessions with each team member; team meeting; submit weekly team performance report; attend TL/ops meeting
- **Monthly:** Formal performance reviews for team; coaching session documentation; contribute to QA calibration session

### Before Tasks
- **Team meeting:** Pull team metrics; identify talking points; address any morale or operational issue; prepare recognition moments
- **1:1 coaching session:** Pull individual agent scorecard; review QA evaluations; review adherence data; identify coaching focus area; prepare development plan update
- **Shift handover:** Prepare handover document (queue status, outstanding escalations, staff issues, any schedule deviations)
- **QA calibration:** Review sample calls; prepare scoring rationale; align with QA team expectations

### After Tasks
- Coaching session -> document coaching notes; update agent development plan; schedule follow-up
- Escalation handled -> close case in CRM; debrief agent; document outcome
- Shift report -> submit to manager; archive
- Performance conversations -> document formally; notify HR if on improvement plan
- New team member training -> log progress; update training checklist

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Schedule adherence in real-time** -- watching adherence on WFM dashboard; if an agent goes off-queue unexpectedly, it affects service levels within minutes
- **Service level thresholds** -- if call queue builds and SL drops below 80/20 (80% answered in 20 seconds), TL must act immediately (call agents back from breaks, alert manager)
- **Coaching session quotas** -- most centres require TLs to complete a set number of coaching sessions per agent per month (e.g., 4 per agent); end of month crunch when behind
- **QA audit score submission deadlines** -- QA scores must be submitted to agents by a certain date so they can respond or appeal
- **Shift bidding processing** -- TL must approve or reject shift change requests within a window
- **Disciplinary action timeframes** -- verbal warnings, written warnings have follow-up and appeal timeframes that must be met
- **Weekly performance report deadline** -- usually due by Monday or COB Friday for the prior week
- **Training completion monitoring** -- TL responsible for ensuring team completes mandatory modules by due date

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Staffing gaps** -- sick calls, no-shows; TL scrambles to cover queue with reduced team
- **Caught between agents and management** -- pressure from above on metrics; pressure from agents on workload and wellbeing
- **Underperforming agents** -- managing a PIP (Performance Improvement Plan) while maintaining team morale
- **Real-time fire-fighting** -- it's very hard to do any meaningful coaching when you're constantly managing live issues
- **Coaching vs. queue** -- when to pull an agent off queue for coaching vs. keeping them on to manage volume
- **Metrics vs. morale** -- metric culture can harm engagement; TLs often feel this tension personally

### Data Sources
- **WFM real-time:** NICE IEX Real Time Adherence, Genesys WFM, Verint real-time dashboards
- **QA:** EvaluAgent, Klaus, NICE Quality Central, Calabrio, MaestroQA
- **CRM:** Salesforce, Zendesk, ServiceNow
- **Telephony/ACD dashboards:** Genesys CX Insights, NICE inContact, Amazon Connect dashboards
- **HR/coaching tools:** Built-in TL coaching modules in QA tools, or manual in Excel/Google Sheets
- **Wallboards:** Real-time queue metrics displayed on floor screens (AHT, SL, abandon rate)

## 3.3 CALL CENTRE MANAGER

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Morning:** Overnight performance summary; operational metrics review; staffing status; escalation review
- **Daytime:** TL meetings; strategic projects; stakeholder liaison; budget management; senior reporting
- **End of day:** SLA/metric summary; HR matters; forward planning
- **Weekly:** Ops meeting with TLs; senior management reporting; cross-functional meetings (IT, HR, Finance, Product)
- **Monthly:** Performance reporting to leadership; forecast review with WFM; budget review; QA calibration review; staff performance cycle
- **Quarterly:** Headcount planning; budget submissions; workforce planning; strategic review
- **Annually:** Budget cycle; workforce planning; technology review; contract renewals

### Before Tasks
- **Senior leadership meeting:** Prepare performance dashboard; identify trends; prepare narrative around any metric deviations; anticipate questions
- **Budget submission:** Gather headcount data, technology costs, training costs, overtime actuals; model scenarios
- **Large-scale change (new product, system, policy):** Impact assessment; training plan; WFM model update; communications plan
- **Recruitment drive:** Define requirements; work with HR on timeline; prepare onboarding schedule; WFM to model training nesting period

### After Tasks
- Ops meeting -> action register; TL accountability; follow-up on prior actions
- Performance report -> distribute to stakeholders; schedule conversations if metrics off-target
- Recruitment -> onboarding plan in place; training coordinator briefed; WFM schedules updated
- Technology incident -> post-incident review; escalation to vendor; corrective action plan
- Escalation from client/customer -> resolution documented; process improvement identified

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **CSAT reporting cycles** -- monthly or weekly CSAT summary to client/leadership; data from survey platform needs to be compiled and analysed ahead of deadline; missed cycles create credibility issues
- **SLA breach thresholds** -- contractual SLAs (usually in outsourced environments) have penalties for breaches; the manager must identify and address SLA risk before the reporting period closes
- **Headcount approval windows** -- budget approval cycles for new headcount have fixed windows; missing the budget cycle means waiting another quarter
- **WFM forecast accuracy reviews** -- weekly comparison of forecast vs. actual; if forecast was significantly off, model must be updated before next week's schedule is published
- **Shift schedule publication deadline** -- schedules must be published to agents by a set date (often 2 weeks out); late publication triggers complaints, union issues, and schedule adherence problems
- **Technology contract renewals** -- WFM, telephony, QA platform contracts; often multi-year; negotiation should start 6-9 months out; auto-renewals with no renegotiation are expensive
- **Training compliance deadlines** -- mandatory training completion for entire centre; manager accountable; end-of-window non-compliance creates HR and regulatory risk
- **Performance review cycles** -- mid-year and annual reviews have fixed completion deadlines; incomplete cycles create legal risk in some jurisdictions

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Contractual SLA exposure** -- missing SLA = financial penalty and potential contract loss
- **Attrition** -- call centre attrition rates of 30-50% annually are common; recruitment is perpetual and expensive
- **Forecast volatility** -- contact volume spikes unpredictably (product launch, PR incident, seasonal); impossible to staff perfectly
- **Technology dependencies** -- entire operation stops if telephony or CRM fails; vendor responsiveness is never fast enough
- **Metric gaming** -- agents and TLs find ways to hit metrics that don't reflect real performance; spotting it is hard
- **Middle-management squeeze** -- accountability for results with limited control over hiring, budget, or technology

### Data Sources
- **WFM platforms:** NICE IEX, Genesys WFM, Verint WFM, Calabrio -- forecasting, scheduling, adherence, occupancy
- **Telephony/ACD reporting:** Genesys, NICE, Amazon Connect, Avaya -- queue metrics, routing, abandon rates, talk time
- **QA platform:** Calibration scores, evaluation trends, auto-QA AI scoring (increasingly)
- **CRM reporting:** Case volumes, resolution rates, FCR, escalation rates
- **CSAT/NPS:** Medallia, Qualtrics, InMoment, SurveyMonkey; or platform-native (Zendesk CSAT)
- **HR system:** Workday, SAP SuccessFactors -- headcount, leave, performance cycles
- **Business intelligence/dashboards:** Power BI, Tableau, Sisense -- custom-built centre-wide dashboards
- **Workforce planning models:** Often Excel-based despite the other systems

> **"Centre-wide mandatory compliance training deadline is in 11 days. Current completion rate is 67% -- 89 agents haven't finished it yet. The 5 TLs with the lowest completion rates on their teams are Smith, Williams, Park, Torres, and Nguyen. Want me to draft a TL-level accountability message for you to send today?"**

> **"Your abandon rate has been above 8% for 3 consecutive days this week -- the threshold in your SLA is 5%. If this pattern holds through end of month, you'll be reporting an SLA breach. The WFM data suggests the 10am-12pm window is the biggest contributor. Do you want a staffing intervention modelled for that window for the rest of the month?"**

## 3.4 QA ANALYST (CALL CENTRE)

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Morning:** Pull call sample for evaluation; check QA queue; review any flagged calls from TLs
- **Daytime:** Evaluate calls against scorecard; write evaluation notes; calibration prep
- **Afternoon:** Submit evaluations; respond to agent disputes; attend calibration or coaching support sessions
- **Weekly:** Complete evaluation quota; calibration session with QA team and TLs; trend report draft
- **Monthly:** Full team QA trend report; category-level analysis; recommendations to management; recalibration of scorecard if needed

### Before Tasks
- **Evaluation session:** Pull random sample + any targeted samples (specific agent, call type, or issue flag); open scorecard; have call recording and CRM notes side by side
- **Calibration session:** Pre-evaluate the calibration call independently before the session; prepare notes
- **Monthly report:** Pull all evaluation data from QA platform; build trend analysis; prepare narrative

### After Tasks
- Evaluation submitted -> agent and TL notified; dispute period opens
- Calibration session -> document scoring consensus; update scoring guidance if alignment was off
- Agent dispute -> review dispute; escalate to QA lead if needed; document resolution
- Monthly report -> distribute to management; follow up on any action items

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Monthly evaluation quota** -- QA analysts usually have a set number of evaluations to complete per month (e.g., 4 per agent  team size = 80+ evaluations); end of month crunch is common
- **Evaluation delivery deadlines** -- agents must receive evaluations within X days of the call (ensures feedback is timely and memorable); missing delivery windows reduces coaching value and can create disputes
- **Calibration session schedule** -- calibration must happen regularly (weekly or bi-weekly) to maintain scoring consistency across evaluators; if sessions are missed, scoring drifts
- **Dispute resolution windows** -- agents typically have 5-7 days to dispute a QA score; QA analyst must respond within a further set timeframe
- **QA scorecard review cycle** -- scorecards should be reviewed quarterly or annually; stale scorecards don't reflect current business priorities
- **Auto-QA model training** -- if AI-assisted QA is in use, the model needs periodic retraining on new call samples

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Quota pressure** -- evaluating 80+ calls per month while doing everything else is a grind
- **Objectivity under social pressure** -- agents know who evaluated their calls; interpersonal dynamics in small centres are real
- **Calibration disagreements** -- TLs and QA analysts often score differently; resolving this requires tact
- **Policy ambiguity** -- evaluating against a policy that isn't clearly written creates inconsistency and disputes
- **Auto-QA transition** -- fear of being replaced by AI-assisted scoring; redefining the role is an active concern in many centres

### Data Sources
- **QA platforms:** EvaluAgent, Klaus/Zendesk QA, Calabrio, NICE Quality Central, MaestroQA, Playvox
- **Call recordings:** Integrated in QA platform or stored in ACD (Genesys, NICE, Amazon Connect)
- **CRM:** To verify agent's case handling against what was said on the call
- **WFM:** For context on agent schedule, adherence on the day of a flagged call
- **Reporting:** QA platform native reporting; Power BI or Tableau for trend analysis

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"You're 12 days into the month and have completed 31 of your 84 required evaluations. At the current pace you'll finish with 62 -- 22 short of quota. Your remaining capacity this month is 14 working days but 3 are impacted by calibration sessions. Do you want me to suggest a daily evaluation target to catch up without burning out?"**

> **"Agent Torres has had 3 evaluations submitted this month. All 3 scored below 70%, and all 3 flagged the same compliance statement as missing. Her TL hasn't scheduled a coaching session yet. Want me to flag this pattern directly to the TL as a coaching priority?"**

> **"Next Thursday's calibration session is on Account Closure calls. The last 3 calibration sessions on this call type showed a 14-point scoring gap between you and the TL team. Want me to pull the last calibration notes and the scoring guide to help you prep a tight rationale?"**

> **"You have 7 agent disputes open. 3 of them have been waiting for a response for 5 days -- the resolution window is 7 days. One is from a high-performer who's never disputed a score before, which might warrant a close look. Want me to pull all 3 for you to review today?"**

## 3.5 WORKFORCE MANAGEMENT (WFM) ANALYST

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Daily:** Real-time monitoring of queue and adherence; intraday adjustments (break reschedules, agent redeployment); exception handling; update actual vs. forecast data
- **Weekly:** Generate and publish next week's schedule; review forecast accuracy from prior week; handle shift swap/leave requests; attend ops meeting
- **Monthly:** Long-range forecast update; headcount modelling; schedule efficiency report; training impact modelling
- **Quarterly:** Staffing model review; capacity plan update; recruitment forecast to HR

### Before Tasks
- **Schedule publication:** Complete forecast model; account for leave, training, and public holidays; run schedule optimisation; validate against SLA targets; get manager approval; then publish
- **Ops meeting:** Prepare forecast accuracy report; flag key risks for upcoming period; model any scenarios management has asked for
- **Capacity plan:** Pull historical volume data; apply trend and seasonality model; incorporate known business changes (campaigns, product launches); stress-test headcount scenarios

### After Tasks
- Schedule published -> respond to agent queries; process immediate swap requests; update leave tracker
- Intraday adjustment -> document reason and outcome; feed back to forecast model for accuracy improvement
- Ops meeting -> update capacity plan with any decisions made; communicate changes to TLs
- Exception processed -> logged in WFM system; payroll notified if applicable

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Schedule publication deadline** -- the schedule for next week must be published by a certain day (often Thursday or Friday for the following Monday start); late publication is a major operational and morale problem
- **Forecast accuracy window** -- the forecast must be finalised before the schedule run; if volume data from the business (marketing campaigns, expected contact drivers) is late, the whole schedule is delayed
- **Leave approval windows** -- agents submit leave requests with deadlines; WFM must process by set dates to maintain coverage targets
- **Shift bidding window** -- if centre uses shift bidding: bid window opens, agents bid, WFM validates and publishes; all time-sensitive steps
- **Real-time SL breaches** -- when queue builds and SL drops, WFM must act within minutes: recall breaks, release agents from training, request overtime
- **Intraday reforecast trigger** -- if actual volume deviates from forecast by >10-15%, an intraday reforecast should be run to update break schedules for the rest of the day
- **Public holiday planning** -- staffing for public holidays requires weeks of advance notice to agents; late notice creates compliance issues

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Forecast accuracy pressure** -- every deviation is visible and attributed to WFM; blame culture in some centres
- **Understaffing exposure** -- when SLAs breach, WFM is in the room getting questioned
- **Data dependency** -- relies on other departments for volume drivers; when they're late, WFM is late
- **Agent satisfaction vs. SLA** -- agents want their preferred schedules; the model requires what it requires; WFM is the "bad cop"
- **Complexity at scale** -- managing multi-skill, multi-channel (voice, chat, email), multi-site scheduling is genuinely complex mathematics
- **Constant intraday volatility** -- real-time events (tech outage, viral social post, large campaign response) can upend the day's plan instantly

### Data Sources
- **WFM platforms:** NICE IEX, Genesys WFM, Verint WFM, Aspect Workforce, Calabrio, Assembled, Playvox WFM
- **ACD/telephony:** Volume, talk time, handle time, routing data -- feeds into WFM forecast
- **CRM:** Contact driver categorisation; complexity data; emerging issue trends
- **HR/leave system:** Leave balances, approved leave, training schedules
- **Business calendar:** Campaign dates, product launches, known contact drivers from Marketing/Product
- **Historical data warehouse:** Volume patterns by day/hour/channel for forecast modelling

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"Next Wednesday is forecasted at 2,840 contacts -- 34% above your rolling average. The trigger appears to be the email campaign going out Tuesday that wasn't in your planning calendar. Your current schedule for Wednesday has 91 agents rostered. Based on your AHT and SLA target, you need 118 to hit 80/20. There's a 3-week lead time for overtime requests -- this needs to go out today. Want me to draft the overtime request and send it to TLs?"**

> **"Forecast accuracy for last week was 82% -- your centre target is 90%+. The biggest miss was Monday morning, where volume was 22% above forecast. Monday mornings have been under-forecast 3 of the last 5 weeks. Want me to apply a Monday morning uplift factor of 18% to next week's model before you run the schedule?"**

> **"Schedule publication for next week is due tomorrow at 3pm. You still have 14 unprocessed leave requests, and the training team hasn't confirmed which agents are in Tuesday's product training session. Missing these will create schedule errors. Want me to send a 'data due today' reminder to both the training coordinator and the leave approvers now?"**

> **"It's 10:15am and you're already tracking at 94% of today's forecast volume with 85% of the day remaining. At this rate you'll breach your SLA by 11:30am. You have 4 agents currently in their break window -- 2 are flexible according to their break parameters. Do you want me to generate a break reschedule for TLs to push now?"**

> **"Agent Martinez has submitted a shift swap request for next Friday. Approving it leaves Team 3 below minimum coverage for the 2pm-6pm window (5 agents vs. required 7). There's another agent on Team 2 with compatible skills who hasn't submitted a swap request but is on a split shift that day. Want me to flag the coverage risk and suggest the Team 2 option as an alternative?"**

## 3.6 TRAINING & ONBOARDING COORDINATOR (CALL CENTRE)

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Daily:** New hire admin; training session facilitation; check training completion dashboards; respond to TL/manager queries about specific agents' progress
- **Weekly:** Review cohort progress; update training schedule; calibration with ops on readiness for nesting/floor release; mandatory training compliance check
- **Monthly:** Report on training completion rates; assess quality of training output (new hire performance vs. tenured agents); update materials if policy changed
- **Quarterly:** Full training material review; learning needs analysis; new training program development if needed

### Before Tasks
- **New cohort onboarding:** System access provisioning (IT must be done before day 1); workstations ready; materials printed/loaded; agenda prepared; buddy/mentor assigned
- **Training session:** Slides updated if recent policy changes; practice scenarios prepared; system sandbox access confirmed; facilitator notes reviewed
- **Nesting/floor release:** Confirm agents are ready (assessment scores, observed competency); brief TLs on each agent's strengths and gaps; handover documentation prepared
- **Compliance training rollout:** Load content to LMS; assign to correct agent groups; set deadline; communicate to TLs

### After Tasks
- Training session -> assessment marked; individual feedback given; progress log updated
- Cohort nesting -> monitor new hire metrics for first 30/60/90 days; escalate to manager if anyone falling behind
- Compliance training cycle -> report completion rates to manager; chase non-completions; escalate if deadline passed
- Policy update -> assess training impact; update affected modules; communicate change to floor with training support
- Training material update -> version control; archive old version; notify TLs

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **New hire start date** -- IT access, system training, and buddy assignments must all be ready before day one; if anything's late, the new hire sits idle on day one -- terrible first impression and costly
- **Nesting window duration** -- nesting period is pre-set (e.g., 2 weeks); transition to floor must happen on schedule for WFM headcount to work
- **Mandatory training deadlines** -- compliance, regulatory, or product training has a hard deadline; if agents aren't trained by a certain date, they may legally be unable to handle certain call types
- **Product/policy launch date** -- training must be completed before the go-live date; launch date is often moved without looping in training coordinator until late
- **Cohort scheduling conflicts** -- training rooms, trainers, and system access all have capacity constraints; conflicting recruitment waves create booking chaos
- **Re-certification deadlines** -- some roles (especially in financial services, healthcare, insurance) require annual re-certification; coordinator must track per-agent, per-certification

### Stress & Anxiety Points
- **Last-minute changes** -- product launches delayed, then rushed; training coordinator told Friday that new product training is needed by Monday
- **System access delays** -- IT provisioning delays are the single most common cause of wasted training time; out of training coordinator's control
- **Under-resourced for scale** -- one coordinator managing onboarding for 50+ new hires simultaneously is common
- **Training quality vs. speed trade-off** -- ops wants agents on the floor fast; training needs time to actually train them; caught in the middle when new hires underperform
- **Content currency** -- policies change faster than materials get updated; agents get trained on outdated info
- **No feedback loop** -- often doesn't find out how agents are performing post-training unless they actively chase TLs; makes it hard to improve the training

### Data Sources
- **LMS (Learning Management System):** Docebo, Cornerstone, SAP SuccessFactors Learning, TalentLMS, Lessonly (now Seismic Learning), LearnUpon
- **HRIS for new hire tracking:** Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, BambooHR, Employment Hero
- **WFM:** For nesting schedule integration and floor release timing
- **QA platform:** To monitor new hire call quality post-release
- **CRM/telephony:** Training call recordings; new hire call stats
- **Spreadsheets:** Almost universally used for tracking training completion, cohort schedules, re-cert deadlines

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"A new cohort of 12 agents starts Monday -- 4 days away. I've checked the IT access request list and 5 of the 12 don't have CRM logins provisioned yet. IT typically takes 2 business days for bulk provisioning. If not escalated today, 5 agents will sit idle on Day 1. Want me to draft an urgent IT request now?"**

> **"The new billing policy goes live in 8 days. The billing module in the training materials still references the old process. I haven't seen an updated policy document yet -- do you have it, or should I chase the Product team today? Training must be complete before go-live."**

> **"Compliance training deadline is in 6 days. Current completion rate is 71%. The 29% outstanding are spread across 6 TL teams. The 3 teams with the lowest rates are Smith (40%), Park (55%), and Torres (58%). Want me to send a targeted reminder to those 3 TLs with the names of the non-completers?"**

> **"The nesting period for the February cohort ends Friday. 3 agents in the cohort scored below 75% on their final assessment. Their TLs haven't been briefed on the specific gaps. Want me to generate individual handover notes for each of the 3, flagging the exact skill areas to watch in their first weeks on floor?"**

> **"Agent Ramirez's insurance product certification expires in 22 days. Under FSCA regulations she cannot handle insurance-related calls without a current cert. The next available recertification course isn't until next month -- that's after her expiry. Want me to check if there's an alternative accredited course or online option that fits within the 22-day window?"**

# APPENDIX: CROSS-ROLE PATTERNS FOR PULSE

## Universal Proactive Triggers (works across all roles)

| Trigger Type | Signal | AI Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Deadline creep** | Document started but not progressed; days narrowing | Alert + offer to continue |
| **Dependency gap** | Task needs input from others; no chase sent | Draft follow-up |
| **Recurring pattern** | Same issue type flagged 3+ times | Surface pattern, suggest fix |
| **Window closure** | Time-limited window (review, claim, bid, renew) approaching | Alert with days remaining |
| **Certification expiry** | Per-person, per-date expiry in portfolio | Individual expiry alerts |
| **Overdue action** | Action item from meeting/task with no update | Chase prompt |
| **Anomaly** | Metric deviated >X% from norm | Surface with context, not just raw number |
| **Resource crunch ahead** | Forecast suggests capacity shortage before it arrives | Model scenarios, surface options |

## The "How Did It Know?" Design Principle

The most powerful Pulse moments are when the assistant surfaces something the user **didn't know they needed to check**:

- **Cross-system correlation** -- "The product launch is Tuesday but training hasn't been scheduled" (connecting marketing calendar to training LMS)
- **Time math** -- "You need X done by Y, but Z takes W days, and today is..." (calendar arithmetic the user hasn't done)
- **Pattern recognition** -- "This is the third time this week this issue appeared" (something the user would only notice if they looked back)
- **Dependency mapping** -- "This task needs sign-off from 3 people, 2 haven't responded, and the deadline is Thursday" (orchestrating through bureaucracy)
- **Threshold breach** -- "You've just crossed the legal/contractual/operational threshold where action is now required"

The best Pulse messages don't ask "have you thought about X?" -- they say **"X needs to happen by [date] and here's the path to get there."**
