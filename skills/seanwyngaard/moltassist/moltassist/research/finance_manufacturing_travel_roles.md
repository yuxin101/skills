## 1. Insurance Agents/Brokers

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Morning (08:00-09:30):** Review overnight emails, check claims updates, scan insurer portals for policy changes or system notifications. Flag renewals due in next 14-30 days.
- **Midday:** Client calls, new business quoting, follow-ups on outstanding documentation. Insurers often release rate updates or scheme changes during business hours.
- **Afternoon (14:00-16:00):** Admin -- policy endorsements, claims submissions, CRM updates, renewal letters.
- **Weekly:** Pipeline review, team huddle (if brokerage). Monday = renewal priority triage. Friday = outstanding docs chase.
- **Month-end:** Renewal book reconciliation, commission statement reconciliation from insurers.

### Before Tasks
- Before a client renewal meeting: Pull current policy schedule, loss ratio history, claims history, market comparison quotes. Check if insurer has changed rates since last renewal.
- Before sending renewal to client: Confirm sum-insured values are still adequate (buildings, contents, vehicles). Check for any lapses in cover or endorsements outstanding.
- Before binding cover: Confirm FICA/KYC docs are on file. Check credit check if required.

### After Tasks
- After binding a policy: Send cover note/certificate of insurance to client. Update CRM. Diarise next renewal. Submit to insurer portal.
- After a claim is lodged: Follow up on assessor appointment, keep client updated, chase insurer if turnaround exceeds SLA (typically 48h for acknowledgement).
- After receiving commission statement: Reconcile against active policy book. Flag discrepancies. Update pipeline forecast.

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Policy renewal dates** -- typically 30/60/90 day windows for contact
- **Premium payment failures** -- policy lapses if premium not received (insurer sends cancellation notice, often 15-day cure period)
- **Annual rate review announcements** from insurers (usually Q4 or mid-year)
- **Claims deadlines** -- some policies require lodgement within 30-90 days of incident
- **FIC/FICA compliance deadlines** -- risk-based approach re-verification cycles
- **FSCA (Financial Sector Conduct Authority) licence renewal** -- annual, tied to CPD hours
- **Professional indemnity PI renewal** -- brokers must maintain their own PI cover
- **E&O (Errors & Omissions) exposure** -- unrenewed or lapsed client policies create liability

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Client whose policy lapsed due to non-payment and then has a claim -- broker caught in liability crossfire
- Insurer withdrawing from a line of business mid-term (e.g., thatch roof cover, flood zones) -- scramble to rehouse clients
- Competing quote from a rival broker -- pressure to match or justify higher premium
- Rate increases at renewal -- client anger directed at broker, not insurer
- Claims that drag on -- client blames broker
- Commission clawbacks from cancellations

### Data Sources
- **Insurer portals:** Santam, OUTsurance, Hollard, Discovery Insure, Momentum, etc. (each is separate, no single API)
- **Broker management systems:** Flexi, Polaris, CRM platforms like Applied Epic or Acturis
- **Loss ratio reports** from insurer
- **Credit bureaus** (TransUnion, Experian) for risk scoring
- **FSCA register** for licence status
- **South African Insurance Association (SAIA)** circulars

## 2. Mortgage Brokers

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Morning:** Check bank rate update emails (banks often send broker bulletins at 07:00). Check outstanding pre-approvals and their expiry dates. Review pipeline (applications submitted, awaiting approval, conditionally approved, ready to register).
- **Midday:** Client consultations, new applications. Banks have submission cutoffs (typically 15:00-16:00 for same-day processing).
- **Afternoon:** Chase banks on outstanding applications. Resolve conditions (salary slips outstanding, deposit proof, etc.). Send update emails to estate agents and clients.
- **Weekly:** Monday = pipeline review + urgent chases. Wednesday = mid-week estate agent check-in. Friday = end-of-week status updates to all clients.

### Before Tasks
- Before submitting an application: Pre-screen affordability, check credit score, verify deposit availability, confirm property valuation has been done.
- Before client meeting: Pull credit report, calculate max loan amount against their income.
- Before formal approval is issued: Verify conditions list from bank -- often requires payslips, bank statements, divorce decree, or entity documents.

### After Tasks
- After approval: Send grant letter to conveyancer, client, and estate agent. Diarise registration date. Chase for bond instruction.
- After registration: Update CRM, trigger commission claim, note client for future refinancing/review.
- After rejection: Identify reason, advise client on remediation (settle debt, save longer), or resubmit to different bank.

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Pre-approval expiry** -- typically 3 months from issue
- **Rate lock expiry** -- if bank offered a fixed rate quote, it expires (often 30 days)
- **SARB MPC meetings** -- repo rate decisions affect prime rate, directly impacts variable bond repayments (6x per year in SA)
- **Offer to Purchase (OTP) deadlines** -- bond condition in the OTP has a hard deadline, failure = OTP lapses
- **Transfer duty deadlines** -- SARS penalties for late payment
- **Bank submission cutoffs** -- missing a daily cutoff delays processing by 24h+
- **Credit bureau enquiry freshness** -- enquiries older than 90 days may not reflect current picture

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- OTP deadline approaching with no approval in sight
- Client's credit score drops between pre-approval and formal application
- Bank valuation comes in below purchase price -- shortfall kills the deal
- Rate hike making the affordability calculation fail post-application
- Estate agent calling every day for updates
- Commission paid only at registration -- months of work can fall through at the last step

### Data Sources
- **Bank broker portals:** FNB BetterBond, Absa Mortgage, Nedbank Home Loans, Standard Bank (each separate)
- **ooba, BetterBond, SA Home Loans** (bond originators with multi-bank submission)
- **Credit bureaus:** TransUnion, Experian, Compuscan (XDS)
- **Lightstone / PropStats** -- property valuations and market comparables
- **Deeds Office** -- registered bonds, transfers
- **SARS eFiling** -- transfer duty receipts

### Proactive AI Opportunities -- "How Did It Know?" Moments

> **"MPC meeting is in 3 days. Current expectations are for a 25bp cut. You have 8 clients in the pipeline with variable rate applications -- a cut would improve their affordability by roughly R340/month on a R1.5M bond. Should I update their affordability worksheets now?"**

> **"The Mokoena application has been with Absa for 19 business days. Their SLA is 10 days. No update on the portal. The OTP deadline is in 6 days. I'd escalate now."**

> **"You have 4 pre-approvals expiring in the next 3 weeks -- none of them have submitted an OTP yet. Do you want me to draft a reminder to each client?"**

> **"FNB's prime-linked rate sheet was updated this morning. Three of your client quotes used last month's rates -- their monthly repayment figures are now slightly off. Want me to recalculate?"**

> **"The Van der Berg registration completed today at the Deeds Office. Commission should be claimable -- do you want me to draft the invoice to your referral partner and log it in the pipeline?"**

## 3. Stockbrokers / Investment Advisors

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Pre-market (07:00-09:00):** Markets open JSE 09:00, NYSE 15:30 SAST. Pre-market: review overnight US/Asian moves, economic calendar, SENS announcements, RNS feeds. Check client portfolio alerts.
- **Market hours:** Execute trades, monitor positions, respond to client queries triggered by market moves.
- **Post-market:** Portfolio review, report generation, CRM notes, client calls for those who check end-of-day.
- **Weekly:** Monday = macro outlook, portfolio positioning review. Friday = performance summary to clients.
- **Monthly:** Statement generation, fee billing, rebalancing reviews.
- **Quarterly:** Formal client reviews (FSP requirement), risk profile reassessment.

### Before Tasks
- Before a client review meeting: Prepare portfolio performance report, benchmark comparison, fee summary, proposed changes.
- Before executing a large trade: Check liquidity, spreads, any price-sensitive announcements pending.
- Before a new investment: Full FICA, risk profile questionnaire (RPQ), needs analysis, suitability assessment -- FSP compliance requirement.

### After Tasks
- After a trade: Contract note to client, update portfolio system, reconcile against custody.
- After market events (major move, earnings, macro shock): Client communication -- proactive update beats panic calls.
- After quarter-end: Generate ROAR (Record of Advice), update CRM, submit fee invoices.

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **JSE SENS announcements** -- price-sensitive, results in trading halts or sharp moves
- **SARB MPC / US FOMC meetings** -- macro volatility events
- **Earnings seasons** -- JSE: Feb/Mar (Dec year-ends), Aug (June year-ends)
- **Dividend record dates** -- clients buying/selling around ex-div
- **Tax year-end (Feb 28 SA)** -- capital gains harvesting, TFSA top-up deadline
- **TFSA annual contribution deadline** -- March 1 new tax year
- **FSP licence renewal** -- annual with CPD requirements
- **Client risk profile expiry** -- typically re-done every 3 years or on major life event
- **Portfolio rebalancing thresholds** -- drift triggers

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Market crash and client panic calling simultaneously
- Client makes a loss and questions suitability of advice -- compliance/legal exposure
- Executing trade on wrong security or wrong direction (fat finger)
- Compliance audit with incomplete advice records
- Fee disclosure disputes
- Concentration risk in a client portfolio that's blown up

### Data Sources
- **Bloomberg Terminal / Reuters Eikon** -- institutional
- **SENS (JSE regulatory news service)**
- **Portfolio management systems:** PortfolioMetrix, Iress, Wealth Platform (South Africa)
- **Morningstar, Fundsdata.co.za** -- fund analytics
- **FNB Securities, Absa Stockbrokers, Standard Bank Online Share Trading** -- execution platforms
- **Sanlam iTrade, EasyEquities** -- retail-side execution
- **FSCA register**

> **"FOMC decision tonight SA time (02:00). Three of your clients have significant USD-denominated exposure. You might want to set alerts and have a market open comment ready for tomorrow morning."**

> **"Tax year-end is February 28 -- 18 days away. Four clients have unrealised capital losses they could harvest to offset gains elsewhere. Combined tax saving estimate: ~R67,000. Should I flag this in their next review agenda?"**

## 4. Tax Agents / Preparers

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Peak season (Jul-Nov in SA):** Non-stop. Every hour is billable. Priority queue based on deadline proximity and complexity.
- **Off-peak (Dec-Mar):** Provisional tax, VAT returns, catch-up work, planning appointments.
- **Daily:** Check eFiling/SARS eFiling portal for new assessments, query letters, audit notifications. Process completed client packs (IRP5s, investment certificates, rental income, foreign income).
- **Weekly:** Chase clients for missing documents, review query responses, send completed returns for client sign-off.

### Before Tasks
- Before filing a return: Verify all income sources declared, SARS auto-populated data reconciled against client's own records, medical aid certificate matches, retirement annuity certificate received.
- Before a SARS audit response: Prepare supporting schedules, gather source documents, prepare reconciliation of each income line.
- Before tax year-end advisory: Review capital gains, allowable deductions, retirement annuity maximisation opportunities.

### After Tasks
- After filing: Send acknowledgement of receipt (AoR) to client. Flag if assessment comes back with a refund due or additional tax payable. Note any audit flags.
- After SARS assessment: Reconcile with expected. If incorrect, prepare Notice of Objection (NOO) -- 30-day window.
- After a refund is released: Notify client. Note SARS turnaround time for future reference.

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **SARS filing season open** -- typically July 1 (SA, individuals). Non-provisional: end October. Provisional: end January.
- **Provisional tax deadlines:** Aug 31 (1st) and Feb 28 (2nd). Late = 10% penalty.
- **VAT return deadlines:** 25th of month following end of tax period (monthly or bi-monthly)
- **PAYE reconciliation (EMP501):** Oct 31 and Feb/Mar year-end
- **IT14 (company tax return):** 12 months after company year-end
- **Notice of Objection window:** 30 business days from assessment date
- **SARS debt repayment/TPA arrangements:** payment dates are hard -- missed = immediate enforcement
- **Foreign tax credit deadlines** -- DTA-dependent
- **SARS audit notifications** -- 21-day response window typically

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- SARS issuing a revised assessment adding unexpected tax -- client angry at agent
- Audit on a high-value client with poor recordkeeping
- Missing a filing deadline due to late client documents
- SARS system downtime during peak filing window (eFiling outages are common)
- Disagreement over allowable deductions -- grey areas create liability
- A client not disclosing income fully -- agent professional risk

### Data Sources
- **SARS eFiling** -- primary platform
- **SARS Online Query System (SOQS)**
- **SARS MobiApp and SARS Branch notifications**
- **Third-party data sources on eFiling** -- auto-populated IRP5, medical, investment data
- **Accounting software:** Sage, Xero, QuickBooks (for business clients)
- **SARS tariff publications, SARS binding rulings**

### Proactive AI Opportunities -- "How Did It Know?" Moments

> **"Mrs. Nkosi's assessment came in R34,000 higher than expected. The difference appears to be SARS auto-populated interest income from Nedbank that wasn't on her documents. The NOO window closes in 18 business days -- do you need time to verify with her?"**

> **"You have 6 provisional tax clients due Aug 31. It's Aug 14. Two haven't responded to your document request from last week. The safe harbour estimate is due -- I can calculate it from last year's taxable income if you want to file on that basis."**

## 5. Auditors

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Engagement rhythm follows audit cycle:** Planning -> Fieldwork -> Completion -> Reporting
- **Planning phase:** Risk assessment, materiality calculation, audit program design, team briefing.
- **Fieldwork:** On-site or remote -- testing controls, substantive procedures, requesting audit evidence.
- **Completion:** Clearing review notes, resolving exceptions, drafting management letter, obtaining representations.
- **Reporting:** Issuing audit opinion, filing with CIPC if applicable.
- **Daily during fieldwork:** Team status meetings, evidence tracking, exception escalation.

### Before Tasks
- Before engagement kickoff: Sign off on engagement letter, confirm independence (no conflicts), set materiality, review prior year workpapers.
- Before issuing an opinion: Complete completion checklist -- all sections signed off, management representations received, subsequent events reviewed.
- Before a board/audit committee presentation: Prepare summary of key findings, unadjusted misstatements schedule, internal control recommendations.

### After Tasks
- After fieldwork: Clear all review notes, archive workpapers in audit software (CaseWare, ProSystem, TeamMate).
- After the audit report is signed: File with CIPC (if required), issue to client, update engagement status.
- After a modified opinion is issued: Document rationale thoroughly -- regulatory and legal exposure is high.

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Client year-end** -- audit clock starts here (most clients: Feb/June/Dec year-ends)
- **CIPC annual return deadline** -- companies must file within specific periods post year-end
- **JSE reporting deadlines** -- listed entities: preliminary results within 3 months, audited within 4 months
- **IRBA (Independent Regulatory Board for Auditors) registration renewal** -- annual
- **CPD hours** -- IRBA requires 40 hours per year (20 verifiable)
- **Audit quality review (AQR) cycles** -- IRBA inspections
- **Engagement letter renewal** -- annually before each engagement starts
- **Subsequent events cutoff** -- typically audit report date; anything after requires separate disclosure
- **Going concern assessment** -- 12-month horizon from reporting date

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Client not providing audit evidence timeously -- delays the entire schedule
- Material misstatement discovered late in engagement
- Independence threats emerging mid-audit (undisclosed relationship, fee dependency)
- Client pressuring for a clean opinion when issues exist -- ethics dilemma
- IRBA inspection selecting this engagement for quality review
- Staff turnover mid-engagement
- Simultaneous busy season across multiple clients

### Data Sources
- **Audit software:** CaseWare (dominant in SA), TeamMate+, ProSystem fx
- **Client's ERP:** SAP, Sage, Pastel, Oracle -- for data analytics
- **CIPC portal** -- company registration, director details, annual returns
- **South African Revenue Service** -- tax compliance status letters
- **IRBA database** -- auditor registration, independence declarations
- **South African Institute of Chartered Accountants (SAICA)**
- **ISA (International Standards on Auditing)** guidance

> **"Your IRBA CPD log shows 14 verifiable hours against the 20 required. The cycle closes in 9 weeks. There are 3 SAICA webinars this month that qualify -- want me to register you?"**

> **"CaseWare shows 4 open review notes on the ABC Retail engagement that are 3+ days old without a response. The reporting deadline is 12 days away. This needs escalation to the audit senior today."**

> **"CIPC has updated its annual return late filing penalty structure -- companies now face R250/day for late filings. Two of your audit clients have returns due in the next 30 days. Worth flagging in your completion memo."**

> **"The going concern assessment for Omega Manufacturing requires looking 12 months forward from June 30. Their overdraft facility expires October 31 -- well within the 12-month horizon. This may need a specific note and possible modification. Have you spoken to the bank yet about renewal?"**

## 6. Payroll Officers

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Payroll cycle drives everything** -- most SA employers: monthly pay (25th or last working day).
- **Week 1-2:** Collect input data -- new starters, terminations, leave taken, overtime, commission, deductions.
- **Week 3:** Process payroll run in system, reconcile variances, check exceptions.
- **Week 4 (crunch):** Final review, sign-off from manager, generate payslips, submit banking file.
- **Pay day:** Release payslips, confirm EFT processed.
- **Daily:** Ad hoc queries from employees -- "my deduction is wrong," tax queries, banking detail changes.

### Before Tasks
- Before processing payroll run: Confirm all input received (HR, finance, line managers). Check for new employees, terminations, salary changes, garnishee orders.
- Before submitting PAYE/UIF/SDL to SARS: Reconcile payroll deductions against EMP201 totals.
- Before year-end (February): Begin EMP501 reconciliation -- all IRP5s must balance with SARS data.

### After Tasks
- After payroll runs: Archive payslips, update leave balances, file submission confirmations, bank confirmation references.
- After EMP201 submission: File proof of payment, log submission reference.
- After a termination: Issue IRP5/IT3(a) within 14 days (SARS requirement). Process pension/provident fund withdrawal documentation.
- After year-end reconciliation: Issue all IRP5s to employees, submit EMP501 to SARS, handle any queries.

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Monthly PAYE/UIF/SDL EMP201 submission:** Due 7th of following month (SARS)
- **Monthly SDL payment:** Same as EMP201 (SETA/SDL levy)
- **Compensation Fund (COID) return:** March 31 annually (Department of Labour)
- **EMP501 bi-annual reconciliation:** End of October (interim) and May 31 (final)
- **IRP5 issue to employees:** By May 31 each year
- **Unemployment Insurance Fund returns:** Monthly via uFiling
- **New minimum wage adjustments:** Usually March 1 annually (National Minimum Wage Act)
- **Public holidays:** Must adjust payroll for pay dates falling on PH -- banking EFTs may not process
- **Employee garnishee orders:** Typically must implement within specific timeframes from court order date

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Banking file rejected by bank (wrong account number, batch error)
- System outage on pay day -- employees not paid
- SARS queries on EMP501 reconciliation errors
- Late input data from managers forcing overtime to meet deadline
- Employee disputing deductions or tax -- angry person at your desk
- Garnishee orders stacking up and causing compliance complexity
- Year-end IRP5 errors discovered after submission

### Data Sources
- **Payroll systems:** Sage 300 People, VIP Payroll (Sage), SimplePay, PaySpace, Pastel Payroll
- **SARS eFiling** -- EMP201, EMP501
- **uFiling** -- UIF submissions
- **Department of Labour -- COID portal**
- **Banking platforms** -- EFT batch submission (Nedbank Business Online, FNB Business, ABSA)
- **HR/HRIS systems** -- SAP SuccessFactors, Workday, BambooHR (for leave and employee data)

## 7. Debt Collectors

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Daily:** Work a debtor list -- calls, SMSes, letter generation. Prioritise by debt age, debt size, payment promise due dates, and legal stage.
- **Morning standup:** What promises-to-pay (PTPs) are due today? Which accounts have crossed into legal threshold?
- **Afternoon:** Follow up on broken PTPs. Update system notes. Escalate to legal/tracing.
- **Weekly:** Performance review against collection targets. Legal hand-offs (accounts where demand letters have expired and no response). Tracing requests for skipped debtors.
- **Month-end:** Report to client (if collecting on behalf) -- collected vs. outstanding, aging analysis.

### Before Tasks
- Before calling a debtor: Pull account history -- how many calls, last payment, promises made and broken, legal status, NCA (National Credit Act) compliance.
- Before sending a legal letter: Confirm demand letter process was followed (Section 129 NCA notice required before legal action).
- Before accepting a payment arrangement: Verify offer against approved parameters. Check if the debtor is over-indebted.

### After Tasks
- After a payment promise: Log exact amount, date, reference in system. Schedule follow-up call 24h after due date.
- After a payment is received: Allocate correctly. Send receipt. Update account status. Recalculate balance.
- After legal hand-off: Hand over complete file -- correspondence history, all notices served. Track legal progress.

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Section 129 notice timing** (NCA) -- 10 business days must elapse before legal action can commence
- **Prescription deadlines** -- debt prescribes in 3 years (Prescription Act) if no acknowledgement or payment; 30 years for judgements
- **Promise-to-pay due dates** -- broken PTP immediately triggers next escalation
- **Legal summons response window** -- debtor has 10 days to respond
- **Default judgement application window** -- if no response, apply at 20 business days
- **Monthly reporting deadlines** for clients
- **Payment arrangement expiry** -- if no payment for X months, arrangement lapses

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Call centre targets vs. ethical collection practices
- Debtors who can't pay and become hostile or suicidal (duty of care obligations)
- NCA non-compliance risk -- collecting on prescribed debt is illegal
- Broken PTPs cascading -- promising reports to client and missing
- Tracing failures on skipped debtors -- hard to collect from a ghost
- Legal costs escalating on accounts with insufficient balance to cover them

### Data Sources
- **Debt collection systems:** Collect+, Debtpack, Adept (SA-specific)
- **Credit bureaus:** TransUnion, Experian, Compuscan -- debtor location, affordability assessment
- **Tracing agencies:** Managed Integrity Evaluation (MIE), LexisNexis
- **Courts system** -- online portal varies by magistrates' court
- **NCR (National Credit Regulator)** -- compliance guidelines

### Proactive AI Opportunities -- "How Did It Know?" Moments

> **"The Dlamini account (R12,400 outstanding) last had a payment in March 2023. That's 35 months ago. No written acknowledgement since. This debt is approaching prescription. If you don't get an acknowledgement of debt signed before the 3-year mark, it cannot be collected. You have approximately 7 weeks."**

> **"Section 129 notice was sent to Mahlangu on the 3rd. 10 business days expire on the 17th -- that's tomorrow. No response has been received. You're clear to hand to legal from tomorrow morning."**

> **"Your client expects the monthly collection report by the 1st -- 4 days away. Current collections are at 67% of target. The shortfall is R89,000 -- mostly driven by 3 large accounts in legal. Do you want me to draft a commentary explaining the pipeline?"**

> **"New NCR guidance on prescribed debt was issued last week. Two of your active portfolios include accounts from 2020 -- you may have accounts approaching the prescription threshold. I'd run an aging audit across those portfolios before the week is out."**

## 8. Supply Chain Managers

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Morning:** Review overnight shipment tracking -- any customs holds, port delays, carrier exceptions. Check inventory levels vs. reorder points. Scan supplier alerts.
- **Midday:** Coordination with procurement, warehouse, and production teams. S&OP (Sales & Operations Planning) alignment.
- **Afternoon:** Issue resolution -- expediting, supplier escalations, customer delivery updates.
- **Weekly:** KPI review (OTIF -- On Time In Full, lead times, supplier performance). S&OP meeting. Carrier performance review.

## 16. Hotel Revenue Managers

### Context
Revenue managers own the pricing and distribution strategy -- they decide what rates to sell, on which channels, with which restrictions, to maximise RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Room). They work with data constantly.

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Morning (07:00-09:00):** Check the overnight pick-up report -- how many rooms booked since yesterday for each future date. Check comp set pricing (what competitors are charging). Review OTA parity.
- **09:00:** Daily revenue meeting with GM and F&B -- yesterday's performance, today's expected pickup, any pricing actions needed.
- **Throughout day:** Rate adjustments (yield management), channel management, responding to corporate rate requests.
- **Weekly:** 90-day forward forecast review, segmentation analysis, group displacement analysis.
- **Monthly:** Month-end performance report, STR (competitive set benchmarking), budget vs. actual.
- **Annually:** Budget season (Q3/Q4) -- forecast full next year by month.

### Before Tasks
- Before a major event in the city: Analyse historical pickup for similar events, set minimum stay restrictions, close out lower rates, open premium rates.
- Before budget submission: Consolidate 12-month forecast, stress test assumptions, align with commercial team.
- Before a group quote is accepted: Run displacement analysis -- does this group block better-paying individual transient bookings?

### After Tasks
- After a high-demand period: Post-event analysis -- what rate did competitors achieve? What was left on the table? What restrictions worked?
- After a month-end: Update STR data, identify comp set shifts, refine forward forecast.

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Events calendar** -- Cape Town Jazz Festival, Cape Town International Convention Centre events, sports events (DHL Newlands, DHL Stormers), public holidays, school holidays
- **Booking windows** -- leisure books 3-8 weeks out, corporate same week, groups 3-12 months out
- **Rate loading deadlines** -- GDS rate updates have implementation lead times (Amadeus/Sabre: 24-48h)
- **OTA contract rate periods** -- Booking.com / Expedia promotional programme enrolment windows
- **STR (competitive benchmarking) data release** -- weekly, on Tuesdays
- **Budget submission deadline** -- typically October/November for following year
- **Group option expiry** -- group holds on room blocks without deposit have expiry dates
- **Minimum stay restrictions** -- must be loaded before demand materialises (too late once rooms are booked)

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Opening a high-demand period too cheap and selling out before the peak
- Keeping rates too high and sitting at 40% occupancy the night before
- Rate parity violation -- OTA undercutting your direct rate (contractual issue)
- A large group cancelling close to arrival, leaving a hole in inventory
- Forecast significantly wrong -- GM questioning the revenue strategy

### Data Sources
