## 1. Doctors / GPs

### 1.1 Daily / Weekly Tasks
- **Patient consultations** (15-20 min slots, back-to-back) -- diagnose, prescribe, refer
- **Chronic disease management** reviews (diabetes, hypertension, asthma, etc.)
- **Repeat prescription** reviews and sign-offs
- **Lab / pathology result reviews** -- flag abnormal values, action or inform patients
- **Referral letters** -- draft and send to specialists
- **Administrative dictation** -- clinical notes, SOAP notes, discharge summaries
- **Call-backs / phone consultations** for routine results or patient queries
- **Ward rounds** (if hospital-affiliated) -- check on admitted patients
- **Team meetings / MDTs** (multidisciplinary team meetings) -- complex case reviews
- **CME / CPD** (continuing medical education) -- mandatory ongoing learning
- **Prescription audits** -- reviewing drug choices, dosages, interactions
- **Sick note / medical certificate** issuance

### 1.2 What They Need BEFORE Tasks / Appointments
- **Patient chart pre-read**: recent visits, current medications, allergies, outstanding results
- **Lab results** already pulled up and flagged before the patient walks in
- **Referral responses** -- has the specialist replied yet?
- **Pending actions** from last visit (did the patient get the blood test ordered?)
- **Vaccination status** -- is the patient overdue for anything?
- **Chronic medication expiry** -- is the patient about to run out of a repeat?
- **Drug interaction checks** pre-loaded for polypharmacy patients
- **Medical aid / insurance pre-auth** status for any planned procedures

### 1.3 Follow-Up AFTER Tasks
- **Result notification** -- call/message patient when labs come back
- **Non-attendance follow-up** -- patients who didn't show for important appointments
- **Referral tracking** -- did the specialist see the patient? Was there a report back?
- **Prescription monitoring** -- has the patient collected their medication?
- **Chronic disease recall** -- patient is due for their 3-month or 6-month review
- **Post-procedure check-in** -- call the day after a procedure or hospital stay
- **Clinical notes finalisation** -- notes left open must be closed off
- **Billing/coding** -- ensure consultation was coded correctly for medical aid claims

### 1.4 Time-Sensitive Triggers
| Trigger | Typical Cycle |
|---|---|
| Chronic disease review recalls | Every 3-6 months |
| Repeat prescription renewals | Monthly to every 6 months |
| Abnormal lab result action | Same day / within 24 hours |
| Controlled substance prescription limits | Monthly (legal limit) |
| CPD/CME credits | Annual requirement (e.g., 30-50 hours/year) |
| Medical license renewal (HPCSA / GMC etc.) | Annual |
| Professional indemnity insurance renewal | Annual |
| Flu vaccine season | April-May (Southern Hemisphere) / Sept-Oct (Northern) |
| Child immunisation schedule reminders | Based on patient DOB |
| Cancer screening recalls (Pap, mammogram, colonoscopy) | Per protocol / patient age |

### 1.5 Data Sources Monitored
- **Practice management system** (e.g., Best Practice, Healtheon, GoodX)
- **Pathology portals** (Lancet, Ampath, National Health Lab in SA)
- **Medical aid portals** (pre-auths, claim status)
- **HPCSA / GMC / AHPRA** (licensing body notices)
- **National/regional health alerts** (outbreak notifications, drug recalls)
- **Drug databases** (MIMS, UpToDate, Medscape)
- **Specialist consultation letters** (faxed or EMR-linked)
- **Prescription monitoring programs** (controlled substances)
- **CPD tracking platform** (e.g., Solutionist Thinkers in SA)

### 1.6 Proactive AI Opportunities
- "You have 3 patients with outstanding lab results from last week -- 1 is flagged abnormal."
- "Mrs. van der Berg's repeat prescription expires in 5 days and no appointment is booked."
- "Your CPD hours are at 18/30 for the year -- renewal is in 4 months."
- "Flu vaccine season starts in 6 weeks -- last year you ordered X doses."
- "Patient X missed their 3-month diabetic review -- last HbA1c was elevated."

## 2. Lawyers / Attorneys

### 2.1 Daily / Weekly Tasks
- **Client consultations** -- intake, strategy sessions, updates
- **Drafting legal documents** -- contracts, pleadings, affidavits, wills, letters of demand
- **Court appearances** -- hearings, trials, directions hearings
- **Legal research** -- case law, statutes, regulatory changes
- **Correspondence** -- opposing counsel, clients, courts, regulators
- **File management** -- opening matters, updating status, closing files
- **Billing / time recording** -- tracking billable hours per matter
- **Due diligence** (corporate/M&A) -- reviewing disclosure documents
- **Negotiations** -- settlement discussions, contract negotiations
- **Trust account management** -- handling client funds (highly regulated)
- **Compliance checks** -- AML (anti-money laundering) KYC verifications

### 2.2 What They Need BEFORE Tasks / Meetings
- **Matter file review** -- last actions taken, open items, client instructions
- **Opponent profile** -- who's on the other side? history of the matter?
- **Court bundle / case documents** consolidated and organised
- **Relevant case law** printed or pinned
- **Deadlines audit** -- are there any filing deadlines in the next 7 days on this matter?
- **Client payment status** -- are there outstanding fees that need to be flagged?
- **Trust account balance** for the matter (if disbursements needed)
- **Regulatory updates** -- any recent statute or rule changes affecting this area?

### 2.3 Follow-Up AFTER Tasks
- **Attendance notes** -- contemporaneous notes of advice given (liability protection)
- **Action list updates** -- what was agreed, by whom, by when
- **Court order / directions** -- capture deadlines imposed by the court
- **Client update communication** -- brief client on outcome of court appearance
- **File billing** -- record time spent immediately after matter
- **Service of documents** -- confirm documents were served and record proof
- **Diarising** next steps / return dates
- **Trust account reconciliation** after disbursements

### 2.4 Time-Sensitive Triggers
| Trigger | Notes |
|---|---|
| Prescription of claims (statutes of limitations) | 3 years typical, varies by claim type and jurisdiction |
| Court filing deadlines | Set by court rules -- missing = case dismissed |
| Notice periods (letters of demand) | Usually 14-30 days before action |
| Contract execution deadlines | Fixed by the deal |
| Compliance reporting (AML, FICA) | Monthly / quarterly |
| Trust account audit | Annual (regulated) |
| Practicing certificate renewal | Annual |
| CPD requirements | Annual (varies: 10-12 hours typical) |
| POPIA / GDPR data breach notification | 72 hours from discovery |
| Lease / contract renewal notice windows | Often 30-90 days before expiry |

### 2.5 Data Sources Monitored
- **Practice management system** (e.g., Clio, LEAP, PracticePanther, GhostPractice)
- **Court portals** (ECOURTS, CaseLines in SA, PACER in US)
- **Legal research platforms** (LexisNexis, Westlaw, SAFLII, Jutastat)
- **Law Society / Bar Council notices**
- **Government Gazette** (new regulations, proclamations)
- **Client portals** (deal rooms, data rooms for M&A)
- **Trust account banking portal**
- **CIPC** (company registrations and status -- SA)
- **Deeds Office** (property searches -- SA)

### 2.6 Proactive AI Opportunities

- "New case law published this week affects the construction dispute you're running."

## 3. Accountants / Bookkeepers

### 3.1 Daily / Weekly Tasks

**Bookkeepers (day-to-day operational focus):**
- Processing invoices and purchase orders
- Reconciling bank statements
- Payroll processing (weekly/bi-weekly/monthly)
- Accounts payable -- paying suppliers on time
- Accounts receivable -- chasing outstanding invoices
- Petty cash management
- Entering transactions into accounting software
- VAT / GST capture and categorisation

**Accountants (oversight and reporting focus):**
- Monthly / quarterly management accounts
- Financial statement preparation
- Tax return preparation and lodgement (income tax, VAT, payroll tax)
- Client advisory meetings
- Audit preparation and support
- Year-end closing procedures
- Cash flow forecasting
- Budget vs actual variance analysis

### 3.2 What They Need BEFORE Tasks / Meetings
- **Client financials up to date** -- are all transactions captured and reconciled?
- **Prior period reports** for comparison
- **Outstanding information requests** -- has the client sent all source documents?
- **Tax deadlines** for this client in the next 30 days
- **SARS / ATO / HMRC correspondence** -- any pending queries or assessments?
- **Audit file** status -- what's still missing?
- **Payroll changes** flagged -- new hires, terminations, salary changes

### 3.3 Follow-Up AFTER Tasks
- **File deliverables** -- lodge returns, send reports to client
- **Follow-up on approvals** -- financial statements needing director sign-off
- **Client query responses** -- document advice given
- **SARS / tax authority payment confirmations** -- ensure payment reflects
- **Post-payroll** -- distribute payslips, file UIF/SDL returns
- **Reconciliation sign-off** -- bank rec approved and filed

### 3.4 Time-Sensitive Triggers

| UIF / SDL submissions (SA) | Monthly |
| Annual financial statements | Within 6 months of year-end |
| Company year-end | Per client (varies -- not always Dec) |
| CIPC annual return (SA) | Annually, within 30 days of anniversary |

| POPIA compliance review | Ongoing |

### 3.5 Data Sources Monitored

## Cross-Cutting Observations

### Universal Patterns (All Professions)

| Pattern | Description |
|---|---|
| **Licensing renewals** | Every professional has an annual license/registration -- AI should track and remind 60/30/14 days out |
| **CPD / CME requirements** | All regulated professions require ongoing education -- track hours and deadline |
| **Client/patient recall cycles** | Every profession has recurring engagement cycles -- AI should know them and trigger proactively |
| **Follow-up lag** | The most common failure point is forgetting to follow up after a task -- AI should auto-generate follow-up reminders |
| **Deadline asymmetry** | Missing deadlines in these professions causes disproportionate harm (legal dismissal, tax penalties, patient harm) -- AI should bias toward early warning |
| **Document expiry** | All professions have documents with expiry dates (policies, mandates, certificates) -- AI should index and track these |
| **Unprompted client communication** | Proactive client updates (even when there's nothing to report) build trust and reduce inbound enquiries |

### The "How Did It Know?" Moments

These are the highest-value AI moments -- anticipating before the professional thinks to ask:

1. **The approaching deadline they forgot about** -- statute of limitations, mandate expiry, licence renewal
2. **The patient/client who's overdue** -- hasn't been seen in X months, hasn't responded
3. **The external event that affects their clients** -- rate change, market move, regulation change, outbreak
4. **The gap between what was agreed and what happened** -- "you were going to follow up on X, you haven't"
5. **The prep they need for tomorrow** -- "you have a complex patient first thing, here's their file summary"
6. **The seasonal trigger** -- flu season, tax year-end, school holidays, spring property market
7. **The compliance risk they didn't see coming** -- CPD hours running low, ROA expired, vaccine record incomplete
