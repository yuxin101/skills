# Business Roles Research -- Proactive AI Assistant Context

> **Purpose:** Document what each professional role actually does day-to-day, what they need prepared in advance, what follow-up looks like, what time-sensitive triggers they face, and what data sources they monitor. The goal is to understand what a proactive AI assistant would need to surface *without being asked*.

---

## 1. CEOs / Founders / Executives

### Daily/Weekly Tasks
- **Daily:** Review overnight emails, scan news for company/industry/competitor mentions, attend internal standups, make decisions on escalated issues, 1:1s with direct reports
- **Weekly:** Executive team meeting (leadership sync), board/investor updates (monthly/quarterly but prep is weekly), review key metrics dashboards, strategic planning sessions
- **Recurring:** Fundraising communications (founders), investor relations, public-facing commitments (interviews, press, conferences), hiring decisions, budget approvals
- **Ad hoc:** Crisis management, major deals, legal reviews, product launches, pivots

### What They Need BEFORE Tasks/Meetings
- **Before any meeting:** Who is attending, their roles, last interaction context, any open action items from prior meeting, relevant recent news about those people/companies
- **Before board meetings:** Clean financial summaries, KPI trends (not raw data -- narrative), risks to flag, decisions that need approval, what's changed since last board pack
- **Before investor calls:** Portfolio company updates, last communication log, what commitments were made, current ask or purpose of call
- **Before press/media:** Talking points aligned with recent messaging, known gotcha questions, recent news that might come up
- **Daily:** A curated briefing -- what happened overnight, what's urgent today, what decisions need to be made

### Follow-Up After Tasks
- Action item tracking from meetings -- who owns what, by when
- Follow-up emails to external parties (investors, partners, press)
- Delegation of tasks to direct reports with deadlines
- Logging decisions made for accountability and future reference
- Board meeting minutes or executive summaries sent within 24-48h

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Board meeting cycles (monthly/quarterly -- prep begins 1-2 weeks out)
- Fundraising rounds (Series A/B etc -- months-long process with many deadlines)
- Regulatory/compliance filing deadlines
- Investor update cadences (monthly for startups, quarterly for mature)
- Employee review cycles (quarterly/annual)
- Company anniversaries, milestones, product launch dates
- Tax and financial reporting periods
- Contract renewal windows (major vendors, partnerships)
- Competitor announcements or funding news (reactive)
- Expiry of equity grants, options vesting cliffs

### Data Sources They Monitor
- **Financial:** P&L, runway, burn rate, ARR/MRR dashboards (Xero, QuickBooks, Stripe)
- **Product:** Active users, churn, feature adoption (Mixpanel, Amplitude, Segment)
- **Sales:** Pipeline health, win rates, deal velocity (Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive)
- **Ops:** Headcount, hiring pipeline, open reqs (Lever, Greenhouse, BambooHR)
- **Comms:** Executive inbox, Slack/Teams (especially #exec, #alerts channels)
- **External:** Google News alerts, Twitter/X for brand mentions, Crunchbase, LinkedIn
- **Calendar:** Personal + PA-managed calendar -- their time is the most constrained resource

### Proactive AI Triggers ("How did it know?")
- "Your board meeting is in 8 days -- your last KPI deck is outdated. Want me to pull the latest numbers?"
- "TechCrunch just covered your competitor -- here's the key quote you'll probably be asked about."
- "You have a call with [Investor Name] in 2h. Your last interaction was 6 weeks ago. Here's a summary."
- "Your Q2 investor update is due next week. Last month's update draft is here -- want me to start a new one?"
- "You haven't responded to [Name]'s email from 3 days ago. It looks important."

## 2. Sales People / Account Executives

### Daily/Weekly Tasks
- **Daily:** Work the inbox (inbound leads, replies, objections), make outbound calls/emails, update CRM after every touchpoint, check pipeline for stalled deals
- **Weekly:** Sales team sync (forecast call), review quota attainment vs. target, prepare demos or proposals for active opportunities
- **Pipeline stages:** Prospecting -> Qualification -> Demo/Discovery -> Proposal -> Negotiation -> Close -> Handoff to CS
- **Ongoing:** Building relationships with prospects and existing accounts, cross-sell/upsell into existing accounts, re-engaging cold prospects

### What They Need BEFORE Tasks/Meetings
- **Before any prospect call:**
  - Company overview (size, industry, recent news, funding)
  - Contact's role, tenure, LinkedIn activity, any prior interactions
  - What they've engaged with (emails opened, pages visited, demo requests)
  - What stage they're at in the pipeline and what the last touchpoint was
  - Competitor landscape (are they using a rival product?)
  - Likely pain points based on their industry/size
- **Before renewal/expansion calls:**
  - Current contract value, term end date, usage stats
  - Support ticket history (any recent problems?)
  - Last QBR notes, any commitments made
  - Expansion opportunities to pitch

### Follow-Up After Tasks
- Same-day CRM update after every call or meeting
- Send agreed-upon resources (case studies, proposals, pricing)
- Schedule next steps (follow-up call, trial start, legal review)
- Internal handoff notes to Sales Engineer, Legal, or CS
- Post-deal: introduction email to Customer Success
- Lost deals: log reason, schedule re-engagement reminder (90 days)

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **End of month/quarter:** Quota deadlines -- everything accelerates in final 2 weeks
- **Deal-specific:** Proposal expiry dates, procurement deadlines, budget cycles (prospect's fiscal year end)
- **Contract renewals:** 90/60/30 day windows -- must be initiated proactively
- **Trial expirations:** Convert or lose during trial window
- **Competitive threats:** Prospect mentions evaluating competitor -- urgency spikes
- **Inbound response time:** First response within 5 minutes drastically improves close rates (speed-to-lead)
- **Follow-up decay:** If 3+ days pass without follow-up, deal momentum drops significantly

### Data Sources They Monitor
- **CRM:** Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive -- pipeline, stage, next action, deal value
- **Email:** Gmail/Outlook with tracking (Outreach, Salesloft, Apollo, Yesware)
- **Calendar:** Demo slots, follow-up calls, internal syncs
- **Prospect intel:** LinkedIn, company website, Crunchbase, G2, news alerts
- **Product usage** (if available): Which features the account is/isn't using
- **Leaderboard/quota tracker:** How they're sitting vs. target
- **Sequencing tools:** Apollo, Outreach, Lemlist -- open rates, reply rates, sequence step status

### Proactive AI Triggers ("How did it know?")
- "Acme Corp hasn't moved in 12 days. Their trial expires in 5. Last email was unopened. Want me to draft a re-engagement?"
- "It's the 25th of March -- 6 days to quarter end. You're 23% short of quota. Here are your 3 most closeable deals."
- "TechCorp's contract renews in 47 days. You haven't initiated renewal conversations yet."
- "[Prospect] opened your proposal 4 times in the last hour -- might be a good time to call."
- "You have a demo at 2pm. Here's a quick brief on the company and the contact's LinkedIn."

## 3. HR Managers

### Daily/Weekly Tasks
- **Daily:** Respond to employee queries (policies, benefits, payroll issues), review escalations from managers, onboarding new hires, offboarding leavers
- **Weekly:** Recruiting pipeline review with hiring managers, HR metrics review (headcount, attrition, open reqs), policy work, L&D (Learning & Development) program coordination
- **Monthly:** Payroll processing and review, compliance checks, headcount reporting to finance
- **Quarterly:** Performance review cycle management, engagement surveys, benefits renewals, HR policy audits
- **Annual:** Compensation review, workforce planning, annual performance reviews, culture/engagement initiatives

### What They Need BEFORE Tasks/Meetings
- **Before hiring manager syncs:** Open reqs status, # applications, interview pipeline status, time-to-fill trend
- **Before employee relations meetings:** Full interaction history with employee, any prior warnings or commendations, relevant policy context
- **Before payroll run:** Leave balances, new starters/leavers, bonus/commission inputs, salary change approvals
- **Before leadership meetings:** Attrition data, headcount vs. plan, engagement scores, any HR risks
- **Before performance cycles:** Which managers have completed reviews, which are overdue, calibration notes

### Follow-Up After Tasks
- Document disciplinary meetings, complaints, or sensitive conversations (same day)
- Send offer letters, contracts, policy acknowledgements after onboarding sessions
- Follow up on outstanding approvals (headcount, salary changes)
- Track completion of mandatory training
- Close out offboarding tasks (equipment return, system access revoked, final pay processed)

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Payroll deadlines:** Strict cutoffs -- late inputs cause real damage
- **New starter start dates:** Onboarding prep must be complete before day 1
- **Probation periods ending:** Review required before formal confirmation
- **Contract expirations:** Fixed-term employees -- renewal or termination decision required
- **Compliance deadlines:** EEO reporting, GDPR audits, labour law compliance
- **Engagement survey windows:** Data collection + action planning cycles
- **Benefits open enrollment:** Annual window, employees must elect before deadline
- **Performance review cycles:** Hard dates -- managers and employees have deadlines
- **Visa/work permit renewals:** Must track expiry dates for foreign national employees

### Data Sources They Monitor
- **HRIS:** BambooHR, Workday, HiBob, BrightHR -- headcount, org chart, leave, contracts
- **ATS:** Greenhouse, Lever, Workable -- recruiting pipeline
- **Payroll:** Gusto, ADP, Sage -- payroll status, changes pending
- **LMS:** Training completion rates, overdue mandatory training
- **Survey tools:** Culture Amp, Lattice, Leapsome -- engagement, eNPS scores
- **Inbox + Slack:** Employee queries, manager escalations, leadership requests
- **Calendar:** Interview slots, onboarding sessions, HR calendar (payroll run dates, review cycles)

### Proactive AI Triggers ("How did it know?")
- "Payroll closes in 2 days. You have 3 pending salary changes not yet approved."
- "Jamie's probation ends next Friday. No review has been scheduled."
- "3 employees haven't completed their mandatory GDPR training -- deadline is EOD Friday."
- "You have 2 new starters on Monday. IT access and desk setup haven't been confirmed."
- "Attrition this month is 2x last month. Here are the leavers and their exit reasons."

## 4. Recruiters

### Daily/Weekly Tasks
- **Daily:** Review new applications, conduct initial screens (phone/video), send interview invites, coordinate interview schedules with hiring managers, send offer letters, update ATS
- **Weekly:** Sourcing (LinkedIn Recruiter, job boards, referrals), pipeline review with hiring managers, candidate feedback collection, req status updates
- **Ongoing:** Building talent pools, managing employer brand, tracking time-to-fill and quality-of-hire metrics, managing agency/vendor relationships

### What They Need BEFORE Tasks/Meetings
- **Before candidate interviews:** CV, job brief, role requirements, what the hiring manager cares about most, compensation range, any red flags from prior screen
- **Before hiring manager syncs:** Current pipeline stage counts, time-in-stage for each candidate, feedback outstanding from interviewers, blockers
- **Before offer calls:** Compensation data, benefits summary, offer terms ready, decision timeline
- **Before sourcing sessions:** ICP (Ideal Candidate Profile), where to look, competitor employees to target, current market rates

### Follow-Up After Tasks
- Send candidate feedback within 24-48h (good practice + legal risk mitigation)
- Update ATS immediately after each interview or decision
- Coordinate debrief meetings when there's disagreement between interviewers
- Send rejection emails promptly to declined candidates
- Post-offer: Coordinate start date, background check, equipment provisioning
- Pre-start: Welcome email, first day logistics to candidate

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Candidate availability:** Top candidates have 1-2 week decision windows -- slow processes lose them
- **Offer expiry:** Standard offer letters have 3-7 day acceptance windows
- **Background check clearance:** Must complete before start date
- **Job posting expirations:** Indeed/LinkedIn posts expire or auto-deactivate
- **Interview feedback SLAs:** Interviewers should complete scorecards within 24h -- delayed feedback stalls pipeline
- **Time-to-fill pressure:** Hiring managers feel every week a role is open
- **Start date commitments:** Once accepted, prep timeline is set in stone

### Data Sources They Monitor
- **ATS:** Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby, Workable -- full pipeline, stage, feedback, notes
- **LinkedIn Recruiter:** InMail response rates, candidate profiles, talent pools
- **Job boards:** Indeed, Glassdoor, job posting performance (views, applies, cost-per-apply)
- **Calendar:** Interview schedules, hiring manager availability
- **Email:** Candidate comms, internal coordination, offer paperwork
- **Recruiting metrics:** Time-to-fill, time-to-hire, offer acceptance rate, source of hire
- **Compensation data:** Levels.fyi, Radford, internal comp bands

### Proactive AI Triggers ("How did it know?")
- "You have 4 candidates waiting more than 5 days for feedback. Two of them have competing offers."
- "The offer to [Candidate] expires tomorrow. No response recorded in ATS."
- "Your Indeed posting for Senior Engineer expired 3 days ago. Applications have stopped."
- "Time-to-fill on the Product Manager role is now 47 days -- your average is 28."
- "3 interviewers haven't submitted scorecards for yesterday's panel. Debrief is in 2h."

## 5. Customer Support / Community Managers

### Daily/Weekly Tasks

#### Customer Support
- **Daily:** Triage inbound tickets (email, chat, phone, social), resolve issues or escalate, update internal knowledge base, track SLA compliance
- **Weekly:** Review CSAT scores, identify top ticket categories, flag product bugs or recurring issues to product/engineering, team huddle
- **Ongoing:** Maintain help docs, run QA on support team responses, handle VIP/escalated customers, coordinate with CS for churn risk accounts

#### Community Managers
- **Daily:** Monitor community platforms (Discord, Slack, Reddit, forum), moderate content, respond to posts/comments, share updates or content, engage active members
- **Weekly:** Community digest/newsletter, engagement metrics review, spotlight member contributions, run events or AMAs
- **Ongoing:** Build moderation guidelines, manage community programs (ambassador programs, events), track community growth KPIs

### What They Need BEFORE Tasks/Meetings
- **Before support shifts:** Queue depth, any major incidents or outages in last 24h, SLA breach risk (tickets near deadline), known bugs or issues to reference
- **Before customer escalation calls:** Full ticket history, customer account value/tier, what went wrong, what's been tried, what resolution options are available
- **Before community events (AMAs, launches):** Briefing on topic/guest, FAQ prep, moderation plan, likely controversial questions
- **Before weekly/monthly reviews:** CSAT trend, ticket volume by category, escalation rate, response time stats

### Follow-Up After Tasks
- Close tickets with resolution documented
- Follow up on promised fixes ("we'll update you when the bug is fixed")
- Post-escalation: Internal debrief, process improvement notes
- Community events: Post-event recap, follow up with engaged members, share recording
- Bug reports: Track fix status and notify affected customers when resolved

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **SLA breaches:** Tickets approaching response/resolution SLA deadlines -- urgent triage required
- **Outages/incidents:** Real-time -- must coordinate communication immediately
- **Churn signals:** Customer expresses frustration or intent to cancel -- escalate same day
- **Social media fires:** Negative viral posts -- response within minutes, not hours
- **Community rule violations:** Must act quickly before situation escalates
- **Product launches:** Pre-launch prep + day-of monitoring spike
- **Seasonal spikes:** Volume surges around product launches, outages, pricing changes, holidays

### Data Sources They Monitor
- **Helpdesk:** Zendesk, Intercom, Freshdesk -- queue, SLA timers, CSAT scores
- **Community platforms:** Discord, Slack, Circle, Reddit -- activity feeds, flagged posts
- **Social listening:** Twitter/X mentions, Brandwatch, Sprout Social
- **Product status:** Status page (Statuspage.io), incident tracking
- **Analytics:** Ticket volume trends, response times, resolution rates, channel breakdowns
- **CRM/CS tools:** Gainsight, ChurnZero -- customer health scores, at-risk accounts
- **Internal channels:** Slack #incidents, #product-updates -- context for what's happening

### Proactive AI Triggers ("How did it know?")
- "You have 7 tickets approaching SLA breach in the next 2 hours. Here they are by priority."
- "There's a spike in tickets about the export feature -- 12 in the last 3h. Looks like a bug. Should I flag to engineering?"
- "A post in your Discord server has 40 reactions and looks heated. You haven't seen it yet."
- "Customer [Name] has submitted 5 tickets this month and CSAT score is 2/5. Churn risk."
- "@YourBrand was mentioned 38 times on Twitter in the last hour -- something may be going viral."

## 6. Executive PAs / Virtual Assistants

### Daily/Weekly Tasks
- **Daily:** Process executive inbox (triage, draft replies, flag urgent), manage calendar (schedule, reschedule, protect focus time), coordinate travel logistics, handle expense reports
- **Weekly:** Prepare weekly briefing pack for executive, send meeting agendas in advance, follow up on outstanding action items from prior week
- **Ongoing:** Manage contacts and relationships (remember names, birthdays, preferences), coordinate across time zones, book venues/restaurants/events, manage subscriptions and vendor relationships, research on demand

### What They Need BEFORE Tasks/Meetings
- **Before scheduling anything:** Executive's current priorities, travel/blackout dates, time zone of all participants, preferred meeting times, venue/platform preferences
- **Before executive meetings:** Full context brief -- who's meeting, company overview, last interaction, purpose, talking points, logistics (location, dial-in, parking)
- **Before travel:** Itinerary finalized with all confirmations (flights, hotels, transfers, meetings mapped to location), time zone briefing, key contacts at destination, passport/visa validity check
- **Before weekly exec prep:** Open action items, upcoming decisions needed, anything hot in the inbox that hasn't been handled

### Follow-Up After Tasks
- Send meeting summaries and action items to all parties within same day
- Log commitments made (the exec said they'd intro X to Y -- schedule this)
- Expense reports submitted within the week
- Travel: collect receipts, cancel what wasn't used, submit loyalty numbers
- Thank-you notes or follow-up emails on exec's behalf when appropriate
- Update contacts with new details from meetings

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Travel booking windows:** Airfare prices spike when booked last-minute -- need 2-4 weeks lead time
- **Visa applications:** Some destinations need 4-8 weeks
- **Gift/card deadlines:** Birthdays, anniversaries, thank-you gifts -- must be ordered ahead
- **Calendar conflicts:** Double-bookings or back-to-back meetings with no buffer or travel time between
- **Reminders before commitments:** "You promised to intro [A] to [B] by end of this week"
- **Invoice/subscription renewals:** Many PAs manage vendor payments -- due dates matter
- **Board prep cycles:** PA often coordinates board pack preparation -- hard deadlines
- **Pre-meeting prep:** Briefing doc must be ready 30+ min before meeting, not after

### Data Sources They Monitor
- **Executive inbox:** Primary source of truth for incoming requests and priorities
- **Calendar:** Exec calendar (and often their own) -- scheduling, conflicts, upcoming blocks
- **Task/project tools:** Notion, Asana, Todoist -- delegated tasks and their status
- **Travel tools:** Concur, TripIt, Google Flights, Booking.com
- **Expense tools:** Expensify, Pleo, Spendesk
- **Contact/CRM:** Notion contacts, Apple Contacts, or full CRM if exec has one
- **Communication:** Slack (exec's channels), WhatsApp (often used for urgent comms)
- **Docs/Drive:** Google Drive or Notion for board packs, agendas, notes

### Proactive AI Triggers ("How did it know?")
- "You have a back-to-back at 2pm and 2:30pm with a 20-minute drive between locations -- conflict."
- "Sean's passport expires in 4 months. He's travelling to a country that requires 6 months validity."
- "You haven't sent the follow-up intro email Sean promised [Name] at last Tuesday's dinner."
- "The board meeting is in 10 days. The board pack hasn't been started yet -- last month's is here."
- "It's [Person]'s birthday this week. Sean usually sends a gift -- want me to find options?"
- "3 action items from last Monday's exec meeting are still open and unassigned."

## Cross-Role Patterns (What All of These Roles Share)

| Pattern | Details |
|---|---|
| **Inbox overload** | All roles live in their email/Slack. Smart triage and prioritisation is universally valued. |
| **Calendar as primary constraint** | Time is the scarcest resource. Anything that saves scheduling effort or surfaces conflicts early wins. |
| **Prep before meetings** | Every role benefits from a pre-meeting brief. The format differs, but the need is universal. |
| **Action item tracking** | Commitments made in meetings fall through the cracks constantly. Tracking these proactively is high-value. |
| **Deadline drift** | Recurring cycles (payroll, board meetings, contract renewals) are known in advance but often left too late. |
| **Context switching** | Most of these roles juggle 10-20 active threads at any time. Surfacing the right context at the right moment is key. |
| **External relationship signals** | When someone hasn't been contacted in a while, or when something changed about them (news, job change), that's a trigger. |

## What Makes an AI Assistant Feel Magical for These Roles

1. **Temporal awareness** -- knows what's coming up and works backward to flag prep in time
2. **Cross-source correlation** -- connects a calendar event to an inbox thread to a CRM record automatically
3. **Decay detection** -- notices when something that should have moved hasn't (stale deal, unanswered email, overdue task)
4. **Quiet intelligence** -- doesn't surface noise, only things that actually matter right now
5. **Context, not just data** -- doesn't just say "you have a meeting at 2pm", says "you have a meeting at 2pm with someone you haven't spoken to in 6 weeks who last asked about pricing"
6. **Knows the rhythm** -- understands that Q-end is high-pressure for sales, that payroll days are sacred for HR, that Monday mornings need briefings
