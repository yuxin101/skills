## 1. Restaurant Owner / Manager

### Daily / Weekly Rhythm

**Opening (7:00-11:00am for lunch service)**
- Check overnight POS reports and cash drawer totals from previous close
- Review reservation list: covers per section, any VIPs, dietary flags, large parties
- Walk the floor: table setup, cleanliness, lighting, music level
- Pre-shift staff briefing (10-15 min): specials, 86'd items, upsell focus, complaints from last service
- Confirm deliveries arrived overnight or early morning; check against invoice
- Unlock cellar/bar; prep float for tills
- Check opening checklists are signed off by opening staff

**Lunch service (11:30am-3:00pm)**
- Floor management: pacing tables, monitoring wait times, handling complaints in real time
- Watch kitchen ticket times via KDS
- Monitor table turns vs. reservation flow
- Communicate 86s to front-of-house as they happen

**Afternoon gap (3:00-5:30pm)**
- End-of-lunch POS report: covers, average spend, voids, comps
- Staff scheduling checks for evening: anyone called in sick?
- Supplier calls, invoice approvals, ordering for next day
- Respond to online reviews from the night before
- Walk kitchen: check prep levels for dinner service

**Dinner service (6:00-10:00pm)**
- Reservation management: staggered seating, walk-ins vs. booked balance
- More revenue-critical than lunch -- higher stakes management
- Monitor bar tab sizes; watch for over-service situations
- Private dining / events may overlap

**End of day (10:00pm-midnight)**
- Z-reads from all tills
- Cash reconciliation against POS
- Tip distribution (depending on model)
- Lock-up and alarm
- Final kitchen walkthrough: fridge temps, cleanliness signoff

**Weekly rhythm**
- Mon: Review weekend trading, staff meeting, payroll prep
- Tue: Deep clean day (often closed or slow), maintenance checks
- Wed: Mid-week ordering, menu review with chef
- Thu: Staff rota for following week published
- Fri-Sat: Peak service; no admin if avoidable
- Sun: Sometimes closed or short service; stock count

---

### Before Tasks

**Before service:**
- Print reservation sheets with dietary notes highlighted
- Brief staff on 86'd items and specials
- Confirm all stations are stocked (bar, POS, pass)
- Check kitchen has received all deliveries (confirm with chef)
- Verify pos float is correct

**Before the week:**
- Publish staff rota (minimum 7 days notice in most jurisdictions)
- Submit weekly orders to suppliers (produce, dry goods, beverages)
- Review forecasted covers vs. last year's same week
- Plan any promotional pushes (social, email)

**Before events (private dining, functions):**
- Confirm final guest count (usually 48h before)
- Pre-charge deposit or confirm payment method
- Brief event staff separately -- set menu, timings, speeches
- Arrange special dcor, AV, or cake orders
- Print personalised menus if required

---

### After Tasks

**End of service:**
- Compare actual covers to reservation estimate -- note variance
- Review any comp'd items or voids with reason codes
- Collect staff feedback: any issues during service?
- Check for noise complaints, incidents, any accidents to log
- Review online reviews posted during/after service (Google, TripAdvisor, Yelp)

**End of week:**
- Food cost calculation: (opening stock + purchases - closing stock) / revenue
- Labour cost as % of revenue
- Waste log review with chef
- Bank reconciliation
- Staff performance notes ahead of reviews

---

### Stress / Anxiety Points

- **Staff no-shows** -- especially on Friday/Saturday night; no buffer time to replace
- **Bad online reviews** -- especially when unjust or during peak booking windows
- **Supply chain failures** -- key ingredient missing day-of service; 86 items disappoint guests
- **Cash flow** -- high fixed costs (rent, staff) against variable revenue; slow January
- **Compliance gaps** -- a lapsed food cert or unlicensed server can trigger closure
- **Kitchen-front tension** -- breakdown in communication leads to service failures
- **POS downtime** -- during peak service, catastrophic
- **Delivery platform commission creep** -- Uber Eats / Deliveroo take 25-35%, eroding margin
- **No-show reservations** -- lost revenue, wasted prep
- **Key staff departure** -- head chef or floor manager leaving with little notice

## 2. Chef / Head Chef

### Daily / Weekly Rhythm

**Pre-service (7:00am-11:00am)**
- Review the day's reservations: covers, dietary requirements, large parties
- Check deliveries: inspect quality, weigh/count against invoice, reject substandard items
- Write or update the prep list for the team
- Brief sous chef and section leads on the plan for the day
- Taste-test any batch-cooked items from previous day

**Mise en place (10:00am-12:00pm)**
- Supervise all sections: stocks, sauces, butchery, pastry prep
- Confirm specials are plated and consistent before they go on the menu
- Walk every section before service starts -- hygiene check, organisation check
- Check all fridge temps are logged (HACCP compliance)

**Service (12:00pm-3:00pm / 6:00pm-10:00pm)**
- On the pass: calling orders, controlling quality and timing
- Expediting: making sure every plate that leaves the kitchen is correct
- Managing kitchen tempo: preventing bottlenecks, staying ahead on volume
- Communicating 86s to front of house as stock depletes
- Managing any mid-service crises (equipment failure, missing ingredient)

**Post-service**
- Review waste: what was prepped but not used?
- Debrief with kitchen team: what went wrong, what can improve
- Update prep lists for next service
- Check and sign off on fridge/freezer temperature logs
- Lock up the kitchen if last out

**Weekly**
- Menu development: recipe testing, costing, sourcing new ingredients
- Supplier meetings or calls: pricing, seasonal availability
- Staff performance reviews / training sessions
- Deep clean day management
- Food cost review with manager/owner

---

### Before Tasks

**Before service:**
- Print prep lists per section
- Confirm all deliveries are in and accounted for
- Verify special dietary orders are flagged to the right station
- Taste all specials before they're called
- Ensure all HACCP temperature checks are up to date
- Tool check: all knives sharp, equipment functioning

**Before the week:**
- Write ordering list based on forecasted covers and current stock
- Plan menu around seasonal availability and cost
- Assign section responsibilities and any training focus

**Before events:**
- Write a specific event prep list (separate from  la carte)
- Pre-portion where possible to reduce service pressure
- Brief the entire kitchen team -- timings, headcount, dietary flags
- Plan plating for service style (buffet vs. plated vs. family style)

---

### After Tasks

- Food waste log: what was prepped but binned? Why?
- Fridge/freezer temp sign-off
- Check stock levels for next service; flag any critical shortages
- Review section cleanliness before leaving
- Update recipe database if anything changed mid-service
- Debrief with sous chef

---

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Food Safety Manager certification | Every 3-5 years | Required for head chef in most jurisdictions |
| HACCP review / audit | Annual (or after incident) | Must be up to date |
| Allergen training renewal | Annual | Legal requirement in many countries |
| Seasonal menu changes | 4x/year | Must be costed, printed, briefed |
| Supplier contract reviews | Annual | Pricing renegotiation window |
| Equipment service (combi oven, fridges) | Annual | Contract with equipment company |
| Food hygiene inspection readiness | Ongoing | Unannounced visits in many markets |
| Staff food hygiene certificate renewal | Every 3 years | Track per employee |

---

### Stress / Anxiety Points

- **Delivery failures** -- key item doesn't arrive day-of, forces menu change or 86
- **Staff no-shows in kitchen** -- one missing section cook can collapse service
- **Equipment failure mid-service** -- oven down, dishwasher broken
- **Allergen errors** -- potential liability, life-threatening consequences
- **Inconsistency complaints** -- dish that's been on menu for 2 years suddenly getting complaints
- **Food cost pressure from owner** -- asked to cut quality to hit numbers
- **Health inspection anxiety** -- especially with new or undertrained staff
- **Burnout** -- kitchen hours are brutal; managing own wellbeing vs. team demands

---

| Inventory / stock system (MarketMan, Kounta) | Par levels, cost per dish, variance |

| Reservation system (via manager) | Cover counts, dietary flags |
| Supplier portals / email | Availability, pricing, delivery confirmations |

## 3. Caf Owner

### Daily / Weekly Rhythm

**Opening (6:00-7:00am)**
- Unlock, alarm off, turn on espresso machine (needs 20-30 min warm-up)
- Check milk and pastry delivery (usually 5-6am drop)
- Prep cold display: cakes, pastries, sandwiches
- Cash float set up at till
- Brief any opening staff (5 min): specials, anything 86'd, day's vibe

**Morning peak (7:00-10:30am)**
- Highest volume period -- espresso bar under maximum pressure
- Queue management and workflow on bar critical
- Watch milk usage -- reorder signal if running low
- Monitor food display: restock pastries, sandwiches

**Mid-morning lull (10:30am-12:00pm)**
- Restock, clean, prep for lunch
- Place any orders for tomorrow (bread, dairy, produce)
- Check bank/POS morning totals

**Lunch (12:00-2:30pm)**
- Food service peaks: toasties, salads, hot specials
- Table turnover management
- Waste check on morning pastries (mark down or 86)

**Afternoon (2:30-5:00pm)**
- Slower period; good for admin, supplier calls, cleaning
- Cake/pastry restock decision for remainder of day
- Review online orders / click-and-collect

**Close (5:00-6:00pm)**
- Cash up and Z-read
- Daily waste log (what wasn't sold)
- Fridge temp checks
- Prep sign-offs, lock-up

**Weekly**
- Mon: Review weekend performance
- Wed/Thu: Bulk ordering, menu specials planning
- Fri: Staff rota for following week

---

### Before Tasks

**Before service:**
- Machine warm-up and group head cleaning
- Check grinder calibration (dose and extraction time)
- Milk levels confirmed
- Display stocked and priced correctly
- WiFi working (caf staple -- guests will ask)

**Before the week:**
- Order coffee beans, milk, syrups, packaging (cups, lids, bags)
- Plan any seasonal specials (pumpkin spice, Christmas drinks)
- Review staff hours vs. forecasted footfall

**Before seasonal pushes:**
- Valentine's Day, Mother's Day, Christmas boxes, gift card season
- Order seasonal cups, packaging, limited ingredients
- Promote via social 2-3 weeks ahead

---

### After Tasks

- Waste log: unsold food items with estimated value
- Espresso machine backflush and group head cleaning
- Grinder brush-out
- Fridge temp sign-off
- Cash reconciliation
- Social media check: any comments, messages, reviews to respond to

---

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Food hygiene certificate | Annual/bi-annual | Owner + key staff |
| Coffee machine service | Every 6 months | Affects drink quality |
| Lease renewal | Every 1-3 years | High stress, negotiate early |
| Health inspection | 1-2x per year | Unannounced |
| Loyalty platform billing | Monthly | Square, Stamp Me, etc. |
| Milk contract renegotiation | Annual | Prices spike in winter |
| Seasonal menu launches | 3-4x per year | New cups, new ingredients |

---

### Stress / Anxiety Points

- **Espresso machine breakdown during morning peak** -- catastrophic for revenue
- **Milk delivery failure** -- can't serve most drinks; 70%+ of revenue at risk
- **Single negative review** -- small cafes disproportionately affected by 1-star reviews
- **Staff turnover** -- barista skills take time to build; high turnover = inconsistent coffee
- **Thin margins** -- coffee cost-of-goods is low, but rent + labour can easily exceed revenue
- **Lease renewal risk** -- many cafs are killed by landlords doubling rent
- **Weather sensitivity** -- cold wet days massively drop footfall for non-city-centre locations

---

### Data Sources

| Source | What It Provides |
|---|---|
| POS (Square, iZettle, Lightspeed) | Sales, item mix, hourly trends |
| Coffee management (Cropster, Typica) | Bean usage, extraction data |
| Review platforms (Google, Yelp) | Ratings and sentiment |
| Accounting (Xero, MYOB) | Daily/weekly P&L |
| Social (Instagram) | Engagement, DM orders, brand |
| Stock/ordering (pen-and-paper common) | Often manual -- AI opportunity |

## 4. Bar Manager

### Daily / Weekly Rhythm

**Opening (2:00-5:00pm typical)**
- Check cash from previous night's close
- Deliveries received and checked against order (kegs, spirits, mixers, garnishes)
- Cellar work: change kegs, check CO2, rotate stock
- Bar setup: mise en place for garnishes, ice ready, speed rail stocked
- Staff briefing: promotions running, any VIPs, events tonight

**Service (5:00pm-2:00am)**
- Manage bar team during service
- Watch pour costs (free-pouring variance, over-pouring staff)
- Handle customer issues, IDing, over-service decisions
- Monitor float levels; replenish if needed
- Coordinate with door staff on capacity
- Event management if live music, DJ, quiz night

**Close (2:00-3:00am)**
- Cash up: till Z-reads, cash count, float check
- Waste and spillage log
- Fridge and stock count if needed
- Staff tip split
- Lock-up

**Weekly**
- Mon/Tue: Quieter nights -- deep cleans, cellar work, ordering
- Thu: Staff rota published for following week
- Fri/Sat: Maximum revenue nights -- full team, full bar
- Weekly cocktail menu review and new development

---

### Before Tasks

**Before service:**
- Stock check all spirits, wines, beers against par level
- Garnish prep (citrus, herbs, olives, etc.)
- Confirm promotions are active in POS
- Ice machine full and running
- Music/lighting systems on and tested
- Brief staff on any events, VIPs, drink specials

**Before the week:**
- Review last week's pour cost and variance
- Place supplier orders (spirits wholesaler, wine, beer)
- Plan any cocktail specials or promotional evenings
- Confirm any event bookings for the week

**Before events (live music, private hire, quiz nights):**
- Confirm ticket/cover charge setup in POS
- Brief extra bar staff on event format
- Pre-batch cocktails if high volume expected
- Confirm sound system / AV is working
- Stock for estimated volume (events typically over-trade vs. quiet nights)

---

### After Tasks

- Pour cost variance report: actual poured vs. POS sales
- Waste and comp log
- Check online reviews (Google, TripAdvisor, Yelp)
- Incident log: any over-service situations, ejections, accidents
- Reconcile cash and card against POS
- Submit daily report to owner if applicable

---

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Liquor/premises licence renewal | Annual | Often tied to local council; missing deadline = closure |
| Responsible service of alcohol (RSA) training | Per staff member, varies by region | Track per employee |
| DPS (Designated Premises Supervisor) licence | Ongoing personal licence | Required in UK; equivalent in other markets |
| Cellar CO2 safety certification | Annual | Gas supply safety |
| Fire safety inspection | Annual | Particularly important in venues with events |
| Insurance renewal (public liability) | Annual | |
| Staff personal licence renewals | Varies by jurisdiction | |
| Payroll | Weekly or bi-weekly | |
| Music licence (PRS/PPL in UK, ASCAP/BMI in US) | Annual | Required for any live or recorded music |

---

### Stress / Anxiety Points

- **Over-service incident** -- liability if a patron causes harm after leaving over-served
- **Licence review** -- any complaint can trigger a review of the licence
- **Staff no-shows on peak nights** -- bar is physically limited without hands
- **Liquor stock theft / staff over-pouring** -- significant margin erosion
- **Bad reviews mentioning service quality or safety** -- especially anything referencing aggressive behaviour or poor safety
- **Cash variance** -- unexplained till shortfalls
- **DJ/band cancellations** -- promoted events that fall through hurt reputation
- **Supplier delivery delays** -- running out of a popular draught beer on a Friday night

## 5. Hotel Manager

### Daily / Weekly Rhythm

**Morning (7:00-9:00am)**
- Overnight report review: occupancy, revenue per available room (RevPAR), incidents
- Handover from night manager: any issues, complaints, maintenance flags
- Walk the property: lobby, breakfast area, corridors, public bathrooms
- Brief department heads: housekeeping, F&B, front desk, maintenance
- Review the day's arrivals and departures with front desk

**Mid-morning (9:00am-12:00pm)**
- Revenue management: review rate vs. competitor set (channel management)
- Housekeeping: departure rooms flagged for priority clean, VIP arrivals prepared
- Maintenance log review: anything guest-facing must be resolved before peak check-in
- Respond to reviews (TripAdvisor, Google, Booking.com, Hotels.com)
- Review F&B bookings (restaurant, room service, events)

**Afternoon (12:00-4:00pm)**
- Check-in peak begins 2pm: presence at front desk
- VIP arrivals: amenities in room, personal greeting if appropriate
- Revenue management: adjust pricing for remaining inventory
- Conference / events: check room setups, AV, catering briefings

**Evening (4:00-8:00pm)**
- Dinner service oversight (F&B manager reports in)
- Events and private dining
- Walk the bar and restaurant
- Handover to duty manager

**Weekly**
- Mon: Weekly trading review; forecast vs. actual
- Wed: Revenue meeting with GM / ownership
- Thu: Department head meetings
- Fri: Weekend event briefings

---

### Before Tasks

**Before the day:**
- Review arrivals list: VIPs, special requests, early check-ins
- Confirm housekeeping have the departure list
- Check maintenance log for any unresolved issues
- Confirm F&B bookings and events for the day

**Before the week:**
- Forecast occupancy and F&B revenue
- Confirm event bookings: weddings, conferences, group reservations
- Review rate strategy vs. competitor pricing
- Publish staff rotas across departments

**Before events (weddings, conferences, large groups):**
- Event brief to all departments 48-72h in advance
- Confirm final numbers with client
- Room block allocation confirmed
- Catering brief to kitchen
- Parking, signage, AV confirmed

---

### After Tasks

- End-of-day trading report
- Incident log review
- Review check-out feedback (survey scores, verbal feedback from front desk)
- Online review monitoring and response
- Maintenance items logged during the day: escalate any urgent items
- Revenue management: update tomorrow's pricing based on current pick-up pace

### Stress / Anxiety Points

- **Overbooking situations** -- walking guests to a competitor; major reputation risk

- **Cash flow** -- large group deposits float operations; if group cancels, cash position changes
- **Revenue dilution** -- selling too cheap too early then peak period fills; money left on table

## 6. Retail Store Manager

### Daily / Weekly Rhythm

**Opening (8:30-9:30am)**
- Float check and till setup
- Walk the floor: displays, pricing, cleanliness, any damage
- Stock replenishment from back room to floor (facing up)
- Brief staff: daily targets, promotions running, any product issues
- Check overnight online orders if click-and-collect is enabled

## Cross-Role Patterns -- Key Insights for Pulse

### Universal Stress Triggers (any role)
1. Staff availability failures (no-shows, last-minute sick)
2. Compliance expiry (licences, certs, permits)
3. Negative online reviews
4. Cash flow gaps
5. Supplier failures (late delivery, wrong order, price changes)

### Universal Data Gaps
Most operators in these roles are running on **fragmented data** -- a POS here, a spreadsheet there, WhatsApp for staff, email for suppliers. No single source of truth. **Pulse's opportunity is to be the connective layer.**

### Proactive AI Design Principles (from research)
1. **Be specific** -- "You might run out of X" beats "Check your stock"
2. **Include context** -- "Last year you" builds trust and shows memory
3. **Offer the action** -- "Want me to" is more useful than just flagging
4. **Time-anchor everything** -- "You have 19 days" beats "This is coming up"
5. **Connect dots across systems** -- Review + staffing + POS in one message is magic
6. **Respect the rhythm** -- Morning briefings, not midnight alerts (unless critical)
