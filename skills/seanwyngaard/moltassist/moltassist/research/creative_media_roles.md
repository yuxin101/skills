# Creative & Media Roles -- Proactive AI Assistant Research

> **Purpose:** What does a proactive AI assistant need to know about these roles to help *without being asked*?  
> The gold standard: the user says *"how did it know I needed that?"*

---

## 1. YouTubers / Video Creators

### Daily / Weekly Rhythm

| Cadence | Tasks |
|---|---|
| Daily | Check comments, reply to top comments, monitor video performance (views, CTR, watch time) in YouTube Studio |
| Daily | Check notifications -- YouTube algorithm changes, community posts, competitor uploads |
| 2-3x/week | Film content (b-roll, talking head, tutorials) |
| Weekly | Edit or oversee editing -- rough cut -> colour grade -> audio mix -> captions |
| Weekly | Upload + publish -- thumbnail, title A/B test, description with SEO keywords, end screens, cards, chapters |
| Weekly | Community tab post or Shorts upload (algorithm feed signal) |
| Monthly | Analytics deep-dive: traffic sources, audience retention curves, subscriber growth, revenue breakdown |

### Before (Pre-task preparation)

- **Before filming:** Script or outline ready, shot list, props/gear checked, location/lighting sorted, B-roll list
- **Before publishing:** Thumbnail tested (CTR preview), title checked against SEO tools (TubeBuddy/VidIQ), description front-loaded with keywords, tags, timestamps added, premiere vs. publish decision, notify email list
- **Before a brand deal video:** Talking points approved by sponsor, disclosure language ("this video is sponsored by") compliant with FTC/ASA, contract reviewed for deliverables (mention count, link in description, exclusivity window)
- **Before a collab:** Guest confirmed, format agreed, release date locked, cross-promo plan in place

### After (Follow-up)

- Monitor first 24-48h performance (CTR, watch time -- YouTube ranks heavily on early signals)
- Reply to comments within first hour (algorithm positive signal)
- Share to community tab, social channels, email newsletter
- Deliver sponsor analytics report (views, clicks on link) if contractually required -- often 30 days post-publish
- Log video into content tracker (topic, keywords, performance)
- Update episode/video backlog -- what worked, what to repurpose

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Upload consistency window | Missing a week can tank algorithm distribution -- audience expects a cadence |
| Sponsor deliverable deadline | Late delivery = breach of contract, clawback risk |
| Trending topic window | YouTube trends are 48-72h; missing it = wasted opportunity |
| Copyright claim (48h window) | Dispute window -- if missed, monetisation goes to claimant |
| Ad revenue dip | Could signal demonetisation, policy violation, or algorithm suppression |
| YouTube Partner Program review | Policy violations can trigger removal; need to act fast |
| End of brand deal exclusivity | Can accept competing sponsors again -- revenue unlock |
| Milestone alerts (100K, 1M subs) | Triggers play button eligibility, PR opportunities |

### Data Sources to Monitor

- **YouTube Studio Analytics** -- impressions, CTR, avg view duration, revenue, traffic sources
- **Google Search Console** -- if linked site/blog
- **TubeBuddy / VidIQ** -- keyword scores, competitor tracking
- **Social Blade** -- channel growth benchmarks
- **Google Trends** -- topic timing
- **Comment sections** -- qualitative feedback, video ideas
- **Email open rates** -- newsletter list health
- **Patreon/membership dashboard** -- churn, new joins
- **Affiliate dashboards** (Amazon, LTK, Impact) -- link clicks and conversions

## 2. Bloggers / Writers / Journalists

### Daily / Weekly Rhythm

| Cadence | Tasks |
|---|---|
| Daily | Check email inbox (pitches, editor feedback, PR outreach) |
| Daily | Monitor Google Search Console -- rankings, impressions, click-through |
| Daily (journalists) | Wire services, breaking news alerts, source check-ins |
| Weekly | Write and publish 1-3 pieces (bloggers) or file 3-5 stories (journalists) |
| Weekly | Keyword research for upcoming content |
| Weekly | Internal linking -- update old posts to link to new ones |
| Monthly | Content audit -- update underperforming posts, redirect dead URLs |
| Monthly | Pitch new ideas to editors or plan editorial calendar |

### Before (Pre-task preparation)

- **Before writing:** Research completed, sources verified, outline done, SEO keyword target chosen, competitor posts reviewed
- **Before an interview:** Background research on subject, questions prepared, recording permission confirmed, scheduling confirmed
- **Before pitching an editor:** Research the publication's tone and recent coverage, pitch angle that fills a gap, word count / format requirements checked
- **Before publishing:** Fact-check pass, SEO meta title/description written, featured image sourced and licensed, internal + external links checked, CTA (subscribe, newsletter) added
- **Before a sponsored/affiliate post:** Disclosure compliant (FTC/ASA), product actually tested, brand talking points cross-checked

### After (Follow-up)

- Share across social channels, newsletters, relevant communities (Reddit, Facebook groups)
- Ping sources/interviewees -- "your quote is live, feel free to share"
- Monitor comments and respond
- Track organic search performance at 30/60/90 days
- Repurpose content: clip to social, expand to email, create a video script
- Update Google Search Console for indexing
- Invoice the client/publication if freelance
- Log the piece in content tracker for repurposing and internal linking

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Editorial deadlines | Hard -- missing files can end relationships with editors |
| Breaking news window | First-mover advantage in SEO and readership |
| Content freshness decay | Posts ranking on page 1 can drop if competitors publish newer versions -- update trigger |
| Affiliate link expiry / product discontinuation | Dead links tank UX and revenue |
| Domain renewal | Letting it lapse = everything gone |
| Newsletter send day | Subscribers expect consistency |
| Embargoed content release time | Publishing early = breach of agreement, source access lost |
| GDPR/privacy policy annual review | Legal compliance |
| Contract invoice due dates | Freelancers: net-30/60 invoices easy to forget |

### Data Sources to Monitor

- **Google Search Console** -- queries, clicks, impressions, position
- **Google Analytics 4 / Plausible** -- traffic, bounce, time on page
- **Ahrefs / Semrush / Ubersuggest** -- keyword rankings, backlinks, competitor content
- **Mailchimp / ConvertKit / Beehiiv** -- open rates, click rates, list growth
- **Google News / Apple News** -- distribution performance
- **Social referral traffic** -- which platforms are actually driving clicks
- **Comment sections / Reddit / Hacker News** -- audience reaction and ideas
- **AP Wire / Reuters** (journalists) -- breaking news
- **HARO / Qwoted** -- source requests

## 3. Social Media Managers

### Daily / Weekly Rhythm

| Cadence | Tasks |
|---|---|
| Daily | Check notifications, mentions, DMs across all platforms |
| Daily | Publish scheduled content (or adjust based on news/context) |
| Daily | Respond to comments -- especially in first hour post-publish |
| Daily | Monitor for brand mentions (social listening) |
| Daily | Review performance of yesterday's posts |
| Weekly | Plan and schedule next week's content calendar |
| Weekly | Create or brief content (graphics, copy, video) |
| Weekly | Influencer/partner coordination check-in |
| Monthly | Full analytics report -- growth, engagement, reach, conversions |
| Monthly | Strategy review -- what's working, what to test |

### Before (Pre-task preparation)

- **Before publishing:** Check for anything in the news that might make the post tone-deaf (reactive crisis management). Confirm assets are sized correctly per platform. Get approval if required.
- **Before a campaign launch:** Creative assets ready, UTM tracking links set up, boosting budget approved, cross-channel coordination confirmed (email + social + paid)
- **Before a product launch social push:** Legal/compliance sign-off on copy, stock availability confirmed, CS team briefed on expected DM volume
- **Before a live broadcast/event:** Equipment tested, backup plan ready, moderator standing by for comments
- **Before client calls/reports:** Pull analytics, prepare narrative (not just numbers -- what's the story?)

### After (Follow-up)

- Engage with comments immediately post-publish (especially first 30-60 mins)
- Document performance in a shared report or dashboard
- Save high-performing content for repurposing
- Capture user-generated content (UGC) and seek permission to reshare
- Send weekly/monthly performance reports to clients or internal stakeholders
- Log what worked for playbook refinement

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Platform algorithm changes | Can tank reach overnight -- need to adapt fast |
| Trending topic / meme window | Relevance decays in hours |
| Negative viral moment / PR crisis | Brand reputation at risk -- need fast, measured response |
| Scheduled post at wrong time | Time-sensitive posts (sales, events) failing to publish = revenue loss |
| Ad account suspension | Immediate revenue stop if running paid social |
| Influencer deal deliverable dates | Late post = brand relationship at risk |
| Client contract renewals | Monthly/quarterly retainer review |
| Seasonal campaign kickoffs | Christmas, Black Friday -- needs 4-6 weeks prep, not 1 week |
| Platform policy changes | New rules on ads, hashtags, content restrictions |
| Competitor launches | Need to be aware and possibly counter-program |

### Data Sources to Monitor

- **Native analytics:** Meta Business Suite, TikTok Analytics, LinkedIn Analytics, X/Twitter Analytics, Pinterest Analytics
- **Third-party scheduling/analytics:** Buffer, Hootsuite, Sprout Social, Later
- **Social listening:** Brandwatch, Mention, Talkwalker, Google Alerts
- **Paid:** Meta Ads Manager, TikTok Ads Manager
- **Competitor tracking:** Phlanx, Social Blade, manual competitor monitoring
- **Trend data:** TikTok Trending, Twitter Trending, Google Trends, Reddit
- **Influencer platforms:** Aspire, Grin, Creator.co

## 4. Marketing Managers / Growth Marketers

### Daily / Weekly Rhythm

| Cadence | Tasks |
|---|---|
| Daily | Check campaign dashboards -- CPL, ROAS, CTR, conversion rates |
| Daily | Review ad spend pacing vs. budget |
| Daily | Check email performance (open rate, click rate, unsubscribes) |
| Weekly | Campaign optimisation -- pause underperformers, scale winners |
| Weekly | A/B test review and iteration |
| Weekly | Sync with sales team on lead quality |
| Weekly | Content/SEO pipeline check |
| Monthly | Full funnel analysis: traffic -> leads -> MQLs -> SQLs -> revenue |
| Monthly | Budget reconciliation and reforecast |
| Quarterly | Strategy review, OKR check-in, planning cycle |

### Before (Pre-task preparation)

- **Before a campaign launch:** Audience segmentation defined, creative assets ready, UTM parameters set up, tracking pixels/events verified in GTM, landing page live and tested, budget approved, legal/compliance sign-off
- **Before a product launch:** Go-to-market plan finalised, sales team briefed, email sequences loaded, PR/comms aligned, retargeting audiences set up
- **Before a board/exec presentation:** Data pulled and story-shaped (narrative, not just metrics), key wins highlighted, risks acknowledged, forward plan clear
- **Before a major paid media push:** Attribution model reviewed, pixel health checked, budget phasing approved
- **Before a partnership/collab:** Audience overlap analysis done, contract terms clear, co-marketing assets agreed

### After (Follow-up)

- Post-campaign wrap report: spend vs. budget, leads vs. target, ROAS, CAC, learnings
- Debrief with sales on lead quality
- Archive winning creative for playbook
- Apply learnings to next sprint
- Retargeting audiences updated based on new pixel data
- Invoice/reconcile any agency or media vendor spend
- Update marketing attribution model with new channel data

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Monthly/quarterly OKR check-in | Miss targets -> exec scrutiny |
| Ad budget pacing | Underspend at end of month = use-it-or-lose-it; overspend = P&L problem |
| Product launch date | Cross-functional hard deadline -- marketing can't slip |
| SEO content publish cadence | Algorithm rewards consistent publishing |
| Email list health / warm-up | List decay over time -- re-engagement campaign needed |
| Competitor product launch | May need rapid counter-messaging or price response |
| Seasonal spend windows | Black Friday, January, back-to-school -- requires early planning |
| Attribution model drift | If tracking breaks (iOS updates, cookie deprecation), data goes dark |
| Agency contract renewals | Quarterly/annual retainers -- evaluate performance before auto-renew |
| CRM / marketing tool renewals | HubSpot, Marketo, etc. -- costly, need negotiation lead time |

### Data Sources to Monitor

- **Web analytics:** GA4, Mixpanel, Amplitude
- **Paid:** Google Ads, Meta Ads Manager, LinkedIn Campaign Manager
- **SEO:** Ahrefs, Semrush, Search Console
- **Email:** HubSpot, Klaviyo, Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign
- **CRM:** Salesforce, HubSpot CRM -- pipeline, MQL->SQL conversion
- **Attribution:** Triple Whale, Northbeam, Rockerbox, or GA4 multi-touch
- **Revenue:** Stripe, Shopify, or CRM-linked MRR/ARR dashboards
- **Customer feedback:** NPS scores, Intercom, Zendesk themes
- **Competitive intel:** Similarweb, SpyFu, Semrush competitor tools

## 5. Graphic Designers / Creative Directors

### Daily / Weekly Rhythm

| Cadence | Tasks |
|---|---|
| Daily | Check Slack/email for feedback, revision requests, new briefs |
| Daily | Triage and prioritise active project queue |
| Daily | Design work: concepting, production, iteration |
| Weekly | Client/stakeholder review calls -- present work, collect feedback |
| Weekly | Brief intake: review new project requests, ask clarifying questions |
| Weekly | Asset management: organise files, update brand libraries |
| Monthly (CD role) | Team creative reviews, portfolio updates, trend research |
| Monthly | Software/plugin updates, licence renewals check |

### Before (Pre-task preparation)

- **Before starting a brief:** Creative brief fully filled out (objective, audience, tone, deliverables, dimensions, file format, brand guidelines), reference/mood board gathered, feedback from last similar project reviewed
- **Before a client presentation:** Mockups and context frames prepared (not just flat assets), narrative explaining the *why* behind design decisions, backup options ready, revision scope and rounds pre-agreed
- **Before a campaign asset production run:** Master files set up correctly (artboards, layers named, reusable components), brand assets (fonts, logos, colours) confirmed current
- **Before handing off to dev:** All specs documented (fonts, spacing, colour hex, export sizes), assets exported at correct resolutions, design system/Figma components annotated
- **Before a product/launch deadline:** All sizes/formats confirmed, file naming convention agreed, printer specs if print (bleed, CMYK, PDF/X-4)

### After (Follow-up)

- Archive final files in agreed folder structure with version history
- Export and deliver all required formats (web, print, social, email)
- Update brand asset library with any new approved elements
- Document client feedback and preferences for future briefs
- Invoice if freelance -- based on milestones or delivery
- Request testimonial/permission to add to portfolio
- Post selected work to portfolio (Behance, Dribbble, personal site)

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Print deadlines | Printers have hard cut-offs -- missing by an hour = job doesn't run |
| Campaign launch dates | Ads, social, landing pages all need assets simultaneously |
| Brand guidelines update | Old assets in circulation can create compliance issues |
| Font/plugin licence renewal | Expired licences = can't open files, missing font rendering |
| Adobe CC / Figma / Sketch renewal | Design workflow stops if lapses |
| Event/conference collateral | Physical print = long lead time (7-14 days minimum) |
| Social asset resizes | Platform spec changes (Meta, LinkedIn) can make existing templates non-compliant |
| Client brand refresh | All templates need updating -- significant workload spike |
| Revision round limits | Freelancers especially -- unlimited revisions = scope creep |

### Data Sources to Monitor

- **Project management:** Asana, Monday.com, Trello, Notion -- task queue and deadlines
- **Client feedback:** Frame.io, Loom comments, Google Slides comments, Figma comments
- **Brand asset libraries:** Brandfolder, Bynder, or internal shared drives
- **Industry trends:** Dribbble, Behance, Pinterest, Awwwards, Muzli
- **Platform spec sheets:** Meta, Google, LinkedIn ad specs (change regularly)
- **Font/stock licences:** Adobe Fonts, Envato, Shutterstock -- renewal dates
- **Portfolio analytics:** Behance, personal site traffic -- inbound leads

## 6. Podcasters

### Daily / Weekly Rhythm

| Cadence | Tasks |
|---|---|
| Daily | Monitor downloads/plays in podcast host dashboard |
| Daily | Check reviews (Apple Podcasts, Spotify) and DMs |
| Weekly | Record 1-2 episodes (solo or with guests) |
| Weekly | Edit audio: clean-up, music, chapters, transitions |
| Weekly | Publish: upload to host, write show notes, SEO description, chapters, timestamps |
| Weekly | Clip 2-4 short-form videos for TikTok/Reels/Shorts from episode |
| Weekly | Email newsletter to subscribers with episode summary |
| Monthly | Guest pipeline review -- book 4-6 weeks out |
| Monthly | Analytics review: growth trend, listener retention, episode performance |
| Monthly | Sponsor performance report delivery |

### Before (Pre-task preparation)

- **Before recording with a guest:** Background research done, questions prepared, equipment tested (both sides), recording consent confirmed, backup recording running (local + cloud), calendar invite with joining link sent
- **Before a solo episode:** Script or outline complete, talking points in order, mic gain checked, water on hand, notifications silenced
- **Before publishing:** Show notes written with links mentioned, episode title SEO-friendly, chapters/timestamps added, cover art correct (some hosts require episode-specific art), cross-promo with guest confirmed
- **Before a sponsor read:** Script/talking points reviewed and approved by sponsor, code/URL confirmed working, disclosure language in place
- **Before a live recording / event podcast:** Venue acoustics assessed, portable recording gear checked, backup audio confirmed

### After (Follow-up)

- Notify guest when episode is live -- provide shareable assets (audiogram, clip, promo graphic)
- Reply to listener comments and reviews
- Monitor downloads in first 48h (benchmark vs. other episodes)
- Clip and schedule social content
- Update guest CRM -- follow-up with future episode idea, relationship maintenance
- Deliver sponsor analytics report (download numbers, promo code redemptions)
- Log episode in content tracker -- topic, guest, performance, repurposing status
- Transcription for SEO / accessibility (Descript, Otter.ai, or similar)

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Publishing cadence | Irregular releases tank subscriber retention and algorithmic ranking |
| Guest no-show / cancellation | Hole in the production schedule -- need backup solo episode plan |
| Trending topic relevance | Newsjacking a topic = spike in downloads if published within days |
| Sponsor deliverable deadline | Late publish = breach; sponsor may pull future bookings |
| RSS feed expiry / hosting renewal | Feed going dark = removed from all directories |
| Apple Podcasts / Spotify featured consideration | Submission windows exist -- requires advance application |
| Listener milestone (10K, 100K downloads) | Triggers PR opportunity, price increase for sponsorships |
| Guest embargo | Some guests can't be live until a certain date (book launch, product launch) |
| Season break announcement | Listeners need managing -- communication plan needed |
| Domain / website renewal | Podcast website going down = SEO and subscriber trust damage |

### Data Sources to Monitor

- **Podcast host dashboards:** Buzzsprout, Transistor, Podbean, Anchor/Spotify for Podcasters, Libsyn -- downloads, listener location, listening platform breakdown
- **Spotify for Podcasters / Apple Podcasts Connect** -- platform-specific analytics, listener demographics
- **Chartable / Podtrac** -- cross-platform ranking and attribution
- **Social analytics** -- clip performance on TikTok, Reels, Shorts, LinkedIn
- **Email platform** -- newsletter open/click rates (list = most valuable owned audience)
- **Review monitoring** -- Apple Podcasts, Spotify, Podchaser
- **Affiliate dashboards** -- sponsor promo code redemption rates
- **Search trends** -- Google Trends for episode topic timing
- **Listener communities** -- Discord, Facebook Group, Reddit -- qualitative insight

## Cross-Role Patterns (What a Proactive AI Should Always Watch)

These triggers apply across *all* the above roles and should always be monitored:

### Universal Time Bombs
| Pattern | Action |
|---|---|
| Domain/hosting renewals approaching (30/60/90 days) | Alert early -- lapses kill entire businesses |
| Tool/software licence renewals | Creative tools expiring = workflow stops |
| Contract deliverable deadlines | Cross-reference calendar with brief scope |
| Invoice due dates (freelancers) | Net-30/60 often forgotten until overdue |
| Tax filing periods | Quarterly/annual -- freelancers especially |

### Universal "What They Forgot" Moments
- They published content but didn't share it to all channels
- They finished a project but didn't invoice
- They booked a guest/collab but didn't confirm the joining details 24h before
- They launched a campaign but didn't set up UTM tracking
- They ended a sponsorship deal but didn't deliver the analytics report
- Their file is sitting in Draft -- never published

### What Makes an AI Indispensable Here
1. **Calendar-aware prep briefs** -- "You have a recording tomorrow, here's what you prepped last time + any news about the guest"
2. **Post-publish checklists** -- "Episode went live -- have you shared it, clipped it, emailed your list?"
3. **Anomaly detection** -- "Your last video got half the usual views in first 24h -- here's what might explain it"
4. **Deadline surfacing** -- "Your hosting renews in 12 days" / "Sponsor report was due yesterday"
5. **Trend alerts** -- "This topic is trending -- you covered something related 6 months ago, here's a repurpose angle"
6. **Quiet reminders** -- "You haven't posted to Instagram in 8 days. Is that intentional?"
7. **Contract awareness** -- "You have a brand deal for 3 posts. You've done 2. Deadline is Friday."
