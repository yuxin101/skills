# Personal Life Research -- Proactive AI Assistant

> **Purpose:** For each life category, document what a proactive AI needs to know to help *before being asked*. The goal: users say "how did it know I needed that?"

---

## 1. Buying a Home

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Get pre-approved for a mortgage (know credit score, income docs, debt-to-income ratio)
- Set and stick to a realistic budget (purchase price, deposit, transfer costs, bond costs, moving costs)
- Maintain a saved properties list with price, location, pros/cons notes
- Track offer deadlines and counter-offer timelines
- Keep a checklist of inspection findings and which are deal-breakers
- Monitor interest rate environment -- fixed vs variable decision
- Coordinate conveyancer (attorney), bank, and seller timelines
- Track all paperwork: offer to purchase, FICA docs, bond application, transfer docs
- Watch for hidden costs: rates clearance, levy clearance, occupational rent, electrical compliance certificate

### What They Need to Know BEFORE
- Their actual affordability (not just pre-approval ceiling -- also monthly comfort budget)
- Transfer duty thresholds (country-specific, e.g. SA: no transfer duty under R1.1M for first-time buyers)
- First-time buyer benefits available (FLISP in SA, etc.)
- The difference between bond registration costs vs transfer costs
- That bond approval  final approval -- conditions can still cause failure
- What a home inspection covers and what it doesn't (e.g. roof, geyser, electrical)
- Sectional title vs freehold -- levies, body corporate, special levies
- Occupational rent -- if they move in before transfer, they owe rent to the seller
- Bond originator vs going direct to a bank (originator is free and shops multiple banks)

### Follow-Up After
- Register for rates and utilities at municipality
- Update home address everywhere (bank, ID, insurers, SARS, etc.)
- Set up home insurance immediately on transfer date
- Change all locks
- Service the geyser and get a compliance certificate if not provided
- Plan a maintenance calendar (gutters, pest inspection, roof, HVAC servicing)
- Keep all transfer documents in a safe place (title deed, COC certificates)
- Note the first bond payment date

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Pre-approval expiry (usually 90 days -- must be renewed)
- Offer to purchase expiry date
- Bond approval conditions deadline
- Occupation date vs transfer date (these differ)
- Rates and utilities registration (usually within 30 days of transfer)
- First bond instalment date
- Property tax / rates payment cycles

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- "Am I being approved in time?" -- proactively remind them of bank response timelines
- Fear of losing the property to another buyer -- alert on deadline proximity
- Confusion about what costs to expect at transfer -- show a cost breakdown unprompted
- Anxiety about inspections -- offer to track inspection items and flag deal-breakers
- Forgetting to cancel rental lease on time -- cross-reference move dates
- Post-purchase overwhelm -- "here's your first-week checklist" without being asked

## 2. Selling a Home

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Decide on listing price (get 2-3 valuations, research comparable sales)
- Choose an estate agent or go private sale
- Prepare the property: repairs, declutter, staging, professional photography
- Get legally required certificates: electrical compliance, beetle/damp inspection, gas compliance, plumbing (country-dependent)
- Understand capital gains tax implications
- Track all offers, counter-offers, and their expiry dates
- Monitor the buyer's bond approval timeline (seller is in limbo during this)
- Coordinate bond cancellation on existing bond (penalties for early cancellation possible)
- Set a realistic occupational handover date

### What They Need to Know BEFORE
- The difference between sole mandate vs open listing
- Bond cancellation notice period (usually 90 days in SA -- serve notice early or pay penalty)
- Capital gains tax calculation (primary residence exclusion, inclusion rate, etc.)
- FICA requirements -- estate agents must verify identity
- That the buyer's bond approval can fall through even after offer acceptance
- Costs to expect: agent commission (5-7.5% + VAT typically), compliance certs, bond cancellation
- Whether their property is in a body corporate and what disclosure is required
- Latent vs patent defects -- what you must disclose

### Follow-Up After
- Confirm final meter readings are done
- Receive and confirm receipt of net proceeds from conveyancer
- Cancel or transfer utilities
- SARS notification if capital gains tax applies
- Cancel home insurance on occupation day (not before)
- Update address records
- Tie up any HOA/levy arrears settlement confirmations

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Bond cancellation notice (serve as soon as offer is signed -- 90-day clock starts)
- Buyer's bond approval window (usually 30 days from offer)
- Compliance certificate validity periods
- Occupation date
- Transfer date (different from occupation)
- CGT must be declared in tax return for that year

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- "Is the buyer's bond going to come through?" -- alert if 3-week silence is unusual
- Anxiety about agent performance / low viewings -- suggest review after 3 weeks
- Forgetting to serve bond cancellation notice early -- major cost if missed
- Worry about compliance costs blowing out -- pre-estimate them
- Not knowing what to disclose vs not -- proactively surface the disclosure rules

## 3. Renting a Home / Moving

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Research rental market in target area (price per m, typical deposit requirements)
- Prepare rental application docs: ID, payslips, bank statements, references, credit check consent
- Understand lease terms: notice period, renewal terms, escalation clause
- Do a thorough ingoing inspection and photograph everything
- Know the deposit rules (held in interest-bearing account, returned within X days of move-out)
- Track lease end date and required notice period
- Arrange movers, packing supplies, van hire well in advance
- Redirect mail / update address
- Set up utilities at new address before moving in

### What They Need to Know BEFORE
- Their rental budget (rule of thumb: max 30% of gross income)
- Notice period required to terminate (typically 1-2 months)
- Escalation rate -- how much the rent goes up annually
- Who is responsible for which repairs (tenant vs landlord)
- Whether pets, painting walls, etc. are allowed
- Credit check will be run -- know their score first
- Whether a surety/guarantor is required

### Follow-Up After
- Keep signed copy of lease + ingoing inspection report
- Set calendar reminder 2 months before lease end (to decide renew/move)
- Confirm deposit receipt and bank account details
- Register with municipality for utilities if needed
- Test all appliances and report issues within first 7 days (before it becomes "your problem")
- Update: bank, employer, SARS, subscriptions, vehicle registration, medical aid

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Lease renewal/exit notice deadline (often 2 months before end)
- Deposit return window (legally defined -- e.g. 14 days in SA after move-out inspection)
- Notice period to current landlord (serve on time to avoid paying double rent)
- Utility connection timing (can't move in without electricity/water)
- Mover booking -- peak periods (month-end) book out weeks ahead

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- "Did I photograph enough during ingoing inspection?" -- remind them proactively
- Anxiety about getting deposit back -- flag the legal rules and checklist
- Forgetting to give notice on time -- calendar alert well in advance
- Overwhelm of address change notifications -- offer a master checklist
- Moving day logistics chaos -- proactively build a day-of timeline

## 4. Budgeting / Managing Personal Finances

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Know their actual monthly income (gross vs net -- people often confuse these)
- Categorise all monthly expenses: fixed (rent, bond, subscriptions) vs variable (groceries, fuel)
- Track discretionary spending -- where the money actually goes vs where they think it goes
- Maintain an emergency fund target (3-6 months expenses)
- Review subscriptions regularly (people forget ones they don't use)
- Understand pay cycle timing vs debit order timing (avoid debit order bounces)
- Track net worth over time (assets minus liabilities)
- Know their tax bracket and implications

### What They Need to Know BEFORE
- The difference between gross and take-home pay
- How to calculate their true hourly cost of living
- The importance of an emergency fund before investing
- What "good debt" vs "bad debt" looks like (bond vs credit card)
- That subscription creep is real -- needs active management
- That budgets fail if they're too strict -- allow a realistic "fun money" category

### Follow-Up After (Monthly)
- Reconcile actual spend vs budget categories
- Flag categories that were over/under
- Review upcoming debit orders for next month
- Check if any annual fees/subscriptions are due

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Salary/pay date -- update budget for month ahead
- Annual subscription renewals (often forgotten, hit account unexpectedly)
- Tax year end (know your country's deadline -- SA: Feb 28)
- Medical aid open enrollment periods
- Insurance renewal dates
- Annual rate increases on rent, bond, insurance

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- End-of-month anxiety: "Am I going to make it?" -- proactively show spend-to-date vs budget
- Surprise debit orders -- flag large upcoming debits 3 days before
- "Where did my money go?" -- categorise and summarise without being asked
- Annual subscription shock -- alert before it hits
- Guilt around overspending -- nonjudgmental framing, "here's how to recover this month"

## 5. Paying Off Debt

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- List all debts: creditor, balance, interest rate, minimum payment, due date
- Choose a payoff strategy: avalanche (highest interest first) vs snowball (smallest balance first)
- Never miss minimum payments -- protect credit score
- Track total interest being paid -- this is motivational when you see it decreasing
- Watch for balance transfer or refinancing opportunities
- Avoid taking on new debt while paying off existing
- Know their credit score and what's affecting it

### What They Need to Know BEFORE
- Avalanche saves the most money; snowball provides quicker psychological wins
- Minimum payments keep you in debt almost forever on high-interest debt
- Consolidation loans can help but only if rate is genuinely lower and they don't reaccumulate
- Negotiating with creditors is possible -- many will accept reduced settlements
- Debt review (SA) / Chapter 13 (US) has long-term credit implications

### Follow-Up After Each Payment
- Update debt tracker (new balance)
- Recalculate payoff date
- Celebrate milestones (debt-free on a card = redirect that payment to next target)
- When debt is cleared: confirm account closure is reflected on credit bureau

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Payment due dates (missing = fees + credit score damage)
- Promotional interest rate expiry (0% balance transfer periods)
- Debt review exits -- legal steps to exit have timelines
- Credit report review (annually minimum)
- Statute of limitations on old debt (country-specific)

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- Payment due date anxiety -- 3-day reminder before each due date
- "Am I making progress?" -- proactively show payoff timeline chart
- Overwhelm from multiple debts -- suggest and track a clear payoff order
- Fear of missing a payment when cash is tight -- alert when account balance is low vs upcoming debt payment
- Motivation decay -- celebrate milestones unprompted

## 6. Gardening / Lawn Care (Seasonal)

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Know their climate zone (affects planting calendar)
- Track what was planted where and when (rotation planning for veg gardens)
- Maintain tools: clean, sharpen, oil before storing for winter
- Fertilisation schedule per plant type
- Watering schedule (varies by season and plant type)
- Pest and disease monitoring -- early intervention is key
- Mulching in autumn to protect roots over winter
- Bulb planting timing (spring blooms need autumn planting)

### What They Need to Know BEFORE
- Last frost date / first frost date for their area
- Which plants need to be started indoors before last frost
- Soil prep timing -- compost added 2-4 weeks before planting
- Water restrictions in their area (municipalities often have seasonal bans)
- Companion planting basics (what to plant near what)

### Seasonal Calendar (Northern Hemisphere reference; flip for Southern)
- **Spring:** Seed starting indoors, soil prep, early cool-weather planting, lawn aeration
- **Summer:** Watering consistency, pest monitoring, harvesting, heat protection for sensitive plants
- **Autumn:** Harvest remaining crops, plant bulbs, mulch, prepare garden beds, clean tools
- **Winter:** Plan next year's garden, order seeds, prune dormant trees/shrubs

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Last frost date (do not plant frost-sensitive plants before this)
- Bulb planting window (autumn -- very specific timing)
- Lawn fertilisation windows (spring and autumn for most grasses)
- Irrigation system winterisation (before first freeze)
- Water restriction seasons
- Mowing season start/end

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- "When can I plant my tomatoes?" -- proactively surface their frost date in spring
- "My lawn looks terrible" -- seasonal care reminder before the problem develops
- Forgetting to water during heat waves -- alert based on forecast
- Not knowing what's wrong with a plant -- offer diagnosis prompts
- Overwhelm of spring garden prep -- proactively build a week-by-week checklist

## 7. Home Repairs and Maintenance

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Maintain a home maintenance calendar (many tasks are annual or seasonal)
- Keep a home manual: appliance manuals, warranty cards, service history, tradesperson contacts
- Track when appliances were installed (to anticipate replacement timing)
- Know how to shut off water main, gas main, and circuit breakers in an emergency
- Photograph and document any damage/repair for insurance purposes
- Keep a list of trusted, vetted tradespeople

### Standard Annual Maintenance Tasks
- Geyser/hot water heater: service, anode rod check (every 2-3 years)
- HVAC/aircon: filter replacement, professional service before summer
- Gutters: clean twice a year (spring and autumn)
- Smoke detector batteries: replace annually
- Roof: visual inspection after storms, professional every 5 years
- Driveway/deck: reseal every 2-3 years
- Pest inspection: annually in high-risk areas
- Chimney: annual sweep if used
- Irrigation system: check heads, winterise before frost

### What They Need to Know BEFORE Starting a Repair
- Is this DIY-safe or do they need a professional?
- Do they need a permit? (structural, electrical, plumbing often require one)
- Is it covered by insurance or warranty?
- What is the cost to do nothing vs fix now vs professional fix?
- Who is the best contractor for this type of work?

### Follow-Up After Repairs
- Keep receipt and record of work done (for future sale / warranty claims)
- Check work quality within 30 days
- Update home maintenance log
- Follow up on any warranty for the repair

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Seasonal maintenance windows (HVAC before summer, gutters before rainy season)
- Warranty expiry on appliances and roofing
- Home insurance renewal -- often requires proof of maintenance
- After storms: prompt inspection of roof, gutters, fence
- Geyser age (replace proactively at 8-10 years before it fails catastrophically)

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- "Something is making a noise" -- help diagnose and triage urgency
- Unexpected large repair = financial panic -- help them think through insurance claim vs out-of-pocket
- Not knowing who to call -- maintain a vetted tradesperson list
- Fear of being overcharged -- offer to help get 3 quotes and evaluate
- Seasonal maintenance overwhelm -- surface the right tasks at the right time of year

## 8. Going to the Dentist / Doctor Appointments

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Know their check-up schedule: dental (6-monthly), GP (annual), specialist (as advised)
- Track vaccinations and boosters (flu shot annually, tetanus every 10 years, etc.)
- Maintain a medication list (name, dose, prescribing doctor, refill date)
- Know their medical aid/insurance benefits and what's covered in-network
- Prepare questions before appointments (people forget what they wanted to ask)
- Track chronic conditions, symptoms, and medication side effects
- Know who their GP, dentist, and specialists are -- and have their numbers saved

### What They Need to Know BEFORE an Appointment
- What documents to bring (ID, medical aid card, referral letter if specialist)
- Their medical aid authorisation requirements for procedures
- Their current medication list (have it written down, not just in memory)
- Fasting requirements if blood tests are involved
- Their family medical history for new doctor visits

### Follow-Up After
- Follow through on referrals -- people often get referred and never go
- Collect test results and understand them
- Refill prescriptions before they run out (don't wait until the last pill)
- Update their personal health record
- Book next check-up appointment before leaving the current one

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Annual check-up due date
- Prescription refill date (alert 2 weeks before running out)
- Vaccination boosters due
- Medical aid benefit year reset (often January -- use benefits before they expire)
- Specialist follow-up appointments (easy to "forget" to book)
- Children's immunisation schedule

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- Health anxiety -- "I have a symptom, is it serious?" -- help triage without replacing a doctor
- Dental anxiety (very common) -- reminders framed with empathy, not guilt
- Prescription running out panic -- proactive refill reminders
- Forgetting to follow up on test results -- alert after expected turnaround
- Medical aid confusion -- "what does my plan actually cover for this?"
- Appointment prep anxiety -- offer a structured pre-appointment checklist

## 9. Shopping (Grocery Planning, Big Purchases, Deals)

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Maintain a running grocery list tied to weekly meal plans
- Know their pantry/freezer inventory to avoid double-buying
- Track price history on frequently bought items (to know what's a real deal)
- Have a pre-commitment process for big purchases (cool-off period, research checklist)
- Track which stores have which loyalty points and maximise accordingly
- Know their "deal thresholds" -- at what price point something is actually worth buying

### What They Need to Know BEFORE a Big Purchase
- Their actual budget for this item
- Whether there's a better time to buy (seasonal sales, model changeover)
- User reviews from trusted sources (not just star ratings)
- Return policy and warranty terms
- Whether buying refurbished/second-hand is viable
- Total cost of ownership (e.g. printer: cheap to buy, expensive to run)

### Follow-Up After Big Purchases
- Register warranty with manufacturer
- Set a reminder to review/return if not satisfied within return window
- Track in home inventory
- Set a reminder for extended warranty purchase window (often closes 30 days after purchase)

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Seasonal sales: Black Friday, Cyber Monday, post-Christmas, mid-year
- Loyalty points expiry
- Price match windows
- Return/refund deadlines
- Subscription free trial ends (flag before auto-charge)

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- "Should I buy this now or wait?" -- help them check if a price drop is likely
- Impulse purchase regret -- gently enforce their own stated cool-off rule
- Grocery shopping without a list = overspending -- proactively build the list
- Free trial auto-conversion anxiety -- alert 3 days before any trial ends
- "Am I getting a good deal?" -- help benchmark the price

## 10. Travel Planning and During Travel

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Destination research: visa requirements, health/vaccine requirements, safety advisories
- Flight and accommodation booking with price tracking
- Travel insurance (often forgotten or left too late)
- Packing list (personalised by trip type and destination)
- Copies of important documents: passport, visa, insurance policy, bookings
- Notify bank about travel to prevent card blocks
- Know emergency contact numbers at destination (embassy, insurance helpline)
- Local transport options and costs at destination
- Currency exchange strategy (card vs cash vs local ATM)

### What They Need to Know BEFORE
- Visa processing time -- can take weeks; don't leave until last minute
- Passport validity requirements (many countries require 6 months beyond travel date)
- Destination-specific health risks and required vaccinations (some need 4-6 weeks lead time)
- Travel insurance fine print -- pre-existing conditions, adventure sports, cancellation terms
- Entry requirements: return ticket, accommodation proof, onward travel
- Best exchange rate strategy for their destination
- Time zone differences and how to manage jet lag
- Local laws and customs (what's legal at home may not be there)

### During Travel
- Keep digital and physical copies of key documents separate from originals
- Monitor flight status changes
- Know airport transfer options at destination
- Track expenses against travel budget
- Emergency contacts: local embassy, travel insurance 24/7 line, bank fraud line

### Follow-Up After
- Expense reconciliation (trip vs budget)
- Submit travel insurance claims promptly if needed
- Update travel diary/photos while memory is fresh
- Check for any pending charges or card fraud
- Loyalty points earned from flights/hotels

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Passport renewal (6 months+ before travel, and processing takes 4-8 weeks)
- Visa application window (specific to destination)
- Vaccine timing (some need 4-6 weeks for full effect)
- Flight check-in window (24-48 hours before)
- Travel insurance purchase (must be before departure, ideally at booking)
- Frequent flyer points expiry

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- "Did I forget anything?" -- proactively run through personalised packing checklist
- Flight delay / cancellation -- immediately surface rebooking options and rights
- "Is my passport valid enough?" -- check at booking time, not departure week
- Visa anxiety -- alert to apply well ahead
- "What do I do if my bag is lost?" -- pre-trip briefing on rights and process
- Currency confusion -- proactively calculate budget in local currency

## 12. Fitness and Health Goals

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Define specific, measurable goals (not "get fit" -- "run 5km without stopping by March")
- Track workouts: type, duration, intensity, recovery
- Monitor progress metrics: weight, measurements, performance benchmarks
- Nutrition tracking: macros or at least general awareness of calories
- Sleep quality -- often the most neglected health lever
- Recovery: rest days, mobility, hydration
- Equipment needs and maintenance (gym membership, running shoes replacement)

### What They Need to Know BEFORE
- Baseline health stats (resting heart rate, blood pressure, weight) to measure progress
- Injury prevention basics for chosen activity
- The importance of progressive overload (doing more over time, not just the same)
- That diet is responsible for ~80% of body composition changes
- Realistic timelines (people consistently overestimate short-term progress, underestimate long-term)

### Follow-Up / Ongoing
- Weekly check-ins vs goals
- Adjust plan when life gets in the way (reduced weeks don't mean failure)
- Periodic deload weeks (intentional rest improves progress)
- Shoe/gear replacement (running shoes: every 500-800km)

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Gym membership renewal
- Event registrations (race, charity event -- training block needed beforehand)
- Seasonal shifts (workout location changes, outdoor vs indoor)
- Recovery after illness (return-to-exercise protocols)
- Annual health metrics review

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- "I missed a week" guilt spiral -- "here's how to get back on track" without judgment
- Plateau frustration -- proactively suggest changes before they give up
- Motivation drought -- surface their own stated why / past progress
- Injury fear -- "this pain seems like something you should get checked" vs "this is normal DOMS"
- "I don't have time" -- help them find 20-minute options, not guilt them
- Gear and subscription costs adding up -- help them audit what they actually use

## 13. Caring for Elderly Parents

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Maintain a document vault: will, power of attorney, medical directives, insurance policies, pension/investment accounts, Medicare/medical aid numbers
- Know their parent's current medications: names, doses, interactions, prescribing doctors
- Track all medical appointments and follow-ups
- Assess home safety: fall risks, bathroom rails, step hazards, kitchen safety
- Know their parent's financial picture: income, assets, debts, recurring expenses
- Have emergency protocol: who to call, what hospital, which GP
- Monitor cognitive and physical decline with objective measures
- Understand the care options continuum: in-home care -> assisted living -> frail care -> palliative care

### What They Need to Know BEFORE It Becomes a Crisis
- Power of attorney must be set up while the parent still has legal capacity
- Care facility waiting lists can be 6-24 months long -- apply early
- Medical aid limits for frail care -- often much less than people assume
- What a falls assessment involves and when to ask for one
- Respite care options (caregiver burnout is a real risk)
- Government/state benefits available for the elderly

### Follow-Up / Ongoing
- Medication refills ahead of time
- Post-appointment: were any referrals given? Follow up on them
- Annual financial review (pension increases, insurance renewals)
- Will and estate planning review if circumstances change

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Power of attorney while capacity exists -- "later" can become too late
- Care facility application timing (long waiting lists)
- Annual medication reviews with GP
- Flu vaccine and other age-specific annual vaccinations
- Pension/annuity annual increases and drawdown reviews
- Medical aid annual contribution increase -- review adequacy

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- "Have I missed something important?" -- proactively surface the document checklist
- Caregiver burnout warning signs -- gentle flag when they haven't mentioned respite
- Medication confusion -- maintain and display the medication list clearly
- Emergency preparedness anxiety -- help them build the emergency card
- Guilt about considering care facilities -- non-judgmental information about options
- Financial planning anxiety -- "how long will their money last?" scenario modelling

## 14. Owning a Car (Maintenance, Insurance, Registration)

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Track service intervals: km-based and time-based (whichever comes first)
- Maintain service history book (affects resale value)
- Monitor tyre condition and pressure (check monthly)
- Know when insurance renews and what it covers (comprehensive vs third party)
- Track licence disc/vehicle registration renewal date
- Keep roadside assistance number saved
- Monitor recall notices for their vehicle make/model
- Know their car's fluid levels and check intervals

### Standard Service Milestones
- Oil and filter: every 10,000-15,000km or 12 months
- Tyre rotation: every 10,000-15,000km
- Brake inspection: every 20,000km
- Transmission fluid: every 40,000-50,000km
- Timing belt: typically 60,000-100,000km (critical -- failure = engine damage)
- Spark plugs: every 30,000-100,000km depending on type

### What They Need to Know BEFORE
- The cost of deferred maintenance vs proactive (timing belt failure = catastrophic)
- Their insurance excess (affects risk-taking decisions)
- The difference between statutory write-off and repairable write-off
- Tyre age matters even if tread is fine (replace at 5-7 years regardless)
- What to do immediately after an accident (photos, third-party info, police report, insurer call)

### Follow-Up After Service
- Confirm service stamp in logbook
- Keep invoice (warranty record, resale value)
- Note next service date/km in reminder system
- For major repairs: confirm warranty on the work done

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Annual licence renewal (penalty fines for late renewal)
- Insurance renewal date
- Roadworthy certificate expiry (required for sale)
- Service intervals (both time and km -- whichever first)
- Warrant of fitness (NZ) / MOT (UK) annual
- Tyre age (5-7 year replacement regardless of tread)

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- "What's that noise?" -- help triage urgency (pull over now vs monitor vs next service)
- Pre-trip long distance anxiety -- proactive "is your car road-trip ready?" checklist
- Accident panic -- immediate step-by-step guide
- Insurance confusion at renewal -- help them compare and ask the right questions
- Licence renewal forgetting -- alert 3 weeks before expiry
- "Should I repair or replace?" -- help model the decision with real costs

## 15. Starting a Side Hustle / Small Business

### What They Need to Do / Track / Remember
- Validate the idea before investing significantly (will people pay?)
- Register the business (and understand the legal structure: sole trader, PTY, LLC)
- Open a separate business bank account -- keep personal and business finances completely separate
- Track all income and expenses from day one (not "I'll sort the admin later")
- Understand tax obligations: income tax, VAT threshold, provisional tax
- Build a simple pricing model that covers costs and includes your time
- Know their break-even point (how much revenue to cover all costs)
- Protect intellectual property if relevant (trademark, copyright)

### What They Need to Know BEFORE Starting
- The market exists and will pay what they need to charge (validate this first)
- Their minimum viable product -- start small, don't over-engineer before selling
- Tax structure and how to set aside tax from day one (the bill always comes)
- That growth without cash flow management kills businesses
- Insurance requirements (public liability, professional indemnity)
- Legal contracts for clients are essential, not optional
- Their own motivations -- lifestyle business vs growth business -- shapes every decision

### Follow-Up / Ongoing
- Monthly P&L review -- are they actually profitable?
- Quarterly provisional tax payments (SA: Aug and Feb)
- Annual financial statements and tax return
- Regular review of pricing (costs increase; prices often don't)
- Client contract renewals and rate reviews
- Business insurance annual review

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- Business registration deadlines (if relevant to their niche)
- VAT registration threshold (register before exceeding it in SA: R1M turnover)
- Provisional tax submission dates
- Annual tax return deadline
- Licence/permit renewals (industry-specific)
- Contract renewal dates with clients

### Stress / Anxiety Points (AI Proactive Opportunities)
- "Is this actually working?" -- proactively calculate their effective hourly rate

- Client payment overdue stress -- draft a polite payment follow-up

## Cross-Cutting Patterns for Proactive AI Design

### Universal Trigger Patterns
| Pattern | AI Action |
|---|---|
| Date approaching a known deadline | Alert 14 days, 7 days, 3 days, 1 day out |
| Silence after a commitment | "Did you follow up on X?" after expected window |
| Seasonal calendar shift | Surface relevant checklist for that life category |
| Large financial event | Offer breakdown of costs and options unprompted |
| High-stress known decision point | Offer framework/checklist before they ask |
| Service/renewal anniversary | Alert 30 days before |
| Post-event follow-up window | Prompt 1-2 weeks after event |

### The "How Did It Know?" Moments
1. Prompting them to book their car service when the odometer milestone is close
2. Alerting that their lease ends in 8 weeks -- "you probably need to give notice now"
3. Reminding them to set aside tax money the month they hit a big client payment
4. Surfacing packing list 5 days before a trip they mentioned 6 weeks ago
5. Alerting that a prescription will run out in 2 weeks based on fill date + days supply
6. Saying "it's autumn -- time to plant bulbs if you want spring blooms"
7. Flagging that a subscription trial converts to paid in 3 days
8. Reminding them that their elderly parent's power of attorney still hasn't been done
9. Saying "you mentioned wanting to run a 5k -- the local race registration closes Friday"
10. Pre-empting school registration stress: "term 4 school applications open next month"

### What Makes AI Assistance Feel Magical (Not Annoying)
- **Timing is everything** -- right information at the moment it's actionable
- **One thing at a time** -- don't overwhelm, surface the most urgent item
- **Non-judgmental** -- especially on money, health, and parenting
- **Proactive on logistics, reactive on opinions** -- offer info, not unsolicited advice
- **Know when to escalate** -- "this sounds like you need a doctor/lawyer/accountant, not me"
- **Remember what they said** -- if they mentioned a goal 3 months ago, check in on it
- **Connect the dots** -- trip mentioned + passport expiry on file = "your passport expires 4 months after that trip, you're fine" or "you need to renew before you go"
