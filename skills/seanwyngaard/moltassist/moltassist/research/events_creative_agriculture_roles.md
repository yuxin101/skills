## 1. EVENT PLANNERS

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Non-event weeks:** Heavy admin -- vendor negotiations, site visits, client calls, proposal writing. Typically 9-6 with flexible scheduling.
- **Event week:** Escalating intensity. T-7: final confirmations. T-3: logistics locked. T-1: site walkthrough, printed run sheets. Event day: 06:00-midnight on-site or on-call.
- **Post-event (3-5 days):** Vendor payments, client debrief, testimonial requests, photo/video collection for portfolio.
- **Seasonal peaks:** Q4 (corporate year-end events, galas), Q2 (spring/summer launches), January (planning season for mid-year events). Summer = outdoor event season = weather anxiety.
- **Lead pipeline rhythm:** Proposals typically go out Monday-Wednesday. Friday is light -- clients review over the weekend and respond early the following week.

### Before Tasks
- Confirm headcounts with clients (usually 2 weeks, 1 week, 48 hours before)
- Send final run sheets to all vendors and internal team
- Confirm AV/lighting equipment delivery windows
- Confirm catering final menu and dietary requirements
- Check weather forecast (outdoor events: monitoring starts 10 days out, hourly checks on event day)
- Confirm venue access times and security/parking arrangements
- Prepare contingency plans (rain, vendor no-show, power outage)
- Briefing calls with MC/host and speakers
- Final payment confirmations for all vendors

### After Tasks
- Client debrief call within 48-72 hours
- Collect and distribute photos/videos from vendors
- Send client thank-you + testimonial request
- Reconcile vendor invoices vs. event budget
- Issue final invoice to client
- Update vendor performance ratings in CRM
- Archive run sheets, floor plans, contracts for future reference
- Post event highlights to social media / portfolio

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Deposit deadlines:** Venue deposits due 30-90 days in advance; missing = losing the date
- **RSVP/headcount cutoffs:** Caterers require confirmed headcounts 2-3 weeks out
- **Vendor booking windows:** Popular photographers/bands book 6-18 months ahead; delay = unavailable
- **Permit applications:** Outdoor events, liquor licences, road closures -- often 4-8 week processing time
- **Insurance certificates:** Venue usually requires event insurance 30 days before
- **Payment schedules:** Vendors typically require 50% upfront + 50% on/before day
- **Run sheet distribution:** 72 hours before is the hard deadline

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Weather turning on an outdoor event
- A key vendor (caterer, AV company) cancelling last minute
- Client adding guests 48 hours before (catering, seating hell)
- Venue double-booking or changing a room layout
- Budget creep -- client expecting extras they didn't budget for
- Managing difficult stakeholders (internal corporate clients especially)
- Income seasonality -- Q1 is typically very slow

### Data Sources
- Google Calendar / Airtable / HoneyBook / Planning Pod
- Weather: OpenWeatherMap, Weather.gov, Yr.no
- Vendor CRM: contacts, availability, pricing history
- Budget tracker: Excel, Airtable, or dedicated tools
- Email (Gmail/Outlook) -- most coordination still happens here
- WhatsApp / Signal -- day-of comms with vendors

### Proactive AI Opportunities
1. **"The Murphy's Law Alert"** -- "Emma's corporate gala is in 9 days. You confirmed AV last week but I don't see a confirmation from the catering team. Want me to send a follow-up?"
2. **"Weather Watch"** -- "The rooftop event on the 24th has a 68% chance of rain. Your rain contingency plan mentions an indoor fallback -- have you confirmed availability with the venue?"
3. **"Deposit Danger"** -- "Venue deposit for the Henderson event is due in 4 days. I don't see a payment confirmed. Want me to draft the payment reminder or pull the invoice?"
4. **"Headcount Drift"** -- "You confirmed 120 guests with the caterer 2 weeks ago. The RSVP tracker shows 138 responses. Want me to flag this to the catering manager?"
5. **"Quiet Pipeline Warning"** -- "You have no new event bookings after June. Based on last year, this is your low season -- want me to draft a 'book summer early' campaign for your mailing list?"

## 2. WEDDING PLANNERS

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **18-12 months out:** Vision consultations, venue shortlisting, photographer/videographer/caterer bookings. This phase is leisurely and creative.
- **12-6 months out:** Vendor contracts signed, guest list building, invitations ordered, cake tastings, dress fittings coordination.
- **6-3 months out:** RSVP management, seating plans taking shape, ceremony rehearsal scheduling, accommodation blocks.
- **3 months -> wedding day:** Weekly (sometimes daily) contact with couples. Run sheets, final confirmations, payments due. Highly intense.
- **Off-season:** Jan-Feb (Southern Hemisphere) is post-peak. Wedding planners use this for portfolio work, style guides, styled shoots, and networking with vendors.
- **Peak season:** Oct-Apr (Southern Hemisphere). Jun-Oct (Northern Hemisphere). Planners often run 3-5 weddings per month in peak.

### Before Tasks
- 3-week check: confirm all vendor final payments
- 2-week check: final headcount to caterer, final seating chart
- 1-week check: rehearsal dinner coordination, distribute run sheets to all vendors
- Day-before: venue setup walkthrough, confirm deliveries (flowers, cake, furniture)
- Day-of: arrive 2-3 hours early, coordinate vendor arrivals, manage timeline from ceremony through reception
- Weather monitoring for outdoor ceremonies starts 10 days out
- Confirm hair & makeup timing with bridal party
- Manage "day-of emergency kit" (sewing kit, stain remover, painkillers, breath mints, etc.)

### After Tasks
- Send personalized thank-you to couple within 48 hours
- Coordinate collection and delivery of wedding photos/video to couple
- Request Google/Facebook review + testimonial within 2 weeks
- Reconcile final budget vs. actuals
- Final vendor payments and reconciliation
- Update vendor relationship notes ("the florist was 20 min late -- note for future")
- Social media post of wedding highlights (with couple's permission)
- Update portfolio website

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Venue booking:** Popular venues book 12-24 months ahead -- delay of even a week can lose the date
- **Photographer/videographer:** Top-tier talent books 12-18 months ahead
- **RSVP deadlines:** Typically 6-8 weeks before; stragglers create seating/catering chaos
- **Dress fittings:** Final fitting must be  2 weeks before to allow alterations
- **Marriage certificate:** Legal filing deadlines vary by country -- often overlooked until too late
- **Honeymoon bookings:** Flights/hotels need booking 6-12 months ahead for peak destinations
- **Music licensing:** Some venues require licensed playlists or approved DJ lists

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Couple conflict or family drama spilling into planning
- Vendor going out of business before the wedding (it happens)
- Weather -- outdoor ceremonies with no backup plan
- Dress arriving late or damaged
- Key vendor (photographer, caterer) having a day-of emergency
- Unrealistic budgets -- couple wants $60k wedding on $20k budget
- Juggling multiple weddings in peak season without double-booking personal bandwidth
- "Bridezilla" dynamics -- managing emotional clients under extreme stress

### Data Sources
- WedHQ, Aisle Planner, WeddingWire, Zola -- wedding-specific planning tools
- Google Sheets / Airtable -- budget, guest list, seating
- Google Calendar -- timeline management
- Weather: Weather.com, Weather Underground (hyperlocal)
- Email + WhatsApp -- primary vendor/couple communication
- Instagram -- vendor discovery and portfolio building

### Proactive AI Opportunities
1. **"The 12-Week Countdown Alert"** -- "The Nguyen-Kowalski wedding is 84 days away. Final headcount is due to your caterer in 12 days and I don't see it scheduled. Want me to send a reminder to the couple?"
2. **"Vendor Ghost Alert"** -- "You signed the Bloom & Co. florals contract 3 months ago but I haven't seen any email from them since. Their last reply was 11 weeks ago. Want me to draft a check-in?"
3. **"Weather Watch -- Ceremony Risk"** -- "The Daniels ceremony is outdoor at Constantia Uitsig in 8 days. Current 10-day forecast shows 40% rain. Want to brief the couple on the indoor backup now?"
4. **"RSVP Stragglers"** -- "Deadline was last Friday. You still have 18 no-responses. Want me to draft a WhatsApp follow-up for the couple to forward to their guests?"
5. **"Review Window"** -- "It's been 12 days since the Okafor wedding. This is the sweet spot to ask for a Google review. Want me to draft a message?"

## 3. PROFESSIONAL PHOTOGRAPHERS (WEDDING, COMMERCIAL, PORTRAIT)

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
**Wedding photographers:**
- Heavily seasonal. Peak = spring/summer/autumn weekends. Fridays/Saturdays are booked months ahead.
- Weekdays: editing (2-6 weeks backlog is normal), client consultations, equipment maintenance
- Jan-Feb: portfolio culling, equipment upgrades, styled shoots for marketing

**Commercial photographers:**
- Project-based. Can vary from one shoot per week to several per day (e-commerce)
- Brief -> concept -> pre-production -> shoot day -> retouching -> delivery
- Turnaround pressure depends on client: advertising agencies expect 48-hour deliveries; editorial can be same-day

**Portrait photographers:**
- Mix of booked sessions (families, headshots, newborns) and ad-hoc
- Seasonal spikes: Christmas mini-sessions (Oct-Nov), back-to-school headshots (Jan-Feb), graduation season (May-Jun)

### Before Tasks
- Confirm shoot details with client (location, time, outfits, mood board)
- Site reconnaissance (especially for weddings and commercial shoots)
- Check weather forecast for outdoor shoots (7-day watch, daily checks T-3)
- Charge all batteries (2-4 sets), format memory cards
- Pack gear (camera bodies, lenses, flash, backup flash, triggers, light stands, reflectors)
- Confirm permits if shooting in public/restricted spaces
- Confirm second shooter or assistant availability
- For weddings: meet venue coordinator, review timeline with couple

### After Tasks
- Cull and select images (typically same day or T+1)
- Color grade / retouch in Lightroom/Photoshop (2-6 weeks for weddings, 24-72 hours for commercial)
- Export and deliver via gallery platform (Pixieset, Pic-Time, Shootproof, Dropbox)
- Send invoice and collect remaining payment
- Follow up with client for testimonial/review (2 weeks post-delivery ideal)
- Clean and check gear for damage
- Back up all RAW files (local + cloud)
- Update CRM with job notes

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Gallery delivery deadlines:** Clients expect weddings in 4-8 weeks; commercial in 24-72 hours
- **Booking windows:** Wedding season books 9-18 months ahead -- a photographer who delays responding loses the client
- **Equipment insurance renewals:** Annual -- if gear is stolen/damaged and insurance lapsed, catastrophic
- **Venue/permit bookings:** Some parks and heritage sites require permits 2-4 weeks ahead
- **Retainer deadlines:** Most photographers require 25-50% retainer to hold a date; sending contracts late loses bookings
- **Album proof deadlines:** If client doesn't approve album design in time, printing is delayed (Christmas delivery pressure)
- **Model/MUA availability:** Creative shoots depend on coordinated availability of multiple people

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Equipment failure mid-shoot (especially during once-in-a-lifetime moments)
- Weather ruining an outdoor session or wedding ceremony
- Memory card failure / file corruption (photographers' nightmare)
- Client expecting "Vogue" results from a basic portrait session budget
- Editing backlog during peak season (15+ weddings in the queue)
- Clients posting unedited previews to social media before gallery delivery
- Income gap in winter/off-season
- Gear theft (cameras are high-value targets)

### Data Sources
- Honeybook / Tve / Studio Ninja -- booking, contracts, invoicing
- Lightroom / Capture One -- editing and cataloging
- Pixieset / Shootproof -- client galleries
- Google Calendar -- shoot scheduling
- Weather: Weather Underground (hyperlocal, critical for outdoor shoots)
- BackBlaze / Synology -- backup systems
- Instagram / Pinterest -- marketing and mood board sourcing

### Proactive AI Opportunities
1. **"The Editing Queue Alert"** -- "You have 4 undelivered wedding galleries. The Petersen gallery is now 5 weeks overdue. They're expecting delivery by end of month -- want me to flag your editing schedule?"
2. **"Weather Rescue"** -- "You have a family portrait session at Boulders Beach this Saturday. Rain is forecast 70%. Want me to draft a rescheduling message to the Hendersons or suggest the shaded backup location?"
3. **"Insurance Gap"** -- "Your camera gear insurance renewal is in 11 days. Based on last year, you missed the renewal window. Want me to set a reminder and pull the insurer's contact details?"
4. **"Review Nudge"** -- "The Liu headshot session was delivered 14 days ago. No review yet -- this is peak timing. Want me to send a follow-up?"
5. **"Booking Pressure"** -- "You have 3 enquiries from this weekend that you haven't responded to. Wedding photographers typically lose a booking if they don't reply within 4 hours. Want me to draft holding responses?"

## 4. VIDEOGRAPHERS

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- Very similar to photographers but with significantly heavier post-production load
- A 1-hour event can yield 6-12 hours of footage -- editing ratio is brutal
- **Wedding videographers:** 1-2 weddings per weekend during peak; editing takes 4-12 weeks per wedding
- **Commercial videographers:** Project-based. Corporate videos, branded content, social media reels, product videos
- **Freelance videographers:** Often balance weddings, corporate gigs, and content creation simultaneously
- **Pre-production weeks** (brief, storyboard, scout) can be as long as shoot + post combined for commercial work

### Before Tasks
- Pre-production meeting: brief, storyboard, shot list
- Location scout (or ask client to send venue floor plan/photos)
- Confirm audio setup -- lavalier mics for speeches, ambient mics for ceremony
- Charge batteries for cameras, drone, gimbal, audio recorders
- Format all cards and drives
- Drone pre-flight check: firmware, battery, airspace permits (SACAA/CAA/FAA)
- Test audio equipment the day before
- Confirm call sheet with crew (if applicable)
- Check weather (critical for drone operations -- wind and rain ground the drone)
- Confirm access logistics (parking, restricted areas, timing)

### After Tasks
- Offload and verify all footage (multi-card redundancy check)
- Create project folder structure, backup to working drive + archive drive
- Assembly edit / rough cut
- Color grade, audio mix, music licensing
- Deliver first cut to client for review
- Incorporate revisions (usually 1-2 rounds contracted)
- Export final deliverables (multiple formats: 4K master, 1080p web, vertical social cuts)
- Upload to Vimeo/Dropbox/WeTransfer for client
- Invoice for final payment
- Request testimonial/review

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Delivery windows:** Commercial clients often have hard launch dates; missing a delivery = contract penalty
- **Music licensing:** If sync license isn't secured before delivery, the whole project stalls
- **Drone permits:** SACAA/CAA require advance application; spontaneous drone flights can be illegal and dangerous
- **Airspace restrictions:** Temporary flight restrictions (TFRs) can pop up on event day -- need same-day awareness
- **Retainer deadlines:** Same as photographers -- delay in contract = lost booking
- **Color grading log files:** Sending LOG footage to client by mistake (it happens) -- needs to be caught before delivery
- **Hard drive lifespan:** Footage drives typically fail after 3-5 years; proactive monitoring prevents catastrophic loss

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Drone grounded by wind or rain on the wedding day
- Audio failure during the vows (irreplaceable moment)
- Footage card failing or getting accidentally formatted
- Editing backlog during peak season
- Clients expecting Hollywood production on YouTube creator budget
- Music licensing fees not budgeted by client
- Hard drive failure mid-project
- Scope creep -- "can you do a quick social cut? And a highlight reel? And a 30-second teaser?"

### Data Sources
- Frame.io / Vimeo Review -- client collaboration and review
- Shotlist / StudioBinder -- pre-production planning
- Google Calendar / Airtable -- project management
- SACAA / CAA / FAA LAANC -- drone airspace authorization
- Musicbed / Artlist / Epidemic Sound -- licensed music
- Weather: Windy.com (critical for drone operations -- shows wind by altitude)
- Hard drive health: CrystalDiskInfo (Windows), DriveDx (Mac)

### Proactive AI Opportunities
1. **"Drone Grounded"** -- "Tomorrow's aerial coverage at the Fitzgerald wedding. Wind forecast is 42 km/h at the venue. Your drone's limit is 30 km/h. Have you prepared a ground-only shot list as a fallback?"
2. **"Music License Hold"** -- "The Malouf corporate video is due in 5 days. I don't see a music license confirmed. The track you noted in the brief (Artlist) needs to be licensed to include in the delivered file."
3. **"Delivery Deadline Risk"** -- "The OmniTech launch video deadline is Friday. The project has been in revision round 2 for 9 days with no client response. Want me to send a deadline reminder?"
4. **"Drive Health Alert"** -- "Your archive drive 2 (4TB WD Red) hasn't been health-checked in 4 months. It's also 3.5 years old. Want me to schedule a disk check tonight?"
5. **"Airspace Flag"** -- "You have a drone shoot at the Cape Town waterfront in 6 days. That zone requires prior authorization. Have you submitted your SACAA permit request? Want me to draft it?"

## 5. DJs / MUSIC PRODUCERS

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
**DJs:**
- Weekend-heavy performance schedule. Fri-Sat = gig nights. Sun-Mon = recovery. Tue-Thu = prep, admin, production
- Set preparation: 2-4 hours per major gig (new tracks, transitions, key matching, BPM mapping)
- Residency DJs: weekly prep with set variations to keep regulars engaged
- Festival/touring DJs: heavy travel logistics, backline riders, sound checks

**Music Producers:**
- Studio time often late nights (creative peak hours)
- Client sessions (beatmaking for artists) vs. personal project time
- Sync licensing work: pitching beats/tracks to advertising/film -- separate pipeline
- Release planning: single rollout, EP production pipeline, mastering deadlines

**Both:**
- Social media presence is career-critical -- regular content required
- Demo reels and mixes need updating every 3-6 months to stay current

### Before Tasks
- Prepare setlist and cue points in DJ software (Rekordbox/Serato/Traktor)
- Export set to USB (backup USB second copy essential)
- Check equipment: CDJs/controllers, mixer, headphones, laptop, cables, adapters
- Confirm setup/soundcheck time with venue
- Confirm backline rider (what's provided vs. bringing own gear)
- Know the audience: corporate? club? wedding? Adjust energy and BPM accordingly
- Confirm payment terms (cash/EFT, on-night or invoice?)
- For production sessions: prepare session files, check sample pack availability, clear disk space

### After Tasks
- Pack down and check all gear returned (cable count especially)
- Post gig recap to socials (stories, reel, tag the venue)
- Send invoice if not paid on-night
- Follow up with venue/booker for future gigs or residency consideration
- Record listener feedback (what tracks hit, what missed -- Evernote/notes)
- Add new tracks from tonight's set into "tested" playlist for future use
- For productions: export stems, archive project files, send to artist

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Gig confirmations:** Verbal bookings that never get contracted = exposure to no-shows on both sides
- **Travel bookings:** Festival gigs require flights/accommodation months ahead; delays = inflated costs
- **Equipment hire:** Specialist gear (CDJ-3000s, specific mixers) books out weeks ahead at major events
- **Release deadlines:** Distributor submission (DistroKid/TuneCore) needs to be 2-3 weeks before release date for Spotify editorial pitching
- **Spotify editorial pitch:** Must be submitted  7 days before release date -- missed = no editorial placement
- **Sync pitch windows:** Ad campaigns have fixed production calendars; late submissions miss the window
- **DJ competition registrations:** Deadlines often 6-8 weeks before the event

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- USB/laptop failure mid-set (the DJ's version of amnesia on stage)
- Venue's CDJs running outdated firmware -- new cue points don't load correctly
- Being given 30 minutes instead of the contracted 90 (venue running late)
- Sound engineer not following the rider -- muddy mix, feedback
- Venue payment disputes ("we said 300, not 500")
- Quiet booking periods (summer in Northern Hemisphere for club DJs, winter for outdoor/festival)
- Copyright claims killing a released mix on YouTube
- Promoting a release with no label backing -- organic reach is brutal

### Data Sources
- Rekordbox / Serato / Traktor -- library management and performance
- Beatport / Traxsource -- new music procurement
- DistroKid / TuneCore -- music distribution
- Spotify for Artists -- release and pitch management
- Soundcloud / Mixcloud -- mix uploads and audience analytics
- Instagram / TikTok -- audience building
- Google Calendar / Notion -- booking and project management

### Proactive AI Opportunities
1. **"USB Sync Alert"** -- "You have a gig at Shimmy Beach Club on Saturday. Your Rekordbox was last exported to USB on October 14th. You've added 34 tracks since then. Want me to remind you to re-export tonight?"
2. **"Spotify Editorial Miss"** -- "Your single 'Drift' drops in 10 days. Spotify editorial pitch must be submitted 7 days before release. The window closes tomorrow -- want me to open the Spotify for Artists pitch form?"
3. **"Ghost Booking"** -- "You confirmed the Investec private event verbally with Mark 3 weeks ago. No contract signed, no deposit received. Event is in 18 days. Want me to draft a follow-up?"
4. **"Festival Flight Squeeze"** -- "You've confirmed the Oppikoppi set. Flights were R1,800 in March. They're now R3,200 and climbing. Want me to surface the booking link?"
5. **"Firmware Trap"** -- "The venue for Saturday uses Pioneer CDJ-2000NXS2s. Based on your Rekordbox version, make sure they're on firmware 1.82+. Want me to add a soundcheck note to your prep checklist?"

## 6. MUSICIANS / BANDS

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Gigging bands:** Weekend-heavy. Thu-Sat for live music. Mon-Wed = rehearsals, admin, recording
- **Session musicians:** Unpredictable. Studio calls often come 24-48 hours ahead. Availability management is key.
- **Touring:** Extended blocks away from home. Travel, soundcheck, performance, overnight, repeat. Exhausting admin overhead.
- **Recording phases:** Studio blocks of days/weeks. Session days are long (10-14 hours). Post-session: mixing, mastering timeline.
- **Off-season/between gigs:** Song writing, demo recording, social content creation, networking, gear maintenance.
- **Income diversity:** Most musicians survive on a mix of gigs, teaching, session work, sync licensing, and streaming royalties.

### Before Tasks
- Confirm gig details: load-in time, soundcheck time, set time, set length
- Confirm backline and stage plot (what's provided, what to bring)
- Confirm payment: amount, method, who to invoice
- Rehearse setlist for new venues or new material
- Instrument maintenance: restring guitars, tune, check electronics, replace batteries in active pickups
- PA/sound check: confirm DI boxes, monitor mix requirements
- For touring: confirm accommodation, transport, meal buyout or catering rider
- Confirm support acts or other performers on the bill
- Weather monitoring for outdoor festival stages

### After Tasks
- Pack down and account for all gear (cases, cables, stands)
- Collect payment (or chase invoice)
- Social media post-gig content (photos, clips from crowd)
- Update gig log / setlist record
- Add venue and booker to CRM with performance notes
- Request social media tag from venue
- Follow up with promoter/booker for future dates
- For recording: export session backups, send mixes to band for feedback

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Tour routing:** Booking a regional tour requires dates confirmed 3-6 months ahead to build a logical route
- **Recording studio bookings:** Good studios book 4-12 weeks ahead
- **Festival applications:** Most festivals close applications 6-9 months before the event
- **Grant applications:** Arts/music grants have fixed windows -- NArts (SA), PRS Foundation (UK) -- yearly deadlines
- **PRO registration:** Songs must be registered with SAMRO/ASCAP/PRS before they're played live or broadcast to collect royalties
- **Merchandise production:** Lead time for T-shirts/vinyl is 3-6 weeks; must order before tour starts
- **Visa applications:** International touring requires work visas -- US O-1/P1 visas take 3-6 months
- **Sync licensing pitches:** Same as DJs -- campaign windows are rigid

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Amplifier/instrument failure on stage (especially without backup gear)
- Promoter cancelling last-minute or going dark
- Getting underpaid or not paid at all (extremely common in live music)
- Sound engineer giving a terrible monitor mix
- Bandmate missing rehearsal or showing up unprepared
- Touring van/transport breaking down
- Recording session going over budget
- Streaming royalties being too low to survive on
- Mental health: touring isolation, financial uncertainty, constant rejection

### Data Sources
- Bandsintown / Songkick -- gig discovery and calendar management
- Google Calendar / Notion -- booking and rehearsal management
- SAMRO / ASCAP / PRS -- royalty collection and registration
- DistroKid / TuneCore -- digital distribution
- Bandcamp -- direct-to-fan sales
- Instagram / TikTok / YouTube -- audience and booking leverage
- QuickBooks / Wave -- income/expense tracking for tax purposes

### Proactive AI Opportunities
1. **"PRO Registration Gap"** -- "You played 'Undertow' at 3 gigs last month. I don't see it registered with SAMRO. Unregistered songs don't collect performance royalties. Want me to pull the registration form?"
2. **"Festival Window Closing"** -- "AfrikaBurn artist applications close in 18 days. You mentioned wanting to apply in December. Want me to surface the application link and your past submission notes?"
3. **"Gear Insurance Check"** -- "Your Fender Stratocaster and Mesa amp are on your 'uninsured gear' list. Combined replacement value is approximately R45,000. Want me to pull gear insurance options?"
4. **"Merch Lead Time"** -- "Your regional tour starts in 31 days. You mentioned wanting to print T-shirts. Most printers need 3-4 weeks. You're at the cutoff -- want me to flag this today?"
5. **"Booker Follow-Up"** -- "You played the Crypt in September and the booker said to follow up in January. It's now February and I don't see any follow-up sent. Want me to draft it?"

## 7. TALENT AGENTS

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Heavy email/calls day.** Talent agents typically spend 60-70% of their day in communication -- pitching clients, negotiating, following up.
- **Mon-Tue:** Review of incoming briefs from event organizers, brands, and production companies. Pitch appropriate roster talent.
- **Wed-Thu:** Negotiation and contracting. This is where deals close or die.
- **Fri:** Confirmations and admin. Reconcile bookings, invoice clients, check upcoming schedule.
- **Ongoing:** Talent development -- scouting, meeting new acts, reviewing demo reels, building roster.
- **Seasonal peaks:** Corporate booking season (Sep-Nov for Dec events), wedding season (Jan-Mar for Oct-Apr events), festival season (Feb-Apr for mid-year festivals).

### Before Tasks
- Confirm all logistical details with the booking client (time, venue, tech rider)
- Forward confirmed details to talent (artist/performer) and their management
- Confirm travel and accommodation arrangements (if applicable)
- Confirm tech rider fulfillment with venue/production
- Send contract to client and chase signature
- Confirm deposit receipt before confirming talent
- Brief the talent on dress code, audience profile, set length, any restrictions (e.g., no profanity for corporate events)
- Confirm on-day contact details for venue and client

### After Tasks
- Confirm event went smoothly -- follow-up call with client and talent
- Issue final invoice (remaining balance)
- Collect and distribute testimonials from clients
- Update booking history in CRM
- Review any post-event issues (late payment, tech rider not fulfilled, performance complaints)
- Talent performance review -- update internal notes
- Follow up with client for next year's booking

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Deposit receipt:** Talent should NOT be confirmed until deposit is received -- agents who skip this get burned
- **Contract signing:** Unsigned contracts = unenforceable agreements; must chase within 5-7 days
- **Exclusive hold windows:** When a booker asks for a hold on a date, that's typically 5-10 days before it expires or gets released
- **Festival lineup announcements:** Being on the lineup drives bookings; announcement timing matters
- **VISA applications:** International artist bookings need work permits -- 2-6 months lead time depending on destination
- **Tech rider fulfillment:** Must be confirmed 2-3 weeks ahead, not 2 days before

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Client expecting A-list talent on C-list budget
- Talent cancelling last-minute (especially high-risk: independent artists without management)
- Double-booking a talent (agent's nightmare -- career-ending if it happens twice)
- Client not paying after the event
- Talent going rogue -- accepting direct bookings that cut the agent out
- Exclusive hold expiring before client makes a decision
- Festival organizers going insolvent mid-season (it happens)

### Data Sources
- CRM: HubSpot, Airtable, or custom solutions -- talent roster, booking history
- Google Calendar -- availability management (critical; must be real-time)
- DocuSign / HelloSign -- contract management
- Accounting: Xero, QuickBooks -- commission tracking and invoicing
- WhatsApp / Email -- primary communication channels
- Eventbrite / Quicket -- event discovery for sourcing potential clients
- Social: Instagram, TikTok -- talent discovery and audience-size verification

### Proactive AI Opportunities

## 8. STAGE / PRODUCTION MANAGERS

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Pre-production phase (weeks/months before):** Technical planning, vendor RFQ, site surveys, crew hiring, equipment procurement. Document-heavy.
- **Load-in week:** 12-16 hour days. Logistics, supplier coordination, staging construction, lighting and AV rigging, cable runs, generator placement.
- **Show days:** Pre-show (T-5 hours): final checks. T-2: sound and lighting check. T-0: doors open. Constant problem-solving throughout.
- **Load-out:** Often overnight after the last act. Full demobilization.
- **Post-production:** Equipment inspection, damage reports, crew payments, production report, debrief.
- **Downtime:** Between shows -- equipment inventory, maintenance, relationship building with rental houses and crew.

### Before Tasks
- Finalize production schedule and distribute to all departments
- Confirm all equipment hire orders (staging, lighting, AV, power, backline)
- Crew call sheets and briefings
- Site survey and risk assessment
- Arrange and confirm all permits (local authority, noise, occupancy)
- Confirm power requirements and generator bookings
- Liaise with artist management to confirm technical riders
- Confirm security, first aid, and traffic management
- Weather monitoring for outdoor shows (wind is critical -- staging and structures have wind limits)
- Confirm load-in access and timing with venue

### After Tasks
- Conduct full equipment inventory against hire agreement
- Document any damage -- photos, reports, insurance claims if necessary
- Crew sign-off and payments
- Return all hired equipment on time (late fees are significant)
- Post-show production report -- what worked, what didn't, recommendations
- Invoice client
- Update supplier/crew performance notes
- Hold debrief meeting with client and key crew

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Equipment hire booking:** Major staging and lighting rigs book out months ahead, especially during peak festival season
- **Permit applications:** Council permits for outdoor events (noise, temporary structures) require 4-12 weeks
- **Wind speed monitoring:** Outdoor staging has defined wind speed limits -- must have a structural engineer's sign-off and an emergency protocol
- **Generator fuel:** Long events need refueling schedules -- running out mid-show is catastrophic
- **Curfews:** Noise curfews are legally enforced; going over = fines, license suspension
- **Safety inspections:** Temporary structures require sign-off from a structural engineer -- can't open without it
- **Artist advance:** Technical rider must be submitted to production 4-6 weeks before large shows

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Structural safety in high wind -- the decision to stop a show is production manager's call and it is enormous
- Equipment arriving late or damaged
- Crew no-shows (day-of substitutions are chaotic)
- Power failure mid-show
- Local council showing up to enforce permit conditions at the worst possible moment
- Artist management sending rider changes 48 hours before show
- Noise complaints triggering a shutdow
- Managing multiple simultaneous stages at a festival
- Budget overruns due to unforeseen site requirements

### Data Sources
- AutoCAD / Vectorworks -- stage and site plotting
- Production management: Showman, Event Planner Pro, Google Sheets
- StageHands / Crew United -- crew sourcing
- Weather: Windy.com (wind speed by altitude), Weather Underground
- PocketRisk / iAuditor -- risk assessments and checklists
- Radio communication: Motorola/Kenwood channels assigned per department
- Supplier contact databases

## 9. FARMERS / AGRICULTURAL WORKERS

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- Governed almost entirely by **season and crop type**, not clock time
- **Grain farmers (wheat, maize, soy):**
  - Apr-Jun (Southern Hemisphere): land preparation, soil testing, fertilizer application
  - Jul-Sep: planting window (narrow -- 2-4 weeks)
  - Sep-Jan: growing season -- irrigation management, pest/disease scouting, weed control
  - Feb-Apr: harvest -- urgent, weather-dependent, runs 7 days a week
- **Fruit farmers (wine grapes, citrus, stone fruit):**
  - Year-round operations but with harvest peaks that define the year
  - Wine grape harvest (SA): Feb-Apr. Citrus: Jun-Sep. Apples: Feb-Mar
  - Pruning, tying, training vines/trees is winter off-peak work
- **Vegetable farmers:** Multiple rotations per year. High intensity, year-round.
- **Livestock farmers:** Daily feeding, health monitoring, breeding cycles. Less seasonal but never truly off.

### Before Tasks
- **Pre-season:** Soil testing, fertilizer plan, input procurement (seeds, chemicals, fuel), equipment servicing
- **Pre-planting:** Confirm water availability, irrigation system check, land preparation (ploughing, discing, ripping)
- **Pre-harvest:** Confirm labour availability (seasonal workers), equipment readiness (combine harvesters, trailers), storage capacity check, pre-book transport/logistics
- **Daily:** Weather forecast check (rain, frost, extreme heat, hail), irrigation scheduling, pest/disease walk (crop scouting)
- **Livestock daily:** Feed and water check, animal health observation, fencing inspection

### After Tasks
- **Post-harvest:** Yield recording, grain/fruit quality grading, storage management
- **Equipment maintenance:** Post-harvest service on all machinery (hours-based intervals)
- **Financial reconciliation:** Actual vs. budget yield, input cost reconciliation
- **Soil sampling:** Post-crop soil tests to assess nutrient depletion
- **Labour settlements:** Seasonal worker payments, COIDA compliance
- **Market records:** Log sale prices, volumes, buyer details
- **Compliance reporting:** GAP (Good Agricultural Practice) certification, export compliance documentation

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Planting windows:** For many crops, planting outside the optimal window (often 3-4 weeks) severely reduces yield -- not recoverable
- **Frost warnings:** A single frost event can destroy an entire season's stone fruit crop in hours
- **Hail warnings:** Same -- hail netting must be down or structures in place *before* the event
- **Spray windows:** Fungicide/pesticide applications have weather windows (wind speed, rain forecast, temperature) -- applying at wrong time = ineffective or illegal
- **Irrigation scheduling:** Moisture deficit triggers are crop-specific and time-critical
- **Export permit applications:** Phytosanitary certificates for citrus, wine, fruit exports require advance application
- **Input procurement:** Seeds and fertilizer sell out in pre-season -- late ordering = paying premium or going without
- **Labour seasonal booking:** Harvest labour pools are shared across regions -- late booking = no workers
- **Water licence renewals:** Irrigation water use licences are legally required and have renewal windows

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Hail or late frost destroying a crop overnight (especially devastating for fruit)
- Drought -- inadequate rainfall or dam levels during critical growth stages
- Equipment breaking down during harvest (every hour of downtime = direct income loss)
- Input price inflation (fertilizer, fuel, chemicals)
- Commodity price volatility -- harvest well but sell low
- Labour unavailability or industrial action during harvest
- Export rejection -- a consignment failing phytosanitary inspection at the border
- Load-shedding/power outages disrupting irrigation pumps, cold storage, processing
- Disease outbreaks (citrus greening, grain rust) spreading from neighbouring farms

### Data Sources
- Weather: Farmers' Weather (SA), Agromet (Agricultural Meteorological Services), SAWS, DTN/AgWeather
- Precision agriculture: John Deere Operations Center, Climate FieldView, Farmlogs
- Soil and crop monitoring: NDVI satellite imagery (Google Earth Engine, Sentinel)
- Input procurement: Senwes, Afgri, Nutrien -- supplier apps and price trackers
- Market prices: SAFEX (SA futures exchange), Agri4All, DALRRD commodity price bulletins
- Labour management: Labournet, SimplePay (SA payroll compliance)
- Water management: DWS water allocation systems, irrigation scheduling apps

### Proactive AI Opportunities
1. **"Frost Kill Window"** -- "A frost warning has been issued for the Hex River Valley tonight -- predicted -2C at 04:00. Your nectarine blocks are in full blossom phase. This is critical. Do you want me to alert your night watch team and pull the frost protection protocol?"
2. **"Planting Window Closing"** -- "Optimal maize planting for your region closes in 8 days based on historical yield data. Rainfall this week has been below threshold for germination. The forecast shows a dry week ahead. Do you want to review the planting decision with your agronomist?"

## 10. AGRICULTURAL CONSULTANTS

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Client visit schedule:** Typically 3-4 farm visits per week, writing reports and recommendations in between
- **Soil and tissue sampling seasons:** Spring (pre-planting) and post-harvest -- intense sampling and analysis periods
- **Spray season:** Ongoing advisory role during growing season. Clients call or WhatsApp with photos of disease/pest problems. Response expected same day.
- **Reporting periods:** Monthly or quarterly reports to clients. End-of-season performance reviews.
- **Conference and CPD season:** Usually late winter (Jul-Aug in SA) -- keeping up with research, maintaining certifications
- **Off-season:** Review last season's results, update recommendations, write proposals for new clients, recharge.

### Before Tasks
- Prepare for farm visits: review last season's notes, soil reports, crop maps
- Confirm appointment with farmer (many farmers change plans based on weather)
- Prepare sampling equipment, GPS, field maps
- Check weather (no point recommending a spray application if rain is forecast)
- Review any current pest/disease alerts for the region
- Prepare updated input recommendation sheets

### After Tasks
- Write field visit report (same day or T+1)
- Submit soil/tissue samples to lab with proper paperwork
- Interpret lab results and write fertilizer recommendations
- Send recommendations to farmer with supporting data
- Update CRM with visit notes, crop status, farmer concerns
- Invoice for visit and report
- Monitor whether recommendations were implemented (feedback loop)
- Compile season-end performance report for client portfolio

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Spray windows:** Consultant must advise within the weather window -- 24-hour response is expected
- **Lab turnaround:** Soil labs take 5-14 days; recommendations are only as good as the data -- delays in sampling = delays in planting decisions
- **In-season pest/disease alerts:** Disease pressure (e.g., wheat rust) can spread rapidly -- same-day response is industry standard
- **Season-end reporting:** Clients expect season review reports before next season's planning begins
- **Chemical registration changes:** If a product is withdrawn or conditions change, consultants must notify clients immediately
- **CPD deadlines:** Professional certification renewal deadlines (AVCASA, CropLife SA) -- missing = loss of registration to give advice

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Being blamed for a crop failure even when the farmer didn't follow advice
- Recommendation given in good faith that turns out wrong due to unusual weather
- Keeping up with rapidly changing chemical registrations and resistance patterns
- Managing too many client farms spread over a large geographic area
- Liability -- wrong fungicide application timing can lead to residue exceedance at export inspection
- Competing with over-the-counter advice from co-ops (who earn sales commission)

### Data Sources
- SABS / CropLife SA -- chemical registration database
- PlantWise / Agrios -- disease identification
- DAFF (DALRRD) -- policy, compliance, and commodity information
- Agri4All, Senwes, Afgri -- input pricing
- Soil labs: Bemlab (SA), AGRI LAB, Fertasa
- Weather: SAWS, DTN, Agromet
- GIS: Esri ArcGIS, Google Earth Pro -- farm mapping

### Proactive AI Opportunities
1. **"Spray Window Alert"** -- "Regional weather shows a dry, low-wind window opening tomorrow between 07:00 and 12:00. You have 3 clients in the area with pending fungicide recommendations. Want me to send a same-day advisory?"
2. **"Lab Results Arrived"** -- "Soil results for the Venter farm (submitted 12 days ago) should be back. Last year they came back on day 11. Want me to check with Bemlab and chase if needed?"
3. **"CPD Deadline"** -- "Your AVCASA registration renewal requires 10 CPD points by September 30th. You currently have 6 logged. There's a CropLife SA webinar on Thursday that offers 2 points. Want me to register you?"
4. **"Rust Alert Propagation"** -- "DAFF issued a yellow rust alert for the Swartland region yesterday. You have 4 wheat clients in that zone. Want me to draft a precautionary spray advisory with the recommended product list?"
5. **"Season Review Due"** -- "The Du Plessis farm's season ended 3 weeks ago. Your typical turnaround for season reviews is 2 weeks. This one is overdue -- want me to flag it and pull last season's notes to start the report?"

## 11. ENVIRONMENTAL OFFICERS

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- Varies widely by employer: government departments, municipalities, mining companies, construction firms, agriculture, NGOs
- **Government/municipal:** Application processing (EIA, waste licences), inspection scheduling, compliance reporting. High admin load.
- **Corporate/mining:** Site monitoring, compliance audits, incident response. Mix of desk and field.
- **NGO/conservation:** Monitoring programs, reporting to funders, community engagement, advocacy campaigns.
- **Permit season:** EIA (Environmental Impact Assessment) submission windows and decision deadlines drive much of the government officer's calendar.
- **Reporting cycles:** Monthly compliance reports, quarterly environmental monitoring reports, annual environmental management plan reviews.

### Before Tasks
- Review application files before site inspection
- Prepare inspection checklists and documentation
- Confirm appointment with facility operator
- Check for any complaints or incidents logged at the site
- Review site's compliance history
- Prepare camera, GPS, sampling equipment
- Check relevant legislation and permit conditions for the site type
- Weather check for field visits (flooding can make sites inaccessible)

### After Tasks
- Write inspection report (typically within 5-10 days)
- Issue compliance notices or directives where violations found
- Load data into monitoring system
- Update case file with findings and follow-up actions
- Escalate serious violations to enforcement unit
- Communicate with facility operator on corrective actions required
- Monthly/quarterly reporting to management
- File maintenance: update permit register

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **EIA decision deadlines:** NEMA (South Africa) has statutory decision timeframes -- missing them creates legal challenges and appeals
- **Permit renewal applications:** Environmental/waste licences have renewal windows -- late submission can create a compliance gap
- **Incident response:** Spill or pollution incident requires immediate response (within hours, not days)
- **Public participation deadlines:** EIA/EMP processes have defined comment periods -- missing these invalidates the process
- **Reporting deadlines:** Statutory reporting to DEA/DFFE -- hard deadlines with legal consequences
- **Compliance notice response periods:** An operator issued a notice typically has 30 days to respond -- tracking this is critical

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Under-resourced: too many applications, too few officers
- Political pressure to approve projects that shouldn't be approved
- Responding to environmental emergencies (spills, fires) with inadequate resources
- Keeping up with legislative changes (NEMA, NWA, NEMA amendments)
- Legal challenges from both developers and NGOs
- Whistleblowing pressures in corporate settings

### Data Sources
- NEAS (National Environmental Authorisation System) -- South Africa
- DEA GIS / DFFE environmental atlas
- Water quality: DWS National Water Quality Monitoring Programme
- Air quality: South African Air Quality Information System (SAAQIS)
- SAPS / disaster management: incident reporting
- BGIS (Biodiversity Geographic Information System)
- IPCC / IPBES -- international climate and biodiversity data

### Proactive AI Opportunities
1. **"EIA Deadline Alarm"** -- "The Kopano Solar Farm EIA application was accepted 82 days ago. NEMA allows 107 days for a decision. You have 25 days remaining. Want me to pull the case file and flag it to your team lead?"
2. **"Inspection Overdue"** -- "Vaalbank Waste Disposal Site is due for a quarterly compliance inspection. Last visit: 94 days ago. Quarterly requirement = 90 days. You're already 4 days overdue. Want me to schedule this week?"
3. **"Incident Pattern Alert"** -- "This is the 3rd pollution complaint from the Limpopo River corridor in 60 days -- all within 5km of the same industrial park. A pattern investigation may be warranted. Want me to compile the incident file?"
4. **"Permit Renewal Window"** -- "The Steelpoort Chrome Mine's waste licence expires in 4 months. Renewal applications typically take 6-8 weeks to process. If they haven't applied yet, they'll be operating without a licence. Want me to send a pre-emptive advisory?"
5. **"Legislative Change Alert"** -- "DFFE published an amendment to GNR 636 (waste classification regulations) last week. You have 6 active waste facility permit applications that may be affected. Want me to flag the amendment to the applicants?"

## 12. PARK RANGERS / CONSERVATION OFFICERS

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
- **Patrol-based:** Daily patrol routes -- vehicle, foot, and sometimes aerial patrols. Routes vary daily to avoid predictability (anti-poaching)
- **Dawn and dusk:** High-activity periods for wildlife and poaching -- most dangerous patrol times
- **Anti-poaching operations:** Intelligence-led, often reactive. Rapid deployment at any time.
- **Wildlife monitoring:** Periodic surveys (weekly/monthly), camera trap servicing, GPS collar data review
- **Community engagement:** Regular meetings with communities adjacent to protected areas
- **Conservation planning:** Seasonal: burning programs (dry season), translocation planning, invasive species clearing
- **Tourism season:** High visitor volumes require increased patrol frequency at access points and popular areas
- **Admin:** Daily incident logs, patrol reports, evidence handling, court preparation for poaching cases

### Before Tasks
- Pre-patrol briefing: current intelligence, threat assessment, patrol area assignment
- Equipment check: firearms (clean, loaded, safety), radio, GPS, first aid kit, water, food for multi-day patrols
- Vehicle check: fuel, tyres, spare, recovery equipment
- Review of overnight camera trap alerts
- Weather check: flash floods, extreme heat, fire risk
- Coordinate with K9 unit if dog tracking is planned
- Review court dates (ranger often primary witness in poaching prosecutions)
- Drone pre-flight check for aerial surveillance patrols
- Coordinate with neighbouring properties / private reserves for joint operations

### After Tasks
- Patrol debrief and incident report (same day -- chain of evidence requirements)
- Upload GPS tracks and waypoints to mapping system
- Service camera traps if visited during patrol
- Evidence handling: photograph, bag, label, and submit any illegal items found
- Vehicle maintenance log updated
- Firearms cleaning and inspection after any discharge
- Update wildlife sighting database
- Follow up on intelligence leads
- Prepare for court if prosecution underway
- Community feedback loop -- report outcomes of incidents to community liaison

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
- **Poaching incidents:** Golden hour for tracking after an incident is the first 2-4 hours -- delay = cold trail
- **Injured wildlife:** Veterinary intervention window varies by species and injury -- some are hours, not days
- **Fire weather:** Fire risk conditions (high temperature + low humidity + high wind) require pre-positioned fire response resources
- **Snare removal campaigns:** Should be timed for dry season when vegetation is low and visibility is highest
- **Wildlife census windows:** Many species have optimal survey windows (breeding season, dry season congregation at water)
- **Court dates:** Rangers must be available and prepared for court -- missed appearances can collapse cases
- **Equipment certification:** Firearm competency certificates, first responder/first aid renewals -- typically annual
- **Seasonal burning programs:** Prescribed burn windows are narrow -- too early is green, too late is dangerously dry

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- Armed poaching encounters -- genuine risk to life
- Finding injured or killed animals -- cumulative trauma
- Court cases collapsing due to evidentiary procedural errors
- Equipment failure in remote areas (radio, vehicle breakdown)
- Under-staffing -- too few rangers for too much ground
- Community hostility when livelihoods conflict with conservation
- Political pressure to allow mining or agriculture in protected areas
- Inadequate forensic/legal support to actually prosecute poachers
- Vicarious trauma and burnout

### Data Sources
- SMART (Spatial Monitoring and Reporting Tool) -- standard anti-poaching/patrol management
- EarthRanger -- real-time wildlife and ranger tracking
- Camera trap platforms: SMART Capture, Wildlife Insights (Google AI)
- GPS collar data: Vectronic, Telemetry Solutions
- Fire monitoring: MODIS / VIIRS (NASA) active fire maps, EFFIS (Europe)
- Weather: Windy.com, SAWS, Weather Underground
- Intelligence: community tip-off systems, informant management
- GIS: ArcGIS, QGIS -- spatial threat analysis
- SANParks / CapeNature systems (SA-specific)

### Proactive AI Opportunities
1. **"Poaching Pattern Alert"** -- "Camera trap data shows 3 triggered events in the northwestern sector between 02:00-04:00 over the last 5 nights with no wildlife ID. This pattern matches previous snaring activity. Recommend increasing night patrol coverage in that quadrant tonight."
2. **"Fire Risk Window"** -- "Tomorrow's conditions: temperature 38C, humidity 12%, south-easterly wind at 45 km/h. Fire danger index is EXTREME. The eastern buffer zone is 8 months since last burn and is high fuel load. Pre-position your fire response team?"
3. **"Collar Battery Alert"** -- "GPS collar on female elephant EA-07 hasn't transmitted in 51 hours. Last location was near the southern waterhole. Battery life for this collar model is 18-24 months -- she was collared 17 months ago. Recommend a priority locate flight."
4. **"Court Date Prep"** -- "You're a witness in the State v. Ndlovu poaching case. The trial date is in 10 days. The case number is CAS 45/2024. Do you have your patrol report and evidence photographs ready for the prosecutor?"
5. **"Community Meeting Due"** -- "Your scheduled monthly community liaison meeting with the Makuleke community is in 3 days. Based on last month's notes, the main agenda item was compensation for livestock predation by lions. Want me to pull the relevant incident records to prepare your briefing?"

## CROSS-CUTTING PATTERNS FOR PULSE

### Universal Triggers Across All Roles
These triggers appear in almost every role and represent high-confidence intervention points for Pulse:

| Trigger | Roles Affected | AI Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Upcoming deadline with no action logged** | All | "X is in N days and I don't see Y confirmed. Want me to" |
| **Weather forecast affecting a planned activity** | Outdoor roles (all) | Context-aware forecast + contingency prompt |
| **Invoice not sent after job completion** | All freelance/creative | "The [job] was completed 5 days ago. No invoice sent. Want me to generate one?" |
| **No testimonial requested after delivery** | All client-facing | "14-day review window is optimal. Want me to draft a request?" |
| **Equipment/certification renewal approaching** | All | Proactive calendar flag with action link |
| **Booking confirmation gap** | All | "Verbal/email confirmation exists but no contract/deposit. Want me to chase?" |
| **Seasonal preparation window closing** | Farmers, musicians, agents | "The window for X opens in N weeks -- based on last year, you should start now" |

### Data Integration Priority (Build Order for Pulse)
1. **Calendar** (Google/iCal) -- most universally connected; all roles live by it
2. **Email** (Gmail/Outlook) -- confirmation gap detection, unsigned contracts, ghost follow-ups
3. **Weather API** -- applies to 10+ roles with radically different risk profiles
4. **CRM / contact data** -- enables relationship-aware nudges ("you haven't spoken to X in 8 weeks")
5. **Invoicing/accounting** -- payment gap detection, cash flow warnings
6. **Industry-specific platforms** -- Rekordbox, SMART, John Deere, Honeybook etc. -- role-specific but high-value integrations

### Emotional Tone for Proactive Prompts
Across all roles, the most effective AI interventions share these qualities:
- **Specific, not generic** -- mention the actual client name, date, amount
- **Action-ready** -- offer to draft, schedule, or surface something immediately
- **Low friction** -- "want me to draft that email?" is one tap away from resolved
- **Non-alarmist but clear** -- "you might want to look at this" vs. "URGENT ACTION REQUIRED"
- **Contextually smart** -- know the difference between a deadline that can slip and one that can't
