## 1. PERSONAL TRAINER (PT)

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm

**Typical day structure:**
- **05:30-09:00** -- Early morning sessions (6am is peak PT hour globally). Back-to-back 1-hour or 45-min slots.
- **09:00-10:30** -- Admin gap: write session notes, review upcoming clients, order supplements/equipment, respond to enquiries.
- **12:00-14:00** -- Lunch block (smaller second wave, weight-loss clients, office workers on lunch break).
- **16:00-20:00** -- After-work surge. Busiest block. Often 4-6 sessions back to back.
- **20:00+** -- Programme writing, social media content, DM enquiries.

**Weekly rhythm:**
- Mon/Wed/Fri: highest demand (clients like structure)
- Sat morning: second busiest block (leisure clients)
- Sunday: dead or personal training only
- Programme review: typically weekly per client
- Admin day: usually Friday afternoon or Monday morning

**Income structure:** Typically package-based (5, 10, 20 sessions) or monthly retainer. Session rates vary R500-R1500 (SA), 40-100 (UK). Income spikes January, post-Easter, pre-summer. Drops December, school holidays.

---

### Before Tasks (pre-session)

- Review client file: last session notes, weight/measurements, injuries flagged
- Check if client is on a calorie deficit phase (adjust intensity)
- Confirm booking 24h in advance (WhatsApp or automated)
- Prepare programme card or app entry for the session
- Ensure gym floor/equipment is booked (if at a commercial gym)
- Check if client has any upcoming events (competition, holiday, wedding) that affect programming
- For new clients: intake form review, PAR-Q (health questionnaire), goal-setting notes

---

### After Tasks (post-session)

- Log session: exercises, sets, reps, weights, perceived effort
- Update client progress tracker (weight, measurements if applicable)
- Note any complaints or injuries mentioned
- Schedule next session (or remind client to book)
- Send follow-up message if it was a tough session or a milestone
- Review client's nutrition log if they're tracking (MyFitnessPal integration common)
- Flag programme progression: if client has been on same weights for 3 weeks, time to increase
- Order resistance bands/equipment if running low

---

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Timing | Action Needed |
|---|---|---|
| Session package expiry | 7 days before last session used | Renewal conversation |
| Client no-show streak | 2+ in a row | Check-in message |
| Client birthday | Day of | Personalised message, possible offer |
| Client event (wedding, race) | 4 weeks out | Peak programme phase |
| PT certification renewal | 60-90 days before expiry | Renew CPD/qual |
| First Aid cert | Annual renewal | Book course |
| January new enquiries | Dec 26 - Jan 7 | Prepare intake forms, schedule consultations |
| Client plateau (no progress) | 3-4 weeks same metrics | Programme redesign |
| Liability insurance renewal | Annual | Renewal |
| Client starting medication | As flagged | Adjust programme, note file |

---

### Stress/Anxiety Points

- **No-shows** -- Lost income, no notice. A 6am no-show at 5:45am is brutal.
- **Client ghosting** -- They stop booking, you don't know why. Awkward to chase.
- **Income gaps** -- Between packages, clients go on holiday without buying new pack.
- **Competing on price** -- Gym floor PTs undercutting rates. Discounting erodes value.
- **Overloading early mornings** -- Burnout from 5:30am starts + 8pm finishes.
- **Admin pile-up** -- Notes, programmes, invoices done on phone at the end of long days.
- **Certification juggling** -- Multiple certs (nutrition, specialisms, first aid) with different expiry dates.
- **Client injury liability** -- Anxiety when a client mentions a new pain.

---

### Data Sources

- **Booking:** Mindbody, Vagaro, Acuity Scheduling, SimplyBook, personal calendar
- **Client tracking:** TrueCoach, PT Distinction, My PT Hub, spreadsheets, paper
- **Nutrition:** MyFitnessPal (client shares logs), Cronometer
- **Payments:** Yoco (SA), Square, Stripe, bank EFT
- **Marketing:** Instagram, TikTok, Google Business Profile
- **Education/CPD:** REPSA, REPS UK, NASM, ACE portals

---

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"Emma hasn't rebooked since her session 3 weeks ago. Her 10-pack expires in 7 days. Want me to send her a check-in message?"**

> **"Marcus has hit the same weight on bench press for 4 sessions in a row. His programme is due for a progression review. Want me to flag it in his file?"**

> **"Your First Aid certificate expires in 34 days. The next HLTAID011 course near you runs March 28. Want me to book it?"**

> **"January starts in 11 days. You have 3 open consultation slots in the first week -- want me to post a 'New Year intake open' story to your Instagram?"**

> **"Jade's wedding is in 6 weeks. Based on her programme timeline, this week should be the start of her peak phase. Want me to update her programme card?"**

> **"You've had 4 no-shows this month from different clients. Your cancellation policy isn't on your booking page. Want me to draft one?"**

## 2. GYM OWNER / MANAGER

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm

**Morning (07:00-10:00):**
- Open checklist: equipment walk-through, cleanliness, staff sign-in, music/climate
- Review overnight bookings and cancellations
- Handle member access issues (key fob, app login)

**Midday (10:00-15:00):**
- Staff scheduling, payroll queries
- Supplier deliveries (protein, cleaning supplies, equipment parts)
- Member complaint resolution
- Class schedule management
- Social media posts/stories

**Evening (15:00-20:00):**
- Peak floor management -- highest foot traffic
- Staff supervision
- Equipment monitoring (is everything working?)
- Sales: convert walk-ins and tour requests

**Weekly:**
- Monday: review weekend report, plan the week
- Friday: payroll, revenue vs targets check
- Bi-weekly: equipment maintenance log review
- Monthly: membership churn report, marketing spend review

---

### Before Tasks

- Confirm class instructors confirmed for the day/week
- Check substitute cover for sick staff
- Review member renewals due this week
- Ensure cleaning schedule completed before doors open
- Equipment safety checks (cables, pulleys, cardio belt wear)
- Confirm group class booking numbers (cancel if under threshold)

---

### After Tasks

- Daily revenue reconciliation
- Member feedback review (Google reviews, NPS if collected)
- Log equipment issues flagged during the day
- Update staff schedule for next week
- Follow up on trial membership conversions
- Send renewal reminders to members lapsing in the next 7 days

---

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Timing | Action Needed |
|---|---|---|
| Equipment service due | Per manufacturer schedule | Book service |
| Membership renewal wave | Monthly, staggered | Automated reminder sequence |
| Instructor certification | Varies per class type | Renewal or replacement |
| Lease/rent due | Monthly | Payment or negotiation |
| January spike prep | November planning | Staffing, marketing, intro offers |
| Member milestone (1 year) | Anniversary date | Recognition message, loyalty reward |
| New competitor opening nearby | As flagged | Competitive response |
| Group class underperformance | Rolling 4 weeks | Replace or restructure class |
| Liability insurance renewal | Annual | Renewal |
| Health & Safety inspection | Annual or triggered | Prep audit |

---

### Stress/Anxiety Points

- **Member churn** -- High-cost acquisition, high-churn rate (especially January joiners by March)
- **Staff reliability** -- PT no-shows leave clients stranded
- **Equipment breakdowns** -- Treadmill down during peak = member complaints
- **Cash flow** -- High fixed costs (rent, staff) vs variable membership income
- **Google reviews** -- One bad review tanks conversions
- **Competing with big chain gyms** on price and facilities

---

### Data Sources

- **Gym management:** Mindbody, Glofox, PushPress, Gymmaster
- **POS:** Square, Yoco, integrated CRM systems
- **Accounting:** Xero, QuickBooks
- **Marketing:** Mailchimp, Instagram, Facebook Ads
- **Equipment:** Supplier portals (Life Fitness, Technogym, etc.)

---

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"Treadmill 3 is due for its 6-month belt inspection. Last service was September 14. Want me to schedule it with your equipment provider?"**

> **"You have 23 memberships lapsing in the next 14 days. 8 of them haven't visited in 30+ days. Want me to draft a win-back email for the at-risk group?"**

> **"Your Saturday 8am HIIT class has averaged 4 bookings over the last 6 weeks against a break-even of 8. Want me to flag it for review or draft a promotion?"**

## 3. SPORTS COACH (TEAM & INDIVIDUAL)

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm

**Team Coach (e.g., football, rugby, netball):**
- Match days: match prep starts 3 days out (tactics, team selection, video analysis)
- Training days: session planning, warm-up structure, drill design, cool-down
- Off-days: video review, player welfare check-ins, scout reports
- Admin: player records, incident reports, governing body paperwork

**Individual Coach (e.g., swim coach, tennis coach, athletics):**
- Session slots: typically 06:00-08:00, 15:00-18:00 (around school/work)
- Programme planning: weekly/monthly periodisation plans per athlete
- Competition prep: peak phases aligned to competition calendar
- Communication: parent liaison (youth athletes), athlete WhatsApp

---

### Before Tasks

- Review last session notes and athlete/team performance data
- Plan session drills and structure (with timing)
- Check athlete injury or availability status
- Confirm venue booking
- Prepare equipment (cones, balls, timing gates, resistance bands)
- For competition week: confirm travel, accommodation, entry lists, warm-up slots

---

### After Tasks

- Log session: what was covered, athlete performance, errors noted
- Update athlete development plan
- Send session summary to parents (youth sport)
- Review video footage if captured
- Identify athletes needing individual attention
- Reconfirm next session
- Report injuries to physio or club medical staff

---

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Timing | Action Needed |
|---|---|---|
| Competition entry deadlines | 2-6 weeks before event | Submit entries, confirm athletes |
| Athlete peak phase | 3-4 weeks before major competition | Increase intensity, reduce volume |
| Coaching accreditation renewal | Annual/biennial | Book refresher course |
| First Aid/CPR | Annual | Renew cert |
| Player contract review (professional) | Per season | Negotiate or release |
| Registration deadlines | Per governing body | Submit team registrations |
| Injury return-to-play | Per athlete timeline | Progressive reintroduction protocol |
| Pre-season (team sports) | 6-8 weeks before season | Intensified training block |
| Athlete burnout signals | Ongoing monitoring | Rest week, mental health check |

---

### Stress/Anxiety Points

- **Selection decisions** -- Dropping a player, managing fallout from parents
- **Governing body compliance** -- Paperwork, safeguarding certifications, insurance
- **Athlete retention** -- Youth athletes quitting, losing good players to rival clubs
- **Parent management** -- Overinvolved parents in youth sport
- **Results pressure** -- Win/loss record tied to job security at club level
- **Funding** -- Grant applications, equipment budgets, facility hire costs

---

### Data Sources

- **Performance:** Catapult, Statsports (GPS vests), Dartfish (video analysis)
- **Scheduling:** TeamSnap, Spond, Google Calendar
- **Athlete management:** Athlete management systems (AMS), spreadsheets
- **Governing body:** National federation portals (Athletics SA, SA Football Association)
- **Communication:** WhatsApp groups, TeamApp

---

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"The provincial championships are 28 days away. Liam's programme should enter peak phase this week. Want me to update his training block?"**

> **"Your ECF coaching licence expires in 47 days. There's a renewal course running April 5 in Cape Town. Want me to register you?"**

> **"Entry deadline for the SA Aquatics Short Course Championships is Friday. You have 4 athletes who qualify based on their PBs. Want me to prepare the entry list?"**

## 4. NUTRITIONIST / DIETITIAN

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm

- **Consultations:** 45-60 min per client. Typically 6-8 clients/day for private practice.
- **Clinical (dietitian):** Hospital rounds, case reviews, MDT meetings
- **Private practice:** Mostly appointment-based, some walk-ins
- **Admin:** Meal plan writing, client progress review, referral letters
- **Marketing:** Instagram content (meal ideas, myth-busting), blog writing
- **CPD:** Ongoing education mandatory for HPCSA/BDA registration

---

### Before Tasks

- Review client file: dietary recall, health goals, lab results if applicable
- Check client's food diary/app logs (Cronometer, MyFitnessPal)
- Prepare personalised meal plan updates
- Review any GP/specialist referral notes
- Confirm appointment (reminder SMS/WhatsApp)

---

### After Tasks

- Write consultation notes
- Update meal plan if changes made
- Send meal plan PDF to client
- Log client metrics (weight, waist, energy levels, symptoms)
- Set follow-up reminder (2-4 weeks typically)
- Refer to GP or specialist if flags raised (blood sugar, eating disorder signs)
- Bill client or submit medical aid claim

---

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Timing | Action Needed |
|---|---|---|
| HPCSA registration renewal | Annual (SA) | Renew and submit CPD log |
| Client lab results due | Per client | Follow up with client or GP |
| Client programme end | Per contract | Book review and renewal |
| Seasonal nutrition trends | Jan (detox season), Sept (spring body prep) | Relevant content marketing |
| Medical aid pre-auth expiry | Per scheme | Renew authorisation |
| Client medication change | As flagged | Update dietary plan |
| Client pregnancy | As flagged | Transition to prenatal protocol |

---

### Stress/Anxiety Points

- **Scope of practice disputes** -- Nutritionist vs. Dietitian legal distinctions
- **Medical aid reimbursement** -- Claim rejections, pre-auth delays
- **Client compliance** -- Clients not following plans, blaming the plan
- **Eating disorder clients** -- High emotional and clinical load
- **Misinformation** -- Instagram "nutrition coaches" undermining evidence-based advice
- **Client cancellations** -- Hard to fill 45-min slots on short notice

---

### Data Sources

- **Client management:** Practice Better, SimplePractice, Healthie
- **Food tracking:** MyFitnessPal, Cronometer, Nutritics
- **Lab results:** Client-provided or integrated with GP systems
- **Medical aid:** Discovery, Momentum, Bonitas portals (SA)
- **Marketing:** Instagram, Pinterest, newsletter platforms

---

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"Sarah's 8-week programme ends this Friday. Her last weigh-in showed 3.2kg lost. She hasn't booked a follow-up. Want me to send a results summary and invite her to continue?"**

> **"Your HPCSA registration renewal is due in 42 days. You need 30 CPD points -- you currently have 22 logged. Want me to find an upcoming webinar to close the gap?"**

## 5. YOGA / PILATES INSTRUCTOR

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm

- **Morning classes:** 06:00-08:30 (highest demand -- before-work crowd)
- **Midday:** Smaller classes, private 1:1 sessions
- **Evening:** 17:30-20:00 second peak (after-work)
- **Saturday morning:** Premium demand slot
- **Admin:** Class prep, playlist curation, marketing, enquiries
- **CPD:** Workshops, trainings, retreats -- ongoing education central to identity

**Class sizes:** Group: 6-20 people. Private: 1-3.
**Income split:** Group classes (lower per-head, higher volume) + private sessions (higher rate) + workshops/retreats (seasonal, high-value)

---

### Before Tasks

- Review class bookings (who's coming, any new students, injuries flagged)
- Prepare sequence: theme, peak pose, modifications for any injuries in the room
- Prepare music playlist
- Set up studio: mats, blocks, straps, bolsters
- For private sessions: review client goals and last session notes
- Send booking confirmation/class reminder

---

### After Tasks

- Tidy studio, reset equipment
- Note any students who struggled or mentioned injuries
- Follow up with new students (welcome message)
- Update class attendance records
- Chase unpaid drop-in payments
- Post class (Instagram Story): "Thanks for joining this morning's flow "
- Update workshop/retreat planning if relevant

---

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Timing | Action Needed |
|---|---|---|

### Stress/Anxiety Points

## 7. HAIRDRESSER / HAIR STYLIST

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm

- **Chair time:** 09:00-18:00 typical, with evening appointments common
- **Appointment types:** Cut (30-60 min), colour (2-4 hours), blow-dry (45 min), treatment (30-60 min)
- **Back-to-back pressure:** No buffer between appointments = no bathroom breaks
- **Admin:** Booking management, stock ordering, DM responses between clients
- **Creative work:** Portfolio shoots, education, trend research (Instagram, Pinterest)
- **Busiest periods:** Pre-Christmas, wedding season (Sept-Nov in SA, May-Aug in UK), Easter

---

### Before Tasks

- Review appointment list: what each client is having done
- Pull client file: last colour formula, processing times, any scalp sensitivities
- Ensure colour mixed and ready (colour processing clients first)
- Check stock of specific products (running low on a colour = crisis mid-appointment)
- Confirm appointments for the next day (no-show risk management)

---

### After Tasks

- Record colour formula used (exact developer, timing, outcome)
- Note client's reaction, what they liked/disliked
- Recommend retail products purchased (or not)
- Rebook client before they leave (6-8 weeks for colour, 4-6 for cuts)
- Update client profile with hair condition notes
- Order products if stock is low after the day's services
- Post before/after to Instagram (with permission)

---

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Timing | Action Needed |
|---|---|---|
| Client rebook window | 6-8 weeks post-colour | Rebooking reminder |
| Client birthday | Day of | Personalised message, possibly discount |
| Product stock low | Based on usage rate | Reorder before stockout |
| Wedding client prep | 6 months out | Book trial, confirm date, plan |
| Seasonal surge (Dec, Easter) | 6 weeks out | Fill calendar, hire temp staff |
| Hairdressing licence renewal | Per jurisdiction | Renew |
| NVQ/SAQA CPD | Annual | Log hours |
| Competitor price change | As flagged | Review own pricing |
| Client allergy test | 48h before colour appointment | Confirm test done |

---

### Stress/Anxiety Points

- **No-shows** -- 2-3 hour colour slot no-show is devastating income loss
- **Last-minute cancellations** -- Can't fill a 3-hour slot with 1 hour notice
- **Product cost inflation** -- Colour, treatments getting more expensive; clients resist price increases
- **Standing all day** -- Physical toll: back, feet, varicose veins
- **Difficult colour corrections** -- Colour gone wrong from another salon = hours of remedial work
- **Client price sensitivity** -- "Can you do it cheaper?"

---

### Data Sources

- **Booking:** Fresha (popular in SA/UK), Treatwell, Vagaro, Mindbody, Square Appointments
- **Client notes:** Fresha built-in, paper cards, spreadsheet
- **Retail/orders:** Supplier portals (L'Oreal Professionnel, Wella, Goldwell)
- **Social:** Instagram (primary portfolio platform)
- **Payments:** Yoco, Square, Zapper (SA)

---

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"Lisa is 7 weeks post-colour and hasn't rebooked. Her roots grow fast -- she'll be uncomfortable soon. Want me to send a 'time for a refresh?' message?"**

## 8. BEAUTICIAN / ESTHETICIAN

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm

- **Appointment-heavy:** Treatments range from 30-min express facials to 2-hour luxury treatments
- **Common services:** Facials, waxing, lash extensions, lash lifts, brow shaping, body treatments
- **Rhythm:** Often 5-8 clients/day, with 10-15 min gaps for room reset
- **Admin:** Product ordering, marketing posts, rebooking
- **Training:** Regular brand training (Dermalogica, ELEMIS, etc.) to maintain brand status

---

### Before Tasks

- Review client file: skin concerns, allergies, last treatment and products used
- Room setup: fresh towels, heated bed, products laid out
- Patch test confirmation (lash extensions, tints, chemical peels)
- Confirm bookings for next day
- For new clients: intake form review

---

### After Tasks

- Write treatment notes: products used, skin response, observations
- Recommend homecare products (retail is significant income stream)
- Rebook client (facials: 4-6 weeks; waxing: 4-6 weeks; lash fills: 2-3 weeks)
- Update client skin progression record (photo comparison useful)
- Laundry/linens prep for next day
- Stock check on disposables (strips, lash glue, etc.)

---

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Trigger | Timing | Action Needed |
|---|---|---|
| Lash fill window | 2-3 weeks | Rebooking reminder |
| Waxing return window | 4-6 weeks | Reminder |
| Facial programme end | Per programme | Review and renew |
| Patch test requirement | 48h before new treatment | Confirm completion |
| Product expiry | Rolling | Dispose and reorder |
| Skincare training/certification | Per brand | Book and attend |
| Seasonal promotions | Pre-summer, pre-holiday, Jan | Launch offer |
| Client birthday | Day of | Message + gift voucher offer |

---

### Stress/Anxiety Points

- **Lash extension liability** -- Allergic reactions, lash damage claims
- **No-shows on premium slots** -- 2-hour facial slot empty = significant loss
- **Retail pressure** -- Expected to upsell products but feels pushy
- **Physical toll** -- Eye strain from precision work (lashes, brows)
- **Keeping up with trends** -- New treatments constantly (Russian lashes, etc.)
- **Client skin reactions** -- Managing expectations when skin purges post-facial

---

### Data Sources

- **Booking:** Fresha, Treatwell, Vagaro, Timely
- **Client records:** Fresha client cards, paper intake forms
- **Retail:** Brand supplier portals (Dermalogica, ELEMIS, Nimue)
- **Social:** Instagram, TikTok (before/after content)

## The "How Did It Know?" Framework for Pulse

### Category 1: Lapsing Clients
Pattern: [Role-specific rebooking window] + [No new booking in system] -> Trigger reminder
**The magic:** Pulse knows their rebooking patterns better than they do.

### Category 2: Expiring Assets
Pattern: [Certification/package/stock] + [days remaining below threshold] -> Alert
**The magic:** Pulse remembers the things that fall through the cracks.

### Category 3: Seasonal Intelligence
Pattern: [Calendar awareness] + [historical booking data] + [lead time required] -> Prep prompt
**The magic:** Pulse thinks ahead so they don't have to.

### Category 4: Anomaly Detection
Pattern: [Client's normal behaviour] + [deviation from pattern] -> Flag
**The magic:** Pulse notices what humans miss in the noise of a busy day.

### Category 5: Workflow Completion
Pattern: [Action taken] + [follow-up action not completed] -> Nudge
**The magic:** Pulse closes the loop automatically (post a photo, send an invoice, request a review).
