## 1. General Contractors / Builders

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
The GC role is fundamentally one of orchestration. A typical day starts at 5:30-6:00 AM -- checking overnight messages from subbies, reviewing tomorrow's weather, and mentally sequencing the day before anyone else is awake.

**Morning (6-8 AM):** Site walkthrough before trades arrive. Confirming who's coming, what they need, what might block them. Any materials that didn't arrive yesterday get chased now. Quick calls to subcontractors.

**On-site (8 AM-3 PM):** Supervising and problem-solving. A GC on a live build typically manages 3-8 subcontractors simultaneously -- plumber in the wet areas, electrician roughing in, carpenter framing, concreters pouring a slab. Conflict between trades (electrician needs the wall open that the tiler just tiled) is a daily reality. The GC referees it.

**Admin (3-6 PM):** Quote prep, invoice review, variation orders, supplier calls, schedule updates. Many GCs work a second shift at the kitchen table after dinner -- the paperwork doesn't stop.

**Weekly rhythm:**
- Monday: Confirm the week's trades schedule, materials confirmed on-site
- Wednesday: Progress photos to clients, variation discussions
- Friday: Subcontractor invoices received and reviewed, site clean-up, weekend security check
- Ongoing: Quote pipeline -- most GCs have 5-15 quotes outstanding at any time

**Project scale reality:** A residential builder might run 2-4 active sites simultaneously, at different stages (slab pour, frame up, lockup, fitout). Each has its own rhythm, and the GC context-switches constantly.

### Before Tasks (Pre-Job)
- **Council/planning permits** -- DA (Development Application) or CDC (Complying Development Certificate) must be approved before breaking ground. Can take 3 weeks to 6 months depending on complexity. GC often manages this on behalf of the client.
- **Building certification** -- Certifier appointed, inspections scheduled at key stages (slab, frame, waterproofing, final). Each inspection must be booked days in advance.
- **Site setup** -- Hoarding, skip bin, temporary toilet, site power (temporary power board), concrete washout area
- **Subcontractor scheduling** -- Confirming subbies are available and have the job in their diary; getting their current insurance certificates
- **Material procurement** -- Structural timber, concrete, windows and doors (these are the long lead items -- windows often 4-8 weeks). Order too late = site delay.
- **Client pre-start meeting** -- Sign-off on drawings, selections, contract
- **Contract execution** -- Builder's contract (HIA or MBA standard forms typically), deposit invoice raised
- **Insurance** -- Contract works insurance (covers the build), public liability, check owner's home insurance is paused or updated
- **Site access** -- Keys, gate codes, neighbour introductions if required, notify council of commencement

### After Tasks (Post-Job)
- **Practical Completion inspection** -- Walk-through with client, defects list (called a "defects schedule" or "punchlist") generated
- **Defects rectification** -- Usually 3 months to 12 months defects liability period; return visits for minor items
- **Occupation Certificate** -- Final council/certifier sign-off; client can't legally move in without this
- **Final invoice / progress claim** -- Retention release (often 5% held until defects period ends)
- **Handover documentation** -- Warranties for appliances, windows, waterproofing, structural; operation manuals; as-built drawings
- **Subcontractor final payments** -- Ensuring subbies are paid out; getting their final compliance documents
- **Tax invoice and accounting** -- GST reconciliation, project cost vs. budget analysis
- **Photo documentation** -- Before/during/after photos for portfolio, insurance records, and future dispute evidence
- **Warranty tracking** -- Structural warranty (commonly 6-10 years in AU/NZ), waterproofing (typically 7 years), workmanship (1-2 years)

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
| Trigger | Typical Timeframe | Consequence if Missed |
|---|---|---|
| Builder's licence renewal | Annual/biennial | Can't legally contract work |
| Public liability insurance | Annual | Void for any claim; subbies won't work |
| Contract works insurance | Per project | Uninsured loss if site damaged |
| Inspection bookings (slab, frame, etc.) | 48-72 hrs notice required | Build stops; can't proceed past stage |
| Payment claims (SOPA / Security of Payment) | Strict statutory deadlines | Lose right to claim |
| Retention release | End of defects period | Cash stuck in client's hands |
| Window/door orders | 4-8 weeks lead time | Frame sits idle waiting for windows |
| Concrete pour weather | Day-of decision | Bad weather pour = structural risk |
| Subbies' licence/insurance | Their renewal dates | GC is liable if subbie isn't covered |
| Seasonal demand | Sep-Feb (AU summer) peak | Subbies fully booked; price goes up |

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- **Cash flow** -- Progress claims tied to stages; if certifier delays sign-off, the GC doesn't get paid. Projects running 15-20% over budget on materials is common.
- **Variations** -- Clients change their minds; if not documented as a variation order with price, the GC eats the cost.
- **Subcontractor reliability** -- Subbies no-showing or running over on another job creates cascading delays.
- **Material delays** -- Supply chain issues (post-2020 reality) mean timber, windows, steel can arrive weeks late.
- **Inspection failures** -- A failed frame inspection means re-work and a rebook. Time and money.
- **Defects disputes** -- Client withholds retention claiming defects that aren't defects.
- **Quoting accuracy** -- Underquoting on a fixed-price contract means absorbing losses. GCs frequently quote 10+ jobs to win 2-3.
- **Multi-site mental load** -- Holding 3+ sites in your head simultaneously, knowing exactly what stage each is at.

### Data Sources
- **Job management:** Buildxact, CoConstruct, Procore (larger builds), Builder Trend, ServiceM8 (smaller operators)
- **Accounting:** Xero, MYOB, QuickBooks
- **Estimating:** Buildxact, Cordell, CostX, or plain Excel
- **Scheduling:** Microsoft Project, Smartsheet, or whiteboard
- **Supplier portals:** Boral, CSR, BlueScope, Bunnings Trade
- **Industry:** HIA (Housing Industry Association), MBA (Master Builders Association)
- **Regulatory:** PlanningPortal (AU), council websites, certifier portals
- **Communication:** WhatsApp (most common on-site), email, phone

## 2. Electricians

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
Electricians split into two broad camps: **service/maintenance** (fault-finding, small jobs, emergency callouts) and **project/installation** (new builds, renovations). Each has a distinct rhythm.

**Service electrician day:**
- 6:30 AM: Check job list for the day (ServiceM8, Tradify, or a text from the boss)
- 7:00 AM: First job -- often a morning callout (no power, faulty board). Load van with commonly needed materials (cable, breakers, GPOs, junction boxes).
- Travel between 4-8 jobs per day. Jobs last 30 min to 3 hours. Lots of windshield time.
- Between jobs: quote jobs on-site, submit completed job cards, photo documentation
- 5 PM: Return to depot or home, restock van, handle paperwork

**Installation electrician day:**
- 6:45 AM: Arrive on site. Roughing-in on a new build can be days of cable-pulling, conduit, and board installation before anything is energised.
- Site-based, 8-10 hour days on larger projects
- End of day: job diary, material orders for tomorrow, report to GC

**Weekly:** Quotes go out Monday/Tuesday, materials ordered Wednesday for Friday delivery, invoices sent Friday for jobs completed that week.

### Before Tasks
- **Licensing verification** -- Check own licence is current; for larger jobs confirm workers' licences are valid
- **Permit/Notice of Electrical Work (NEW)** -- In most AU states, a form must be lodged with the network provider for certain work (new circuits, boards, etc.)
- **Scope review** -- Read the electrical drawings/schedule; identify if any DBs, metering, or solar/battery involvement requires additional sign-offs
- **Materials list and order** -- Cable schedule, board specs, fittings. Electrical wholesalers (Rexel, Electromax, NHP) usually deliver next-day but need order by 3 PM prior day.
- **Load calculation** -- For new boards or significant load additions, calculating demand before sizing cables and breakers
- **Safe Work Method Statement (SWMS)** -- Required for high-risk work (working near live conductors, in roof spaces during extreme heat, working at height)
- **Client confirmation** -- Confirm access and that any asbestos-containing materials (common in pre-1990 buildings) have been identified

### After Tasks
- **Testing and tagging** -- After installation, all circuits must be tested (insulation resistance, polarity, earth continuity). Results recorded.
- **Certificate of Electrical Safety (CES)** -- Mandatory in most AU states; lodged with energy distributor within 7 days of completion
- **Notice of Electrical Work (NEW)** -- Filed with distributor; inspector may audit
- **Job sign-off** -- Client signs the job card; photos of completed board and cable routes taken
- **Invoice** -- Same-day or next-day invoicing. Electricians who delay invoicing notoriously hurt their cash flow.
- **Warranty documentation** -- Labour warranty typically 12 months; product warranties passed through
- **Van restock** -- Replace consumed materials; update van inventory
- **Follow-up quotes** -- "While I'm here" upsell opportunities documented (switchboard upgrade, EV charger, smoke alarms)

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
| Trigger | Timeframe | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Electrical licence renewal | Annual (AU) | Illegal to work; voided insurance |
| Certificate of Electrical Safety (CES) lodgement | 7 days post-completion | Fine; compliance failure |
| Public liability insurance | Annual | Uninsured exposure |
| Test & Tag on own tools/equipment | 3-12 months (varies by environment) | Prohibited equipment; WHS non-compliance |
| RCD testing (own business) | Every 3 months in some states | Non-compliance |
| Energy distributor connection bookings | 5-10 business day lead time | New build sits without power |
| Solar/battery system accreditation | Annual (CEC accreditation) | Can't legally install solar |
| Seasonal: summer AC installs | Oct-Dec | Fully booked; material shortages |
| Apprentice supervision ratios | Ongoing | Training contract at risk |

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- **On-call/after-hours callouts** -- Loss of power is an emergency; clients expect fast response, often 10 PM on a Sunday
- **Hidden work in old buildings** -- Open a wall in a 1960s house and find knob-and-tube wiring, asbestos, or illegal previous work. Scope blows out.
- **Material not on van** -- Driving back to the wholesaler or depot costs billable time. Getting the van kit right is a constant optimisation.
- **Compliance paperwork timing** -- CES must be lodged within 7 days; easy to forget on busy weeks
- **Liability for others' work** -- If a client asks you to connect to existing wiring and that wiring has a fault, liability questions arise
- **Quoting unseen work** -- "Just replace the switchboard" can turn into a full re-wire when the board is opened
- **Apprentice management** -- Supervision requirements, training logs, TAFE coordination

### Data Sources
- **Job management:** ServiceM8, Tradify, Simpro, AroFlo
- **Accounting:** Xero, MYOB
- **Licensing:** Energy Safe Victoria, QBCC, NSW Fair Trading (state-specific portals)
- **Material suppliers:** Rexel, Electromax, NHP, MIDDY'S -- most have online ordering/account portals
- **Industry body:** NECA (National Electrical and Communications Association)
- **Solar accreditation:** CEC (Clean Energy Council) portal
- **Standards:** AS/NZS 3000 (Wiring Rules) -- updated periodically

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"Your electrical contractor licence (Vic) expires in 34 days. Here's the Energy Safe Victoria renewal link. Last year you needed your CPD log -- you've completed 6 of the required 8 hours. Here are two online courses that count toward the remaining 2."**

> **"The CES for the Thornbury job (completed Monday) hasn't been lodged yet. Today is day 6 -- it's due tomorrow. Want me to pre-fill the form from the job details and send it to you to sign and submit?"**

> **"Your test & tag certificate for the 240V power tools expires next month. Bright Safety did last year's at $180 -- want me to book them again?"**

> **"The distributor connection booking for 44 River St is scheduled for Friday. It's now Wednesday and you haven't received confirmation. Distributors often cancel with 24 hours notice. Want me to call them to verify, and flag the GC if there's a risk?"**

Want me to add a site survey note to your quote template as a reminder to check metering before finalising price?"**

## 3. Plumbers

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
Plumbing splits into **residential service** (blocked drains, leaks, hot water), **new construction** (roughing-in, fitout), and **commercial/industrial**. Service plumbers live reactively; installation plumbers operate more predictably.

**Service plumber day:**
- 6:30 AM: First callout often booked previous evening. Van stocked with most-used items (flexi hoses, o-rings, push-fit fittings, hot water anodes, cameras for drain inspection).
- 4-6 jobs per day depending on complexity. Emergency calls jump the queue.
- Drain camera is a revenue-generator -- visual evidence sells the job.
- End of day: job cards, invoicing, quoting for larger work found during service calls.

**Construction plumber day:**
- Site-based. New build residential has distinct phases: slab penetrations (before concrete pours), rough-in (after frame, before plasterboard), and fitout (after walls are closed).
- Each phase has a hard handover with the certifier/inspector.

**Weekly rhythm:** Quote Monday/Tuesday, lodge any permit applications mid-week, follow up outstanding invoices Friday.

### Before Tasks
- **Plumbing/drainage permits** -- Most states require a plumbing permit for new work; submitted to the local water authority or building certifier
- **AS/NZS 3500 compliance check** -- Review drawings against the plumbing code
- **Product selection/approval** -- All fixtures must be WaterMark certified; verify products before ordering
- **Material orders** -- Pipe, fittings, hot water units (often 3-5 day lead for specific sizes), tapware
- **Site access and timing** -- Coordinate with GC; slab penetrations must happen before the pour (hard deadline)
- **Asbestos/hazmat check** -- Pre-1990 buildings; cement sheeting around pipes may be asbestos
- **Trade scheduling coordination** -- Plumbing rough-in must precede plasterboard; hot water must be installed before gas or electrical connects

### After Tasks
- **Certificate of Compliance -- Plumbing (CCP)** -- Mandatory in most states, lodged with water authority within set timeframe
- **Pressure testing** -- Water supply and gas lines must be pressure tested; results recorded
- **Drain inspection** -- CCTV footage of new drainage before backfill in some states
- **Client sign-off and handover** -- Demonstrate operation of hot water, show shut-off valve locations
- **Invoice** -- Often stage-based on large jobs; final invoice at fitout completion
- **Warranty documentation** -- Hot water system warranties (typically 5-10 year tank warranty, 1 year labour), flexi hose warranties
- **Job photos** -- Before/after, particularly for drainage work that will be buried
- **Maintenance reminders set** -- Hot water anode replacement every 5 years; tempering valve check annually

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
| Trigger | Timeframe | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Plumbing licence renewal | Annual/biennial (state) | Illegal to practise |
| Certificate of Compliance lodgement | Within days of completion | Compliance failure; inspector audit |
| Slab penetration window | Before concrete pour | Can't be retrospectively added |
| Hot water unit order | 3-5 days for standard; weeks for solar/heat pump | Client without hot water |
| Flexi hose replacement reminder | 10-year max life (industry standard) | Flexi hose failure = significant water damage |
| Gas compliance certificate | Per installation | Gas retailer won't connect without it |
| Backflow prevention device testing | Annual (commercial/certain residential) | Council compliance failure |
| Seasonal: hot water demand | Winter | Hot water callouts spike dramatically |
| Water authority inspection bookings | 3-7 days notice | Cannot cover drain or proceed |

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- **Hidden leaks after closing walls** -- A minor leak concealed in a wall causes major damage over months; liability risk
- **Hot water unit availability** -- Wrong size ordered, right size out of stock; client without hot water is an emergency
- **Material compatibility in old buildings** -- Mixing copper and galvanised steel without dielectric unions causes electrolytic corrosion
- **Blocked drain emergencies** -- Clients expect same-day response for sewage backups; being unavailable = losing the job and the client
- **Council/water authority audit** -- Unlodged certificates catch up; retrospective compliance is expensive
- **Flexi hose liability** -- "Plumber installed this 12 years ago" -- industry-wide awareness issue
- **Gas work crossover** -- Plumbers in some states can do gas; scope creep into electrical through hot water systems creates compliance complexity

### Data Sources
- **Job management:** ServiceM8, Tradify, Simpro, AroFlo
- **Permits/compliance:** Local water authority portals (Sydney Water, Melbourne Water, SA Water, etc.), state plumbing regulators
- **Standards:** AS/NZS 3500 (Plumbing and Drainage)
- **Suppliers:** Reece, Tradelink, Actrol (refrigeration), Rinnai/Rheem/Dux/Bosch (HWS portals)
- **WaterMark database:** ABCB WaterMark certification search
- **Industry:** Master Plumbers Australia, MPAQ (QLD), PICAC (training)

### Proactive AI Opportunities

> **"You installed a flexi hose at 31 Acacia Drive 9 years ago -- industry guidelines recommend replacement at 10 years. Want me to schedule a maintenance check reminder for the client in 8 months, or reach out now to offer a proactive inspection?"**

> **"The Certificate of Compliance for the Morrison job hasn't been lodged. It's been 6 days -- most states require lodgement within 5 business days. Want me to pre-fill the form?"**

> **"You have a slab pour booked for the Henderson build on Friday. Your penetrations are scheduled for Thursday -- that's cutting it to a single day. The GC mentioned the pour might move to Thursday. Want me to check with them now so you can reschedule if needed?"**

> **"Reece has a 3-day backorder on the 315L Rheem units -- you have two jobs next week needing hot water replacement. The Rinnai equivalent is in stock. Want me to flag this to you before you confirm the jobs?"**

> **"Three of your commercial clients are due for annual backflow prevention device testing this month. I can send them a reminder email with your booking link -- want me to draft those?"**

## 4. HVAC Technicians

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning) splits into **installation** and **service/maintenance**. Service techs live on maintenance contracts and breakdowns; installers work on new builds and fit-outs.

**Service tech day:**
- Maintenance contracts are the bread and butter -- predictable, scheduled, often quarterly or annual filter cleans, system checks, report generation
- Breakdown calls interrupt the schedule. Summer heatwaves trigger a wave of AC failures simultaneously.
- Commercial HVAC service involves plant room visits, BEMS (Building Energy Management System) access, large rooftop units
- Documentation-heavy: service reports, refrigerant logs (regulated), gas handling records

**Installation day:**
- New split system install: 1-2 systems per tech per day if all goes smoothly
- Commercial fitout: multi-week projects, coordinating with builder, electrician, hydraulic engineer
- Ducted system install in new home: 1-2 days plus commissioning

**Weekly:** Monday plan the maintenance schedule, follow up outstanding service reports from previous week, mid-week materials order.

### Before Tasks
- **ARC (Australian Refrigeration Council) licence check** -- Must hold ARC ARCTICK licence to handle refrigerants. Renewal is annual.
- **Refrigerant procurement/transfer record** -- Refrigerant movement is tightly regulated; must be logged
- **Equipment selection and compatibility** -- HVAC equipment has complex compatibility requirements (indoor/outdoor unit matching, refrigerant type R410A vs R32 vs R134a)
- **Electrical coordination** -- New install needs dedicated circuit; coordinate with electrician
- **Structural check** -- Wall penetrations, roof penetrations, outdoor unit placement (weight-bearing, clearances)
- **Equipment order** -- Long lead times for commercial units (4-12 weeks); residential splits usually 1 week
- **SWMS for roof/elevated work** -- Falls from height is the primary risk in HVAC
- **Council approvals for commercial** -- Some installations require DA/building approval

### After Tasks
- **Refrigerant handling log** -- Every gramme of refrigerant used, recovered, or disposed of must be logged (Environmental Protection of the Environment Operations Act)
- **ARC compliance certificate** -- Issued for refrigerant work
- **Commissioning report** -- System tested to spec: temperatures, airflow, pressures, electrical draw documented
- **Client handover and training** -- Show client how to operate, change filters, set schedules
- **Service report** -- For maintenance visits: findings, work done, recommendations
- **Invoice** -- Especially for maintenance contracts: automated/recurring invoicing
- **Warranty registration** -- Daikin, Mitsubishi, Fujitsu all have manufacturer warranty registration portals; missed registration = no warranty
- **Follow-up maintenance scheduling** -- Book next service before leaving site (ideally)

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
| Trigger | Timeframe | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| ARC ARCTICK licence renewal | Annual | Illegal to handle refrigerants |
| Refrigerant handling records | Ongoing (per transaction) | Heavy fines; EPA investigation |
| Maintenance contract schedules | Quarterly/annual per contract | SLA breach; losing contracts |
| Equipment warranty registration | Usually within 30 days of install | Warranty void |
| Commercial unit orders | 4-12 weeks | Build program impacts |
| Summer peak demand | Nov-Feb (AU) | Fully booked; can't respond to breakdowns |
| R22 phase-out compliance | Ongoing (R22 is banned; no new supply) | Illegal refrigerant handling |
| Filter replacement reminders (client) | Quarterly-annually | System efficiency/warranty |
| OH&S confined space cert | 2 yearly | Cannot work in plant rooms |

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- **Summer heatwave surge** -- Every phone ringing at once; impossible to service all clients same-day. Long-term clients vs. new callers prioritisation is stressful.
- **Refrigerant log compliance** -- Easy to fall behind; retroactively reconstructing logs is time-consuming and legally risky
- **Equipment availability** -- Specific commercial units on long backorder; job can't complete; client angry
- **Warranty registration amnesia** -- Installing 3-4 systems a week and forgetting to register warranties is common; when a unit fails 2 years later, the client is furious
- **Incorrect diagnosis on commercial** -- A misdiagnosed commercial unit fault = expensive parts ordered incorrectly, multiple site visits, unhappy facilities manager
- **Gas handling fines** -- EPA enforcement on refrigerant handling is real and increasing

### Data Sources
- **Job management:** Simpro, AroFlo, ServiceM8, FieldAware
- **ARC portal:** arctick.org -- licence, refrigerant movement logging
- **Accounting:** Xero, MYOB
- **Manufacturer portals:** Daikin Partner portal, Mitsubishi Electric Trade portal (warranty registration, technical docs, parts ordering)
- **Parts suppliers:** HVAC industry distributors (Air Factory, Beijer Ref, Temperature Technology)
- **Standards:** AS/NZS 1668, AS/NZS 3823 (equipment performance), AIRAH guidelines
- **Industry:** AIRAH (Australian Institute of Refrigeration, Air Conditioning and Heating)

> **"Your ARC ARCTICK licence expires in 19 days. You can't legally handle refrigerants without it -- renewal takes up to 10 business days to process. Here's the renewal form. Do you want me to submit a renewal reminder to your calendar as a hard block?"**

I can see none of them have been registered on Daikin's warranty portal yet -- registration is required within 30 days of installation for the extended warranty to apply. ## 5. Carpenters / Joiners

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
Carpenters cover a huge range: **formwork** (concrete), **framing** (structural), **fitout/finishing** (doors, frames, skirtings), and **cabinet-making/joinery**. Most small operators focus on fitout and finishing carpentry.

**Fitout carpenter day:**
- 6:30 AM: Arrive on site. Finishing carpentry happens after the plasterer has finished; work is sequential and blocked easily.
- Hang doors, install architraves, skirtings, window reveals, robes, shelving. Detail-oriented work.
- 4-5 PM: Pack up tools, review tomorrow's materials, communicate with GC on any issues.

**Framing carpenter day:**
- Heavier work; timber framing of walls, floors, roofs. More physically demanding, faster-paced.
- Material volumes are large -- timber deliveries need crane or hiab; coordinate with site.

**Cabinet-maker/joiner (workshop-based):**
- Morning: Workshop fabrication of kitchen/laundry/robe carcasses.
- Afternoon or separate day: On-site installation.
- Measure -> design -> fabricate -> install cycle often spans 3-6 weeks per job.

### Before Tasks
- **Measure and quote** -- Precision measuring is critical in joinery; an error on a kitchen layout can cost thousands in wastage
- **Material list** -- Timber species, profile selections, hardware (hinges, handles, drawer runners)
- **Timber order** -- Lead times: standard pine 1-3 days; feature timber (spotted gum, blackbutt, VJ boards) can be 1-3 weeks
- **Hardware order** -- Blum, Hettich, Hfele hardware; specific handles often have 2-4 week lead
- **Site readiness check** -- Plastering finished, painting primed? Carpentry can't go in over wet paint
- **Tools prepared** -- Nail guns serviced, compressor checked, blades fresh, laser level charged
- **SWMS** -- For height work (roof framing), plant operation (nail guns, circular saws)

### After Tasks
- **Defects walk** -- Check all doors align, hardware operates, joins are tight
- **Touch-up list** -- Minor gaps, nail fills, silicone runs typically left for painter to coordinate
- **Job photos** -- Portfolio documentation; before-and-after
- **Invoice** -- Often on completion; large jobs may have progress claims
- **Product warranty handover** -- Door manufacturer warranties, hardware warranties (Blum 25-year guarantee)
- **Clean-up** -- Sawdust, offcuts, packaging removal; leaving a clean site is a professional standard that clients notice

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
| Trigger | Timeframe | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Carpenter's licence (where required) | Annual | Cannot legally contract |
| Tool servicing (nail gun, saw blades) | Ongoing/scheduled | Dangerous equipment; quality issues |
| Timber delivery windows | Must align with site readiness | Timber sitting in weather = damage |
| Kitchen measure-to-delivery lead time | 3-8 weeks for custom joinery | Kitchen can't be completed |
| Hardware backorder items (handles) | Weeks-months for imported | Job can't finalise |
| Seasonal: new home builds | Sep-Feb peak | Booked out months ahead |
| Post-flood housing repairs | Demand surge after weather events | Opportunity + stress spike |

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- **Measure errors** -- Ordering custom joinery to wrong dimensions; client waits extra weeks, cost absorbed
- **Site readiness delays** -- Waiting on plasterer/painter before work can start; idle time costs money
- **Damage to finished work by other trades** -- Freshly installed skirtings kicked by plumber's toolbox; need for touch-up
- **Timber quality variability** -- Ordered timber arriving cupped, bowed, or with knots in visible locations
- **Hardware substitutions** -- Specified handles discontinued or unavailable; client unhappy with substitution
- **Small job scheduling** -- Half-day jobs are hard to schedule efficiently; long travel for small revenue

### Data Sources
- **Job management:** Tradify, ServiceM8, AroFlo, or basic Google Sheets/Calendar
- **Accounting:** Xero, MYOB, QuickBooks
- **Suppliers:** Timber & Hardware (Bowens, Big River, Timber Merchants), Hardware (Hfele, Blum, Bunnings Trade)
- **Joinery design:** Cabinet Vision, KD Max, plain AutoCAD, or hand sketches
- **Industry:** HIA Carpentry, TAFE training records, apprenticeship portals

## 6. Painters & Decorators

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
Painting is often the last trade through a building -- every other trade delay flows into the painter's schedule. Residential painters juggle prep, undercoat, and finish coat days across multiple sites.

**Typical day:**
- 6:30 AM: Load drop sheets, masking tape, brushes, rollers. Review the day's site -- what stage is each area?
- First hour on site is often prep: sanding, filling, masking, priming new work
- Main painting through the day. Interior paint is weather-independent; exterior is not.
- End of day: clean brushes/rollers (significant time), review next day's masking, update job progress

**Site management reality:** A 4-bedroom house exterior repaint might be 5-8 days across 2-3 painters. Weather windows drive everything. An interior fitout paint job on a new build involves multiple visits: prime the plaster, sand/fill, undercoat, top coat -- 3-4 separate site visits, each dependent on drying time.

**Weekly:** Quote Monday/Tuesday, material order mid-week (paint stores usually have next-day for in-stock colours), follow up outstanding quotes Friday.

### Before Tasks
- **Surface assessment** -- Condition of existing paint, presence of lead paint (pre-1970 buildings), mould, water damage. Affects quotation significantly.
- **Lead paint testing** -- Mandatory assessment for pre-1970 homes; XRF test or wipe test. Different removal protocols for lead-containing coatings.
- **Colour consultation/specification** -- Client provides colour scheme or painter helps select; colour codes locked before ordering
- **Material ordering** -- Interior vs. exterior, primer, undercoat, topcoat. Premium paints (Dulux Wash&Wear, Taubmans 4L) vs. trade-grade.
- **Masking/protection materials** -- Drop sheets, tape, plastic sheeting for floors and furniture
- **SWMS** -- For heights (ladders, scaffolding), lead paint handling, confined spaces
- **Scaffolding booking** -- If required (2-storey or higher exterior), scaffold needs 48+ hours notice

### After Tasks
- **Touch-up walk** -- Inspect all surfaces in good light; holidays (missed patches), runs, and edge lines
- **Client walk-through** -- Demonstrate finish quality; agree on any touch-ups required
- **Paint codes documentation** -- Leave a card with every colour code used in every room; clients need this for touch-ups
- **Invoice** -- Often completion-based; large jobs may have a mid-project progress claim
- **Warranty** -- Most residential painters offer a 2-5 year warranty on workmanship; paint manufacturer warranties are separate
- **Dispose of paint** -- Leftover paint labelled and left for client, or disposed of responsibly (paint recycling programs)
- **Equipment maintenance** -- Clean and store spray equipment; inspect rollers and brushes

### Time-Sensitive Triggers

| Public liability insurance | Annual | Uninsured; can't work on most sites |
| Scaffolding hire return | Per hire agreement | Daily charges accumulating |
| Weather windows (exterior) | Day-of decision | Can't paint in rain or extreme heat |
| Seasonal: new homes (fitout) | Sep-Feb | Booked out; scheduling pressure |
| Colour discontinuation | Manufacturer notice | Client's touch-up colour unavailable |

- **Weather for exterior** -- Can't paint in high humidity, rain, or above 35C (paint doesn't cure correctly). Jobs pushed back without warning.
- **Surface condition surprises** -- "It just needs a fresh coat" turns into full prep when old paint is peeling everywhere.

- **Colour approval** -- Client approves colour on a small swatch, hates it on the wall. "That's not what I expected" conversations.
- **Quote undercutting** -- Painting is easy to undercut; low-quality competitors quote unrealistic prices.

## 7. Tilers / Flooring Specialists

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
Tilers are a mid-sequence trade in any build -- they come after waterproofing, before grouting, before final fitout. Flooring specialists (vinyl, carpet, hybrid) are often last trade in, after painting.

**Tiler day:**
- 6:30 AM: Load trowels, angle grinder, tile saw, adhesive, grout. Tiles for the day must be on-site.
- Day is divided: substrate prep (screed, waterproof membrane, backer board) is separate from laying tiles; laying tiles separate from grouting (typically 24 hours after laying).
- Tile saw noise means early starts can be a neighbour/building management issue.
- 4 PM: Clean tools, check grout timing for tomorrow, ensure next day's tiles are staged.

**Flooring specialist day (vinyl/carpet/hybrid):**
- Often faster-paced: entire room can be floored in a day.
- Measure -> order -> acclimate (timber and hybrid floors need 48-72 hours on-site before install) -> install.

### Before Tasks
- **Surface preparation check** -- Substrate must be flat, dry, and structurally sound. Tolerance for large-format tiles is 3mm over 3m. Tiler must assess and potentially screed before tiling.
- **Waterproofing confirmation** -- Wet areas must have membrane applied and passed inspection before tiling. If the waterproofer just finished, there's a minimum cure time (often 24-48 hours).
- **Tile selection and quantity calc** -- Calculate tiles needed with wastage factor (typically 10-15% for standard layouts, 15-20% for diagonal or patterned). Order in single batch -- dye lot consistency is critical.
- **Tile order and delivery** -- Tiles are heavy; delivery logistics matter. Italian porcelain can be 4-8 weeks from overseas.
- **Adhesive and grout selection** -- Must be appropriate for tile size, application, and substrate. Large-format tiles need higher-quality adhesive.
- **Underfloor heating coordination** -- If UFH installed under tiles, must be confirmed operational before tiling; can't be activated for 28 days after install.
- **Layout plan** -- Where does the first tile go? Layout lines affect the visual result significantly; agreed with client beforehand.

### After Tasks
- **Grouting** -- Minimum 24 hours after laying; exact timing per adhesive spec
- **Grout sealing** -- Particularly for natural stone; often 7-14 days after grouting for full cure
- **Client walk-through** -- Inspect for hollow tiles (tapping test), lippage (tile edges not level), grout line consistency
- **Silicone expansion joints** -- Movement joints at corners, junctions, and large areas; different colour to match grout
- **Invoice** -- Often stage-based: adhesive stage + grout stage, or single completion invoice
- **Left-over tile storage recommendation** -- Client needs to keep spare tiles from the same batch for future repairs
- **Grout and tile care instructions** -- What to use for cleaning; what not to use (acid cleaners on natural stone, etc.)

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
| Trigger | Timeframe | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Tiling licence registration (states vary) | Annual | Illegal to contract |
| Waterproofing inspection booking | 48-72 hours notice | Can't tile over uninspected membrane |
| Tile delivery (imported) | 4-8 weeks for some products | Site waiting; GC pressure |
| Dye lot matching window | Order all at once or accept risk | Colour mismatch if reordering from different batch |
| Adhesive working time | 30-60 min (open time) | Wasted adhesive, disrupted layout |
| Grout cure before grouting | 24 hours minimum | Adhesive failure |
| Underfloor heating activation | 28 days post-tile | UFH damage, tile cracking |
| Seasonal: new home builds | Sep-Feb | Fully booked; supply chain pressure |

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- **Substrate failures** -- "The waterproofing is done" but it's actually not up to standard, or not inspected yet. GC pressure to start causes compliance risk.
- **Tile lippage** -- Large-format tiles (600x1200) are unforgiving; if the substrate isn't perfectly flat, the result is visible and the client will notice.
- **Wrong tile quantity** -- Ordering short by 2-3m means stopping work to reorder; new batch might not match the dye lot.
- **Hollow tiles** -- Not pressing tiles correctly into adhesive creates hollow spots that crack over time; hard to detect until it's too late.
- **Colour variation** -- Natural stone especially has variation; client expects uniform appearance from showroom sample.
- **GC timeline pressure** -- "Can't you grout today?" -- yes, but the adhesive hasn't cured and it'll fail in a year.

- **Job management:** ServiceM8, Tradify, or simple calendar
- **Accounting:** Xero, MYOB
- **Tile suppliers:** National Tiles, Amber Tiles, Beaumont Tiles (all have trade accounts)
- **Adhesive/grout suppliers:** Mapei, Ardex, Bostik -- all have technical spec documents and calculators
- **Waterproofing inspectors:** Local council/certifier portals
- **Industry:** Tile Council, Australian Tile Council standards

## 8. Landscapers / Gardeners (Professional)

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
Professional landscapers operate across **project work** (garden design, construction) and **maintenance contracts** (regular mowing, pruning, seasonal work). Maintenance is the recurring revenue engine; projects are higher margin but sporadic.

**Maintenance crew day:**
- 6:00 AM: Load mowers, blowers, trimmers, edgers, chemicals. Route planned the previous evening.
- 3-8 properties per day depending on size. Residential maintenance runs are often 45 min to 2 hours per property.
- Efficiency is everything: proximity of jobs, equipment availability, crew size.
- End of day: refuel equipment, blade maintenance, record any issues found at properties.

**Project day:**
- Site-based for duration of project (landscaping a new build or renovation garden)
- Heavy equipment: bobcat, skid-steer, tipper, mini excavator often hired
- Materials: soil, mulch, plants, tubing, irrigation fittings, pavers/retaining wall blocks, instant turf
- Multiple subcontractors possible: irrigation specialist, concreter for paths, electrician for outdoor lighting

**Weekly rhythm:** Quote visits Mon/Tue, maintenance run Tue-Thu, project work Fri or dedicated weeks.

### Before Tasks
- **Site survey and soil test** -- pH, drainage, soil type. Affects plant selection and soil amendment requirements.
- **Design and approval** -- Client signs off on design/plan before any work; can be hand-drawn or 3D render
- **Council permits** -- Tree removal permits, retaining wall approvals (typically >600mm height), building over sewer easements
- **Utility locating** -- Dial Before You Dig; underground gas, water, electrical, telecom. Landscaping involves excavation.
- **Material ordering** -- Turf (ordered to deliver same day as installation; it dies if left rolled for 24+ hours), pavers, plants (nursery lead time), soil, mulch
- **Irrigation design** -- Pipe routing, zone planning, controller specification
- **Plant sourcing** -- Specific species from specialist nurseries may have 2-4 week lead time
- **Equipment hire booking** -- Bobcat, mini-ex, tipper. Peak periods have limited availability.

### After Tasks
- **Turf establishment care** -- Client instructions: watering schedule for first 6 weeks is critical. If they don't water correctly, turf dies.
- **Irrigation commissioning** -- Program controller, test all zones, adjust heads
- **Mulching** -- Applied at end of project; final aesthetic
- **Handover walkthrough** -- Demonstrate irrigation system, identify plants and care requirements
- **Invoice** -- Often deposit + completion; large projects may have progress claims
- **Planting warranty** -- Many landscapers offer 3-12 month planting guarantee (excluding client neglect)
- **Maintenance contract offer** -- Transition project client to ongoing maintenance relationship
- **Seasonal follow-up** -- Spring mulch refresh, autumn leaf clean-up -- opportunities to stay in contact

## 9. Solar Panel Installers

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
Solar is a growth sector with strong and predictable demand. Work is a mix of **residential rooftop installs** (common: 6.6kW to 13kW systems) and **commercial systems** (larger, more complex, higher revenue). Battery storage (Tesla Powerwall, Sungrow, AlphaESS) adds complexity and margin.

**Typical installation day:**
- 6:30 AM: Load panels, inverter, mounting hardware, DC cable, conduit, isolators onto van/trailer
- Residential 6.6kW system (20 panels): 2 installers, 6-8 hours. One on roof, one at board.
- Commercial system: multi-day project with detailed plan
- End of day: commissioning and testing, system handover, STCs paperwork, lodgement

**Weekly rhythm:** Sales team (or owner-operator quoting) runs Mon-Wed; installs Thu-Fri or Mon-Fri depending on volume. CEC accreditation and STC documentation are the key admin tasks.

### Before Tasks
- **CEC accreditation check** -- All installers must hold CEC accreditation. The business must also hold CEC accreditation to issue STCs.
- **Network connection application** -- Distributed Energy Resource (DER) connection must be applied for with the local DNSP (Distributed Network Service Provider: Ausgrid, Energex, etc.) before installation. Processing time: 5-30+ business days depending on network load.
- **System design** -- Panel layout, string sizing, inverter selection, tilt/azimuth calculation, shading analysis
- **Structural assessment** -- Roof type, age, condition. Tile roofs, metal roofs, flat roofs all have different mounting systems. Older roofs may not bear the load without reinforcement.
- **STC pre-calculation** -- Small-scale Technology Certificates are the financial rebate mechanism; STC number determined by system size and postcode zone
- **Materials order** -- Panels, inverter, mounting system, cabling, isolators, monitoring hardware. Panel availability can vary (some brands on backorder).
- **Site visit** -- Roof condition, meter box location, proposed inverter location, internet connectivity for monitoring
- **Safety** -- SWMS for working at height; must work in pairs on roof per Safe Work requirements

### After Tasks
- **Commissioning and testing** -- System energised, output verified, monitoring platform configured
- **Grid connection** -- Notify DNSP that system is live; in some states this is a formal notification step
- **STC creation and assignment** -- STCs must be created in the REC Registry within 12 months of install. Most installers assign STCs to an aggregator at point of sale (reduces upfront cost for client), but there's admin required.
- **Certificate of Electrical Safety (CES)** -- Lodge with state electrical regulator
- **Client handover and training** -- Explain monitoring app, how to read the system, what to do if error light shows
- **Invoice** -- Often net of STC discount already applied
- **Manufacturer warranty registration** -- Panel warranties (typically 25-year product, 25-year performance), inverter warranties (10 years standard)
- **Monitoring setup** -- SolarEdge monitoring, Fronius Solar.web, Sungrow iSolarCloud -- client gets app access
- **Follow-up: battery storage quote** -- Many clients upgrade to battery 6-18 months after initial solar install

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
| Trigger | Timeframe | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| CEC installer accreditation | Annual | Can't legally install or claim STCs |
| Network connection approval | 5-30 business days | System can't be turned on |
| STC creation deadline | 12 months post-install | STCs lapse; significant financial loss |
| Feed-in tariff application | Per energy retailer | Client loses FiT revenue if not applied for |
| Inverter warranty registration | Usually 30 days | Voided extended warranty |
| Grid capacity constraints | Ongoing | DNSP may limit system size or reject connection |
| Seasonal demand (summer) | Sep-Feb (AU) | Fully booked; 6-8 week wait common |
| STC price fluctuation | Daily (market-determined) | Quote locked in at wrong STC value |
| Panel price changes | Ongoing (volatile) | Quote profitability risk |

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- **Network connection approval delays** -- DNSP approval can take weeks; client waits; installer has collected deposit but can't install
- **Roof condition surprises** -- Quote assumed good condition; arrive and find cracked tiles, rotted battens, asbestos ridge capping
- **STC paperwork errors** -- An error in the REC Registry filing can hold up the rebate; complex admin
- **Panel/inverter substitutions** -- Quoted brand out of stock; substituting requires client agreement and CEC re-check
- **Height safety** -- Working on steep roofs, tile roofs that crack underfoot. Risk is real and present every day.
- **Battery installation complexity** -- Battery storage involves high-voltage DC; requires specific training and increases liability significantly
- **Feed-in tariff erosion** -- FiT rates keep dropping; clients who were sold on economics may be disappointed

- **Accounting:** Xero, MYOB

## 10. Roofing Contractors

### Daily/Weekly Rhythm
Roofing is unambiguously weather-dependent and physically demanding. Work types: **new roof construction**, **reroofing** (replace old tiles/metal), **repairs** (leak investigation and fixing), and **gutters/downpipes**.

**Repair/gutter day:**
- 6:30 AM: Load ladders, safety equipment, sealants, tiles, gutter sections, screws/brackets
- 3-6 repair jobs per day for small operators. Leak diagnosis is investigative -- not always obvious.
- Roof work is exposed to weather, heat, and wind constantly. Physical toll is significant.
- End of day: update job cards, follow up on quoted repairs, restock common materials

**New roof or reroof:**
- Multi-day project. On a new build, the roof frame goes up (carpenter's work), then the roofer installs sarking, battens, and tiles/metal.
- Scaffold access in many cases. Crane lifts for large tile loads.
- Sequence: frame inspection -> sarking -> battens -> roof cover -> ridges -> flashings -> gutters

### Before Tasks
- **Roofing licence** -- Required in most states for over-roof, new roof, and structural work
- **Safety planning** -- Height risk assessment is mandatory. Edge protection, scaffold, or anchor points required depending on slope and height.
- **Scaffold booking** -- 48-72 hours minimum lead time; required for most 2-storey or pitched roof work
- **Material quantity and order** -- Tile count (complex; depends on roof geometry), metal sheets, sarking, battens, flashings. Over-ordering is preferable to running short mid-roof.
- **Tile/material matching** -- For reroofs, matching existing tiles (colour, profile) is difficult. Older profiles may be discontinued.
- **Inspection of existing structure** -- Before reroofing, check for structural damage, wet/rotten timber, rusted fixings
- **Weather window planning** -- Never start a major roof opening in uncertain weather; rain on an open roof can cause catastrophic damage

### After Tasks
- **Inspection and leak test** -- Hose test in some cases; visual inspection of all flashings, ridges, valleys
- **Clean up** -- Old tiles, packaging, offcuts removed. Roof debris in gutters removed.
- **Client walk-through** -- Often involves showing photos (client can't easily inspect their own roof)
- **Gutter/downpipe check** -- Gutters pitched correctly for water flow; all connections sealed
- **Invoice** -- Often completion-based; deposits common
- **Photo documentation** -- Before, during, after photos are essential for roofing; client can't see it, documentation protects both parties
- **Warranty** -- Labour warranty typically 5-10 years; manufacturer warranties on materials (Colorbond: 25 years paint, 12 years structural; tile warranties vary)
- **Flashing and sealant inspection reminder** -- Recommend 5-yearly inspection

### Time-Sensitive Triggers
| Trigger | Timeframe | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Roofing contractor licence renewal | Annual/biennial (state) | Illegal to contract |
| Height safety equipment inspection | 6-monthly (harnesses, anchors) | Cannot use; WHS non-compliance |
| Scaffold hire return | Per hire agreement | Daily hire charges accumulating |
| Weather window | Day-of / 48-hour forecast | Major risk if wrong decision |
| Tile availability (discontinued profiles) | When identified | Can't complete job; need to source NOS stock |
| Summer storm season demand | Oct-Mar (AU) | Surge in emergency repair calls |
| Colorbond/BlueScope price changes | Quarterly | Quote profitability risk |
| Post-hail inspection surge | After any hail event | Massive short-term demand spike |
| Seasonal: new builds | Sep-Feb | Fully booked; subcontracting pressure |

### Stress/Anxiety Points
- **Weather risk** -- Starting a reroof when weather turns = catastrophic water damage to the entire building interior. The decision to start, continue, or pause is high-stakes.
- **Tile matching** -- Client wants their 1985 double-roman terra cotta tiles matched. They may not exist anymore.
- **Leak tracing** -- Water ingress point and visible damage point are often metres apart. Diagnosing correctly the first time is crucial; a second visit for a continued leak is reputation damage.
- **Height safety** -- Falls from roofs are a leading cause of death in construction. The pressure to work faster without full safety setup is constant and dangerous.
- **Emergency callout during storms** -- Clients with active roof damage expect immediate response. Safety risk at exactly the time demand is highest.
- **Insurance assessors** -- Storm damage insurance work involves working with assessors who may not understand roofing; pricing battles are common.
- **Scaffold cost blowouts** -- If a job runs over, scaffold hire continues; can eat margin quickly.

- **Material suppliers:** Boral Roofing, Monier (tiles), BlueScope Steel/Colorbond (metal roofing), Lysaght (gutters/profiles)
- **Fasteners/accessories:** ITW Buildex, Ramset (specialist fixings portals)

## Cross-Role Patterns for Pulse Product Design

### Universal Pain Points
1. **Licence/certification expiry** -- Every trade has at least 1-3 licence/accreditation renewals with real consequences for expiry
2. **Insurance renewal** -- Public liability is universal; many have additional policy types
3. **Cash flow gaps** -- Invoice-to-payment timing is a constant stress for small operators
4. **Quote staleness** -- Material prices change; old quotes become loss-making
5. **Compliance paperwork** -- Post-job certificates (CES, CCP, etc.) are commonly delayed or forgotten
6. **Material lead times** -- Long-lead items ordered too late are a primary cause of project delays
7. **Weather dependency** -- Roofing, painting, landscaping, and concreting are all weather-sensitive
