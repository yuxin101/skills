# Home Improvement Video Maker — Renovation and Home Repair Videos

The bathroom was last updated in 1987, the tiles are a shade of pink that hasn't been manufactured since the Reagan administration, and the homeowner's plan is to "just rip it all out this weekend" — a sentence that has preceded more marital arguments than any other phrase in the English language. Home improvement content is YouTube's most reliable long-form category because it solves an immediate, expensive problem: hire a contractor for $15,000 or watch a 20-minute video and do it yourself for $3,000 in materials and one trip to the emergency room for a minor tile-saw incident. The format works because viewers aren't watching for entertainment — they're watching with a tape measure in one hand and a phone in the other, pausing every thirty seconds to replicate what they just saw. This tool transforms raw renovation footage into polished home improvement videos — demolition montages with satisfying wall-removal sequences, step-by-step installation guides with numbered callouts on every critical measurement, before-and-after reveals with matching camera angles, tool-list overlays that show exactly what to buy before starting, mistake-and-fix sequences that show what goes wrong and how to recover, cost-breakdown graphics that track every dollar from demo to completion, and the final reveal that makes the viewer believe they can do this too. Built for DIY YouTubers documenting renovation projects, contractors creating portfolio and educational content, real-estate flippers showcasing transformation value, home-improvement brands producing how-to libraries, property managers creating maintenance-tutorial archives, and any homeowner who started a project, filmed it, and wants to turn the footage into something that helps the next person avoid their mistakes.

## Example Prompts

### 1. Full Bathroom Renovation — Start to Finish
"Create a 15-minute bathroom renovation video from demolition to finished reveal. The pink 1987 bathroom gets a modern update. Budget: $4,800 for a homeowner doing their own labor. Demo day (0-90 sec): satisfying demolition montage — pry bar under the first pink tile (slow-mo crack and release), mirror removal, vanity pulled off the wall (show the plumbing capping process — don't skip this, people flood their houses), old flooring ripped up (reveal the subfloor condition, show the moisture test). Running cost counter starts: Demo supplies: $45. Plumbing rough-in (90-180 sec): show the new supply lines and drain relocation. This is where people mess up: 'Measure your new vanity BEFORE moving the drain. I moved the drain 4 inches to the left, then discovered the vanity I ordered needs it 2 inches to the right. That's a $200 lesson in reading spec sheets.' Show the correct measurement process — tape measure on screen, specs overlay. Cost: Plumbing supplies: $340. Waterproofing (180-240 sec): this is the most skipped step and the most important. Show the waterproof membrane being applied to the shower walls and floor — 'Skip this and you're renovating again in 3 years because the subfloor is rotting. Ask me how I know.' Show the seam tape, the corners, the drain collar seal. Cost: $180. Tile (240-420 sec): the hero section. Layout planning — dry-lay the tiles on the floor first (show the spacer grid). Thin-set mixing (show the right consistency — it should hold on the trowel without dripping). Wall tile installation: notched trowel technique, spacer placement, level checking every 3 rows (show the laser level). The tricky parts: inside corners (show the miter cut vs bullnose debate), the shower niche (frame it, waterproof it, tile it — show each step), outlet and pipe cutouts with a hole saw. Grouting: float technique, 45-degree angle, wipe with a damp sponge. Cost: Tile + supplies: $1,200. Vanity and fixtures (420-540 sec): vanity installation — find the studs (show the stud finder), level it (shim if needed), connect the plumbing (show the P-trap assembly). Faucet installation: follow the manufacturer sequence (they all claim 10 minutes — it's 45). Mirror hanging, light fixture wiring (show the breaker shut-off — 'If you skip this, the video ends here and the sequel is filmed from a hospital'). Cost: Vanity + fixtures: $1,600. Finishing (540-600 sec): caulk the joints (show the tape-and-smooth technique for clean lines), install the toilet (wax ring placement matters — 'Off-center and you'll smell the consequences'), towel bars, accessories. Cost: Final touches: $220. Reveal (600-720 sec): the door closes. Then opens to the finished bathroom — same camera angle as the 'before' demo shot. Slow pan: the tile wall, the vanity, the fixtures, the floor. Before-and-after split screen: 6 matching angles. Total cost graphic animated: $4,785. Time: 3 weekends. 'The bathroom that looked like 1987 now looks like 2026. Total cost: $4,785 in materials and roughly $15,000 in contractor fees I didn't pay.'"

### 2. Quick Fix Tutorial — Leaky Faucet Repair
"Build a 5-minute leaky faucet repair tutorial. This is a search-driven video — someone has a dripping faucet right now and they want it fixed tonight. No backstory, no channel intro. Opening (0-10 sec): dripping faucet close-up — the steady drip sound amplified. 'That drip is wasting 5 gallons a day and costing you $20 a month. You can fix it in 15 minutes with $8 in parts. Here's how.' Tools needed (10-25 sec): animated overlay of everything they need — adjustable wrench, Allen key set, replacement cartridge (show how to find the model: 'Look under the handle cap for a brand name, or Google the faucet shape + "replacement cartridge"'). Step 1 — Turn off water (25-45 sec): show the shut-off valves under the sink. 'Righty-tighty. If the valves are stuck, don't force them — hit them with penetrating oil and wait 10 minutes. Forcing a corroded valve is how a faucet repair becomes a plumbing emergency.' Turn on the faucet to drain remaining pressure. Step 2 — Disassemble (45-120 sec): remove the handle cap (show the pry tool), remove the Allen screw, lift the handle. Show the cartridge — 'This is the part that's failing. The O-rings are worn.' Pull the cartridge (show the cartridge-puller tool if it's stuck). Side-by-side: old cartridge vs new cartridge — show the worn O-ring vs the fresh one. Step 3 — Replace (120-160 sec): new cartridge in — align the tabs. 'If it doesn't drop in smoothly, it's rotated 180 degrees. Don't force it.' Reassemble in reverse order: cartridge, handle, screw, cap. Step 4 — Test (160-200 sec): turn on the shut-offs slowly. Run the faucet: hot, cold, both. Check under the sink for leaks with a flashlight and a paper towel. 'Paper towel under the connections. Wait 5 minutes. If it's dry, you're done.' The result (200-300 sec): the faucet running cleanly, no drip. 'Total time: 15 minutes. Total cost: $8 for the cartridge, $0 for the labor. That drip was a $240/year water bill increase, and now it's fixed.' End card with related repairs: toilet running, garbage disposal jam, clogged drain. Clean, instructional aesthetic — well-lit under-sink shots (headlamp visible for realism), numbered step overlays, tool callouts with product names."

Show the brush technique for panel doors (center panel first, then stiles, then rails). Before-after of one door: the transformation is dramatic. Cost: $250 for primer, paint, and supplies. Project 2 — New hardware (180-220 sec): old brass pulls removed, new matte-black pulls installed. Before-after: same cabinets, different personality. Cost: $85 for 20 pulls. Project 3 — Countertop refinishing (220-340 sec): not replacing — refinishing with an epoxy countertop kit. The pattern reveals itself as each row goes on. Before-after: blank wall to subway-tile look. Cost: $165. Final reveal (480-540 sec): wide shot of the complete kitchen — same angle as the opening. Split screen before-after. Total cost animated: $1,100. 'Same cabinets. Same layout. Same appliances. Completely different kitchen. The biggest change was $250 in paint. The second biggest was $85 in hardware. Under $350 did 80% of the transformation.'"

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Show the mistake, not just the method** — "I moved the drain 4 inches, then discovered the vanity needs it 2 inches right" is more educational than a perfect installation. The AI sequences mistake-then-correction moments when you describe errors in your prompt, building viewer trust.
2. **Keep a running cost counter visible** — Every material purchase added to an on-screen total lets viewers budget in real time. The AI maintains an animated cost overlay that updates at each section break when cost_tracker is enabled.
3. **Film the before and after from identical angles** — The split-screen reveal only works when both frames are taken from the exact same position. Describe your camera placement once and the AI matches the angles for the before-after composition.
4. **Include the safety step even when it's boring** — "Turn off the breaker" and "cap the water supply" are the steps that prevent disasters. The AI adds safety-callout graphics with warning icons when you describe safety-critical steps.
5. **End with the total cost vs contractor estimate** — "$4,785 in materials vs $15,000 with a contractor" is the payoff that validates the DIY approach. The AI renders cost-comparison graphics on the final reveal card.
