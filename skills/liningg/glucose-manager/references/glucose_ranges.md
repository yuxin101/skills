# Glucose Reference Ranges

## Normal Blood Glucose Levels

### Fasting Glucose (No food for 8+ hours)
- **Normal**: 3.9 - 5.5 mmol/L (70 - 100 mg/dL)
- **Prediabetes**: 5.6 - 6.9 mmol/L (101 - 125 mg/dL)
- **Diabetes**: ≥ 7.0 mmol/L (≥ 126 mg/dL)

### Post-Meal Glucose (2 hours after eating)
- **Normal**: < 7.8 mmol/L (< 140 mg/dL)
- **Prediabetes**: 7.8 - 11.0 mmol/L (140 - 199 mg/dL)
- **Diabetes**: ≥ 11.1 mmol/L (≥ 200 mg/dL)

### HbA1c Levels (3-month average)
- **Normal**: < 5.7%
- **Prediabetes**: 5.7% - 6.4%
- **Diabetes**: ≥ 6.5%

## Target Ranges for People with Diabetes

### Before Meals (Pre-prandial)
- **Type 1 Diabetes**: 4.0 - 7.0 mmol/L (72 - 126 mg/dL)
- **Type 2 Diabetes**: 4.0 - 7.0 mmol/L (72 - 126 mg/dL)
- **Pregnancy with diabetes**: ≤ 5.3 mmol/L (≤ 95 mg/dL)

### After Meals (Post-prandial, 1-2 hours)
- **Type 1 Diabetes**: < 9.0 mmol/L (< 162 mg/dL)
- **Type 2 Diabetes**: < 10.0 mmol/L (< 180 mg/dL)
- **Pregnancy with diabetes**: ≤ 6.7 - 7.8 mmol/L (≤ 120 - 140 mg/dL)

### Bedtime
- **General target**: 6.0 - 8.0 mmol/L (108 - 144 mg/dL)
- Avoid going to bed with very low or very high glucose

## Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar)

### Classification
- **Mild hypoglycemia**: 3.0 - 3.9 mmol/L (54 - 70 mg/dL)
  - Symptoms: Shakiness, sweating, hunger, anxiety
  - Action: Eat 15-20g fast-acting carbohydrates
  
- **Moderate hypoglycemia**: 2.2 - 2.9 mmol/L (40 - 52 mg/dL)
  - Symptoms: Confusion, irritability, difficulty concentrating
  - Action: Requires assistance to treat
  
- **Severe hypoglycemia**: < 2.2 mmol/L (< 40 mg/dL)
  - Symptoms: Unconsciousness, seizures, coma
  - **Medical emergency**: Call emergency services immediately

### Treatment
**15-15 Rule**: 
1. Eat 15 grams of fast-acting carbs (glucose tablets, juice, candy)
2. Wait 15 minutes
3. Check glucose again
4. Repeat if still below 4.0 mmol/L (72 mg/dL)

## Hyperglycemia (High Blood Sugar)

### Classification
- **Mild hyperglycemia**: 10.0 - 13.9 mmol/L (180 - 250 mg/dL)
  - Symptoms: Thirst, frequent urination, fatigue
  - Action: Increase water intake, light exercise if appropriate
  
- **Moderate hyperglycemia**: 14.0 - 22.0 mmol/L (250 - 400 mg/dL)
  - Symptoms: Blurred vision, headache, difficulty concentrating
  - Action: Check for ketones, consult care plan
  
- **Severe hyperglycemia**: > 22.0 mmol/L (> 400 mg/dL)
  - Symptoms: Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, rapid breathing
  - **Medical emergency**: Seek immediate medical attention

### Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) Warning Signs
- Blood glucose > 13.9 mmol/L (250 mg/dL)
- Ketones present in urine or blood
- Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain
- Rapid, deep breathing (Kussmaul breathing)
- Fruity-smelling breath
- Confusion or difficulty staying awake

**DKA is a medical emergency. Seek immediate medical help.**

## Time in Range (TIR) Targets

International consensus recommendations for continuous glucose monitoring:

- **Target Range (3.9-10.0 mmol/L / 70-180 mg/dL)**: > 70% of time
- **Below Range (< 3.9 mmol/L / 70 mg/dL)**: < 4% of time
- **Very Low (< 3.0 mmol/L / 54 mg/dL)**: < 1% of time
- **Above Range (> 10.0 mmol/L / 180 mg/dL)**: < 25% of time
- **Very High (> 13.9 mmol/L / 250 mg/dL)**: < 5% of time

## Glucose Variability

### Coefficient of Variation (CV)
- **Target**: < 36% (lower is better)
- **Moderate variability**: 36% - 50%
- **High variability**: > 50%

High glucose variability is associated with increased risk of complications.

## Unit Conversion

### mmol/L to mg/dL
**Formula**: mg/dL = mmol/L × 18.018

**Quick Reference**:
- 4.0 mmol/L = 72 mg/dL
- 5.5 mmol/L = 100 mg/dL
- 7.0 mmol/L = 126 mg/dL
- 10.0 mmol/L = 180 mg/dL
- 13.9 mmol/L = 250 mg/dL

### mg/dL to mmol/L
**Formula**: mmol/L = mg/dL ÷ 18.018

**Quick Reference**:
- 70 mg/dL = 3.9 mmol/L
- 100 mg/dL = 5.5 mmol/L
- 126 mg/dL = 7.0 mmol/L
- 180 mg/dL = 10.0 mmol/L
- 250 mg/dL = 13.9 mmol/L

## Important Notes

1. **Individual targets may vary** based on age, duration of diabetes, comorbidities, and hypoglycemia awareness. Always consult your healthcare provider.

2. **Pregnancy**: Targets are stricter. Work closely with your healthcare team.

3. **Elderly or frail individuals**: May have higher targets to prevent hypoglycemia.

4. **Children and adolescents**: Targets may differ based on age and development.

5. **Always verify with your healthcare provider** what targets are appropriate for your specific situation.
