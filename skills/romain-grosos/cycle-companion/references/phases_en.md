# Cycle Phases - Reference EN

## Overview (default 28-day cycle)

| Phase | Days | Hormones | Energy | Mood | Fertility |
|-------|------|----------|--------|------|-----------|
| Menstruation | D1-5 | Estrogen + progesterone dropping | Low | Fatigue, withdrawn | Very low |
| Follicular | D6-12 | Rising estrogen | Rising | Good mood, creative | Rising |
| Ovulation | D13-15 | LH peak + max estrogen | Peak | Extroverted, confident | **PEAK** |
| Luteal | D16-21 | Rising progesterone | Decreasing | Calm, introspective | Low to none |
| PMS | D22-28 | Progesterone + estrogen crash | Very low | Irritable, hypersensitive | None |

## Menstruation (D1-D5)
- **Physio**: Drop in estrogen and progesterone triggers uterine contractions
- **Common symptoms**: Cramps, fatigue, headaches, back pain, low mood
- **Needs**: Rest, warmth, comfort food, emotional space
- **Bring**: Heating pad, dark chocolate, warm drinks, calm presence
- **Avoid**: Important discussions, pressure, demanding plans
- **Fertility**: Very low, but not zero — with short cycles, ovulation can happen early

## Follicular Phase (D6-D12)
- **Physio**: FSH stimulates follicles, estrogen rises progressively
- **Characteristics**: Growing energy, sociability, openness, creativity
- **Opportunities**: Best time to bring up difficult subjects, plan projects
- **End of phase**: Peak confidence, rising libido
- **Do**: Outings, new activities, meaningful conversations
- **Fertility**: Rising — sperm can survive up to 5 days in the reproductive tract, so the fertile window opens before ovulation

## Ovulation (~D13-D15)
- **Physio**: LH spike 24h before egg release, peak estrogen
- **Characteristics**: Maximum energy, easy communication, charisma, high libido
- **Actual window**: 12-24h for the egg, but "high phase" lasts 2-3 days
- **Do**: Romantic dates, outings, ambitious joint projects
- **Fertility**: **PEAK** — egg is viable for 12-24h after release. Total fertility window: ~6 days (5 days before + ovulation day)

## Luteal Phase (D16-D21)
- **Physio**: Corpus luteum secretes progesterone preparing the uterus for possible pregnancy
- **Characteristics**: More introspective energy, need for stability, less social
- **Do**: Calm quality time, cozy activities, home-cooked meals, be predictable
- **Fertility**: Low to none — egg is no longer viable, implantation is occurring or impossible

## PMS - Premenstrual Syndrome (D22-D28)
- **Physio**: Sharp drop in progesterone and estrogen when no pregnancy occurs
- **Common symptoms**: Irritability, crying, bloating, breast tenderness, fatigue, cravings
- **Individual variation**: PMS intensity varies widely — calibrate empirically
- **Fertility**: None — infertile phase of the cycle
- **Golden rules**:
  1. Never minimize ("it's just hormones") — it is real and physiological
  2. Do not argue against emotions
  3. Anticipate needs before she has to ask
  4. Postpone all important decisions

## Fertility Window — Summary

The fertility window covers approximately **6 days** per cycle:
- **D10-D13** (28-day cycle): rising fertility — sperm survive up to 5 days
- **D14-D15**: peak — ovulation, egg viable for 12-24h
- **D16**: last chance — residual declining fertility

### Fertility levels
| Level | Meaning | Days (28-day cycle) |
|-------|---------|---------------------|
| Low | Very low probability | D1-D7, D17-D28 |
| Moderate | Possible but unlikely | D8-D9 |
| High | Fertile window open | D10-D13, D16 |
| **Peak** | Maximum probability | **D14-D15** |

> **Note**: These estimates assume a regular cycle. Irregular cycles, stress, illness, or travel can shift ovulation. This is **not** a reliable contraceptive method.
