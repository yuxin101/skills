## First-Timer Notes (International Shows)

**ATA Carnet**: If bringing demo hardware (laptop, medical device prototype, etc.) that you're bringing back, get an ATA Carnet from your local Chamber of Commerce. It's the customs carnet for temporary exports. Takes 2–3 weeks and costs ~£200–400. Without it, you'll pay import duties at the German border or have the equipment held.

**MEDICA Shuttle**: Messe Düsseldorf is well-connected by U-Bahn (U78/U79, stop Messe Nord/Süd). Shuttle buses from central hotels run during show hours. Build in 20 minutes travel time from most central hotels.

**Show floor hours**: MEDICA opens at 10am daily. Move-in is usually the Sunday before. Get there early on Sunday — build time is limited and you want a buffer to fix problems.

**Interpreter services**: MEDICA has a high percentage of German and Japanese visitors who prefer communication in their language. If your team is English-only, consider renting a part-time interpreter for Hall 10–15 where clinical visitors concentrate.
