# Error Taxonomy for IELTS Reading Review

This document classifies common IELTS reading errors into categories. When analyzing a wrong answer, identify which category applies and use the corresponding improvement advice.

## Category 1: Synonym / Paraphrase Recognition Failure

**Description:** The passage uses a synonym or paraphrase of the question's keyword, and the test-taker fails to recognize the equivalence.

**Examples:**
- Question says "financial__(adj)" → Passage says "economic"
- Question says "


" → Passage says "children / young people"
- Question says "


" → Passage says "the


 of the world"

**Improvement:** Actively build a synonym accumulation table after every passage. Reference the 538 keywords list for known IELTS synonyms.

---

## Category 2: NOT GIVEN vs FALSE Confusion

**Description:** Choosing FALSE when the passage simply doesn't discuss the topic (should be NOT GIVEN), or choosing NOT GIVEN when there is direct contradicting evidence (should be FALSE).

**Key Rule:**
- **FALSE** = The passage explicitly says the **opposite** of the question
- **NOT GIVEN** = The passage does **not discuss** the topic at all, or discusses it but without taking a clear position that matches or contradicts

**Improvement:** Apply the Three-Step Method every time — find topic → find stance → verify with a specific sentence.

---

## Category 3: Over-Inference

**Description:** Drawing a conclusion that the passage does not explicitly state. The test-taker adds their own reasoning or world knowledge on top of what the author wrote.

**Examples:**
- Passage discusses ancient archaeological methods → Test-taker infers "translating ancient texts" was also mentioned (it wasn't)
- Passage says a task requires "a wider range of skills" → Test-taker infers "more demanding" (not the same thing)

**Improvement:** Ask: "Can I point to the **exact sentence** that says this?" If no → do not choose it.

---

## Category 4: Repeating Stem Words in Fill-in-the-blank

**Description:** Writing a word that is already present in the question stem, making the answer grammatically redundant.

**Examples:**
- Stem: "...about their ___" → Wrong answer: "about their experiences" (duplicates "about" or "their")
- Stem: "The ___ of..." → Wrong answer: "The the process of..." (duplicates "The")

**Improvement:** After writing the answer, **re-read the complete sentence** with the answer filled in. Check for any duplication.

---

## Category 5: Grammar Mismatch (Concessive / Conditional Clauses)

**Description:** Misunderstanding sentence structure, especially:
- Concessive clauses (`however + adj/adv` = `no matter how`)
- Conditional clauses that don't assert facts
- Double negatives

**Examples:**
- "However far from reality..." = "No matter how far from reality..." (acknowledges it IS far from reality)
- NOT "This is close to reality"

**Improvement:** Parse the sentence structure before interpreting meaning. Identify the main clause vs. subordinate clauses.

---

## Category 6: Incomplete Option Matching (Multiple Choice)

**Description:** Selecting an option where only the first half matches the passage. The second half introduces information not present in the passage.

**Improvement:** Check **every keyword** in the option against the passage. All must match, not just some.

---

## Category 7: Vocabulary Gap

**Description:** Not knowing a critical word in the passage or question, leading to misunderstanding.

**Note:** This is only flagged when the unknown word is a high-frequency IELTS word (in the 538 list or COCA top 3000). Low-frequency/specialist vocabulary gaps are noted but not treated as a core error.

**Improvement:** Focus vocabulary building on the 538 keywords list and accumulate words encountered in real Cambridge tests.

---

## Category 8: Time Pressure / Carelessness

**Description:** The test-taker understood the passage and could have answered correctly but made an error due to rushing or insufficient checking.

**Improvement:** Build in a final checking step for every passage before moving on.
