# Compliance and Positioning

## Source handling rules
- credit the creator and source video
- summarize, interpret, and teach
- do not copy long transcript passages
- do not mirror-post the original video content
- keep reuse of original media minimal unless rights are clear

## Positioning rule
This workflow is not for “content theft.”
It is for:
- commentary
- education
- summarization
- beginner translation
- practical reframing

## ClawLite bridge rule
ClawLite should appear as the practical next step, not as a forced interruption.

Good bridge examples:
- “The concept is strong. The real blocker is setup friction.”
- “If you want to try this without the setup pain, start with ClawLite.”
- “This video explains the idea. ClawLite helps with the practical first step.”

Avoid:
- fake claims about endorsement
- pretending the source creator supports ClawLite
- hard-sell CTA overload
- rewriting the creator’s whole transcript as your own article
