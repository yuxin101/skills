## Template 1: General Civil Complaint Framework

```
[CAPTION]

IN THE [COURT NAME]
OF [COUNTY/STATE]

[Case No. (if known): _______________]

[PLAINTIFF NAME],                )
                                )
          Plaintiff,            )
                                )
    v.                          )
                                )
[DEFENDANT NAME],               )
                                )
          Defendant.            )
                                )
________________________________)

COMPLAINT

[INTRODUCTION]

1.  Plaintiff [NAME], [residing at/located at] [ADDRESS], brings this action 
    against Defendant [NAME], [residing at/located at] [ADDRESS], for 
    [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF CLAIM].

[PARTIES]

2.  Plaintiff [NAME] is [DESCRIBE PLAINTIFF - individual/corporation/etc.].

3.  Defendant [NAME] is [DESCRIBE DEFENDANT - individual/corporation/etc.].

[JURISDICTION]

4.  This Court has jurisdiction over this matter because [EXPLAIN JURISDICTION 
    BASIS - e.g., amount in controversy exceeds $X, federal question, diversity].

[VENUE]

5.  Venue is proper in this Court because [EXPLAIN VENUE BASIS - e.g., events 
    occurred here, defendant resides here].

[FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS]

6.  [Date/Time]: [Describe first key event]

7.  [Date/Time]: [Describe second key event]

8.  [Continue chronological narrative]

[CAUSES OF ACTION]

COUNT I: [CLAIM TYPE - e.g., Breach of Contract]

9.  Plaintiff incorporates by reference all preceding paragraphs.

10. On [DATE], Plaintiff and Defendant entered into a [TYPE OF AGREEMENT].

11. Under the terms of the agreement, Defendant agreed to [OBLIGATIONS].

12. Plaintiff performed its obligations under the agreement by [ACTIONS TAKEN].

13. Defendant breached the agreement by [DESCRIBE BREACH].

14. As a direct result of Defendant's breach, Plaintiff has suffered damages 
    in the amount of $[AMOUNT].

COUNT II: [SECOND CLAIM TYPE, IF APPLICABLE]

[Repeat structure]

[PRAYER FOR RELIEF]

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court:

    a. Enter judgment against Defendant in the amount of $[AMOUNT];
    b. Award prejudgment interest;
    c. Award costs and attorney fees [if applicable];
    d. Grant such other relief as the Court deems just and proper.

DATED: [DATE]

Respectfully submitted,

_________________________
[ATTORNEY NAME/PLAINTIFF NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[PHONE]
[EMAIL]

[VERIFICATION - if required]

I, [NAME], declare under penalty of perjury that the foregoing is true and 
correct.

Executed on [DATE].

_________________________
[NAME]
```

## Template 2: Debt Collection Complaint

```
[CAPTION]

COMPLAINT FOR MONEY OWED

[PARTIES]

1.  Plaintiff [NAME] is [individual/corporation] engaged in [business].

2.  Defendant [NAME] is [individual/corporation] [residing at/doing business at] 
    [ADDRESS].

[FACTS]

3.  On or about [DATE], Defendant entered into a [written/oral] agreement with 
    Plaintiff for [DESCRIPTION OF GOODS/SERVICES].

4.  Under the terms of the agreement, Defendant agreed to pay Plaintiff 
    $[AMOUNT] for [GOODS/SERVICES].

5.  Plaintiff performed its obligations by providing [GOODS/SERVICES].

6.  Defendant has failed to pay the amount owed despite demand.

7.  The amount currently due and owing is $[AMOUNT], consisting of:
    a. Principal: $[AMOUNT]
    b. Interest: $[AMOUNT]
    c. [Other charges]: $[AMOUNT]

[CLAIM]

8.  Defendant owes Plaintiff $[AMOUNT] for [GOODS/SERVICES] provided.

[PRAYER]

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff requests judgment against Defendant for:
    a. $[AMOUNT] principal;
    b. Prejudgment interest at [RATE]% from [DATE];
    c. Post-judgment interest;
    d. Costs of suit.
```

## Template 3: Property Damage Complaint

```
[CAPTION]

COMPLAINT FOR PROPERTY DAMAGE

[PARTIES]

[FACTS]

1.  On [DATE], at approximately [TIME], at [LOCATION], Defendant 
    [DESCRIBE CONDUCT].

2.  Defendant's conduct caused damage to Plaintiff's [PROPERTY DESCRIPTION].

3.  The damage was caused by Defendant's [negligence/intentional conduct/recklessness].

4.  As a result, Plaintiff has incurred:
    a. Repair costs: $[AMOUNT]
    b. Replacement costs: $[AMOUNT]
    c. Loss of use: $[AMOUNT]
    d. [Other damages]: $[AMOUNT]

[CLAIM]

5.  Defendant is liable to Plaintiff for the damage to Plaintiff's property.

[PRAYER]

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff requests judgment for:
    a. Compensatory damages of $[AMOUNT];
    b. [Punitive damages, if applicable];
    c. Costs and attorney fees;
    d. Other relief.
```

## Template 4: Personal Injury Complaint

```
[CAPTION]

COMPLAINT FOR PERSONAL INJURY

[PARTIES]

[FACTS]

1.  On [DATE], at [LOCATION], Defendant [DESCRIBE CONDUCT].

2.  At all relevant times, Defendant owed Plaintiff a duty of [DESCRIBE DUTY].

3.  Defendant breached this duty by [DESCRIBE BREACH].

4.  Defendant's breach was a direct and proximate cause of Plaintiff's injuries.

5.  As a result, Plaintiff suffered:
    a. Physical injuries [DESCRIBE];
    b. Medical expenses of $[AMOUNT];
    c. Lost wages of $[AMOUNT];
    d. Pain and suffering;
    e. [Other damages].

[CLAIM - NEGLIGENCE]

6.  Defendant was negligent in [DESCRIBE CONDUCT].

7.  Defendant's negligence caused Plaintiff's injuries.

[PRAYER]

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff requests:
    a. Compensatory damages;
    b. Medical expenses;
    c. Lost wages;
    d. Pain and suffering;
    e. [Punitive damages if applicable];
    f. Costs and attorney fees.
```

## Common Cause of Action Elements

### Breach of Contract
1. Valid contract existed
2. Plaintiff performed or was ready to perform
3. Defendant breached the contract
4. Damages resulted from the breach

### Negligence
1. Defendant owed a duty of care
2. Defendant breached that duty
3. Breach was the cause of injury
4. Plaintiff suffered damages

### Fraud/Misrepresentation
1. Defendant made a false representation
2. Defendant knew it was false (or acted recklessly)
3. Defendant intended to induce reliance
4. Plaintiff relied on the representation
5. Plaintiff suffered damages

### Unjust Enrichment
1. Plaintiff conferred a benefit on Defendant
2. Defendant knew of the benefit
3. It would be inequitable for Defendant to retain the benefit

### Conversion (Theft of Property)
1. Plaintiff owned/possessed property
2. Defendant intentionally interfered with property rights
3. Plaintiff is entitled to damages

## Prayer for Relief Options

### Monetary Relief
- Compensatory damages (actual losses)
- Liquidated damages (contract-specified)
- Prejudgment interest
- Post-judgment interest
- Attorney fees (if contract or statute allows)
- Costs of suit

### Equitable Relief
- Specific performance (compel action)
- Injunctive relief (order to stop action)
- Declaratory judgment (clarify rights)
- Rescission (cancel contract)
- Reformation (rewrite contract)

## Jurisdiction Checklist

### Federal Court
- Federal question (28 U.S.C. § 1331)
- Diversity jurisdiction (28 U.S.C. § 1332):
  - Parties from different states
  - Amount in controversy exceeds $75,000

### State Court
- General jurisdiction (most cases)
- Limited jurisdiction (small claims, probate, etc.)

### Venue
- Where defendant resides
- Where events occurred
- Where property is located
