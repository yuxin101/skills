# Phase 3 — Brand Chain

**Purpose:** From "users use you" to "users remember you and recommend you."
**Prerequisite:** Phase 1 complete, at least one real piece of user feedback (payment not required).
**Preceding file:** Read `~/opc-guide/bmc-[project-name]-[date].md`
**Dual entry point:**
- Has real users → build brand from real feedback
- No real users → build hypothetical brand from Phase 1 persona (marked "needs real user validation")
**Output:** Brand positioning document + word-of-mouth language

---

## Read Preceding File

Before starting, read the user's BMC document:

```
~/opc-guide/bmc-[project-name]-[date].md
```

Extract Q1 (who pays) and Q3 (your solution) as the starting point for brand positioning.

---

## Entry Point Check

Ask at the start:

> "Do you have real users using your product right now? Even one person counts."

- **Yes** → enter the real brand chain
- **No, only an idea** → enter the hypothetical brand chain, but mark each section "needs validation"

---

## Link 1: How Do Users Talk About You?

> "When a user goes home and mentions you to their partner or colleague, what do they say?"

Not product features. What do they **feel**?

**Follow-up:**

> "What is the one thing about your product that surprises them most?"

**Red flags:**
- "Users say it's really good" → "What does 'good' mean? What are their exact words?"

**If no real users:**

> "What do you guess they would say?" → mark as "hypothetical, needs real user validation"

---

## Link 2: Three Words

> "If your brand could only be described with three words, what are they?"

Not "professional", "efficient", "innovative" — anyone can use those.

Only words that belong to **you**.

**Follow-up:**

> "Do your competitors also use these three words?"

> "Of these three words, which one is in your bones, and which one are you trying to project?"

**Red flags:**
- All three words can be found on competitors' websites → not a brand, industry standard

**If no real users:**

> "These three words are your promise to users. After they try the product, will they认同 these three words?" → mark as "needs validation"

---

## Link 3: Who Is Your Enemy?

> "Who do you NOT serve? Whose money do you choose not to take?"

Not "everyone".

Specific, nameable people you'd rather lose than serve.

**Follow-up:**

> "Is there one person you think of where your first instinct is 'that person is not my customer'?"

**Red flags:**
- "Everyone is my customer" → no brand, no positioning

---

## Link 4: Word-of-Mouth Language

> "If a user recommends you to a friend who has the same problem, what do they say?"

Give me one sentence they would **actually say** — not advertising copy.

**Follow-up:**

> "Does your name appear in that sentence? Or do they describe the problem so vaguely they can't remember who solved it for them?"

Format:

```
Word-of-mouth language: [the exact sentence a user would say]
```

**If no real users:**

> "What do you hope they'd say? Then go find them and ask if they actually say it." → mark as "needs validation"

## Connection to BMC

Brand doesn't come from nowhere — it grows from the BMC:

- **User persona** ← BMC Q1 (who pays)
- **Value proposition** ← BMC Q3 (your solution)
- **Word-of-mouth** ← BMC Q5 (how to find users)
- **Who your enemy is** ← BMC Q4 (why you) determines who you don't serve

The brand document should reference the corresponding BMC modules.

## Closing

- **Strongest signal:** From Phase 1 to Phase 3, what is the gap between how users see you and how you see yourself?
- **One action:** How do you get the next user to say the word-of-mouth language you want?
- **Optional invitation:** "BMC, execution, brand — all three chains are done. What is the next thing?"
