# Mission & Purpose Design

## Core Concept

The defining advantage of the most impactful builders isn't intelligence,
capital, or luck -- it's purpose. A bold enough mission creates
gravitational pull, attracting talent, capital, and attention as a natural
consequence.

**Bold mission -> Best talent -> Better products -> More capital ->
Next impossible project -> repeat**

The opposite is also true: incremental goals attract incremental talent
and produce incremental results.

## The Purpose Spectrum

Help the user locate where they are:

| Level | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Job** | Trading time for money | "I need to pay rent" |
| **Career** | Building skills and status | "I want to become VP of Eng" |
| **Calling** | Work is the mission itself | "I'm building X because the world needs it" |
| **Civilizational** | The mission transcends you | "Make humanity multiplanetary" |

Most people operate at Job or Career level. The framework's goal is to
help them find their Calling -- or at least move one level up.

## Step-by-Step Application

### Step 1: The Dissatisfaction Audit

Ask: "What specifically frustrates you about your current work? Not
vague feelings -- concrete things that bother you."

Common patterns:
- "I'm not learning anything new" -> Growth stagnation
- "Nothing I do matters" -> Impact deficit
- "I'm doing it for the money" -> Misaligned incentives
- "I could be replaced by anyone" -> Uniqueness gap
- "I don't care about the outcome" -> Purpose void

### Step 2: The Obsession Inventory

Ask: "What problems do you think about even when nobody's paying you
to? What do you read about for fun? What makes you angry when you see
it done wrong?"

Help the user identify 3-5 obsessions. Real purpose usually lives at
the intersection of:
- What you can't stop thinking about
- What you're unusually good at (or could become good at)
- What the world actually needs (and would pay for)

### Step 3: The Scale Test

For each obsession, ask: "If you succeeded completely at solving this
problem, how many people would it affect? How would the world be
different?"

This isn't about grandiosity -- it's about finding work where success
has compounding returns. A mission that serves 1,000 people might be
fulfilling. A mission that serves 1 billion creates gravitational pull.

### Step 4: The 10-Year Gut Check

Ask: "Imagine working on this problem for the next 10 years. Some years
will be miserable -- funding dries up, teams quit, critics pile on.
Does the mission still feel worth it?"

If yes -> strong purpose signal.
If no -> probably a passing interest, not a purpose.

### Step 5: The Mission Statement

Help the user craft a single sentence:

**"I will [action] so that [outcome for others]."**

Rules:
- Must be specific enough to guide daily decisions
- Must be large enough to not be "completable" in a year
- Must make the user slightly uncomfortable with its ambition
- Must focus on impact for others, not personal gain

**Key distinction**: "Don't make the thing to make the money. Make the
money so that you can make the thing." The mission comes first; the
business model serves the mission, not the other way around.

### Step 6: The First Move

Purpose without action is daydreaming. Ask:

"What is the smallest possible step you could take this week that moves
you toward this mission?"

The goal isn't to quit your job tomorrow. It's to create momentum.
Start before the world is ready. Don't wait for market validation --
when Tesla started, nobody said they wanted an electric car.

## Important Nuances

- **Purpose != passion.** Passion is about how you feel. Purpose is about
  what you're willing to endure. Purpose is more durable.

- **Purpose can evolve.** You don't need to find your "one true calling"
  today. You need a direction worth moving toward. It will sharpen.

- **The intensity trade-off is real.** Extreme purpose often comes with
  extreme personal cost. Be honest about this. Not everyone wants or
  should want civilizational-scale ambition. A fulfilling calling that
  lets you be present with your family is a valid and excellent outcome.

- **Purpose without competence is delusion.** Help users be honest about
  their current skill level and what they'd need to develop.

- **Create more than you consume.** The economy is positive-sum. A
  zero-sum mindset leads to morally questionable behavior. Purpose should
  be about expanding the pie, not claiming a larger slice.
