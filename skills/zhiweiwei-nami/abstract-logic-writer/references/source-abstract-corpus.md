## 1. Orbital edge service orchestration

Driven by the expansion of Low-Earth Orbit (LEO) constellations and on-orbit virtualization, orbital edge computing has emerged as a viable paradigm for ubiquitous space services. Existing service orchestration approaches typically adopt a resource-centric perspective, prioritizing physical resource metrics over high-level logical dependencies, potentially impacting the service continuity amidst the dynamic satellite networks.
To bridge this gap, this paper introduces a task-centric collaborative service orchestration framework, uniquely utilizing mission semantics as the persistent governing signal.
It operates in two stages. First, to anchor allocation to stable logical constraints, an intent parsing mechanism translates abstract mission goals into logical Service Function Chains (SFCs). A BERT-based network fuses semantic signals with resource competition states to decouple potential functional conflicts among concurrent tasks.
Then, a bounded neuro-symbolic search executes task-context-driven SFC embedding. By symbolically modeling service execution and employing a Graph Neural Network (GNN) with a semantic penalty term, the framework shields service continuity from physical resource fluctuations. Simulations on a 108-satellite Walker Delta constellation demonstrate that this task-centric approach maintains high service robustness under severe resource contention and topological shifts, achieving the millisecond-level planning latency required for real-time orbital orchestration.

## 2. Low-altitude foundation model

Low-altitude aerial missions require multiple UAVs to execute long-horizon workflows under explicit safety, regulatory, and resource constraints. These operations are inherently nonstationary: traffic and environmental conditions vary online, while airspace rules and mission procedures evolve over time, altering feasible actions and state-transition regularities. Classical symbolic planners enforce preconditions and effects but demand extensive domain modeling that transfers poorly across procedures; LLM-based planners offer flexible interfaces yet struggle to provide state-consistent feasibility under edge-scale latency and compute budgets. We present \textbf{LaFo}, a \textbf{L}ow-\textbf{a}ltitude \textbf{Fo}undation model that couples symbolic action schemas with a compact graph-transformer prior over state transitions. LaFo maps heterogeneous domains into a Relation-Reified Semantic Graph and employs a Semantics-Guided Graph State Encoder to align entities and operators via hierarchical knowledge. {\color{blue}Pretrained via goal-conditioned next-state prediction on 9 IPC domains, LaFo achieves state-of-the-art overall performance. Furthermore, when deployed as an $A^{*}$ heuristic in nonstationary UAV tasks, LaFo exceeds the success rates and systematically minimizes node expansions to maximize search efficiency, operating under a compact 1.5GB footprint and sub-0.3s forward latency for resilient edge execution.

## 3. Lightweight cloud-native satellite system

The rapid commercialization of LEO satellite Internet is transforming constellations into service platforms that must support low-latency, resilient, and continuously evolving networked applications.
However, realizing this vision is hindered by the rigidity of traditional satellite architectures, where tightly coupled software lifecycles struggle to adapt to the intermittent connectivity and strict SWaP (i.e., size, weight, and power) constraints of the orbital edge. To bridge this gap, this article proposes a distributed lightweight cloud-native satellite system. By synthesizing dynamic resource virtualization with partition-tolerant container orchestration, the proposed architecture constructs a resilient execution substrate that effectively masks the inherent volatility of physical resources and topology. Complementing this stability, a knowledge-driven governance mechanism is introduced to internalize the control plane, leveraging lightweight neuro-symbolic reasoning to autonomously translate mission intents into optimal service chains. This full-stack integration decouples logical service continuity from physical link intermittency, outlining a viable path toward future self-optimizing, multi-tenant constellation ecosystems.

## 4. Aerial Environment and Rules Ontology

Low-altitude autonomous systems must reason over physical environments, information quality, and legal constraints. We present the Aerial Environment and Rules Ontology (AERO), a modular RDF/OWL ontology suite that integrates these domains through an AERO core aligned with GeoSPARQL, OWL-Time, SOSA/SSN and PROV-O. The release includes four Turtle files covering the core schema and three domain modules for physical, informational and legal-regulatory knowledge. We also provide an LLM-driven, human-in-the-loop workflow and scripts for ontology extension and knowledge-graph instantiation. Technical examples illustrate how the ontology can support consistent knowledge extraction from regulatory and standard documents and structured retrieval for constraint-aware planning. The ontologies and code are openly available in public repositories to facilitate reuse in knowledge graphs, retrieval-augmented agents and safety analysis for low-altitude operations.

## 5. LLM-SuSA

The proliferation of Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite constellations demands effective Spectrum Utility Situational Awareness (SuSA). However, traditional Artificial Intelligence models exhibit poor performance when applied to new scenarios, as they fail to capture the relationships between non-quantifiable data. To address this limitation, we propose a novel semantic-enhanced framework, dubbed LLM-SuSA, which uses a Large Language Model (LLM) fine-tuned with Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) as a domain-specific semantic encoder. This LLM converts heterogeneous textual inputs into rich semantic vectors. Then the vectors were concatenated with numerical data and processed by an enhanced Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) pipeline to predict key performance indicators (KPIs). Experiments show that our model significantly outperforms both numerical-only baselines and semantic baselines without domain-specific fine-tuning. Our work validates that a domain-adapted LLM encoder effectively enhances the accuracy and generalization of SuSA models for dynamic satellite environments.

## 6. LoGe-Planner

Complex aerial operations necessitate the tight coupling of discrete task sequencing and continuous trajectory generation, collectively known as Task and Motion Planning (TAMP). However, the stochastic dynamics and high-density interactions of low-altitude environments often fracture this coupling, leading to brittle solution performance. To reconcile mission dependencies with flight dynamics, we propose {LoGe-Planner}, a multi-tier LLM-collaborative framework bridging logic abstraction with geometric grounding. Recognizing the combinatorial explosion of high-density operations, LoGe-Planner first uses a cloud-hosted Hypotheses-to-Theories (HtT) module to extract context-aware, auditable decomposition rules from exploration traces, and applies them to partition a global mission dependency graph into causality-preserving macro-tasks. This decomposition reduces long-horizon combinatorics while maintaining precedence constraints. An edge-deployed neuro-symbolic TAMP solver then grounds each macro-task into dynamically feasible, geometrically verified trajectories under time-varying resource occupancy. Finally, a terminal Tiny-LLM, parameter-efficiently adapted via LoRA, performs embodied anomaly interpretation from onboard state signals and triggers targeted replanning when environmental or platform conditions deviate from the current plan. Across five simulated urban-operation scenarios with infrastructure bottlenecks and stochastic disturbances, extensive simulations demonstrate that LoGe-Planner maintains a 95\% success rate and reduces planning latency by 3.5 times.

## 7. AirFogSim

Vehicular Fog Computing (VFC) is significantly enhancing the efficiency, safety, and computational capabilities of Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS), and the integration of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) further elevates these advantages by incorporating flexible and auxiliary services. This evolving UAV-integrated VFC paradigm opens new doors while presenting unique complexities within the cooperative computation framework. Foremost among the challenges, modeling the intricate dynamics of aerial-ground interactive computing networks is a significant endeavor, and the absence of a comprehensive and flexible simulation platform may impede the exploration of this field. Inspired by the pressing need for a versatile tool, this paper provides a lightweight and modular aerial-ground collaborative simulation platform, termed \texttt{AirFogSim}. We present the design and implementation of AirFogSim, and demonstrate its versatility with five key missions in the domain of UAV-integrated VFC. A multifaceted use case is carried out to validate AirFogSim's effectiveness, encompassing several integral aspects of the proposed AirFogSim, including UAV trajectory, task offloading, resource allocation, and blockchain. In general, AirFogSim is envisioned to set a new precedent in the UAV-integrated VFC simulation, bridge the gap between theoretical design and practical validation, and pave the way for future intelligent transportation domains. Our code will be available at \url{https://github.com/ZhiweiWei-NAMI/AirFogSim}.
