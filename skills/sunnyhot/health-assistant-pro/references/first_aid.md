# Emergency First Aid Reference

## 🔴 Life-Threatening Emergencies

### CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)
**When:** Person unresponsive, not breathing

**Steps:**
1. Check responsiveness
2. Call emergency (or ask someone to)
3. Place on back
4. 30 chest compressions (center of chest, 2 inches deep)
5. 2 rescue breaths
6. Continue until help arrives or person responds

### Choking
**Signs:** Can't speak/cough, hands to throat

**Adult/Child:**
1. 5 back blows between shoulder blades
2. 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver)
3. Repeat until object expelled

**Infant:**
1. 5 back blows
2. 5 chest compressions
3. Repeat

### Severe Bleeding
1. Apply direct pressure
2. Elevate wounded area
3. Do not remove blood-soaked cloth
4. Apply more pressure if needed
5. Call emergency

### Heart Attack
**Signs:** Chest pain, arm/jaw pain, sweating, nausea

**Action:**
1. Call emergency immediately
2. Have them sit/lie down
3. Give aspirin if not allergic
4. CPR if unresponsive

### Stroke (FAST Test)
- **F**ace: Smile uneven?
- **A**rm: One arm weak?
- **S**peech: Slurred?
- **T**ime: Call emergency immediately

## 🟡 Common Emergencies

### Fever

**Adults:**
- <38°C: Rest, fluids
- 38-39°C: Rest, fluids, fever reducer
- >39°C: Seek medical care
- Any fever + rash: Seek care immediately

**Children:**
- Under 3 months: Any fever >38°C = seek care
- 3-6 months: >39°C or 48h = seek care
- Over 6 months: 48h = seek care if no improvement

### Burns

**Degrees:**
- 1st: Red, painful (sunburn) - cool water, aloe
- 2nd: Blisters - don't pop, medical care
- 3rd: White/black, numbness - emergency

**Treatment:**
1. Cool water 10-20 min
2. Don't apply ice/butter
3. Cover with clean bandage
4. Pain relievers

### Sprains & Strains

**RICE Method:**
- **R**est: Don't use injured area
- **I**ce: 20 min on, 20 min off
- **C**ompression: Elastic bandage
- **E**levation: Above heart

**Seek care if:**
- Can't bear weight
- Severe swelling
- Deformed joint

### Allergic Reactions

**Mild (hives, itching):**
- Antihistamine
- Cool compress

**Severe (anaphylaxis):**
- Difficulty breathing
- Swelling of face/throat
- **Use epinephrine if available**
- Call emergency immediately

### Dehydration

**Signs:**
- Dark urine
- Dry mouth
- Dizziness
- Headache

**Treatment:**
- Small sips of water/ORC
- Electrolyte solutions
- Avoid plain water alone if severe

## 🟢 Minor Issues

### Headaches

**Tension:**
- Rest, dark room
- Over-counter pain reliever
- Massage neck/shoulders

**Migraine:**
- Rest in quiet room
- Cold pack
- Pain reliever + anti-nausea

**Seek care:**
- Worst headache ever
- With fever/stiff neck
- After head injury

### Cold & Flu

**Rest & Fluids**
**Symptom relief:**
- Runny nose: Antihistamine
- Congestion: Decongestant
- Sore throat: Warm liquids, honey
- Fever: Acetaminophen/ISeek carebuprofen

**:**
- Over 3 days fever
- Difficulty breathing
- Dehydration

### Upset Stomach

**Causes:** Food, stress, virus

**Treatment:**
- BRAT diet (Banana, Rice, Applesauce, Toast)
- Clear liquids
- Avoid dairy, fatty foods

**Seek care:**
- Blood in stool/vomit
- Severe abdominal pain
- Over 2 days

## 📱 When to Seek Care

### Call Emergency (120)
- Chest pain
- Difficulty breathing
- Severe bleeding
- Stroke symptoms
- Severe allergic reaction
- Unconscious

### Seek Care Today
- High fever
- Severe pain
- Dehydration
- Symptoms not improving

### Self-Care OK
- Minor cuts
- Mild cold
- Temporary fatigue
- General discomfort

---

*First aid is not a substitute for professional medical care. When in doubt, seek professional help.*
