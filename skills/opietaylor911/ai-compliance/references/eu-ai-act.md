# EU AI Act Reference

## Overview
Enacted 2024, applies from 2026. Risk-based framework classifying AI systems into tiers.

## Risk Tiers

### Unacceptable Risk (PROHIBITED)
- Social scoring by governments
- Real-time remote biometric surveillance in public spaces (with narrow exceptions)
- AI exploiting vulnerabilities of specific groups
- Subliminal manipulation causing harm
- Emotion recognition in workplaces/schools (with exceptions)
- Biometric categorization inferring sensitive attributes (race, political opinion, etc.)

### High Risk (Article 6 + Annex III)
Requires conformity assessment, registration in EU database, ongoing monitoring.

**Categories:**
- Biometric ID and categorization
- Critical infrastructure management
- Education/vocational training (access, assessment)
- Employment (recruitment, performance, promotion)
- Essential services (credit scoring, insurance, benefits)
- Law enforcement
- Migration/asylum/border control
- Administration of justice

**Obligations for High-Risk AI:**
- Risk management system (Article 9)
- Data governance (Article 10)
- Technical documentation (Article 11)
- Record-keeping/logging (Article 12)
- Transparency to users (Article 13)
- Human oversight measures (Article 14)
- Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity (Article 15)
- Conformity assessment before market placement
- Register in EU AI database
- Post-market monitoring
- Incident reporting to authorities

### Limited Risk
- Chatbots: must disclose AI nature
- Deepfakes: must label as AI-generated
- Emotion recognition systems: must inform users

### Minimal/No Risk
- Spam filters, AI in video games, etc.
- No specific obligations (encouraged to follow voluntary codes)

## GPAI (General Purpose AI Models)
- Providers must maintain technical documentation
- Train on copyright-compliant data
- Publish training data summaries
- Systemic risk models (>10^25 FLOPs): additional obligations including adversarial testing, incident reporting, cybersecurity measures

## Key Compliance Questions
1. What is the intended purpose of the AI system?
2. Does it fall under any prohibited use category?
3. Does it fall under Annex III high-risk categories?
4. Is the AI used in an EU context or affecting EU residents?
5. Is the provider an EU entity or placing product on EU market?
6. Is human oversight in place?
7. Is there a risk management system?
8. Are logs maintained?
9. Is there transparency to end users?

## Penalties
- Prohibited use violations: up to €35M or 7% global turnover
- High-risk non-compliance: up to €15M or 3% global turnover
- Providing incorrect information: up to €7.5M or 1% global turnover
