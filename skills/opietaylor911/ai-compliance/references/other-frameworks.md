# Other AI Compliance Frameworks

## OECD AI Principles (2019, updated 2024)
Adopted by 46+ countries. Five principles:
1. **Inclusive growth** — AI should benefit people and planet
2. **Human-centred values** — respect rule of law, human rights, democratic values
3. **Transparency & explainability** — stakeholders should understand AI decisions
4. **Robustness, security, safety** — AI should be secure and safe throughout lifecycle
5. **Accountability** — AI actors should be accountable for proper functioning

**Compliance questions:**
- Are AI systems designed with human rights in mind?
- Is there explainability for decisions affecting individuals?
- Are accountability chains documented?

## GDPR (AI Context)
Applies when AI processes personal data of EU residents.

**Key obligations for AI:**
- Lawful basis for processing (Article 6)
- Data minimization — only use data necessary for the purpose
- Purpose limitation — don't reuse data for other AI training without consent
- Right to explanation for automated decisions (Article 22)
- DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment) required for high-risk processing
- Privacy by design and by default
- Data retention limits

**Compliance questions:**
- Does the AI process personal data?
- Is there a lawful basis for that processing?
- Does the AI make automated decisions affecting individuals?
- Has a DPIA been conducted?
- Are data subjects informed?

## IEEE Ethically Aligned Design
Voluntary principles for AI ethics:
- Human wellbeing as a priority metric
- Transparency in autonomous systems
- Accountability — clear chain of responsibility
- Avoiding misuse through access controls

## UK AI Safety Framework
- Voluntary for now (post-AI Safety Summit)
- Focus on frontier/systemic risk models
- Safety evaluations before deployment of powerful models
- Incident reporting encouraged

## Singapore Model AI Governance Framework
- Practical implementation guidance
- Internal governance structures
- Determining human involvement in AI decisions
- Operations management (data, model risk)
- Customer relationship management

## Key Cross-Framework Themes
These appear across ALL major frameworks — address these first:
1. **Risk assessment** before deployment
2. **Human oversight** of consequential AI decisions
3. **Transparency** to users that they are interacting with AI
4. **Data governance** — quality, provenance, minimization
5. **Accountability** — named owners for AI systems
6. **Monitoring** — ongoing performance and incident tracking
7. **Documentation** — lifecycle records, decisions, assessments
8. **Fairness/bias** — evaluate and mitigate discriminatory outcomes
