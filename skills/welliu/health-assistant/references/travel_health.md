# Travel Health Preparation Checklist

## Pre-Trip Planning (2-4 weeks before)

### ☎️ Documents
- [ ] Travel insurance policy
- [ ] Emergency contacts list
- [ ] Medical history summary
- [ ] Prescription medications (extra supply)
- [ ] Doctor's note for medications
- [ ] Vaccination records
- [ ] Blood type card
- [ ] Allergies list

### 💊 Medications Kit
#### Essential
- [ ] Regular prescription medications (10+ day supply)
- [ ] Pain relievers (ibuprofen/paracetamol)
- [ ] Antihistamines
- [ ] Anti-diarrheal medication
- [ ] Rehydration salts
- [ ] Motion sickness pills

#### First Aid
- [ ] Band-aids/adhesive bandages
- [ ] Antiseptic wipes
- [ ] Antibiotic ointment
- [ ] Tweezers
- [ ] Scissors
- [ ] Thermometer

#### Destination-Specific
- [ ] Altitude sickness medication
- [ ] Malaria prevention (if needed)
- [ ] Sun protection
- [ ] Insect repellent
- [ ] Water purification tablets

### 🧳 Clothing
- [ ] Weather-appropriate clothing
- [ ] Comfortable walking shoes
- [ ] Extra layers (airplane is cold)
- [ ] Rain gear/umbrella
- [ ] Sun hat
- [ ] Sunglasses
- [ ] Sleep mask/earplugs (flight)

## During Travel

### 💧 Hydration
- Drink bottled water
- Avoid ice from tap water
- Limit caffeine/alcohol
- Eat water-rich foods

### 🍽️ Food Safety
- Eat well-cooked foods
- Avoid raw salads (if water unsafe)
- Peel fruits yourself
- Avoid street food (if unsure)
- Wash hands frequently

### 🏃 Activity
- Stretch during long flights
- Walk every 2-3 hours
- Stay active on layovers
- Adjust to local time gradually

## Post-Trip

### ⚠️ Watch For
- Fever
- Diarrhea (persistent)
- Skin rashes
- Unusual fatigue
- Any symptoms lasting >1 week

### 📋 Re-Establish Routine
- Resume normal medication schedule
- Check if any prescriptions need refills
- Note any health changes to discuss with doctor
