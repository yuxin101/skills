# MLM Objection Handling — 50+ Responses

## Money Objections

### "I don't have the money"
- "I totally understand. Most people don't have extra cash lying around. What if I showed you how to get started for less than you think, and how most people earn back their initial investment quickly?"
- "That was me too. Can I show you the payment options? Many people start smaller and upgrade as they see results."

### "It's too expensive"
- "Compared to what? If this could replace your income, what would that be worth?"
- "I get it. What if we looked at it as an investment rather than an expense? What do you currently spend monthly on [coffee/dining out/subscriptions]?"

### "I can't afford to lose money"
- "That's smart thinking. What if there was a satisfaction guarantee? You can try the product risk-free."
- "I felt the same way. That's why I started part-time while keeping my job. No risk to my income."

## Time Objections

### "I don't have time"
- "Neither did I — that's exactly WHY I started. I needed something flexible that worked around my schedule. Can I show you how I fit it in with just 5-10 hours a week?"
- "What if you could build this in the pockets of time you already have — lunch breaks, commute, evenings after kids are in bed?"

### "I'm too busy right now"
- "When would be a better time? In 6 months, will you be less busy, or will you just be 6 months older and still busy?"
- "I hear that a lot. Most successful people I know are busy. That's actually a good sign — you know how to get things done."

### "Maybe later"
- "I respect that. What would need to change for 'later' to become 'now'?"
- "Totally fair. Can I check back in 30 days? What would be the best way to reach you?"

## Skepticism Objections

### "Is this a pyramid scheme?"
- "Great question — I asked the same thing. In a pyramid, money flows up with no real product. We sell [product] that people actually buy and use because they love it. I get paid when customers purchase, not just when I recruit."
- "I understand the concern. The difference is simple: we have real products, real customers, and you can earn more than the person who recruited you."

### "Is this one of those things?"
- "Ha! I know what you mean. I was skeptical too. What specifically concerns you? Let me address that directly."
- "If you mean network marketing, yes. If you mean a scam, absolutely not. Let me show you the difference."

### "I've tried MLM before and it didn't work"
- "I appreciate you sharing that. What company was it? What do you think went wrong?"
- "That's actually valuable experience. What would need to be different this time for you to succeed?"

### "These things never work"
- "For some people, you're right. But for others, it's life-changing. The difference is usually the system and support. Want to see how our approach is different?"
- "I used to think that too. Then I met people actually making it work. What would it take to show you this is different?"

### "I don't believe in MLM"
- "I respect that. Can I ask what specifically you don't believe in? The products, the business model, or something else?"
- "Fair enough. What if this wasn't MLM — what if it was just a great product you could share and earn from? Would that interest you?"

## Personal Objections

### "I'm not a salesperson"
- "Perfect — neither am I! This isn't about selling. It's about sharing something you believe in. Do you ever recommend restaurants or movies to friends?"
- "The best people in this business aren't salespeople. They're just authentic and share what they love."

### "I don't know enough people"
- "You know more than you think. Let's make a list together — I bet we get to 50 names in 15 minutes."
- "That's fine — this business helps you MEET people. I've made more friends in the last year than the previous five."

### "I'm not good at this"
- "How do you know? Have you tried? The skills are learnable, and I'll teach you everything."
- "You don't have to be good — you just have to be coachable. I'll walk you through it."

### "My spouse won't support it"
- "I totally understand — my spouse was skeptical too at first. Would it help if we did a call together so they can ask questions?"
- "What specifically are they concerned about? Maybe I can help address that."

### "I don't want to bug my friends"
- "I felt the same way. Here's the thing — you're not bugging them, you're offering them an opportunity. If a friend told you about a great investment, would you feel bugged?"
- "What if I told you there's a way to share this without being pushy? It's all about the approach."

### "People will judge me"
- "Some might. But others will respect you for taking action on your goals. Who do you want to spend your energy on?"
- "I worried about that too. Turns out, the people who judge you weren't going to support you anyway."

## Product Objections

### "I don't need the product"
- "That's fine — not everyone does. But do you know anyone who might? You can build a business just by connecting people."
- "What if you tried it first? Most people who become successful started as customers who loved the product."

### "I can find something cheaper"
- "You might find something cheaper, but will it be the same quality? Let me show you what makes this different."
- "Price is important. So is value. Let me show you the cost-per-use and the benefits you're getting."

### "I'm happy with what I use"
- "That's great! What do you currently use? I'm curious how it compares."
- "Totally fair. Would you be open to trying this alongside what you use and seeing the difference?"

## Business Model Objections

### "Only people at the top make money"
- "I hear that a lot. In our comp plan, you can actually earn more than the person who recruited you. Want me to show you how?"
- "That's true in some companies. In ours, it's based on what you actually do, not when you joined."

### "I'd rather have a real job"
- "I respect that. What if this could be your side income while you keep your job? Extra $500-1000/month?"
- "This can BE a real job for some people. But for most, it's just a great side hustle."

### "The market is saturated"
- "People have said that about every successful company. There's always room for someone who works hard and serves people well."
- "Do you know anyone personally who does this? If not, that's your answer — it's not saturated in YOUR network."

## Delay Tactics

### "Send me more information"
- "I'd be happy to. What specifically would you like to know more about? That way I can send you exactly what's helpful."
- "Sure — what's the best email? And when should I follow up to discuss it?"

### "Let me do some research"
- "Great idea. What specifically do you want to research? I might be able to point you in the right direction."
- "Perfect. How long do you need? I'll check back [specific day]."

### "I need to pray about it"
- "I respect that completely. When do you think you'll have clarity? I'll follow up then."
- "Absolutely. Would it help to know more about the company's values and mission while you're praying?"

### "I need to think about it"
- "Of course. What specifically do you want to think through? I might be able to help with that right now."
- "Totally fair. What would help you make a decision? More information? Talk to someone on my team?"

## The "I Need to Check With..." Objections

### "I need to talk to my spouse"
- "Smart move. Would it help if we scheduled a call together so they can ask questions directly?"
- "What do you think they'll want to know? Let me help you prepare for that conversation."

### "I need to talk to my accountant/lawyer"
- "Great idea for any business decision. What specific questions do you think they'll have?"
- "That's wise. If they have questions about the business structure, I can connect them with our compliance team."

## Converting "No" to "Not Yet"

### "I'm not interested"
- "I appreciate you being direct. Can I ask what you ARE interested in? Maybe there's another way I can help."
- "No problem at all. Would it be okay if I checked back in a few months? Things change."
- "Totally respect that. If anything changes, you have my number."

### "Stop contacting me"
- "I apologize if I came on too strong. I'll respect your wishes. Thanks for your time."
- (Note: Always respect this — remove from follow-up list immediately)

## Power Phrases

- "I totally understand — I felt the same way before I started."
- "Great question — a lot of people ask that."
- "What would it take to get you to yes?"
- "If [objection] wasn't an issue, would you be interested?"
- "I hear you. Can I ask one more question?"
- "That's exactly why I thought of you."
