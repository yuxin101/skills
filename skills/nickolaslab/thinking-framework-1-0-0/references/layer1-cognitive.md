# Layer 1 — Cognitive Architecture

This layer maps the conscious, observable cognitive structure:
how the target thinks, decides, frames problems, and processes uncertainty.
Apply to every framework load before presenting the Framework Card.

---

## C1 — Mental Models

**What to find:** The 2–5 core maps the target uses to explain reality.
Not what they *say* they believe — what their decisions *reveal* they believe.

**Excavation method:**
1. Identify the target's domain of mastery — that's usually where their primary model lives
2. Look for cross-domain applications: does the model migrate? (e.g., a biologist who applies evolution to business)
3. Find the *master model* — the one lens that colors all others
4. Note which models are explicitly stated vs. inferred from behavioral pattern

**Model families to test:**
- **Systems / Feedback**: Does the target see loops, emergence, unintended consequences?
- **Statistical / Probabilistic**: Do they reason in base rates, distributions, sample errors?
- **Evolutionary**: Selection pressure, adaptation, environment fit, survival logic?
- **Physics / Engineering**: First principles, constraints, energy/leverage, entropy?
- **Economic**: Opportunity cost, incentives, marginal utility, revealed preference?
- **Historical**: Cycles, pattern recognition, Lindy effect, what survives time?
- **Psychological**: Bias awareness, motivation structures, social dynamics?
- **Game-theoretic**: Payoff matrices, equilibria, strategic interaction?

**Output:**
```
PRIMARY MODEL   : [Name] — [How applied] — [Why it dominates their thinking]
SECONDARY MODELS: [Model] — [Domain where invoked]
MASTER LENS     : [The one that colors everything else]
BORROWED FROM   : [Which disciplines / fields]
```

## C2 — Decision Heuristics

**What to find:** The fast rules that drive choices, especially under time pressure
or incomplete information. These are the cognitive shortcuts that are so deeply
internalized they often operate below conscious awareness.

**How to identify them:**
1. Find 5+ documented decisions by the target
2. Extract the common denominator — what principle connects them?
3. Test: "If they faced a new situation, what would they default to?"
4. Find the *negative space*: what do they consistently refuse or avoid?

**Quality test for a heuristic:**
"Would this rule actually change someone's decision in a real situation?"
If no → too vague. Keep sharpening.

**Common heuristic structures:**
- Inversion: "How would I guarantee failure?" → do the opposite
- Pre-mortem: "Assume it failed in 5 years. Why?" → fix those now
- 10/10/10: "How will I feel about this in 10 min, 10 months, 10 years?"
- Second-order thinking: "And then what? And then what?"
- Asymmetry filter: "Is the upside worth the downside, even if unlikely?"
- Survivorship test: "Would this still work if I strip out the luck?"
- Simplicity filter: "Can a smart 12-year-old understand this?"

**Output per heuristic:**
```
HEURISTIC   : "[The rule as an actionable principle]"
Triggers on : [What situation activates it]
Evidence    : [Where this appears in documented behavior or statements]
Exception   : [When they override it — and why]
```

## C3 — Utility Function

**What to find:** What the target is *actually* optimizing for.
This is almost never fully stated. It must be reverse-engineered from
trade-offs — what they sacrificed to get what.

**The hidden utility problem:**
Many targets state altruistic utility (humanity, society, truth) while
behavioral evidence reveals something more personal (legacy, dominance,
validation, escaping a specific fear). Surface BOTH. The tension is data.

**Excavation method:**
1. Find their most costly trade-offs: what did they give up that most people wouldn't?
2. Find their non-negotiables: what did they refuse to trade, ever?
3. Find their regret patterns: what losses genuinely bothered them?
4. Find their enemies: who/what do they push against hardest? (Anti-utility reveals utility)

**Output:**
```
STATED UTILITY  : [What they say they're optimizing for]
REVEALED UTILITY: [What trade-offs reveal they actually optimize for]
HIDDEN UTILITY  : [The unspoken driver — inferred from behavioral evidence]
ANTI-UTILITY    : [What they demonstrably refuse to optimize for, and why]
```

## C4 — Problem Framing

**What to find:** How the target *redefines the question* before answering it.
The most powerful thinkers don't just answer better — they ask different.

**Signature reframing moves to identify:**
```
First Principles    : Strip analogies; what are the irreducible components?
Inversion           : "How would I guarantee failure here?"
Scale Shift         : Zoom out to civilization or in to unit economics
Stakeholder Flip    : Whose perspective is structurally excluded from this framing?
Time Compression    : "What does this look like in 20 years? In 200?"
Constraint Flip     : "What if this limitation is the actual asset?"
Absurdist Extension : "What if we took this logic to its absolute extreme?"
Question the Question: "Why are we assuming this is the right problem?"
Counterfactual      : "What would the world look like if X hadn't happened?"
```

**Output:**
```
SIGNATURE MOVE   : [Their most characteristic reframing technique]
ABSTRACTION LEVEL: [Where they prefer to operate: tactical / strategic / meta]
WHAT THEY IGNORE : [Aspects of standard framing they systematically drop]
CANONICAL EXAMPLE: [A known case where their reframing led to a breakthrough]
```

## C5 — Risk & Uncertainty Posture

**What to find:** How the target relates to the unknown, uncontrollable, and unpredictable.
This dimension separates thinkers more than almost any other.

### 5a. Risk Profile
```
ASYMMETRY SEEKING   : Do they hunt bets where upside >> downside?
LOSS AVERSION LEVEL : Strong (avoids losses hard) / Moderate / Low (accepts losses as data)
LEVERAGE COMFORT    : High amplification / Moderate / Conservative / None
UNCERTAINTY TOLERANCE: Can they act with 40% information, or need 80%?
RUIN SENSITIVITY    : How paranoid about existential / irreversible outcomes?
```

### 5b. Uncertainty Processing
```
HOW THEY MEASURE  : Probability estimates / Scenario planning / Intuition / Experiments
HOW THEY REDUCE   : Data gathering / Waiting / Small bets / Building optionality
HOW THEY ACT      : What's their decision threshold under high uncertainty?
HOW THEY UPDATE   : Fast pivot on new data / Slow to change / Resistant?
```

### 5c. Tail Risk
```
WORST-CASE AWARENESS: Do they explicitly model catastrophic scenarios?
PROTECTION BEHAVIOR : What do they hedge at all costs?
RECOVERY MINDSET    : Prevention-first or resilience-first?
```

## C6 — Time Horizon Architecture

**What to find:** How the target weights present vs. future — and the structural
reasons (wealth, beliefs, temperament, institutional backing) that enable their
time horizon.

```
PRIMARY HORIZON  : Immediate / Tactical (months) / Strategic (years) / Generational / Civilizational
DECISION HORIZON : How far ahead do decisions extend?
FEEDBACK PATIENCE: How long will they wait for evidence of correctness?
SHORT-TERM STANCE: How do they navigate near-term pressure / noise?
COMPOUNDING LOGIC: Do they explicitly think in compound effects over time?
ENABLING FACTOR  : What structural advantage lets them hold this horizon?
```

## C7 — Contradiction & Paradox Architecture

**What to find:** The apparently inconsistent positions the target holds simultaneously
— and the higher-order logic that makes them coherent.

Great thinkers don't resolve paradoxes. They hold them as *productive tension*.
Identifying the paradox structure reveals more about the framework than any single position.

**Common paradox patterns:**
```
Simplicity ↔ Depth        (simple rules driven by profound understanding)
Patience ↔ Urgency        (long horizon, explosive short-term execution)
Confidence ↔ Doubt        (strong conviction, genuinely open to update)
Empathy ↔ Detachment      (deeply understands others, not moved by them)
Control ↔ Trust           (designs tight systems, then trusts them fully)
Humility ↔ Ambition       (minimal ego claims, civilization-scale goals)
Consistency ↔ Adaptation  (principled, yet radically context-sensitive)
Scarcity ↔ Abundance      (operates from scarcity logic in some domains, abundance in others)
```

**Output:**
```
CORE PARADOX     : [The central tension — stated as a true paradox, not just "they balance X and Y"]
RECONCILING LOGIC: [How they justify or inhabit this tension]
UNACKNOWLEDGED   : [Contradictions they don't seem aware of — feeds into blind spots]
```
