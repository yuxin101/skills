# Social Distribution Posts

After the content is written, propose a **LinkedIn post** and a **Twitter/X post** to promote it. These are standalone pieces of content that create enough curiosity or value to drive clicks — not summaries.

## LinkedIn post (1200-1500 characters)

- Open with the strongest hook from the article (rewritten for LinkedIn's feed format)
- Give 30-40% of the value upfront (enough to be useful on its own)
- End with a reason to read the full issue (the part LinkedIn doesn't give them)
- Add a CTA: link to the Substack issue
- Line breaks after every 1-2 sentences (LinkedIn feed rewards visual whitespace)
- No hashtags unless the user specifically wants them (developer audiences find them performative)

## Twitter/X post (single tweet or 2-3 tweet thread)

**Single tweet version:** the most surprising or counterintuitive claim from the article + link.

**Thread version** (if the article has multiple strong hooks):

- Tweet 1: Hook with the boldest claim. No link yet.
- Tweet 2-3: One key insight per tweet, standalone value.
- Final tweet: CTA + link to the full issue.
- Keep threads short (3-4 tweets max). Long threads have steep drop-off.
- Use the article's data points, numbers, or counter-narrative angles as tweet fuel.

## Adaptation rules (both platforms)

- Don't announce ("I just published..."). Lead with the insight, not the meta-action.
- If ghostwriting, match the client's social voice (which may differ from their newsletter voice).
- Each post should work even if the reader never clicks the link. The link is a bonus, not the point.
