# Example: B2B Physical Product

**Use this file when the product is:** a physical good, material, or component sold to businesses. Includes: packaging, ingredients, industrial supplies, raw materials, branded merchandise, equipment, hardware, specialty chemicals, contract manufacturing.

---

## Worked case: Sustainable F&B Packaging Supplier
*Compostable and recyclable packaging for US food & beverage brands (snacks, beverages, DTC food) — United States*

### Product classification
- **Model:** B2B-Physical
- **Buyer:** Procurement manager / Founder at US F&B brand (snacks, beverages, DTC food)
- **Revenue unit:** Annual packaging supply contract per brand customer
- **Geography:** US-only (domestic manufacturing + logistics)

---

### Step 1 — Revenue unit definition

The revenue unit is an annual packaging supply contract sold to a US F&B brand at **~$80,000/yr average contract value.**

**Pricing derivation:**
- Packaging typically represents 5–15% of COGS for F&B brands (Food Marketing Institute data)
- Target customer: small-to-mid F&B brand, $1M–$20M annual revenue
- Median revenue of target brand: ~$5M; packaging at 10% COGS (assuming 40% gross margin, COGS = $3M): packaging spend = ~$300K/yr total
- Our addressable share (primary pouch/bag/bottle format only): ~25–30% of total packaging spend = **$75,000–$90,000/yr → round to $80,000 ACV**
- Comp check: Hero Packaging, noissue, Elevate Packaging — contract minimums typically $15K–$50K; annual run rates for mid-size brands $60K–$120K ✓

---

### Step 2 — TAM (Bottom-up)

**Universe identification via NAICS:**
- NAICS 311 (Food manufacturing): ~27,500 US establishments — source: US Census Economic Census 2022
- NAICS 312 (Beverage manufacturing): ~5,200 US establishments — source: US Census 2022
- NAICS 445 (Specialty food retailers / DTC food brands): ~8,000 relevant brands — source: Specialty Food Association 2024
- Total universe: ~40,700 F&B manufacturers and emerging brands

**Gross TAM filter:**
- Remove very large manufacturers (Nestlé, PepsiCo, Mars, Kraft Heinz — ~200 firms) who have locked-in, multi-year Tier 1 supplier contracts not accessible to new entrants: −0.5%
- Remove micro-producers (<$250K revenue, no packaging procurement budget): −35%
- Relevant universe: 40,700 × 64.5% = **~26,250 firms**

**TAM calculation:**
```
26,250 firms × $80,000 ACV = $2.1B
```

**TAM: $2.1B** (range: $1.6B–$2.6B)

---

### Step 3 — TAM (Top-down cross-check)

**Sources:**
- Global sustainable food packaging market: $227.96B (Grand View Research, 2024)
- North America share: ~33% = $75.2B
- US share of North America: ~85% = $63.9B
- SMB/indie brand addressable portion (excluding Tier 1 CPG majors, ~8% of market): ~12% = **$7.7B**

**Top-down TAM: $7.7B**

**Divergence note:** Top-down ($7.7B) >> Bottom-up ($2.1B). The top-down figure inflates TAM because it includes all packaging types (primary, secondary, tertiary, logistics) and all customer sizes. Bottom-up is more precise — it reflects only the addressable contract opportunity within our specific format and customer tier. **Bottom-up is preferred.** Top-down used only as ceiling validation: $2.1B is well within the $7.7B ceiling, so bottom-up is internally consistent.

---

### Step 4 — SAM

**Filters applied to 26,250 relevant firms:**

| Filter | Rate | Remaining | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Active sustainability mandate or commitment | −52% | 12,600 | 360researchreports: ~48% of F&B brands have active sustainability pledges (2024) |
| Willing and able to pay sustainable packaging premium (10–30% above conventional) | −30% | 8,820 | Category data: ~70% of sustainability-pledged brands have budget for premium packaging |
| Accessible via direct sales / trade channels (Fancy Food Show, KeHE, UNFI network) | −20% | 7,056 | Est. trade show + distributor reachability |

**SAM:**
```
7,056 firms × $80,000 ACV = $564M
```

**SAM: $564M** (range: $440M–$680M)

---

### Step 5 — SOM

**Penetration benchmark:** 2–5% of SAM by Year 3 for new B2B physical product supplier (trade distribution ramp benchmarks; comparable packaging startup data)

| Year | Penetration of SAM | Revenue | Customers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | 0.4% | ~$2.3M | ~28 brands |
| Year 2 | 1.2% | ~$6.8M | ~85 brands |
| Year 3 | 2.5–4.0% | $14.1M–$22.6M | 176–282 brands |

**Comp validation:**
- **noissue** — sustainable packaging for small brands; reached $20M+ ARR within 4 years, ~3,000 brand customers
- **Hero Packaging** — compostable mailers/pouches; bootstrapped to $8M ARR Year 3 in AU/US market
- **Elevate Packaging** — certified sustainable B2B packaging; ~200 brand clients at avg. $70K–$100K contracts

**SOM (Year 3): $14M–$22.6M revenue**

---

### Key assumptions to validate

1. **$80,000 average contract value** — most sensitive assumption; derived from target brand revenue tier; validate with 20 brand procurement interviews before sales planning
2. **48% sustainability mandate rate** — from 360researchreports; refresh annually as this number is rising rapidly (was ~30% in 2021)
3. **26,250 relevant firms** — the micro-producer removal (−35%) is estimated; validate against Specialty Food Association member count and SPINS database
4. **2.5–4% Year 3 penetration** — requires trade show presence, distributor partnerships (KeHE/UNFI), and 6–8 week lead times competitive with incumbents; validate each lever independently

## B2B Physical Product general notes

### Critical difference from SaaS: the incumbent supply chain lock-in problem
For B2B physical products, the single most important SAM filter is **existing supplier relationships**. Large buyers (Tier 1 CPG companies) have multi-year contracts, volume commitments, and switching costs that make them effectively inaccessible to new entrants. Always identify and remove these locked accounts from SAM before calculating.

Rule of thumb: the top 10–20% of firms by revenue in any category control 60–80% of spend — but are the hardest to penetrate. The bottom 80–90% of firms are the accessible SAM for a new entrant.

### Preferred data sources by sub-category

| Sub-category | Firm count source | Market size source | Trade channel |
|---|---|---|---|
| F&B packaging | Census NAICS 322/326 | Grand View, Mordor, PMMI | PMMI, FPA, Pack Expo |
| Ingredients / additives | Census NAICS 311 supplier side | Mintel, IBIS | FMI, IFT |
| Industrial supplies | Census NAICS 332/333 | IBISWorld, Frost & Sullivan | NAM, MFGCON |
| Branded merchandise | Census NAICS 315/323 | ASI Research (PPAI) | PPAI, ASI |
| Specialty chemicals | Census NAICS 325 | IHS Markit, Grand View | ACC, SOCMA |
| Equipment / machinery | Census NAICS 333 | IBISWorld, Freedonia | PMPA, AMT |

### ACV benchmarks for B2B physical products
| Customer size | Annual spend (addressable) |
|---|---|
| Micro brand (<$500K revenue) | $5,000–$20,000/yr |
| Small brand ($500K–$5M revenue) | $20,000–$80,000/yr |
| Mid-market brand ($5M–$50M revenue) | $80,000–$300,000/yr |
| Large brand ($50M–$500M revenue) | $300,000–$1,500,000/yr |
| Enterprise / Tier 1 CPG ($500M+) | $1.5M–$10M+/yr (typically locked) |

### SAM filter guidance for B2B physical
| Filter | Typical reduction | How to estimate |
|---|---|---|
| Remove Tier 1 incumbents with locked supply chains | −5 to −15% of firm count, −40 to −70% of spend | Public 10-K supplier disclosures; industry concentration ratios |
| Willingness to pay premium (sustainable / certified) | −20 to −50% | Industry surveys (e.g. Nielsen ESG surveys, Euromonitor) |
| Minimum order viability (can the firm meet MOQs?) | −10 to −30% | Your own MOQ vs. target firm's typical order volume |
| Geographic / logistics reachability | −5 to −20% | Shipping zone coverage vs. firm location data |
| Trade channel access (distributor, rep network) | −10 to −25% | Trade association membership, distributor territory coverage |

### Common B2B physical product TAM/SAM errors
- **Using total category market size as TAM** — the $227B global packaging market is not the TAM for a niche sustainable pouches supplier; always filter to your format, tier, and geography first
- **Ignoring MOQ (minimum order quantity) constraints** — many small brands cannot meet industrial MOQs; this is often the binding SAM constraint for physical product startups
- **Forgetting lead time competitiveness** — if incumbents offer 2-week lead times and you offer 8 weeks, a large portion of the SAM is functionally inaccessible until operations scale
- **Double-counting when using both NAICS 311 and NAICS 445** — food manufacturers (311) and food retailers/DTC brands (445) can overlap; deduplicate carefully
