# Research Brief Generator — OpenClaw Skill

**A comprehensive research brief on any topic, case, or person — in seconds.**

For journalists, podcasters, writers, and researchers who need to get up to speed fast before diving deeper.

---

## What it does

Give it a topic. Get back a full structured brief:

- **60-second overview** — the absolute essentials, fast
- **Key facts and timeline** — chronological bullet list, disputed facts flagged
- **Key people and organisations** — who matters and why
- **Core questions** — the unanswered and contested angles worth exploring
- **Story angles and narratives** — obvious, overlooked, human, and systemic frames
- **Source directions** — where to look next (records, journalism, experts, archives)
- **Related rabbit holes** — adjacent topics to explore
- **Content warnings** — flagged where relevant

---

## Who it's for

- True crime and investigative podcasters
- Journalists researching an unfamiliar beat
- Non-fiction writers starting a new project
- Content creators who want depth before they record or write
- Anyone who needs to understand something quickly and thoroughly

## Installation

```bash
npx clawhub@latest install tetsuakira-vk/research-brief
```

---

## Usage

```
Use research-brief on: [topic, case, person, or event]
```

Examples:
```
Use research-brief on: the Jonestown massacre
Use research-brief on: Elizabeth Holmes and Theranos
Use research-brief on: the Bre-X gold mining fraud
```

---

## Features

- Works on any topic — criminal cases, historical events, people, organisations, controversies
- Disputed and unverified facts clearly flagged
- Story angles section helps identify what's been missed by mainstream coverage
- Source directions guide further research without inventing fake URLs
- Content warnings included automatically for sensitive material
- Honest about knowledge limits — won't fabricate for obscure topics

---

## Requirements

- OpenClaw installed and running
- Any supported LLM (Claude recommended for research depth)
- No API keys required

---

## Tips

- More specific inputs produce better briefs — "the 1994 Rwandan genocide" beats "genocide"
- Use the core questions section as your episode or article outline
- Use the angles section to find the story nobody else is telling
- Run it before every research session to orient yourself before going into primary sources
