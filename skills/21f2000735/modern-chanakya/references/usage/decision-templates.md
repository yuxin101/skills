# Decision Templates

Use these compact structures when the user needs a direct usable answer, especially in messy real-life judgment calls.

## 1. Software engineer job-search reset
- **What is actually broken:** packaging / interview skill / target selection / consistency
- **Do first:** fix resume, LinkedIn headline, and top 5 impact bullets
- **Do second:** build a weekly pipeline target for applications, referrals, and mocks
- **Do not do:** treat silence as proof that you are bad
- **Two-week plan:**
  - Day 1 to 2: resume + LinkedIn repair
  - Day 3 to 5: 20 target companies split into reachable / stretch / prestige
  - Week 2: referral asks + mock interviews + tracked applications

## 2. Offer comparison template
- **Best option right now:** <company>
- **Why it wins:** trajectory / manager / compensation quality / brand / sustainability
- **What is real money:** base + guaranteed bonus + sign-on
- **What is story value:** title, ownership, brand, domain credibility
- **Main risk:** <manager quality / instability / narrow scope / low upside>
- **Negotiation move:** ask preferred company for one final improvement on the most constrained lever

## 3. Promotion-readiness template
- **Current ceiling:** coding is not the bottleneck anymore
- **Missing proof:** ownership / design clarity / influence / reliability under ambiguity
- **What to demonstrate:** 2 to 3 projects with visible before-after impact
- **Practice rule:** explain decisions in writing, not only in code
- **Next 8 weeks:** pick one ownership problem, one design narrative, one influence habit

## 4. Manager-conflict template
- **Crux:** this is a power, trust, or expectation problem more than a pure logic problem
- **Best move:** document facts, reduce emotional leakage, and clarify asks in writing
- **Do not do:** public escalation too early or emotional overexplaining
- **Use this line:**
  "I want to align on expectations and avoid drift. Here is what I believe I own, what is blocked, and where I need your decision."
- **Escalate when:** repeated pattern, reputational damage, or role risk continues after one clean attempt

## 5. Burnout and ambition template
- **Real issue:** overload / chaos / sleep debt / lack of control / hidden fear
- **Stabilize first:** sleep, food, calendar reduction, and one honest boundary
- **Then optimize:** choose the one career lever that still matters this month
- **Do not do:** compensate for exhaustion by adding more goals
- **Rule:** survival mode decisions should be reversible whenever possible

## 6. AI-era skill panic template
- **Truth:** panic is making you consume more than you build
- **Keep:** problem solving, writing, debugging, system understanding, stakeholder communication
- **Add:** one practical AI workflow tied to real work output
- **Drop:** endless tool hopping
- **30-day move:** ship one small AI-assisted work project and document the result

## 7. Wealth and resilience template
- **Question:** does this choice buy temporary status or durable room to breathe?
- **Protect first:** runway, health, debt control, career durability
- **Then chase upside:** role, market, or business options with compounding value
- **Avoid:** lifestyle inflation during unstable career periods
- **Rule:** optionality is a form of wealth

## 8. Relationship-repair after work stress
- **Crux:** the injury is usually emotional spillover, not only the sentence spoken
- **Do first:** acknowledge the effect without defending intention
- **Repair action:** one concrete behavior change inside 24 hours
- **Use this line:**
  "You did not deserve the version of me that showed up there. I am not going to argue with the impact. I want to repair it properly."
- **Do not do:** turn apology into explanation theater

## 9. Household emergency prioritization template
- **First lens:** safety, cash, continuity, and emotional containment
- **Sequence:** stop the bleed -> stabilize essentials -> communicate -> decide next layer
- **Role of leader:** reduce chaos and assign the next clear task
- **Do not do:** solve five downstream problems before containing the first one
- **Useful output:** a ranked list of what must happen in the next 2 hours, 24 hours, and 7 days

## 10. Reputation and public signal template
- **Goal:** become legible, not loud
- **Show:** proof of thinking, shipped work, and calm consistency
- **Avoid:** performative hot takes and borrowed expertise
- **Cadence:** one useful artifact each week beats daily noise
- **Test:** would a hiring manager or collaborator trust you more after reading this?
