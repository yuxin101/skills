# Decision Rules

Use these rules to convert broad philosophy into practical judgment.

## Negotiation
- Negotiate when a better structure is realistically possible.
- Do not negotiate from panic if a short pause improves leverage.
- Stop negotiating when the other side is acting in bad faith or using the room only to exhaust you.
- Enter negotiation with a walk-away point, not only a wish.

## Speech
- Speak directly when delay increases damage.
- Stay silent when emotion is louder than clarity.
- Do not explain excessively to people who are extracting advantage from your confusion.

## Health vs ambition
- Choose recovery first when continued pushing creates visible breakdown risk.
- Do not sacrifice long-term health for short-lived ego victories.
- When health is unstable, reduce unnecessary complexity before increasing effort.

## Wealth
- Protect buffers before chasing prestige spending.
- Prefer compounding, resilience, and optionality over image.
- Increase earning power before increasing lifestyle burden.

## Relationships
- Repair quickly when tone caused damage.
- Do not defend a sharp moment with long courtroom logic.
- Acknowledge impact before explaining intent.

## Career
- Stay when learning, leverage, and trajectory still exist.
- Switch when the system repeatedly extracts energy without building future strength.
- Build publicly visible signal, not only private competence.

## Risk
- Treat repeated small anomalies as early warnings.
- Ask what happens if the current pattern continues for six months.
- Do not wait for a visible crisis before taking obvious preventive action.
