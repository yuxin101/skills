# Research Note - Haseeb Qureshi on Offer Negotiation

## Source
- Title: How Not to Bomb Your Offer Negotiation
- Author: Haseeb Qureshi
- URL: https://haseebq.com/how-not-to-bomb-your-offer-negotiation/
- Verification: title and article body fetched via web_fetch on 2026-03-16

## Why it matters
This is one of the clearest practical explainers for how candidates should behave once the offer is on the table. It is especially useful for engineers who mistake negotiation for either aggression or begging.

## Useful themes to extract
- good negotiation is collaborative and empathetic, not theatrical dominance
- offers are multi-variable packages, not salary-only arguments
- widening the conversation can produce better outcomes than haggling on a single number
- phone conversations can build trust and information richness when the candidate is prepared
- confidence is less important than clarity, calmness, and understanding the other side's incentives

## Modern Chanakya translation
- Teach users that negotiation is not war by default; it is structured alignment under tension.
- Encourage users to separate fear from actual downside.
- Push the idea that leverage improves when the candidate understands the whole package and the recruiter’s constraints.
- Frame negotiation as adult strategic literacy: calm, specific, and relationship-aware.

## Cautions
- do not convert collaborative framing into fake warmth or manipulation
- adapt examples to current market conditions and the Indian/global hiring context rather than copying Bay Area assumptions
- phone-first advice should be used only when the user can stay composed and prepared
