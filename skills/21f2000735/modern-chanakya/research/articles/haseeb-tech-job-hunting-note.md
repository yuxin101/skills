# Research Note - Haseeb Qureshi on Breaking Into Tech

## Source
- Title: How to Break Into the Tech Industry—a Guide to Job Hunting and Tech Interviews
- Author: Haseeb Qureshi
- URL: https://haseebq.com/how-to-break-into-tech-job-hunting-and-interviews/
- Verification: title and article body fetched via web_fetch on 2026-03-16

## Why it matters
This article is useful because it treats job search as a system rather than a sequence of emotional episodes. That is highly compatible with Modern Chanakya’s goal of teaching practical strategy to ordinary engineers.

## Useful themes to extract
- job hunting is fundamentally a numbers game and requires trust in process math
- networking is often the highest-leverage lane for creating interviews
- preparation quality matters, but pipeline volume matters too
- interview performance requires both technical skill and self-presentation
- sustained job search needs mindset management, not only tactical checklists

## Modern Chanakya translation
- Teach engineers to manage job search like a campaign: pipeline, prep, feedback loops, and emotional discipline.
- Normalize repeated rejection without letting the user collapse into self-story.
- Encourage a split between market-generation work (applications, referrals, outreach) and conversion work (DSA, stories, system design, clarity).
- Emphasize that consistency beats occasional heroic effort.

## Cautions
- some examples reflect a different hiring era and geography, so treat tactics as patterns rather than exact market truth
- do not over-index on brute-force applications if the user’s signal is clearly weak and needs repair first

## Skill modules strengthened
- software engineer career strategy
- discipline and consistency
- social presence and opportunity flow
- resilience under uncertainty
