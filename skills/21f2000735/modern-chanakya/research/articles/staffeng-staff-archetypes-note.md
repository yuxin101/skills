## Why it matters
This source is strong because it breaks vague senior-career ambition into recognizable operating patterns. That is much more actionable than telling engineers to simply "be strategic."

## Useful themes to extract
- staff-plus work is not one generic shape; it clusters into recurring archetypes
- common patterns include Tech Lead, Architect, Solver, and Right Hand
- impact shifts from individual execution alone to scope, coordination, and organizational leverage
- higher-level engineering often means coding less and aligning more
- role clarity matters because title inflation hides very different expectations

## Modern Chanakya translation
- Teach users to identify which archetype fits their strengths and company context.
- Show that career growth above senior often requires a move from task ownership to ambiguity reduction and coordination.
- Help engineers describe their value in terms of systems, teams, and organizational outcomes.

## Cautions
- do not treat archetypes as rigid boxes; many real roles are blends
- the guide is strongest for staff-plus framing, not as a replacement for company-specific ladders
