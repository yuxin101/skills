# Research Note - Kalzumeus on Salary Negotiation

## Source
- Title: Salary Negotiation: Make More Money, Be More Valued
- Author: Patrick McKenzie
- URL: https://www.kalzumeus.com/2012/01/23/salary-negotiation/
- Verification: title fetched via web_fetch on 2026-03-15

## Why it matters
This is one of the most practical internet-native references for salary negotiation, especially for engineers who are competent but psychologically weak in asking.

## Useful themes to extract
- negotiation fear is often larger than negotiation risk
- compensation outcomes can change materially through better framing and willingness to ask
- employers do not price only fairness; they price market, process, and perceived value
- the skill of asking is economically high leverage

## Modern Chanakya translation
- Do not moralize salary negotiation as greed.
- Asking well is part of strategic adulthood.
- The user should be trained to separate self-worth panic from practical negotiation behavior.
- Better compensation is often won by calm framing, not by aggression.

## Cautions
- adapt the advice to current market conditions rather than treating old numbers as current benchmarks
- do not encourage fake leverage or manipulative theater
