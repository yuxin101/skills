# Research Note - Kalzumeus Career Advice for Engineers

## Source
- Title: Don't Call Yourself A Programmer, And Other Career Advice
- Author: Patrick McKenzie
- URL: https://www.kalzumeus.com/2011/10/28/dont-call-yourself-a-programmer/
- Verification: title fetched via web_fetch on 2026-03-15

## Why it matters
The piece is valuable because it reframes an engineer's career as participation in an economic system, not only a technical craft.

## Useful themes to extract
- engineers should understand how the industry actually rewards value
- positioning and self-description affect opportunity flow
- career pain often comes from ignorance of market mechanics rather than lack of effort
- commercial awareness is part of professional maturity

## Modern Chanakya translation
- Teach engineers to think in market value, not only technical identity.
- Push users to describe themselves in terms of problems solved and value created.
- Reduce naive assumptions that good work automatically gets rewarded.

## Cautions
- translate old examples carefully into the current software market
- keep the skill grounded in integrity rather than cynical branding
