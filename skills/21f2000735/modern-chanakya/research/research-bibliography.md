# Modern Chanakya Research Bibliography

Working research list to ground the public `modern-chanakya` skill in published papers and scholarly material instead of intuition alone.

## Selection rule
Prioritize sources useful for:
- strategy
- ethics
- leadership
- negotiation
- risk and foresight
- corporate/statecraft translation from Arthashastra / Chanakya material
- burnout and resilience
- software engineer career strategy

## Current collected papers / formal sources

1. **Strategic Thinking in Arthashastra and its Corporate Relevance: An Exploration of Ancient Indian Wisdom for Modern Business**  
   - Author: G. Jain  
   - Venue: *Ushus Journal of Business Management* (2025)  
   - Signal: corporate relevance, strategic management, leadership, governance, ethics

2. **Arthashastra and its applicability in modern corporate world: a comprehensive analysis**  
   - Author: N. Chatterjee  
   - Venue: *International Journal of Humanities and Information…* (2025)  
   - Signal: ethical leadership, transparency, accountability, modern corporate translation

3. **Leadership Lessons from Ancient Indian Epics: Ramayana, Mahabharata, and Arthashastra**  
   - Author: A. Diwan  
   - Signal: strategic thinking, moral courage, duty vs desire, leadership framing

4. **Qualities and Skills of Leaders (With Reference To Kautilya Arthashastra)**  
   - Authors: S. Chatterjee, S. B. Vyas  
   - Year: 2017  
   - Signal: value-based leadership, culture, ethical strategy, conflict-cost reduction

5. **Sustainable Leadership Qualities for Organizational Leader Mentioned in Kautilya's Arthashastra**  
   - Author: V. Pandya  
   - Signal: leadership qualities, ethics, governance, organizational application

6. **Strategic thinking and contemporary business—Thoughts from an ancient Indian thinker**  
   - Author: V. K. Gupta  
   - Venue: *Paradigm* (2007)  
   - Signal: strategy, moral leadership, business application of Chanakya

7. **Dharma to Arthashastra—Ancient Indian Philosophies for Strategic and Ethical Decision Making**  
   - Author: S. R. Kalathil  
   - Signal: strategic and ethical decision-making framework, governance, leadership

8. **Burnout: A Review of Theory and Measurement**  
   - Year: 2022  
   - URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8834764/

## How to use these sources
When adding guidance to the public skill, prefer grounding claims in themes that recur across multiple sources, for example:
- strategic thinking requires timing, governance, and long-horizon judgment
- leadership is tied to self-control, ethics, and role clarity
- negotiation is calmer and more multi-dimensional than most users assume
- burnout is not mere weakness; it often reflects chronic work-stress mismatch
- software-engineer growth depends on market signal, leverage, communication, and organizational usefulness
- modern application requires translation, not blind copying from ancient statecraft
