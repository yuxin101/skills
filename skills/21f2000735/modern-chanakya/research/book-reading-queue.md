# Modern Chanakya Book Reading Queue

Working list of books to inform the public Modern Chanakya skill. Prioritize books that improve strategic thinking, ethics, leadership, negotiation, resilience, and modern professional judgment.

## Core Chanakya / Arthashastra books
1. **Arthashastra** — Kautilya / Chanakya  
   Purpose: primary source for statecraft, governance, intelligence, power, and strategic logic.

2. **Chanakya Neeti** — attributed to Chanakya  
   Purpose: distilled aphorisms on character, caution, speech, trust, discipline, and worldly judgment.

3. **Corporate Chanakya** — Radhakrishnan Pillai  
   Purpose: modern management interpretation; useful for corporate translation, though it should be cross-checked rather than copied.

4. **Chanakya in Daily Life** — Radhakrishnan Pillai  
   Purpose: practical life application; useful for seeing how ancient principles are translated into routine modern behavior.

5. **Katha Chanakya** — Radhakrishnan Pillai  
   Purpose: narrative framing and story-based explanation of Chanakya principles.

## Supporting books for strategy / power / negotiation
6. **The 33 Strategies of War** — Robert Greene  
   Purpose: compare modern/Western framing of strategy with Chanakya-derived approaches.

7. **The 48 Laws of Power** — Robert Greene  
   Purpose: useful as a contrast text; extract what is strategically insightful but reject what is corrosive or manipulative.

8. **Thinking, Fast and Slow** — Daniel Kahneman  
   Purpose: modern decision-making biases and judgment limits.

9. **Never Split the Difference** — Chris Voss with Tahl Raz  
   Purpose: negotiation tactics, emotional calibration, leverage, and practical communication.
   Status: note created.

10. **The Art of Strategy** — Avinash K. Dixit, Barry J. Nalebuff  
    Purpose: game theory and strategic interaction in modern settings.

11. **Antifragile** — Nassim Nicholas Taleb  
    Purpose: resilience, optionality, volatility, and systems that gain from disorder.

12. **Atomic Habits** — James Clear  
    Purpose: behavior design and practical self-governance; useful for translating wisdom into repeatable routines.

13. **The Staff Engineer's Path** — Tanya Reilly  
    Purpose: staff-level growth, organizational leverage, technical leadership, and career calibration beyond title chasing.
    Status: note created.

14. **Designing Your Work Life** — Bill Burnett, Dave Evans  
    Purpose: practical career-design thinking, energy calibration, and sustainable experimentation under uncertainty.

## Reading notes rule
For each book, extract:
- 5–10 useful principles
- what is timeless
- what needs modern reinterpretation
- what should never be copied blindly
- which skill modules it strengthens
