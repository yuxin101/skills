- Verification: abstract and article text fetched via web_fetch on 2026-03-16

## Why it matters
This review is useful because it frames burnout as a work-structure problem as much as an individual experience. That is a better fit for Modern Chanakya than shallow advice about simply becoming tougher.

## High-signal takeaways
- burnout is a progressive response to chronic work stress, not a one-bad-day event
- the literature repeatedly centers exhaustion, cynicism/depersonalization, and reduced efficacy
- burnout creates costs for both individuals and organizations
- prevention and reduction require changes in work conditions, not only private coping tricks
- measurement is messy; definitions and prevalence estimates vary, so confidence should stay modest

## Modern Chanakya translation
- Diagnose burnout as a system-level mismatch: workload, control, support, role clarity, recovery, meaning, and environment.
- Avoid framing resilience as endless endurance.
- Treat reduced efficacy as a meaningful signal, not just laziness or weak discipline.
- Build responses that combine self-governance with work redesign or exit strategy when needed.

## Cautions
- do not present burnout prevalence numbers with false precision
- do not turn psychological constructs into fake medical certainty
- burnout and depression can overlap; the skill should stay supportive and non-clinical
