Last reviewed: March 17, 2026.

Primary authority pointers:

Read this with `framework/applicability-role-matrix.md` and `controls/shared-responsibility.md` if the service boundary or deployment model is unclear.

## Why this domain matters

Required contractual safeguards must be complete before a workload can claim PRS-3. A team may be technically ready and still not be approved for PHI use if contracting is incomplete.

## Review questions

- Is a BAA required for the in-scope relationship?
- Is the need driven by the actual service boundary and role, not by vendor branding or assumption?
- Is the signed contractual artifact complete before go-live?
- Are downstream subcontractor obligations understood where applicable?

## Minimum expectations by stage

- PRS-2: BAA need is analyzed during vendor and role review
- PRS-3: required BAAs or equivalent contractual prerequisites are complete
- PRS-4: contracts remain current for the live scope

## Strong evidence

- signed BAA
- contract matrix tied to the workload
- role analysis for each in-scope service provider

## Common blockers

- team assumes no BAA is needed without role analysis
- BAA exists at company level but does not clearly cover the scoped workload
- contract execution lags behind technical approval

## Recommended next actions

- document whether a BAA is required for each in-scope relationship
- complete required contract execution before approval
- retain contractual scope records with the workload review package
