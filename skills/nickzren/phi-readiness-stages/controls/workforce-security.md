# Workforce Security

Last reviewed: March 17, 2026.

Primary authority pointers:

- HIPAA Security Rule workforce security requirements
- HHS Security Rule overview

## Why this domain matters

A workload can appear technically strong while remaining operationally weak if workforce responsibilities, access expectations, and training are vague.

## Review questions

- Are in-scope roles and responsibilities defined?
- Are access expectations aligned with those roles?
- Is training required for in-scope operators?
- Are termination or transfer processes relevant to the workload?

## Minimum expectations by stage

- PRS-1: baseline data-handling expectations are documented
- PRS-2: operating responsibilities are assigned and workforce readiness is defined
- PRS-3: required go-live training is completed
- PRS-4: recurring workforce controls remain current where applicable

## Strong evidence

- responsibility matrix
- training roster
- operational runbook ownership
- onboarding and offboarding procedures

## Common blockers

- unclear support owner
- admins have production access with no role record
- training is planned but not complete before go-live

## Recommended next actions

- assign role-based operational ownership
- complete and retain in-scope training records
- align access reviews to role assignments
