# Incident Response

Last reviewed: March 17, 2026.

Primary authority pointers:

- HIPAA Security Rule security incident procedures
- HHS cybersecurity guidance and newsletters

## Why this domain matters

Incident response is required before PHI go-live and remains a core operating discipline at PRS-4.

## Review questions

- Is there a defined incident-reporting and escalation path?
- Are security incidents triaged and handled through a documented process?
- Is the workload included in the response scope?
- Are exercises or readiness checks performed where required?

## Minimum expectations by stage

- PRS-1: incident reporting path identified
- PRS-2: incident-response procedure documented for the workload
- PRS-3: monitoring, alerting, and escalation are enabled before cutover
- PRS-4: response readiness and evidence are current

## Strong evidence

- incident-response procedure
- escalation matrix
- on-call ownership
- incident records or tabletop exercises

## Common blockers

- generic corporate policy with no workload applicability
- no operational owner for alerts
- no evidence of readiness after go-live

## Recommended next actions

- map the workload into incident procedures
- define on-call and escalation ownership
- retain response-readiness evidence
