# Governance, Procedures, and Operating Roles

Last reviewed: March 17, 2026.

## Why this domain matters

Technical controls do not support PRS-2 or higher unless the workload also has approved procedures, explicit ownership, and an operating model that can survive turnover and routine change.

## Review questions

- Which policies or procedures apply to this workload?
- Are they approved or only drafted?
- Who owns security, operations, support, and approval decisions for the workload?
- Are residual-risk decisions and exceptions handled through a defined process?
- Are operating responsibilities explicit enough for approval and live use?

## Minimum expectations by stage

- PRS-1: foundational governance prerequisites exist and ownership is assigned
- PRS-2: applicable procedures are approved and operating roles are assigned
- PRS-3: approval and support ownership are explicit for the scoped PHI workload
- PRS-4: governance artifacts remain maintained and aligned with current operation

## Strong evidence

- approved procedures or policy applicability record
- responsibility matrix
- owner roster
- exception or residual-risk approvals
- support ownership documentation

## Common blockers

- policies exist only at a generic company level with no workload applicability
- ownership is assumed but not assigned
- approval responsibilities are unclear
- exceptions are informal and not durable

## Recommended next actions

- map approved procedures to the specific workload
- assign and record operating ownership by role
- require durable records for exceptions and residual-risk decisions
