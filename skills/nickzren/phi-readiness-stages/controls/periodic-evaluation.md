# Periodic Evaluation and Reassessment

Last reviewed: March 17, 2026.

## Why this domain matters

PRS-4 is an operating condition, not a one-time milestone. A workload should not remain at a mature stage if evaluations, refresh cycles, and reassessment after change have lapsed.

## Review questions

- Is there a defined cadence for technical and non-technical evaluation?
- Are assessments refreshed after material change?
- Are control owners aware of reassessment triggers?
- Is there current evidence that evaluation actually occurred?

## Minimum expectations by stage

- PRS-2: a review or refresh plan is expected for readiness artifacts
- PRS-3: approval package includes ongoing review expectations
- PRS-4: periodic technical and non-technical evaluation is current and reassessment after material change is evidenced

## Strong evidence

- evaluation schedule
- annual or periodic review record
- reassessment artifacts after major architecture or vendor change
- review calendar or control-owner attestations

## Common blockers

- last full review is stale
- major scope or architecture changes occurred without reassessment
- there is no documented trigger for re-review
- ownership for periodic evaluation is unclear

## Recommended next actions

- define and document periodic evaluation cadence
- require reassessment after material scope, vendor, or architecture changes
- retain current review records with the workload evidence set
