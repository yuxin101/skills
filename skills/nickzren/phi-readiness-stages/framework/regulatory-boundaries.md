# Regulatory Boundaries

PRS is designed around workload readiness for PHI handling. Its primary regulatory baseline is the HIPAA Security Rule because stage assignment is heavily about safeguards, approval, and live operations. That does not mean PRS fully resolves all HIPAA or non-HIPAA obligations.

## 1. Security Rule

This is the primary baseline for PRS stage assignment. It is most relevant to:

- technical safeguards
- administrative safeguards
- physical safeguards
- periodic evaluation
- contingency planning

## 2. Privacy Rule

Privacy Rule issues are adjacent and often relevant, especially for:

- whether PHI is in scope
- who is a covered entity or business associate
- permissible uses and disclosures
- minimum necessary
- notices, authorizations, and individual rights

PRS can flag missing procedures or unclear ownership that would matter for Privacy Rule readiness, but PRS does not by itself determine full Privacy Rule compliance.

## 3. Breach Notification Rule

Breach readiness matters to PRS because operational readiness is weaker if the team lacks documented incident and breach-response pathways. But PRS does not by itself determine whether a specific incident legally triggers breach notification.

Use PRS to assess:

- whether breach-related procedures exist
- whether ownership is assigned
- whether evidence retention can support an investigation

Do not use PRS alone to conclude:

- whether a particular incident is a reportable breach
- whether the correct notifications have already been made

## 4. State law and other regimes

HIPAA does not erase state-law review. More protective state privacy or breach rules may still apply. Other health-data rules may also apply when a workflow is outside HIPAA scope.

PRS should therefore:

- flag state-law review when jurisdiction-specific patient or consumer data is involved
- flag FTC or consumer-health review when a health-related workflow appears to sit outside HIPAA scope
- flag special categories or high-sensitivity contexts
- avoid claiming that a strong PRS stage resolves all privacy obligations

## 5. PRS reporting rule

Every thorough assessment should include a short boundary note:

- what PRS decided
- what PRS did not decide
- whether Privacy Rule, Breach Notification Rule, state law, FTC, or other review is still needed
