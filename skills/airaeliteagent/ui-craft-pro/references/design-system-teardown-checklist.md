# Design System Teardown Checklist

Use this when studying a public design system, component library, or polished product UI.

## 1. Product fit
- What kind of product is this for?
- Who uses it most?
- Does it optimize for clarity, speed, trust, delight, or spectacle?

## 2. Tone
- What 3-5 adjectives describe the UI best?
- Does it feel calm, dense, premium, playful, technical, institutional, or cinematic?

## 3. Token DNA
- What is the real color strategy?
- How many neutrals vs accents?
- What radius scale is repeated?
- What shadow scale is repeated?
- Are borders doing most of the work?

## 4. Typography DNA
- Is hierarchy driven by font size, weight, spacing, or contrast?
- Does the system use a mono companion font?
- Is it editorial, product-first, or utilitarian?

## 5. Rhythm
- Is spacing generous, compact, or mixed by context?
- Are sections separated by whitespace, borders, background changes, or cards?
- How wide is reading content?

## 6. Components
- How do buttons feel?
- How heavy are cards?
- How obvious are inputs, tabs, tables, and sidebars?
- What makes components feel like one family?

## 7. Motion
- Are transitions invisible, helpful, premium, expressive, or flashy?
- Which motions are functional vs decorative?

## 8. Page structure
- What is the common hero/docs/dashboard/settings shell?
- How does the system handle proof, CTA, empty state, and error state?

## 9. Accessibility posture
- Does the system appear contrast-safe?
- Are states obvious without color alone?
- Does density hurt readability?

## 10. Clone safely
- What should be preserved exactly as logic?
- What should be adapted into your brand?
- What should never be copied literally?

## 11. Product-family label
Before you leave the teardown, assign the source to a family:
- developer
- enterprise workflow
- command/productivity
- knowledge/content
- creative/premium launch
- universal foundation

This makes it easier to reuse references appropriately instead of copying one famous product everywhere.
