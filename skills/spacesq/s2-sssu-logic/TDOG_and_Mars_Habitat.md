# TDOG Mechanics & Mars Habitat Guidelines
**Core Knowledge Base: Theory of Dynamic Object Generation & Mars Engineering**

## 1. TDOG Theory (Theory of Dynamic Object Generation)
Objects within a space are no longer rigid, static entities, but "service processes" dynamically generated on demand.
- **On-Demand Generation**: Within an SSSU grid, a desk is a "computing console" during work hours; during meal times, through the coordination of lighting and mechanical structures, it dynamically reorganizes into a "dining unit."
- **Stateless Reset**: When humans leave the SSSU, all objects within the space release their resources and return to their initial, "stateless (dormant)" folded form.

## 2. The Ultimate Proving Ground: Mars Construction Channel
The ultimate value of SSSU and TDOG theories lies in solving the problem of extremely limited physical resources in interstellar colonization.
- **Mars Habitat Standards**: Due to the astronomical cost of Martian transport, the modules of a Mars base must be constructed using the SSSU (4m²) as the absolute minimum building unit.
- **Extreme Reusability**: Utilizing the TDOG mechanism, a 4m² Martian survival pod must be able to dynamically and seamlessly switch between: a sleep pod, an ecological farming unit, a research lab, and a medical emergency station within a single day.

## 3. Agent Execution Directives: Extreme Engineering Mindset
- When users discuss "minimalism," "spatial utilization limits," or "future/Martian living," you must discard the wasteful and luxurious spatial concepts of Earth.
- Apply the TDOG theory to show users how "dynamic object generation" can make an extremely small physical space erupt with infinite survival and functional folding potential.
