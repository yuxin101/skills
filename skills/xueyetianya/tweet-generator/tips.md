# Twitter/X Tips & Best Practices

## Algorithm Insights (2024-2025)
- **Replies and quotes** weigh more than likes for reach
- **Images and videos** get 2-3x more engagement than text-only
- **Threads** get boosted when each tweet gets engagement
- **External links** get suppressed — put links in replies, not the main tweet
- **First hour** is critical — engagement velocity determines reach
- **Bookmarks** are a hidden signal — "save-worthy" content gets boosted

## Tweet Anatomy
- **280 characters** max (but 70-100 chars often perform best)
- **Line breaks** increase readability and dwell time
- **One clear idea** per tweet
- **End with engagement bait** (question, controversial take, or "RT if...")

## Thread Best Practices
- First tweet = HOOK (must stand alone and be compelling)
- Number your tweets: "1/10", "2/10" or use 🧵
- Each tweet should be valuable independently
- End with a summary + CTA ("Follow for more", "Bookmark this thread")
- Self-reply within 30 seconds to keep the thread connected

## Viral Patterns
1. **Contrarian take**: "Unpopular opinion: [thing everyone secretly agrees with]"
2. **Listicle**: "10 [things] that [benefit]. A thread 🧵"
3. **Story arc**: "In 2019, I was broke. Today I [success]. Here's what happened:"
4. **Hot take + evidence**: Bold claim backed by data
5. **Relatable humor**: Niche observations about shared experiences

## Posting Schedule (General)
- **Best days**: Tue, Wed, Thu
- **Best times**: 8-10 AM, 12-1 PM, 5-6 PM (audience timezone)
- **Worst time**: Late night (unless targeting insomniacs)
- **Frequency**: 3-5 tweets/day + 1-2 threads/week

## Bio Tips
- 160 chars max — every word counts
- Include: What you do + Who you help + Personality
- Use line breaks (they work in bios)
- Skip: "Father of 3" unless it's relevant to your content
- Consider: "Building [project]" or "[Role] at [Company]"
