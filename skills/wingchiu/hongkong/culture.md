# Culture - HongKong

## Everyday Norms

| Situation | What helps |
|-----------|------------|
| Escalators | Stand right, walk left |
| Queues | Respect them, people do notice |
| Public transport | Efficient, quiet, no drama |
| Restaurants | Service can be fast and direct, not rude |

## Money

| Method | Use |
|--------|-----|
| Octopus | Transit, convenience stores, many casual purchases |
| Credit card | Hotels, malls, many restaurants |
| Cash | Handy for smaller shops and old-school spots |

## Food Timing

| Meal | Best window |
|------|-------------|
| Breakfast | 7:30-10:30 |
| Dim sum | 10:00-13:30 |
| Tea time / snacks | 15:00-17:00 |
| Dinner | 18:30-21:00 |

## Language

- Cantonese is the local default
- English is common in hotels, business districts, and many restaurants
- A few polite phrases or just being patient goes a long way

## Weather Reality

- Humidity changes the feel of everything
- Summer is draining fast; build indoor breaks
- Heavy rain can appear suddenly and ruin an overpacked schedule

## Tourist Traps To Flag

| Thing | Better framing |
|-------|----------------|
| Peak Tram at prime time | Go early or use another route |
| Symphony of Lights | Nice if nearby, not a trip-defining event |
| Temple Street shopping | Go for vibe, not quality buying |
| Harbor cruise packages | Star Ferry often does enough |
