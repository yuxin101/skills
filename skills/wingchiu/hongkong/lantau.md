# Lantau

## Why Go

Lantau is where airport logistics, big-ticket attractions, beaches, villages, and some of Hong Kong's best easy-access hikes all collide.

## Main Zones

| Area | Best for | Note |
|------|----------|------|
| Tung Chung | Airport hotels, outlet mall, transit hub | Functional, not charming |
| Ngong Ping | Big Buddha, monastery, cable car | Go early |
| Tai O | Stilt-house village, seafood, slow pace | Worth it if paired with Ngong Ping or a hike |
| Discovery Bay | Families, expats, waterfront | Pleasant but not essential for most trips |
| Disneyland | Families | Needs its own day |

## Big Buddha / Ngong Ping

- Cable car is scenic if visibility is good
- If it's foggy, windy, or queues are ugly, bus is fine
- Go early to avoid both crowds and heat
- Po Lin Monastery works best as a calm stop, not a rushed photo box

## Tai O

- Better for atmosphere than "must-see architecture"
- Good for seafood snacks, boaty village feel, and a slower half-day
- Not worth a full dedicated day unless they really like village wandering

## Airport / Layover Uses

| Situation | Best move |
|-----------|-----------|
| Short layover | Stay near airport or Tung Chung |
| 8-12 hour layover | Tung Chung + cable car if weather is clear |
| Last day flight | Sleep on Lantau only if the early departure matters |

## Common Mistake

Trying to combine Disneyland and Ngong Ping in one day. Don't.
