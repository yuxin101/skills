# Food Guide - HongKong

## What To Prioritize

| Category | What it is | Best context |
|----------|------------|--------------|
| Dim sum | Brunch-style Cantonese small plates | Morning to lunch |
| Cha chaan teng | Hong Kong cafe comfort food | Breakfast, tea time, fast meals |
| Roast meats | Goose, char siu, roast pork | Lunch or dinner |
| Wonton noodles | Fast, precise, essential | Anytime light meal |
| Seafood | Sai Kung, Lei Yue Mun, island trips | Day trips |
| Dai pai dong style cooking | Open-air / old-school energy | Better with guidance |
| Desserts | Tofu pudding, mango sweets, egg tarts | Afternoon / late night |

## Dishes To Know

| Dish | What it is |
|------|------------|
| Har gow | Shrimp dumplings |
| Siu mai | Pork and shrimp dumplings |
| Char siu bao | BBQ pork bun |
| Cheung fun | Rice noodle rolls |
| Pineapple bun | Sweet bun, often with butter |
| French toast HK style | Rich, peanut-buttery, condensed milk |
| Milk tea | Strong tea with evaporated milk |
| Wonton noodles | Shrimp wontons, thin noodles, clear broth |
| Roast goose | Signature Cantonese roast |
| Claypot rice | Best in cooler weather |

## Good First-Timer Strategy

1. One classic dim sum meal
2. One cha chaan teng breakfast
3. One roast meat rice plate
4. One noodle shop
5. One seafood meal if they have time

## Areas By Food Style

| Area | Best for |
|------|----------|
| Central / Sheung Wan | Coffee, bakeries, mixed dining, polished Cantonese |
| Wan Chai | Roast meats, bars, new-meets-old |
| Jordan | Noodles, cha chaan teng, efficient classics |
| Mong Kok | Snacks, dessert, busy-value eating |
| Sham Shui Po | Budget local food and street snacks |
| Sai Kung | Seafood |

## Food Warnings

- Tim Ho Wan is fine, but not the only answer
- Temple Street food is about atmosphere more than best-in-city quality
- Long queues sometimes matter, but not every viral place is worth the wait
- Cantonese banquet seafood prices can climb fast if they are not paying attention
