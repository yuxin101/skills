# Kowloon

## Areas

| Area | Best for | Feel |
|------|----------|------|
| Tsim Sha Tsui | Harbor, museums, convenience | Tourist-heavy but useful |
| Jordan | Efficient base, food | Central, practical |
| Yau Ma Tei | Markets, old Hong Kong feel | Lived-in, dense |
| Mong Kok | Street energy, snacks, shopping | Chaotic, intense |
| Sham Shui Po | Local food, markets, electronics, fabric | Rougher, more authentic |
| Prince Edward | Flower market, bird garden, food spillover | Local, walkable |

## What To Do

### Tsim Sha Tsui Promenade
- Do it near sunset
- Skip the Symphony of Lights if expectations are high; the skyline itself is the point
- Avenue of Stars is fine, but the harbor view matters more than the celebrity handprints

### Markets
| Market | Worth it? | Note |
|--------|-----------|------|
| Temple Street | Medium | Go for atmosphere and snacks, not shopping quality |
| Ladies' Market | Low-medium | Tourist bargaining, repetitive goods |
| Goldfish / Flower / Bird markets | Medium | Best when paired with Mong Kok / Prince Edward wandering |

### West Kowloon Cultural District
- Good for a slower afternoon
- M+ is worth it if they like modern visual culture
- Harborfront lawn is one of the best open skyline spaces in the city

## Fast Kowloon Night

1. Wonton noodles in Jordan
2. Walk Nathan Road into Mong Kok
3. Snack stops in side streets
4. End at Tsim Sha Tsui promenade or a harbor bar
