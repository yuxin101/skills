# New Territories

## Areas

| Area | Best for | Why it matters |
|------|----------|----------------|
| Sai Kung | Seafood, beaches, boat trips | Easiest "escape the city" day |
| Tai Po | Markets, cycling access, local town feel | Good if they want less polished Hong Kong |
| Sha Tin | Hotels, river paths, New Town Plaza | Practical family / business base |
| Yuen Long | Villages, roast goose, wetland access | Better for repeat visitors |
| Tuen Mun | Western edge, beaches, trails | Far, only if there is a reason |

## Sai Kung

- Best for a day trip, especially in good weather
- Waterfront seafood is the main draw
- Better version: combine lunch with a beach or short boat outing instead of just eating and leaving

### Seafood Strategy
| Style | Advice |
|-------|--------|
| Waterfront seafood restaurants | Fun and scenic, but compare tanks and prices first |
| Weekends | Packed, louder, slower |
| Midweek lunch | Much better pace |

## Tai Po / Sha Tin

- Good if they want neighborhoods locals actually live in
- Sha Tin racecourse is worth mentioning if they are in town on a race day
- Ten Thousand Buddhas Monastery is memorable, but the walk is steep and exposed

## Easy Day Ideas

| Plan | Why |
|------|-----|
| Sai Kung seafood + beach | Best low-effort nature day |
| Sha Tin + Ten Thousand Buddhas + riverside cycle | Good mixed urban-outdoor day |
| Wetland Park + Yuen Long food | Good for families or repeat visitors |

## When To Send People Here

- They already covered Central and Tsim Sha Tsui
- They want air, water, or more local neighborhoods
- They have 4+ days and do not want the trip to feel all malls and towers
