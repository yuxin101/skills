# Accommodation - HongKong

## Best Areas

| Area | Best for | Tradeoff |
|------|----------|----------|
| Central | Business, first-timers, nightlife | Expensive, rooms can be small |
| Sheung Wan | Better value than Central, food, walkability | Still pricey |
| Wan Chai | Balanced base | Mixed street feel block to block |
| Causeway Bay | Shopping and convenience | Crowded, less character |
| Tsim Sha Tsui | Easy transport, harbor access | Tourist-heavy |
| Jordan | Smart value base | Less polished, but efficient |
| Sha Tin | Families, bigger hotels, racecourse | Less iconic atmosphere |
| Tung Chung | Airport convenience | Not a city-trip vibe |

## Quick Rules

- For 2-3 day first trips: stay on Hong Kong Island or Jordan / Tsim Sha Tsui
- For food-first travelers: Jordan, Yau Ma Tei, Sheung Wan, Wan Chai
- For families: Sha Tin, Discovery Bay, or larger branded hotels with easy MTR access
- For early flights: airport hotel or Tung Chung only if the timing really matters

## What To Warn About

- "Near Central" can still mean a steep uphill walk
- Cheap rooms can be tiny even by big-city standards
- Harbor view premiums are often not worth it if they will be out all day
- Airport hotels are convenient, not atmospheric
