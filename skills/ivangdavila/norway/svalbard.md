# Svalbard

## Best Fit

- Use Svalbard only when the user explicitly wants expedition atmosphere, Arctic wildlife context, coal-town history, or a very different Norway experience.
- It is not a casual add-on to a standard fjords trip.
- Svalbard is high-cost, high-novelty, and strongly weather-shaped.

## What Makes It Different

| Factor | Why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Remote Arctic setting | Logistics, pricing, and weather are less forgiving |
| Polar-bear safety rules | Movement outside settled areas is not casual |
| Seasonal light extremes | Trip design changes completely by month |
| Excursion dependence | Many best experiences are guided, not spontaneous |

## Good Use Cases

- Winter or shoulder travelers who care more about Arctic atmosphere than about classic fjord driving.
- Return visitors who already covered mainland Norway.
- Users willing to spend on guided experiences and strong gear.

## Planning Constraints

- Build around flight timing, not around overpacked activity lists.
- Keep expectations clear: weather can cancel or soften the experience.
- Users should understand that independent wandering is limited compared with mainland towns.

## Trap Avoidance

- Do not mix Svalbard casually into a short "see all of Norway" plan.
- Avoid selling it as a budget aurora shortcut.
- Do not understate the cold, wind, darkness, or guided-tour dependency.

## Planning Rule

Recommend Svalbard only when the user is consciously choosing remoteness and Arctic seriousness, not just chasing another pin on the map.
