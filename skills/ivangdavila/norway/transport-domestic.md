# Transport Domestic

## Choose the Dominant Mode First

Norway rewards plans where one transport mode leads and the others support it.

## Best Mode by Trip Shape

- **Rail-first:** Oslo to Bergen corridor, Oslo to Trondheim, users who want scenery without winter-driving stress.
- **Flight-first:** south plus Arctic combinations, Lofoten or Tromso add-ons, short trips where time matters more than overland continuity.
- **Ferry-and-bus-first:** classic fjord segments where the scenery is the transport.
- **Car-first:** remote west-coast loops, Lofoten shoulder or summer self-drive, and users who want detours more than city time.

## Core Tools

- Use Entur as the default planner when stitching different public-transport operators.
- Use Vy directly when the rail product is central to the decision.
- Use Avinor for airport realities and NOR-WAY or local operators for longer bus links.

## Rail Logic

- Rail is strong on a few corridors, not as a universal national solution.
- Scenic segments are worth paying for when the user values the journey, not just speed.
- Rail becomes especially valuable when the alternative is a costly car used mainly to follow the same corridor.

## Flight Logic

- Internal flights are often the cleanest way to combine south and north.
- Use them to protect a trip shape, not to create more stops.
- Add buffer when chaining remote weather-sensitive areas with same-day international departures.

## Ferry and Express Boat Logic

- Ferries are sometimes transport, sometimes an attraction, sometimes a choke point.
- Distinguish simple vehicle ferries from longer scenic boat products that should be treated as trip highlights.
- In summer and on popular scenic corridors, booking behavior matters more than users expect.

## Bus Logic

- Buses often solve the last mile better than travelers expect, especially outside rail corridors.
- They are useful for one-way scenic trips where a rental car would be wasteful.

## Common Transport Errors

- Planning Norway with a generic Europe assumption that trains go everywhere conveniently.
- Overconnecting the whole trip through one same-day chain of flight, bus, ferry, and hotel transfer.
- Renting a car when one scenic rail or boat link would remove stress and parking cost.
