# Itineraries

## 5 Days - First Norway Without Car Stress

- Base in Oslo for 2 nights and Bergen for 2 nights.
- Use one scenic rail segment or Norway in a Nutshell-style day.
- Good for first-timers, shoulder season, and users who want culture plus one scenic corridor.
- Skip Lofoten, long fjord drives, and "just add Tromso."

## 7 Days - Western Fjord Classic

- Fly into Oslo, rail west, then base in Flam or Bergen, and finish in Bergen or return by flight.
- Best for scenic rail, fjords, villages, and a moderate planning load.
- Works without a car if the user accepts timetables and fewer detours.
- Add a rental car only if the user wants scenic loops more than urban time.

## 7 Days - Arctic Winter Base Trip

- Fly to Tromso and stay 4-6 nights in one base or two nearby bases at most.
- Build around weather flexibility: aurora nights, one culture day, one snow or sea day, one spare day.
- Best for winter users who want northern lights without risky driving.
- Do not combine with western fjords unless there is an internal flight and a longer total trip.

## 10 Days - Oslo Plus Fjords

- Oslo 2 nights, scenic rail corridor 1-2 nights, Bergen 2 nights, fjord village or side loop 2-3 nights.
- Strong fit for first-timers who want cities, rail, ferries, and one deeper nature segment.
- With car: add Aurland, Hardanger, or a controlled loop.
- Without car: keep bases near strong transport nodes.

## 10-12 Days - Lofoten Focus

- Fly north, choose 2-3 bases max, and keep one full buffer day.
- Good for photographers, hiking-minded travelers, and summer or shoulder-season self-drive.
- Add Vesteralen only if total trip length supports it and the user accepts more road time.
- Weak fit for city-first travelers or users on tight budgets.

## 14 Days - South Plus Arctic Split

- Oslo plus west or southwest for 6-7 days, then fly north for 5-6 days in Tromso or Lofoten.
- This is the minimum shape that can justify both fjords and Arctic north.
- Use internal flights to protect the trip; do not try to connect everything by road.

## 16-18 Days - Grand Norway

- One south corridor plus one north corridor, never every famous stop.
- Examples:
  - Oslo -> Bergen/western fjords -> Tromso
  - Stavanger -> Bergen -> Trondheim -> Lofoten
- Needs strong discipline on hotel count, flight timing, and weather buffers.

## Downgrade Rules

- If the user has under 7 days, choose one corridor.
- If traveling in winter without snow-driving confidence, use base trips over loops.
- If the user wants both fjords and aurora, cut city count first, not nature days.
