# Entry and Documents

## Route the Legal Basics First

- Norway is in Schengen but not in the EU. Do not assume EU shopping, roaming, customs, and identity rules are all identical.
- Start with UDI for the actual stay pathway: visa-free visitor, visitor visa, family visit, or another status.
- For many non-EU travelers, the real constraint is the Schengen 90-in-180 rule, not just "Can I enter Norway?"
- For EU, EEA, and Nordic travelers, entry is usually simpler, but ID and airline documentation still matter.

## Questions That Change the Plan

- What passport is the traveler using?
- Is this a Norway-only trip or one stop inside a larger Schengen journey?
- Are they adding Svalbard, cruise arrivals, or onward flights with separate document checks?
- Is the traveler a minor, dual national, or traveling with a surname mismatch?

## Practical Checks Before Booking

1. Confirm visa or visa-free status with UDI, not blogs.
2. Check passport validity and blank-page reality before buying non-refundable flights.
3. Make sure the user understands total Schengen stay math if they are also visiting other countries.
4. If the trip includes Svalbard, treat it as a separate planning step because the travel chain and practical requirements differ from a normal mainland route.

## On Arrival and During the Trip

- Keep passport or accepted national ID easy to access for flights, ferries, hotels, and some excursion operators.
- Save flight confirmations, accommodation addresses, and return or onward proof in one place.
- If the user is renting a car, confirm license acceptance and whether they may need an international driving permit.
- If carrying high-value outdoor equipment, drones, or large cash, check customs guidance in advance.

## Insurance and Health Logic

- Push for travel insurance on any trip with ferries, winter sports, hiking, or remote areas.
- If the plan includes Svalbard, glacier activities, or winter self-drive, assume evacuation costs matter.
- EU and EEA travelers should still confirm how their health coverage works in Norway before departure.

## Red Flags

- "I already have Schengen days left, so I'm fine" without counting other recent Schengen travel.
- Booking Svalbard as if it were just another domestic city break.
- Assuming airline staff will accept expired ID or damaged passports because the traveler is staying inside Europe.
- Leaving visa checks until the week of departure.
