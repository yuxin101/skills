# Fjords and Scenic Routes

## The Classic Fjord Corridor

The highest-value Norway scenic logic usually sits in the west, with Bergen as the easiest anchor and Oslo as the easiest pairing city.

## Strong Fjord Patterns

- **Oslo -> scenic rail -> Flam/Aurland -> Bergen:** best first-timer corridor without car dependence.
- **Bergen with day trips or one village add-on:** best for a simpler scenic trip.
- **Bergen -> Hardanger -> Sognefjord loop:** best for users willing to self-drive.
- **Stavanger -> Lysefjord:** best for a tighter southwest version with one iconic fjord focus.

## Scenic Roads to Treat as Products

- National Scenic Routes are not just transfers. They are part of the attraction logic.
- Build them only when the user actually wants long drive days, viewpoints, and weather-dependent pacing.
- Good candidates are weaker in winter and stronger in summer or stable shoulder windows.

## What to Skip When Time Is Short

- Do not force Geiranger, Trollstigen, Flam, and Bergen into one rushed mini-trip.
- Choose one fjord personality:
  - rail and village scenery
  - road-loop scenery
  - hike-linked fjord scenery
  - city plus one fjord taste

## When Fjord Trips Fail

- Rainy or low-visibility days with no flexible backup
- Too many one-night stays
- Rental car plans that ignore ferry queues and mountain-road fatigue
- Cruise-style attraction sampling without enough time to enjoy the actual place

## Practical Output Pattern

- Name the anchor city
- Name the one must-win scenic corridor
- Name the optional upgrade if time and weather are kind
- Name the first cut if the route becomes too ambitious
