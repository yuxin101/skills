# Accommodation

## Choose Bases, Not Pins

In Norway, accommodation strategy is mostly about reducing transfer friction and weather risk.

## Best Base Patterns

- **Oslo:** good for arrival recovery, museums, and rail departures. Stay near the station if continuing west.
- **Bergen:** strongest urban base for classic fjord access without committing to a car-heavy trip.
- **Flam, Aurland, Balestrand, Geiranger, or similar villages:** only use when scenery is the point and the user accepts quiet evenings and limited backup options.
- **Tromso:** best winter base if the user wants tours, restaurants, and no constant hotel moves.
- **Lofoten:** pick 2 bases max, usually one east-central and one west, instead of sleeping somewhere new every night.

## When City Hotels Beat Cabins

- Short trips
- Winter travel with uncertain roads
- Users who rely on rail, bus, or tours
- Families who need food, bathrooms, and weather backup nearby

## When Cabins or Rorbuer Make Sense

- Summer or shoulder-season scenic trips
- Users who want sunrise or late-evening light without long drives back
- Travelers who will actually self-cater enough to offset cost
- Lofoten, fjord villages, and nature-heavy routes where place experience matters

## Common Hotel Mistakes

- Booking a scenic cabin that adds 90 minutes of road time every day.
- Choosing airport hotels to save one night and losing the trip atmosphere.
- Staying one night per stop on a route that should have two-base logic.
- Ignoring parking fees, ferry timing, or late check-in constraints.

## Practical Filters

- Private bathroom and heating quality matter more than in lower-cost destinations.
- In remote zones, check cancellation terms carefully because weather can force reroutes.
- In winter, confirm road access and snow-clearing reality before choosing isolated stays.
- For city stays, prioritize walkability to station, ferry terminal, or airport bus over a slightly lower nightly rate.

## Family and Accessibility Angle

- Ask about elevator access, step-free bathrooms, parking-to-room distance, and breakfast timing.
- In scenic areas, many charming stays are less practical for strollers, heavy luggage, or reduced mobility.
