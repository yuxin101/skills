# Opening and Closing

## Opening Priorities

1. Confirm safety, cleanliness, and storefront readiness before the first customer arrives.
2. Verify POS, payment devices, internet, music, lights, and key equipment.
3. Check cash float, safe access, receipt paper, bags, and essential consumables.
4. Walk hot zones first: entry, promo tables, queue area, and top-selling fixtures.
5. Refill known fast movers before trade starts.
6. Read overnight notes, returns pending, online pickup queue, and urgent incidents.

## During the First 30 Minutes

- Confirm displays match current prices and active promotions.
- Check obvious out-of-stocks and damaged packaging.
- Assign who owns replenishment, service lead, and break coverage.
- Make sure the team knows the day's target and the likely busy window.

## Closing Priorities

1. Cash-up with a second check on discrepancies, refunds, and suspicious transactions.
2. Recover the sales floor so the next opening does not start behind.
3. Replenish fast movers and prep high-risk lines for the morning.
4. Record incidents: shrink, complaints, equipment issues, or staffing gaps.
5. Lock stockroom, sensitive inventory, tills, and access points in the same order every night.
6. Leave the next-shift note with concrete actions, not vague warnings.

## Hard Rules

- Do not skip the opening walk because traffic starts early.
- Do not close by cleaning only the visible zones; cash, stock, and notes matter more.
- Do not carry unresolved discrepancies into the next day without logging them.
- Do not leave promo execution to memory; confirm it physically.
