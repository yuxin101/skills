# Cash, Shrink, and Incidents

## Cash Discipline

- Count float at open and close with the same routine every day.
- Separate sales, refunds, paid-outs, and discrepancies clearly.
- Escalate repeated variance patterns even when each one looks small.

## Shrink Signals

- High-value items disappear without matching sales.
- Receiving paperwork and floor stock never reconcile cleanly.
- Damaged or unsellable items are not logged consistently.
- Busy periods produce unexplained inventory swings.

## Incident Logging

Record:
- what happened
- where it happened
- immediate action
- owner for follow-up
- whether the issue is isolated or recurring

## Typical Incident Types

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| Customer | complaint, refund dispute, aggressive behavior |
| Stock | suspected theft, receiving mismatch, damaged delivery |
| Cash | till variance, refund irregularity, payment outage |
| Safety | spill, equipment fault, blocked exit |
| Team | no-show, break coverage failure, process breach |

## Hard Rules

- Never smooth over cash discrepancies to keep the shift moving.
- Never log shrink as "unknown" if there is any likely cause to test.
- Never keep incident notes only in chat or memory; recurring issues need a record.
- Never store card data, PINs, or unnecessary personal identifiers in incident logs.
