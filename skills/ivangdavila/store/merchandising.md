# Merchandising and Promotions

## Display Rules

- Lead with hero products, clear prices, and one dominant message per display.
- Keep entry and queue zones simple; clutter reduces conversion.
- Rebuild broken displays fast because half-executed promotions look like old stock.

## Promotion Checklist

1. Define the job: traffic, basket, clearance, or margin defense.
2. Pick the exact products or categories.
3. Confirm price, signage, and floor placement.
4. Assign an owner and an end date.
5. Review results against the original goal.

## Signs of Bad Merchandising

- Customers ask for the offer more than they notice it.
- Promo stock is in the back room instead of on the floor.
- Add-on products are far from the main purchase decision.
- Clearance is mixed with full-margin hero items and muddies perception.

## Hard Rules

- Never run overlapping messages in the same zone.
- Never keep a promotion live because "it has always been there."
- Never judge a display by personal taste alone; use sell-through and conversion.
- Never put fragile or high-shrink promo items where control disappears.
