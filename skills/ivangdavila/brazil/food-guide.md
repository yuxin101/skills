# Food Guide

## Use Food to Choose the Route

- Sao Paulo wins on density, variety, and depth.
- Rio wins when user wants scenic city plus strong but easier food planning.
- Salvador and Bahia win for identity, seafood, and food as cultural experience.
- Minas Gerais wins for comfort food, sweets, and slower culinary travel.
- Amazon wins for fish and ingredient distinctiveness, not for broad restaurant variety.

## Useful Food Pairings

- Sao Paulo for multi-day restaurant exploration
- Salvador for culture plus food
- Minas Gerais for inland food-first loops
- Rio for first-timers who want strong food without making it the only point

## Practical Rules

- Put one meal ambition per day, not three.
- For beach-heavy days, bias toward neighborhoods that recover easily at dinner time.
- In heat-heavy cities, lunch structure matters more than users expect.

## Food Traps

- Treating every famous market or old-center area as the best place to stay.
- Forcing luxury reservations into a route that already has transfer fatigue.
- Underestimating how much Sao Paulo can outperform other cities for dedicated food travelers.
