# Regions

## Use Brazil as a Portfolio of Trip Products

| Region | Best for | Main warning |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| Rio de Janeiro | First trip, iconic scenery, beach plus city | High transfer friction if hotel choice is wrong |
| Sao Paulo and Southeast | Food, culture, business, rainy-day resilience | Weak beach value unless paired with coast |
| Salvador and Bahia coast | Afro-Brazilian culture, music, warm-water coast | Humidity, holiday spikes, and airport-coast transfers |
| Foz do Iguacu | Falls, birding, easy 2-3 night add-on | Border logic can complicate short stays |
| Manaus and Amazon | Jungle lodges, river logic, wildlife | Needs time and weather tolerance |
| Pantanal and Bonito | Fauna, clear-water river activities, slower nature | Seasonal window matters more than city convenience |
| Florianopolis and Santa Catarina | Beach quality, summer families, lighter urban intensity | Seasonal traffic and car dependence in parts |
| Recife and Noronha | Colonial city plus premium island add-on | Noronha is expensive and capacity-sensitive |
| Minas Gerais and colonial core | Food, history, road loops, slower pace | Better by car or with selective bases |
| Brasilia and Chapada | Architecture plus savanna waterfalls and hikes | Road logistics and climate windows matter |

## Good Combinations

- Rio + Iguacu
- Rio + Salvador
- Sao Paulo + Rio
- Sao Paulo + Minas Gerais
- Recife + Noronha
- Pantanal + Bonito
- Brasilia + Chapada dos Veadeiros

## Bad Short-Trip Combinations

- Rio + Amazon + Iguacu in one week
- Sao Paulo + Salvador + Noronha + Rio in ten days
- Pantanal + Amazon + coast without recovery nights
- Two beach states plus one jungle block in one short trip

## Route Rule

For 7-10 days: one anchor plus one contrast block.

For 11-16 days: two blocks with one internal flight.

For 17-21 days: two major blocks plus one tactical add-on only if transport is clean.
