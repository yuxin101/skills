# Budget and Costs

## Budget Bands

| Band | What it usually means |
|------|-----------------------|
| Budget | Simple rooms, busier zones, local eats, selective flights |
| Mid-range | Better-located stays, some domestic flights, regular ride-hailing |
| Premium | Top neighborhoods, islands, lodges, private transfers, flexible timing |

## Where Trips Usually Break Budget

- Last-minute domestic flights
- Checked baggage on multi-flight routes
- New Year, Carnival, and school-holiday lodging spikes
- Noronha, premium beach areas, and rainforest lodges
- Repeated airport transfers because the route is too fragmented
- Paid tours that become mandatory in nature-heavy destinations

## Brazil-Specific Budget Logic

- Rio and Salvador can be affordable or expensive depending on location discipline.
- Sao Paulo is often more expensive for food and hotels, but it can reduce weather-risk waste.
- Pantanal, Bonito, and Noronha should be treated as experience-led budgets, not generic Brazil averages.
- A cheap flight combined with bad arrival time can create expensive transfer and safety stress.

## Safer Planning Rule

- Price the whole route, not each night in isolation.
- Add buffers for baggage, airport transport, island fees, guide costs, and weather-driven replans.
- If budget is tight, cut one destination before cutting hotel location and arrival safety.
