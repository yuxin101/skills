# National Parks and Nature

## Nature in Brazil Is Not One Product

- Iguacu is easy-access waterfall tourism.
- Noronha is a capacity-limited marine destination.
- Amazon is lodge and river logistics.
- Pantanal is wildlife timing.
- Chapada trips depend on roads, rains, and guide structure.

## Planning Questions First

- Is the priority scenery, wildlife, snorkeling, diving, hiking, or waterfalls?
- Does user want lodge immersion or urban comfort with day trips?
- Are heat, insects, rougher roads, or small-airport logistics acceptable?

## Booking Rules

- Check official park or island requirements before promising spontaneous access.
- Some nature destinations depend on paid guides, timed entries, boats, or environmental fees.
- For islands and rainforest lodges, book transport and lodging together when possible.

## Health Rules

- Check yellow-fever guidance early for interior and rainforest plans.
- Use sun, heat, and hydration planning as core logistics, not a final note.
- For river, jungle, and remote routes, include insect strategy and evacuation distance in the recommendation.

## Best Use Cases

- Iguacu: 2-3 nights
- Amazon: 4-6 nights minimum if jungle immersion is the main goal
- Pantanal plus Bonito: 5-8 nights
- Noronha: 4 nights minimum if the island is the purpose, not an add-on
