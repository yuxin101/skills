# Entry and Documents

## Start With Passport Logic

- Brazil entry rules depend on nationality, not on general travel blogs.
- Confirm the exact passport used for entry before building the route.
- For some nationalities, especially U.S., Canadian, and Australian passports, Brazil restored visa requirements and eVisa pathways in 2025. Verify the live rule before booking.

## Practical Entry Checklist

- Passport with adequate remaining validity and physical condition.
- Visa or eVisa if required for that passport.
- Proof of onward or return travel when airlines or border officers may ask for it.
- Accommodation details for the first stay.
- Travel insurance details and emergency contacts.

## Health and Vaccine Planning

- Yellow fever is not a universal urban-city issue, but it matters for some Brazil ecosystems.
- If route includes Amazon, Pantanal, Iguacu area, or other interior nature zones, check official health guidance early and leave enough time before travel.
- Do not promise last-minute vaccine solutions for remote-nature routes.

## Domestic Travel Documents

- Keep the original passport accessible for domestic flights and border hops.
- Store digital copies separately from the physical passport.
- For multi-stop trips, note exactly where the passport is needed again: airport check-in, island flights, cross-border day trips, and hotel registration.

## Planning Rules

- Solve entry first for visas, then route.
- Solve vaccines first for wildlife-heavy trips, then park bookings.
- Re-check entry rules if adding Argentina, Paraguay, or Uruguay side trips.
