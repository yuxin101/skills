# Rio de Janeiro

## Best For

- First-time Brazil trips
- Beach plus city in one base
- Scenic icon routes, light hiking, and nightlife

## Stay Logic

- Ipanema and Leblon are the easiest premium-first choices.
- Copacabana works well if user wants easier pricing and still accepts a busier feel.
- Santa Teresa is for atmosphere and views, not for easiest logistics.

## Route Design

- 4 nights is the minimum for a satisfying first trip.
- 5-6 nights works better if adding beaches, museums, Tijuca, or day trips.
- Do not overstuff every day; heat, traffic, and beach downtime are part of the destination.

## Who Should Not Default to Rio

- Travelers who want lower-stress urban movement above all
- Travelers who care more about food and culture density than scenery
- Users with very low comfort for beach-city security tradeoffs

## Strong Pairings

- Rio + Iguacu
- Rio + Salvador
- Rio + Paraty or Costa Verde

## Operational Notes

- Give an explicit airport-arrival plan, especially at night.
- Keep beach-day carry minimal.
- Recommend a clear return-home transport method after nightlife.
