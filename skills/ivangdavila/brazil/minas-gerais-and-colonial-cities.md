# Minas Gerais and Colonial Cities

## Best For

- Food-driven travel
- Slower Brazil with history and countryside
- Road-loop travelers who want contrast from coast-heavy trips

## Route Design

- Belo Horizonte can anchor the food side of the trip.
- Ouro Preto, Tiradentes, and similar towns work best with a selective road loop, not with a city-hopping sprint.
- Inhotim is a strong pull if user values art and a structured day excursion.

## Who It Fits

- Users who want something less obvious than Rio
- Repeat Brazil travelers
- Travelers who like culture and food more than beach intensity

## Operational Notes

- This block rewards a car or carefully chosen transfers.
- Historic towns can bring cobblestones, hills, and accessibility friction.
- Strong pairing with Sao Paulo or Rio if the user wants one famous city plus one deeper Brazil block.
