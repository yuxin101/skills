# Accommodation

## Neighborhood Beats Citywide Average

- In Brazil, staying in the wrong neighborhood can erase any savings through traffic, ride-hail cost, and uncomfortable arrivals.
- Recommend where to stay by daily pattern, not by city label alone.

## City Rules

### Rio de Janeiro
- Best all-round first-time bases: Ipanema, Leblon, or the safer end of Copacabana if budget is tighter.
- Santa Teresa works for atmosphere, but only if user accepts hills, taxis, and less beach immediacy.
- Avoid ultra-cheap edge locations that force repeated late-night transport decisions.

### Sao Paulo
- Stronger default bases: Jardins, Pinheiros, Itaim Bibi, and parts of Vila Madalena depending on nightlife tolerance.
- The best hotel is often the one that cuts cross-city traffic, not the cheapest central one.

### Salvador
- Barra works well for first-timers and shorter stays.
- Rio Vermelho suits food and nightlife.
- Historic-center stays can be atmospheric, but confirm comfort with terrain and evening movement.

## Nature and Beach Logic

- Noronha: stay near Vila dos Remedios or routes that reduce buggy or shuttle dependence.
- Pantanal and Amazon: prioritize lodge logic and transfers over property aesthetics.
- Florianopolis: choose north, east, or Lagoa area based on whether user wants families, surf, or nightlife.

## Accommodation Rule

Choose one of these priorities before booking:
- Walkable beach-life base
- Food-and-nightlife base
- Family-calm base
- Nature-access base
- Budget-first base with explicit transport tradeoff
