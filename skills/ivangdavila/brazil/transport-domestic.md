# Transport Domestic

## Flights Are Often the Real Backbone

- Brazil is large enough that internal flights often save entire days, not just hours.
- Use buses for regional moves, not as a default replacement for every flight.
- In short trips, each internal flight should justify the time it removes from the ground experience.

## Air Travel Rules

- Check baggage policy before booking the lowest fare.
- Leave airport buffers when changing terminals, airports, or city ends.
- Use official passenger-rights guidance for delays and cancellations rather than relying on airline summaries only.

## Bus and Ferry Logic

- Long-distance buses can work well for overnight or scenic regional connections where airports add little value.
- Ferries and boats are route-critical in island or Amazon contexts; weather and daylight matter more than map distance suggests.
- Keep one backup plan for any connection that depends on weather or a single departure.

## Urban Mobility

- Metro and commuter rail can be useful in Rio and Sao Paulo, but ride-hailing is often the simpler late-night or airport transfer tool.
- Do not overpromise walkability in cities with heat, hills, traffic, or fragmented neighborhoods.

## Routing Rule

For most international visitors:
- One internal flight is normal in a 7-10 day trip.
- Two internal flights are reasonable in a 12-16 day trip.
- More than that needs a strong reason.
