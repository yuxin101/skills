# Accessibility

## Accessibility Needs Destination Choice, Not Just Hotel Choice

- Brazil accessibility varies sharply by neighborhood, beach structure, terrain, and age of infrastructure.
- Historic centers, hillside neighborhoods, and older pousadas can add major friction even when the city itself is manageable.

## Better Bets

- Newer hotel corridors in Rio and Sao Paulo
- Planned island or lodge stays only if the property explains access honestly
- Family-oriented beach areas with easier vehicle access and flatter movement

## Friction Zones

- Cobblestone colonial towns
- Steep neighborhoods and hill districts
- Boat-heavy or buggy-heavy islands
- Trail and waterfall destinations without adapted access

## Planning Rules

- Ask about walking tolerance, stairs, chair use, and transfer assistance needs before choosing the region.
- Use official airport and airline assistance processes early for domestic flights.
- Bias toward fewer bases and simpler transfers.
