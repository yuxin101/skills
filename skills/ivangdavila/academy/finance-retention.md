# Finance and Retention

Academies usually think growth first and collections later. The stronger move is to design pricing and retention together.

## Pricing Structure

Clarify:
- one-time fee vs recurring fee
- deposit requirements
- installment logic
- pause policy
- make-up policy
- refund boundaries

If these are fuzzy, support and finance end up negotiating manually.

## Cash Collection Rules

Track a short ladder:
1. payment due
2. soft reminder
3. failed payment follow-up
4. escalation before the next class block
5. pause or stop according to policy

Make the ladder explicit so nobody improvises.

## Discount Discipline

Discounts should have a reason:
- early commitment
- sibling or volume logic
- low-demand slot fill
- strategic scholarship

Random discounts train the market to wait and hurt trust with current students.

## Retention Economics

The most useful numbers are often:
- average lifetime value
- renewal rate
- occupancy by class
- delinquency rate
- revenue per teacher hour

Those numbers show whether the model is healthy, not just whether money came in this month.

## Save vs Exit

Not every student should be saved.

Try to save when:
- the issue is schedule, onboarding, teacher fit, or payment friction

Let go cleanly when:
- the academy is the wrong fit
- expectations are impossible
- behavior hurts staff or class quality

Good exits protect reputation and team energy.
