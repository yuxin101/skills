# Academy Operating Models

Pick the closest model before giving structure. Most academy mistakes come from copying systems from the wrong model.

## 1. Cohort Academy

Examples: bootcamps, exam intensives, seasonal programs.

Main traits:
- fixed start and end dates
- clear seat count
- launch windows matter
- staffing is planned around one calendar

Optimize for:
- launch cadence
- cohort fill rate
- onboarding readiness
- completion and upsell into the next level

## 2. Membership Academy

Examples: language academies, arts schools, martial arts, recurring enrichment.

Main traits:
- monthly recurring revenue
- rolling enrollments or small entry windows
- class continuity matters more than launch drama
- retention is usually the growth engine

Optimize for:
- stable timetables
- class occupancy
- attendance consistency
- renewal and family lifetime value

## 3. Lesson-Based Academy

Examples: tutoring centers, music schools, private coaching with packages.

Main traits:
- 1:1 or semi-private delivery
- teacher matching matters a lot
- schedule friction creates churn fast
- unit economics depend on utilization

Optimize for:
- teacher utilization
- schedule fit
- session-pack consumption
- teacher-student matching quality

## 4. Multi-Site or Online Academy

Examples: branch-based centers, hybrid schools, creator academies.

Main traits:
- same offer runs across locations or delivery layers
- quality drifts unless rules are standardized
- support load can scale faster than teaching
- dashboards must compare sites, cohorts, or channels cleanly

Optimize for:
- operating consistency
- activation and attendance
- substitute or support coverage
- manager accountability

## Decision Rule

If unclear, identify:
1. how revenue is collected
2. how teaching is delivered
3. what capacity actually limits growth

Those three answers reveal the right model faster than labels do.
