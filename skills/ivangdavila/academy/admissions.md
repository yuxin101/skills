# Admissions and Enrollment

Admissions is not just lead capture. It is the system that turns the right prospects into students without breaking delivery.

## Funnel Stages

Use a simple progression:
1. inquiry
2. qualified lead
3. assessment or trial
4. offer sent
5. deposit or payment
6. enrolled
7. onboarded

Each stage needs:
- owner
- expected response time
- exit criteria
- next action

## Qualification Questions

Before pushing an offer, confirm:
- student age or level
- goal and urgency
- preferred format and schedule
- budget reality
- decision-maker
- blockers that could kill enrollment later

## Response Standards

- inbound inquiries should get a useful reply fast, not just a generic acknowledgment
- trials and assessments need a defined follow-up window
- no lead should end a day without a next step or clear disqualification

## Offer Discipline

Do not send vague offers.

Every offer should state:
- program or class
- start date
- frequency
- duration
- price
- payment terms
- what happens if they pause or miss sessions

## Enrollment Guardrails

Do not oversell classes with:
- weak occupancy economics
- no teacher assigned
- no room or timetable certainty
- unclear onboarding

The wrong enrollment is often worse than no enrollment.

## Common Conversion Leaks

- slow response after inquiry
- trial felt good but there was no immediate close sequence
- pricing was discussed without anchoring the outcome
- schedule mismatch discovered too late
- parents or decision-makers were not included early enough

## Best Next Move

When the user asks for growth help, first find whether the real leak is:
- lead quality
- conversion script
- offer clarity
- schedule fit
- trust and proof

Do not default to "run more ads" before checking the funnel.
