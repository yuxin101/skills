# Visitor Lodging - Thailand

## Bangkok

### Best for first-time visitors
- Riverside: scenic, easy boat routes, mixed luxury and mid-range.
- Sukhumvit (BTS zones): strong transit + food + nightlife access.
- Siam/Chidlom: central retail and rail convenience.

### Common mismatch
- Staying far from BTS/MRT to save small rent but losing hours daily.

## Chiang Mai

### Area fit
- Old City: walkable culture and cafes.
- Nimman: remote-worker friendly, modern apartments, café density.
- Riverside: calmer stays for slower pace.

## Phuket and Krabi

### Phuket
- Bang Tao: family and long-stay comfort.
- Kata/Karon: balanced beach-tourist experience.
- Patong: nightlife-heavy, often noisy.

### Krabi
- Ao Nang: practical transfer base.
- Railay: scenic but logistics-constrained.

## Gulf Islands

- Samui: broad lodging choices by budget.
- Phangan: split between party zones and wellness zones.
- Tao: diving-oriented compact base.

## Booking Strategy

- Book flexible rates in rainy season.
- Read flood/noise comments, not only photo quality.
- Confirm internet speed if user needs remote work.
- Verify cancellation policy for island and festival dates.

## Price Bands (Typical)

| Category | Bangkok | Chiang Mai | Phuket/Samui |
|----------|---------|------------|--------------|
| Budget | THB 600-1,500/night | THB 500-1,200/night | THB 700-1,800/night |
| Mid-range | THB 1,800-4,500/night | THB 1,200-3,200/night | THB 2,000-6,000/night |
| Premium | THB 5,000+ | THB 3,500+ | THB 6,000+ |

## Common Traps

- Choosing by photo aesthetics only, ignoring commute and noise.
- Booking non-flex rates during unstable weather windows.
- Ignoring distance to hospitals and schools for family stays.
