# Transport - Thailand

## National Movement Stack

1. Domestic flights for long distances and time-sensitive transfers.
2. Rail for cost-efficient and scenic intercity routes.
3. Intercity buses and vans where rail is limited.
4. Ferries and speedboats for island access.

## Bangkok Mobility

- BTS and MRT corridors are high-leverage for daily planning.
- Ride-hailing fills gaps where rail coverage is weak.
- Peak traffic can destroy naive ETA assumptions.

## Chiang Mai and Secondary Cities

- Local ride-hailing and shared transport are common.
- Car or motorbike can improve flexibility, with safety trade-offs.

## Islands and Coastal Movement

- Weather and sea conditions can delay ferry operations.
- Keep same-day international departure risk low by adding buffer days.

## Cost Guidance

| Mode | Typical range |
|------|---------------|
| Urban rail ride (Bangkok) | THB 17-70 |
| Ride-hailing short urban ride | THB 80-250 |
| Domestic economy flight | THB 900-2,500 |
| Intercity rail ticket | THB 300-1,200 |
| Ferry segments | THB 300-900 |

## Traps

- Booking hard-timed meetings after same-day island transfers.
- Choosing remote lodging with no reliable transport fallback.
- Recommending scooters to users with low road-confidence.
