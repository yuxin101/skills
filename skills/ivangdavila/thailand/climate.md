# Climate and Seasons - Thailand

## National Pattern

Thailand has year-round heat with regional differences in rain timing and intensity. Planning quality depends on month and region, not just annual averages.

## Core Seasons

| Season | Typical impact |
|-------|-----------------|
| Hot season | High heat load, lower daytime comfort |
| Rainy season | Storm disruptions, ferry and road uncertainty |
| Cooler season | Better comfort, stronger tourism demand |

## Regional Nuance

- Bangkok: heat and humidity dominate much of the year.
- North: smoke season can outweigh temperature comfort.
- Andaman and Gulf coasts: monsoon patterns differ by side and month.

## Air Quality

- Burning-related pollution can materially affect north Thailand.
- Health-sensitive users should plan with live AQ data, not historical averages only.

## Planning Rules

- Schedule outdoor-heavy activities early or late in the day.
- Keep backup indoor plans for storm windows.
- In smoke-sensitive months, prioritize south/coastal alternatives.
