# Teaching in Thailand

## Main Teaching Paths

- International schools (higher qualification expectations).
- Private bilingual schools.
- Language centers and private institutions.
- University and specialized education roles.

## Practical Reality

- Compensation varies sharply by institution tier and location.
- Bangkok and Phuket often pay more but cost more.
- Chiang Mai may pay less with better cost balance.

## Qualification Considerations

- Degree and credential requirements vary by school category.
- Experience and native-level English can materially change options.
- Work-authorization compliance is non-negotiable.

## Job-Selection Checklist

- Contract clarity: salary, teaching load, holidays, visa support.
- School reputation and teacher retention pattern.
- Commute and quality-of-life fit with salary package.

## Traps

- Accepting unclear contracts tied to informal visa promises.
- Comparing offers by salary only, ignoring workload and housing costs.
- Underestimating term-time intensity and admin burden.
