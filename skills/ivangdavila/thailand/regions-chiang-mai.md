# Regions - Chiang Mai and North

## Best For

- Remote workers prioritizing lower cost and calmer pace.
- Culture-focused travelers and long-stay learners.
- Users who want mountain access and slower urban tempo.

## Core Strengths

- Lower housing and daily spending than Bangkok or Phuket.
- Strong café and coworking ecosystem around Nimman and Old City areas.
- Good base for north Thailand loops.

## Friction Points

- Burning season can materially affect air quality and outdoor life.
- Smaller high-income job market compared with Bangkok.
- Specialist healthcare still narrower than Bangkok.

## Area Orientation

| Area | Fit |
|------|-----|
| Old City | Heritage, walkability, visitor-first convenience |
| Nimman | Remote-worker density, modern apartments, café ecosystem |
| Riverside | Quieter long-stay profile |

## Usage Guidance

- For health-sensitive users, treat smoke-season dates as blocking constraints.
- For long stays, test internet quality at property level, not neighborhood averages.
- Pair Chiang Mai with one Bangkok block for big-ticket admin and healthcare tasks.
