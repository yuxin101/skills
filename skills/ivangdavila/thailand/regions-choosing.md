# Regions Choosing Framework - Thailand

## Step 1: Anchor the Primary Goal

- Trip efficiency
- Relocation and daily quality of life
- Remote work productivity
- Family systems (school + healthcare)
- Business setup and network density

## Step 2: Score Candidate Regions

Use a 1-5 score for each item:
- Cost fit
- Climate and air-quality fit
- Mobility fit
- Healthcare fit
- School fit (if needed)
- Work/business fit
- Lifestyle fit

## Step 3: Apply This Matrix

| User profile | Primary recommendation | Backup recommendation |
|-------------|------------------------|-----------------------|
| First-time visitor | Bangkok + one contrast region | Bangkok + Chiang Mai |
| Budget remote worker | Chiang Mai | Bangkok outer BTS zones |
| Family relocation | Bangkok or Bang Tao (Phuket) | Chiang Mai with Bangkok fallback |
| Founder and operator | Bangkok | Phuket for hospitality-specific models |
| Lifestyle sabbatical | Samui/Phangan or Phuket | Chiang Mai slow-living route |

## Step 4: Pilot Before Commitment

- Short pilot stay before annual rental or school-year commitment.
- Validate real commute, noise, internet, and healthcare access.
- Re-check legal and visa feasibility before long financial commitments.

## Hard Stops

- If legal status is unclear, pause relocation decisions.
- If health needs are complex, prioritize top-tier medical-access cities.
- If user hates humidity, avoid overpromising tropical comfort.
