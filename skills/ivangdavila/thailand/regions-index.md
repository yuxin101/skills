# Regions Index - Thailand

## Thailand at a Glance

| Region style | Best for | Watch-outs |
|-------------|----------|------------|
| Bangkok metro | Career, finance, major healthcare, global connectivity | Traffic, heat, pollution windows, higher rent |
| Northern hub (Chiang Mai) | Lower-cost living, culture, remote work | Burning season, smaller salary market |
| Andaman coast (Phuket/Krabi) | Beach lifestyle, tourism economy, premium hospitality | Seasonal spikes, weather disruption |
| Gulf islands (Samui/Phangan/Tao) | Wellness, diving, lifestyle stays | Transport dependence, weather volatility |
| Isan and secondary provinces | Low cost, local immersion, slower pace | Lower English-service density, fewer specialist services |

## Decision Anchors

1. Purpose: short trip, scouting, relocation, or business launch.
2. Budget tolerance for housing, schools, and healthcare tier.
3. Climate tolerance: humidity, rain, smoke, and commute exposure.
4. Mobility confidence: transit, driving, scooter, or flights/ferries.

## First-Pass Matching

- User needs strong hospitals and international schools: Bangkok or major Phuket zones.
- User wants lower cost and work-life calm: Chiang Mai-focused strategy.
- User is lifestyle-first with flexible schedule: island or coast bases.
- User is deep-culture and local immersion: north plus selected secondary provinces.

## Sequencing Rule

Start with one primary base and one backup base. Expand only after timeline, legal status, and budget are clear.
