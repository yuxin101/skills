# Visitor Tips - Thailand

## Arrival Checklist

- Verify entry method and TDAC requirements before departure.
- Keep passport validity buffer and proof of onward travel when required.
- Download ride-hailing and map apps before arrival.

## Money and Payments

- Carry mixed payment strategy: card + ATM cash withdrawals.
- ATM fees are common; batch withdrawals reduce repeated fees.
- QR payment is strong locally but not universal for non-residents.

## Connectivity

- Buy airport SIM/eSIM from major providers.
- Confirm data cap and hotspot rules if working remotely.
- Save key offline maps for islands and mountain routes.

## Health and Comfort

- Hydration and heat management are core productivity issues.
- Use trusted bottled or filtered water strategies.
- Keep insect protection for tropical and rural zones.

## Practical Behavior

- Dress appropriately for temples and government offices.
- Keep voice and body language calm in conflict situations.
- Avoid risky road behavior, especially on scooters.

## Festival and Holiday Effects

- Songkran can disrupt normal routines, transport, and electronics safety.
- Loy Krathong creates peak demand in certain cities and river zones.
- Long weekends can spike domestic travel and accommodation prices.

## Quick Safety Defaults

- Use licensed transport and platform booking trails.
- Keep digital and paper copies of key documents.
- Share transfer plans for late-night intercity movement.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Overpacking daily plans in high heat and traffic windows.
- Assuming every island connection runs on schedule in monsoon periods.
- Treating visa and stay rules as fixed from old forum posts.
