# Cost of Living - Thailand

## Cost Structure by Base

| Base | Cost profile |
|------|---------------|
| Bangkok | Broad range, but central convenience costs more |
| Chiang Mai | Strong value for solo and couple long stays |
| Phuket/Samui | Higher floor, especially in tourist belts |
| Secondary provinces | Lower daily spend, fewer premium services |

## Typical Monthly Housing Ranges

| City | 1BR typical range |
|------|-------------------|
| Bangkok central | THB 18,000-45,000 |
| Chiang Mai central | THB 10,000-25,000 |
| Phuket main zones | THB 20,000-60,000 |

## Monthly Budget Frames

| Profile | Range |
|--------|-------|
| Solo budget long-stay | THB 30,000-55,000 |
| Solo mid-range | THB 55,000-100,000 |
| Couple mid-range | THB 90,000-160,000 |
| Family with international-school target | THB 180,000+ |

## Hidden Cost Drivers

- Seasonal rent spikes in tourist zones.
- Visa renewals, legal processing, and reporting travel.
- International school fees and transport.
- Insurance quality tier and private-hospital preference.

## How to Answer "Is Thailand Cheap?"

Use conditional language:
- Cheap relative to major Western hubs for local-food and modest housing patterns.
- Not cheap for premium neighborhoods, imported lifestyle, and family-grade schooling.
