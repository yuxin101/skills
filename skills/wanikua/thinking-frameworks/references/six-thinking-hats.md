# Six Thinking Hats

**Six Thinking Hats** is Edward de Bono's parallel thinking framework that separates thinking into six distinct modes, each represented by a colored hat. Instead of everyone arguing from entrenched positions, the group (or individual) systematically wears each hat in turn, ensuring all dimensions of a problem are explored without the confusion of mixing them. It prevents the loudest voice or dominant emotion from hijacking the analysis.

---

Analyze the following problem by wearing each of the **Six Thinking Hats** in sequence. Give each hat full, genuine effort.

**Problem / Topic:**
$ARGUMENTS

---

## 🔵 Blue Hat — Process & Meta-Thinking (Facilitator)

*The Blue Hat manages the thinking process itself.*

- What exactly is the **subject** of our thinking? Restate it clearly.
- What is the **goal** of this analysis? What do we need to decide or understand?
- What **sequence** of hats will be most productive? (Default: Blue → White → Red → Yellow → Black → Green → Blue)
- What are the **constraints** (time, resources, context)?

---

## ⚪ White Hat — Facts & Information (Data Analyst)

*The White Hat deals ONLY with objective data. No opinions, no interpretations.*

- What **facts and data** do we currently have about this topic?
- What are the **key numbers, statistics, and measurements**?
- What information is **missing** that we need?
- What information is **disputed or uncertain**? (State both sides without judging.)
- What is the **source and reliability** of our data?
- Distinguish clearly between: **verified facts** / **believed but unverified** / **gaps in knowledge**

---

## 🔴 Red Hat — Feelings & Intuition (Gut Check)

*The Red Hat captures emotions, hunches, and intuitions — WITHOUT justification. No need to explain why you feel this way.*

- What is the **gut reaction** to this problem/proposal?
- What **emotions** does it evoke? (Excitement? Anxiety? Skepticism? Enthusiasm? Dread?)
- What does **intuition** suggest — even if it can't be logically justified?
- How might **other stakeholders** feel about this? (Users, customers, team members, competitors)
- Are there any **aesthetic or visceral** responses? ("This feels elegant" / "This feels clunky")
- What are the **unspoken fears** or **hopes** surrounding this topic?

---

## 🟡 Yellow Hat — Benefits & Optimism (Advocate)

*The Yellow Hat seeks value and benefits. Be genuinely constructive — this is NOT about being naively positive, but about finding real value.*

- What are the **best-case outcomes** if this works?
- What **specific benefits** does this approach offer? (List concretely, not vaguely)
- What **opportunities** does this create that don't currently exist?
- Who benefits most, and how?
- What is the **logical basis for optimism**? (Not just "it could be great" — why specifically?)
- What **existing strengths** can we leverage?
- Even if the overall idea has problems, what **elements** are genuinely valuable and worth preserving?

---

## ⚫ Black Hat — Risks & Caution (Devil's Advocate)

*The Black Hat is the critical judge — finding risks, dangers, and weaknesses. This is the most natural hat for many people, but it must be used fairly, not as a weapon.*

- What could go **wrong**? List specific failure modes.
- What are the **risks** (probability × impact)?
- What **weaknesses** exist in this plan/idea?
- Does this contradict any **known facts** (from White Hat)?
- What **past precedents** suggest this could fail?
- What are the **worst-case scenarios**?
- What **legal, ethical, or regulatory** concerns exist?
- Is the timing right, or are there **environmental factors** working against this?
- Where are the **hidden costs** (financial, social, opportunity)?

---

## 🟢 Green Hat — Creativity & Alternatives (Innovator)

*The Green Hat generates new ideas, alternatives, and possibilities. Judgment is suspended — all ideas are welcome.*

- What are **alternative approaches** we haven't considered?
- Can we **modify, combine, or adapt** existing ideas?
- What **provocative ideas** challenge our assumptions? (Even if impractical)
- How could we overcome the risks identified by the Black Hat?
- What would a **completely different type of organization/person** do?
- What if we **changed one fundamental constraint**?
- Generate at least **5 fresh ideas or modifications**, ranging from conservative to radical.
- What **experiments** could we run to test uncertain areas?

---

## 🔵 Blue Hat — Synthesis & Next Steps (Wrap-Up)

*Return to the Blue Hat to synthesize findings.*

- **Summary**: What did each hat reveal?
  - Facts (White): _
  - Feelings (Red): _
  - Benefits (Yellow): _
  - Risks (Black): _
  - Alternatives (Green): _
- **Key tensions**: Where do the hats conflict? (e.g., Yellow sees opportunity but Black sees risk)
- **Decision or recommendation**: Based on the complete analysis, what should we do?
- **Next steps**: What are the concrete, immediate actions?
- **What needs more exploration?** Which hat's analysis needs deeper work?

---

The power of Six Hats is in the **separation** — by wearing one hat at a time, we avoid the muddle of trying to be creative, critical, and factual simultaneously. Each mode gets its full due.
