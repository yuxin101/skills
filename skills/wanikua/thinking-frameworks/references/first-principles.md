# First Principles Thinking

**First principles thinking** is the practice of stripping away all assumptions, conventions, and analogies to reduce a problem to its most fundamental truths — then reasoning upward from those truths to construct novel solutions. Popularized by Aristotle and practiced relentlessly by Elon Musk, it replaces "because that's how it's always been done" with "what do we know to be true, and what can we build from there?"

## Step 1: State the Problem Clearly

- Restate the problem in the simplest possible terms.
- What exactly are we trying to achieve? What is the desired end state?
- Separate the **goal** from the **current method** of achieving it. (The goal is fundamental; the method is not.)

## Step 2: Identify Current Assumptions and Conventions

- List every assumption, convention, and "best practice" currently surrounding this problem.
- For each, label it:
  - **Physical law / hard constraint** — Cannot be violated (physics, math, legal requirements)
  - **Convention** — "How it's usually done" but not a law of nature
  - **Assumption** — Believed to be true but not rigorously verified
- Be ruthless. Most "constraints" are actually conventions in disguise.

## Step 3: Decompose to Fundamental Truths

- Strip away all conventions and assumptions. What remains?
- What are the **irreducible components** of this problem?
- What do we know to be true from:
  - Physics / chemistry / biology (natural laws)
  - Mathematics / logic (formal truths)
  - Verified empirical data (measured facts)
  - Economic fundamentals (supply, demand, incentives)
- Express these as simple, concrete statements.

## Step 4: Reconstruct from the Ground Up

- Starting ONLY from the fundamental truths identified above, reason forward:
  - What is the **theoretical best** solution unconstrained by convention?
  - What would someone with zero knowledge of the existing approach design?
  - What new combinations or architectures become possible when you ignore "how it's always been done"?
- Generate at least **3 novel approaches** built from first principles.

## Step 5: Reality Check and Feasibility

- For each novel approach:
  - What are the **real** constraints (not conventions) that limit it?
  - What would it cost (time, money, effort) to implement?
  - What is the gap between the first-principles ideal and practical reality?
  - What intermediate steps could bridge that gap?

## Step 6: Compare with Conventional Approach

- How does the first-principles solution differ from the conventional one?
- Where does convention actually align with first principles (i.e., the current way is already optimal)?
- Where are the biggest divergences — and what opportunities do they represent?

## Step 7: Actionable Output

- Recommend the most promising first-principles approach with a concrete implementation path.
- Identify the single biggest assumption that, if challenged, would unlock the most value.

---

Remember: first principles thinking is expensive and slow — that's why it's reserved for problems where convention may be leading us astray. If convention works, say so. But if there's a 10x improvement hiding behind unexamined assumptions, find it.
