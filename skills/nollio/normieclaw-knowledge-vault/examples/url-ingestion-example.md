## How to Do Great Work
**Source:** https://paulgraham.com/greatwork.html
**Type:** Article
**Author:** Paul Graham
**Date:** July 2023
**Length:** ~25 min read

### Executive Summary
Paul Graham outlines a unified framework for doing great work across any field. The core thesis: great work happens when you find something you're excessively interested in, develop deep expertise, then notice gaps that others have missed. It's not about following a predetermined plan — it's about cultivating curiosity and having the courage to pursue what you find.

### Key Takeaways
1. **Interest is the compass.** Don't try to plan your way to great work. Instead, follow what genuinely fascinates you — even if it seems impractical. The best work comes from compulsive interest, not discipline.
2. **Boldness beats caution.** The biggest risk isn't making mistakes — it's staying safe. Great work requires attempting things that might not work and being willing to look foolish.
3. **Copy then diverge.** Start by learning existing work in your field. As you develop taste and skill, you'll naturally notice what's missing or wrong. That's where your original contribution lives.
4. **Work on hard problems you can see.** Don't go looking for "important problems" in the abstract. Work on things that are hard and interesting to YOU — importance often follows.
5. **Consistency compounds.** Small amounts of focused work every day beat sporadic bursts. Great work is almost always the result of years of steady effort, not genius moments.

### Actionable Insights
- Pick the intersection of your top 3 interests and explore it deeply for 6 months before judging the results
- Maintain a "curiosity log" — write down things that surprise you or don't make sense. These are potential research threads
- Share work early and often to get feedback, even when it feels premature

### Notable Quotes
> "The way to figure out what to work on is by working." — Paul Graham
> "If you're not sometimes surprised by what you write, you're not pushing hard enough." — Paul Graham
