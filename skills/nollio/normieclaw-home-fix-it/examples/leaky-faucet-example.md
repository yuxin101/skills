**User:** [Uploads photo of water pooling at the base of a bathroom sink faucet] "Why is my sink doing this?"

**Home Fix-It:**
**Diagnosis:** Based on the water pooling specifically at the base of the handle when turned on, you likely have a worn-out O-ring or a cracked faucet cartridge in your Moen bathroom faucet.

**Difficulty Rating:** 🟢 2/5 (Beginner DIY - approx. 30-45 minutes)

**Cost Estimator:**
- **Cost to DIY:** $10 - $25 (Parts only)
- **Cost to Hire Pro:** $120 - $175 (Labor + Parts)

**⚠️ Safety First:** 
YELLOW ZONE: Turn off the hot and cold water supply valves under the sink before starting to avoid flooding!

**Parts Needed:**
- Replacement cartridge or O-ring kit (Take the old one to Home Depot/Lowe's to match it exactly)
- Plumber's grease

**Tools Needed:**
- Allen wrench (usually 1/8" or 3/32")
- Adjustable wrench or Channellock pliers (Workaround: regular pliers with a rag to protect the finish)
- Flathead screwdriver

**Step-by-Step Fix:**
1. **Shut it off:** Turn the two oval valves under the sink clockwise until tight. Turn on the faucet to bleed remaining water.
2. **Remove the handle:** Look for a tiny set screw under the handle lever. Use your Allen wrench to loosen it and pull the handle straight up.
3. **Remove the retaining nut:** Use your adjustable wrench to unscrew the dome or brass nut holding the cartridge in place.
4. **Pull the cartridge:** Pull it straight out. Note the orientation!
5. **Replace and grease:** Apply plumber's grease to the new O-rings/cartridge, insert it, and reassemble.
