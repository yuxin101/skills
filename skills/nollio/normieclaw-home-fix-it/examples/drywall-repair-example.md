**User:** [Uploads photo of a 3-inch hole in the living room drywall] "A doorknob went through the wall. How do I fix this?"

**Home Fix-It:**
**Diagnosis:** That is a classic doorknob puncture hole, approximately 3 inches wide. Since it's relatively small, we can patch it easily without needing to cut back to the studs.

**Difficulty Rating:** 🟢 2/5 (Beginner DIY - active work: 20 mins, total time with drying: 24 hrs)

**Cost Estimator:**
- **Cost to DIY:** $15 - $30 (Patch kit & paint)
- **Cost to Hire Pro:** $150 - $250 (Minimum service call fee)

**⚠️ Safety First:** 
GREEN ZONE: Safe DIY. No major hazards. Ensure the area is clear of dust.

**Parts Needed:**
- 4x4 inch self-adhesive drywall patch (aluminum mesh)
- Small tub of lightweight joint compound (spackle)
- Sanding sponge (fine grit)
- Touch-up paint matching your wall

**Tools Needed:**
- 4-inch or 6-inch putty knife (Workaround: A sturdy plastic scraper or even an old credit card for very small holes)
- Utility knife to clean the edges

**Step-by-Step Fix:**
1. **Prep the hole:** Use the utility knife to scrape away any loose paper or jagged drywall edges around the hole. It should be relatively clean.
2. **Apply the patch:** Peel the backing off the self-adhesive patch and stick it directly over the hole. Press firmly.
3. **First coat:** Load a small amount of joint compound onto your putty knife. Smear it over the patch, pressing it through the mesh. Feather the edges out slightly wider than the patch. Let dry completely (check the tub for times, usually a few hours).
4. **Sand and second coat:** Lightly sand the dried compound until smooth. Apply a second, thinner coat, feathering the edges even wider to blend it into the wall. Let dry.
5. **Finish:** Sand smoothly, wipe away dust, and paint!
