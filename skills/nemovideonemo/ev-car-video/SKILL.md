# EV Car Video Maker — Electric Vehicle Reviews and EV Lifestyle Videos

The range says 310 miles, the highway trip is 280 miles, and the driver just discovered that "310 miles" was measured at 55 mph on a warm day with the AC off and the tires inflated to precisely the pressure that makes the ride feel like sitting on a park bench — conditions that exist in a lab but never on an actual road trip where the temperature is 38°F, the heater is blasting, and the speed is 75 mph because the truck behind you is impatient. Electric vehicle content is the most debate-generating automotive category because every video triggers a comments war between EV evangelists who insist charging is "not a problem" and skeptics who act like running out of charge means being stranded forever in a cornfield. The channels that cut through the noise are the ones that show real charging stops with real timestamps, real range at real highway speeds, and honest opinions about when an EV is the right car and when it isn't. This tool transforms raw EV footage into polished electric vehicle videos — real-world range tests with mile-by-mile efficiency tracking, charging-session recordings with animated charge-curve graphs, home-charging setup walkthroughs with electrical-panel close-ups, energy-cost-per-mile comparisons against gas equivalents, cold-weather range-loss documentation with temperature overlays, and the long-distance road-trip format that answers the only question every potential EV buyer has: "Can I actually drive this thing to Grandma's house?" Built for EV-focused YouTube channels, automotive journalists covering the electric transition, dealership marketing teams creating EV education content, charging-network companies producing user-guide videos, fleet managers building EV-training libraries, and anyone who bought an electric car and wants to share whether the ownership experience matches the brochure.

## Example Prompts

### 1. Real-World Range Test — Highway Efficiency
"Create an 8-minute real-world range test video. The EV is rated at 310 miles EPA. We're testing it at actual highway speed. Opening (0-15 sec): the trip computer reset to zero — full charge, 100%, 310 miles estimated. 'The EPA says 310 miles. The EPA also tested this at 55 mph. Nobody drives 55 on the highway. Let's find out the real number at 75 mph with the climate on.' Test conditions (15-45 sec): text overlay — Temperature: 52°F, Climate: 72°F set, Tires: factory all-seasons at 42 PSI, Speed: cruise at 75 mph, Route: highway loop with minimal stops. 'Same conditions as a normal person on a road trip. No hypermiling, no drafting behind trucks, no eco mode.' Driving (45-300 sec): highway footage with a data overlay: remaining range, speed, efficiency (Wh/mi), outside temperature, and percentage. Checkpoint updates every 50 miles: 50 miles driven, range dropped by 68 miles. 'We're consuming 1.36 miles of range per actual mile at this speed.' At 100 miles: range shows 172 remaining (consumed 138 of estimated for 100 actual). Efficiency: 310 Wh/mi. 'EPA assumes 250 Wh/mi. Real highway at 75 mph is 310 Wh/mi — a 24% penalty.' At 150 miles: range anxiety segment. 'We're at 38% battery. The next charger is 45 miles away. Do we push to 200 miles or charge now?' Show the charging map on the screen — the decision-making process. At 200 miles: arrive at charger with 12% remaining. 'EPA said 310. At 75 mph with climate, the real number is roughly 240 miles usable range.' Show the final trip computer: 200 miles driven, 88% battery consumed. Extrapolation graphic: 100% would yield approximately 227 miles at this speed. Charging (300-400 sec): plug in at a DC fast charger. Show the charging curve animated: 10% to 80% — timestamp on screen. The first 50% charges fast (28 minutes). 80% to 100% slows dramatically (another 35 minutes). 'This is why you charge to 80% on road trips. The last 20% takes as long as the first 70%.' Cost: $14.20 for the session — show the price per kWh and total. Gas equivalent: 'A comparable gas SUV at $3.50/gallon would cost $28 for the same 200 miles. The EV is half the fuel cost even at DC fast-charging prices.' Verdict (400-480 sec): range reality graphic — EPA 310 vs Real Highway 227 (a 27% gap). 'Every EV has this gap. Budget for 70-75% of the EPA rating at highway speed. In cold weather, budget for 60%.' Closing: 'The range is real enough for 95% of daily driving. For road trips, you plan charger stops. That's the trade-off in 2026.'"

### 2. Home Charging Setup — Installation Guide
"Build a 6-minute home EV charging installation video. Opening: the Level 1 charger that came with the car plugged into a regular outlet — 'This adds 4 miles of range per hour. If you drive 40 miles a day, it recovers overnight. If you drive 80 miles, you're slowly falling behind. Here's how to install a Level 2 charger that adds 30 miles per hour.' Electrical panel assessment (0-80 sec): open the panel — show the breaker layout. 'You need a 50-amp 240V circuit. That requires two open slots in your panel for a double-pole breaker.' Show the breaker identification. 'If your panel is full, you need either a sub-panel ($300-$500 installed) or a load-sharing device.' Panel capacity check: main breaker rating (200A shown), total existing load calculation — 'Add up every breaker. If the total plus 50A exceeds 80% of your main breaker, you need a panel upgrade or load management.' Charger selection (80-140 sec): show three options on screen with specs and prices. Hardwired 48A ($450-$650): fastest, permanent install. Plug-in 40A with NEMA 14-50 ($350-$500): portable, can take it when you move. Smart charger with app ($500-$700): schedule charging for off-peak rates, monitor energy use. 'If you own the house, hardwire. If you rent or might move, NEMA 14-50 plug.' Installation (140-320 sec): show the electrician's process (or DIY if permitted — 'Check your local codes. Some jurisdictions require a licensed electrician for 240V circuits.'). Run the 6-gauge wire from the panel to the garage (show the wire gauge — 'This is 6 AWG copper for a 50A circuit. Undersizing wire is a fire hazard.'). Mount the charger on the wall — show the bracket, the level, the mounting bolts. Wire connection: breaker off, wire into the charger terminal block (labeled: L1, L2, Ground). New 50A breaker installed in the panel. First charge test (320-380 sec): plug in the car. Show the charger light going green, the car's screen showing 'Charging — 9.6kW.' 'That's 32 miles of range per hour. A full charge from empty: about 10 hours overnight. You'll wake up to 100% every morning.' Cost analysis (380-420 sec): charger: $500. Installation: $500-$1,200 depending on panel distance and local rates. Monthly electricity: show the math — '12,000 miles/year ÷ 3.5 mi/kWh = 3,430 kWh × $0.12/kWh = $411/year in electricity. Gas equivalent at 28 mpg and $3.50/gallon: $1,500/year. Savings: $1,089/year.' The charger pays for itself in 6-12 months. Closing: the car plugged in overnight, the app showing charging scheduled for off-peak hours (11 PM - 6 AM). 'You'll never go to a gas station again for daily driving. That alone is worth the installation.'"

### 3. Cold Weather Range — Winter Driving Reality
"Produce a 5-minute cold-weather EV range test. Opening: thermometer showing 18°F. The car display shows 100% charge, estimated range: 215 miles (vs 310 rated). 'It's 18 degrees and the car already lost 30% of its range just by being cold. Let's see what's left after driving.' Cold battery science (0-50 sec): animated battery cross-section — 'Lithium-ion batteries have higher internal resistance when cold. The ions move slower through the electrolyte. That means less available energy and slower charging.' Show the preconditioning feature: 'If you preheat the car while plugged in, the battery and cabin warm up on grid power, not battery power. That's free range.' The drive (50-250 sec): 40-mile commute simulation — highway and city mix. Climate set to 72°F, heated seats on, heated steering wheel on. Efficiency overlay throughout. 'Heated seats use 75 watts. The cabin heater uses 3,000-5,000 watts. That's why heated seats are the cheat code — they keep you warm while using 1/50th the energy of heating the air.' At 20 miles: efficiency at 420 Wh/mi (vs summer average of 280). Range remaining: 128 miles from 100%. At 40 miles: arrived with 68% battery, range showing 108 miles. 'Forty miles of driving consumed 32% of the battery. In summer, that's 18%. The cold tax is real.' Charging in cold (250-320 sec): plug in at a DC fast charger. The charging speed: 45kW instead of the usual 150kW. 'The battery is cold. The car is limiting charge speed to protect the cells.' After 10 minutes of preconditioning the battery: speed climbs to 120kW. 'Some EVs precondition automatically when you navigate to a charger. If yours doesn't, this is the wait.' Mitigation tips (320-360 sec): animated tips list — precondition while plugged in, use heated seats over cabin heat, park in a garage (even unheated: 30°F warmer than outside), keep the battery above 20% in extreme cold, schedule departure time so the car preps itself. Closing: the winter range reality graphic — Rated 310 → Cold Highway Real: ~185 miles (40% loss). 'An EV in winter is still cheaper per mile than gas. But you need to plan for 40% less range and slower charging. That's the honest answer.'"

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Show the data overlay throughout driving segments** — Speed, efficiency, remaining range, and temperature on screen lets viewers follow the energy consumption in real time. The AI generates persistent data HUDs from your described metrics.
2. **Animate the charging curve** — The 10-80% charge speed dropping over time is the core insight for road-trip planning. The AI renders animated charge-curve graphs from your charging session timestamps.
3. **Compare EV cost to the gas equivalent** — "$14 for 200 miles vs $28 in gas" makes the value proposition concrete. The AI generates side-by-side cost graphics when you provide both EV charging costs and gas-equivalent calculations.
4. **Include the range gap honestly** — EPA vs real-world at actual highway speed is the most requested data point. The AI creates comparison graphics showing rated vs tested range with the percentage gap highlighted.
5. **Show preconditioning as a practical tip** — Preheating while plugged in is the single most effective range-preservation technique. The AI highlights preconditioning segments with energy-savings callouts when you describe the feature.
