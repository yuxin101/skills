# Market Entry & Exit

## Metadata
- **Name**: market-entry-exit
- **Description**: Barrier analysis for industry attractiveness
- **Triggers**: barriers to entry, entry barriers, exit barriers, market attractiveness

## Instructions
Analyze barriers that influence companies' decisions to enter or leave markets.

## Framework

### Types of Barriers

| Type | Description | Examples |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **Structural Barriers** | Economies of scale, capital requirements, brand equity | Manufacturing, utilities, telco |
| **Behavioral Barriers** | Expected retaliation, customer switching costs, channel conflicts | Software, retail, services |
| **Regulatory Barriers** | Licenses, patents, standards, tariffs | Pharma, finance, healthcare |

### Porter's Exit Barriers Framework

| Barrier Type | Typical Indicators |
|----------------|-------------------|
| **Entry Barriers** | Capital intensity, brand loyalty needed, access to distribution, cost disadvantages |
| **Exit Barriers** | Specialized assets, long-term contracts, strategic relationships, emotional attachment |

## Tips

- Assess both entry and exit - don't ignore leaving dynamics
- Barriers change over time - monitor regularly
- Consider industry lifecycle - mature vs growing
- Evaluate both demand and supply sides
- Regulatory barriers often outweigh market barriers
- Exit analysis prevents sunk cost fallacies - be objective
